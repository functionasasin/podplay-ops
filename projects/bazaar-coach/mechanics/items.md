# Item System

## Overview

Items are the core building blocks of every run. You acquire them from merchants, PvE encounters, events, and level-up rewards. Items go on your board (rug) to activate during combat, or in your stash for storage and passive effects.

## Item Sizes

Items come in three sizes, each occupying a different number of board slots:

| Size   | Slots | Notes                                      |
|--------|-------|--------------------------------------------|
| Small  | 1     | Cheapest, least powerful individually       |
| Medium | 2     | Mid-range power and cost                    |
| Large  | 3     | Most powerful, most expensive, space-hungry |

## The Board (Rug)

The rug is your active play area. Items placed here trigger during combat (both cooldown-based and passive effects).

- **Starting slots**: 4
- **Slots gained per level**: +2
- **Maximum slots**: 10
- Board space is limited -- you must choose carefully between many small items vs. fewer large items
- Managing board space is one of the most important strategic decisions

### Board Layout Examples (10 slots)

- 10 Small items (maximum item count)
- 5 Medium items
- 3 Large + 1 Small
- 2 Large + 2 Medium
- 1 Large + 2 Medium + 2 Small
- Mix and match based on your build

## The Stash (Storage Chest)

The stash is your reserve inventory. Items placed here do NOT activate during combat, but:

- **Start-of-Day and Each-Hour effects still trigger from the stash** (e.g., Lemonade Stand, Money Tree, Cash Register)
- Items in stash are safe storage -- hold items you plan to use later or that have out-of-combat effects
- **Stash also has 10 slots total**
- Stash space is limited -- you can't hoard indefinitely

### What Works From Stash

- Income-generating items (gold per day)
- Start-of-day effects
- Each-hour effects
- Passive value scaling

### What Does NOT Work From Stash

- Cooldown-based combat triggers
- On-condition triggers during combat
- Start-of-combat triggers
- Adjacency effects

## Item Quality Tiers

Items have quality tiers that determine their power level:

| Tier     | Color  | Notes                                           |
|----------|--------|-------------------------------------------------|
| Bronze   | Brown  | Starting tier, weakest stats                     |
| Silver   | Gray   | First upgrade, noticeable improvement            |
| Gold     | Yellow | Strong mid-game tier                             |
| Diamond  | Blue   | Endgame tier, very powerful                      |
| Legendary| Orange | Rarest, cannot be transformed                    |

See [upgrades.md](upgrades.md) for details on how upgrading works.

## Item Triggers

Items activate through different mechanisms:

| Trigger Type     | Description                                       |
|------------------|---------------------------------------------------|
| Cooldown         | Repeating timer (e.g., every 5 seconds)           |
| On Condition     | When a condition is met (e.g., "when you heal")   |
| Start of Combat  | Once at the beginning of each fight               |
| Start of Day     | Once at the beginning of each day (works from stash) |
| Each Hour        | Once per hour of the day (works from stash)       |

## Buying and Selling Items

- **Buying**: From merchants at listed price. Enchanted items in shops cost **double**.
- **Selling**: Sell at any time for **half the purchase price**. Some items/skills modify sell value.
- If there aren't enough open slots on the rug or stash, you cannot add the item there.

## Board Management Tips

- Prioritize items that synergize with each other over individually strong items
- Position items to maximize adjacency bonuses
- Don't fill your board completely if it means adding weak items -- empty slots are better than bad items
- Rotate items between rug and stash as your build evolves
- Income items belong in the stash (they work from there)
- Sell early-game items once they're outclassed -- don't let them take up space
