# Upgrade and Tier System

## Item Quality Tiers

Items progress through quality tiers that improve their stats and effects:

| Tier      | Color  | First Available | Notes                                    |
|-----------|--------|-----------------|------------------------------------------|
| Bronze    | Brown  | Day 1           | Starting tier, basic stats               |
| Silver    | Gray   | Day 3           | First real power bump                    |
| Gold      | Yellow | Day 7           | Strong mid-game items                    |
| Diamond   | Blue   | Day 9           | Endgame power, very strong               |
| Legendary | Orange | Varies          | Rarest tier, special rules apply         |

## How Upgrading Works

To upgrade an item, you need **a duplicate of the same item at the same quality tier**. When you acquire a duplicate:

- The item upgrades one tier: Bronze -> Silver -> Gold -> Diamond
- The upgraded item retains the enchantment of the newer (incoming) copy
- Stats and effects improve (but not always ALL effects -- some may stay the same)
- Sell value increases with tier

### Upgrade Paths

1. **Buy a duplicate from a merchant**: If you already own a Bronze Sword and buy another Bronze Sword, your Sword upgrades to Silver
2. **Item effects that trigger upgrades**: Some items have effects that upgrade other items on your board
3. **Level-up rewards**: Certain levels offer "Upgrade a Bronze item" or "Upgrade any item" as a reward
4. **PvE encounter rewards**: Some monsters drop upgrade opportunities
5. **Events**: Random events can offer item upgrades

## Diamond Tier Special Rules

- Diamond items **cannot be upgraded further**
- Once you own a Diamond-tier version of an item, **that item will no longer appear in merchant shops** as a buy option
- This prevents you from wasting gold on items you can't use

## Legendary Items

- Legendary items (orange border) are the rarest tier
- They **cannot be transformed** (the Transform effect does not work on them)
- They are acquired through special events, PvE drops, or rare merchant offerings
- Legendary items are extremely powerful build-defining pieces

## Skill Upgrades

Skills follow the same tier progression as items:

- Bronze -> Silver -> Gold -> Diamond -> Legendary
- Upgrading skills works the same way: acquire a duplicate of the same skill at the same tier
- Each tier upgrade substantially boosts the skill's passive effect
- Skills can be acquired from Skill Trainers (Pip, Argenta, Orlin, Adira) or PvE encounters

## Upgrade Strategy

### Early Game (Days 1-3)
- Focus on acquiring items first, don't chase upgrades yet
- Level 4 offers "Upgrade a Bronze item" -- have a good target ready

### Mid Game (Days 4-7)
- Start looking for duplicates of your core items
- Silver items from Silver merchants are the backbone of your build
- Level 10 gives enchantments, not upgrades -- but enchanted items become upgrade priorities

### Late Game (Days 8+)
- Gold and Diamond merchants appear
- Level 13 offers "Upgrade any item" -- save this for your best item
- Diamond-tier items from level-up rewards or shops define your endgame power
- Prioritize upgrading items that are central to your win condition

### What to Upgrade First

1. Your highest-impact item (main damage dealer or key synergy piece)
2. Items with cooldowns (higher tier = better per-activation value)
3. Items that scale with tier (some items gain new effects at higher tiers)
4. Avoid upgrading items you plan to sell soon
