# Economy System

## Gold

Gold is the primary currency in The Bazaar. You spend it to buy items from merchants, reroll shop offerings, and acquire skills.

### Starting Gold

- **Default start**: 8 Gold base + bonuses from your chosen start-of-run option
- **Income Start** (most common): 20 Gold and 7 Income total
- Other start options trade gold for an enchanted item or a skill

### Earning Gold

1. **Income**: At the start of each day, you receive gold equal to your Income stat. Default starting Income is 5 base (7 with Income Start option). Income can be increased by items, skills, and events throughout the run.
2. **PvE encounters**: Defeating monsters grants gold based on their tier:
   - Bronze monsters: 2 gold
   - Silver monsters: 3 gold
   - Gold monsters: 4 gold
   - Diamond monsters: 5 gold
   - Legendary monsters: 6 gold
3. **Selling items**: You can sell items at any time for half their purchase price (or adjusted value if modified by item effects). Upgrading items increases their sell value.
4. **Events**: Random events like A Strange Mushroom or The Shrouded Figure can offer free gold.

### Spending Gold

- **Buying items** from merchants
- **Rerolling** merchant shops
- **Buying skills** from skill trainers

## Merchants (Shops)

Merchants are the primary way to acquire items. They appear during hours 0-4 of each day (before the PvP fight at hour 5).

### Merchant Tiers and Availability

| Merchant Tier | First Appears | Reroll Cost | Item Quality |
|---------------|---------------|-------------|--------------|
| Bronze        | Day 1         | 2 gold      | Bronze items |
| Silver        | Day 3         | 4 gold      | Silver items |
| Gold          | Day 7         | 6 gold      | Gold items   |
| Diamond       | Day 9         | 8 gold      | Diamond items|

### Rerolling

- Rerolls are **unlimited** but cost gold based on the merchant's tier
- Rerolling replaces the current shop offerings with new random items from the merchant's pool
- **Early game**: Reroll sparingly. Bronze rerolls (2 gold) are affordable; Silver rerolls (4 gold) are expensive relative to your early gold supply
- **Item pools are small early on**, so rerolling a specific size or type gives decent odds of finding what you want
- Enchanted items appear naturally in merchant shops at **double the normal price**

### Specialized Merchants

Some merchants specialize in specific item categories (e.g., weapons only, aquatic items, potions). Higher-day merchants tend to be more specialized but offer more powerful items.

## Selling Items

- Sell price = **half the purchase price** (base)
- Some items and skills modify sell value
- You can sell items at any time between fights
- Selling is how you free up board space and recoup gold for better items
- Key strategy: Don't hold onto early-game items too long. Sell them when they no longer contribute to your build

## Income

Income determines your gold generation each day. Higher income = more gold each day = more flexibility.

- **Starting income**: 5 base (7 with Income Start)
- Income can be boosted by items (e.g., Money Tree, Cash Register, Lemonade Stand)
- Income-generating items work from the **stash** -- they don't need to be on your board
- Some items scale in power based on your current Gold or Income values
- Boosting income early is powerful for long runs but weakens your early combat

## Economy Tips

- Keep at least 2 gold in reserve by end of Day 1 for emergency rerolls
- Don't reroll Silver shops early -- 4 gold is too expensive with limited funds
- Income items in stash still generate gold each day
- Track your gold carefully before PvE encounters -- monster rewards help fund your next purchases
- Sell items that no longer fit your build direction rather than hoarding them
