# Enchantment System

## Overview

Enchantments are modifiers that enhance an item's effects. Each item can hold **only one enchantment at a time**. Applying a new enchantment to an already-enchanted item **overwrites** the previous enchantment.

## All Enchantment Types

| Enchantment  | Effect                                                                 |
|--------------|------------------------------------------------------------------------|
| Deadly       | Adds or upgrades **Crit Chance** (+50% crit chance)                    |
| Fiery        | Adds or upgrades **Burn** effect                                       |
| Golden       | Adds or upgrades **Value** (increases gold earned from item)           |
| Heavy        | Adds or upgrades **Slow** effect (slows 2 enemy items)                 |
| Icy          | Adds or upgrades **Freeze** effect (freezes enemy items)               |
| Obsidian     | Adds or upgrades **Damage** effect (double damage)                     |
| Radiant      | Grants **immunity**: item cannot be Frozen, Slowed, or Destroyed       |
| Restorative  | Adds or upgrades **Heal** effect                                       |
| Shielded     | Adds or upgrades **Shield** effect                                     |
| Shiny        | Adds or upgrades **Multicast** (+1 multicast)                          |
| Toxic        | Adds or upgrades **Poison** effect                                     |
| Turbo        | Adds or upgrades **Haste** effect (gives haste to 2 items)             |

## How Enchantments Are Acquired

1. **Start-of-run selection**: The Enchanted Start option gives you one enchanted Bronze item
2. **Merchant shops**: Enchanted items appear naturally in shops at **double the normal price**
3. **Level-up rewards**: Level 10 is the key level -- you get **two random enchantments** to apply. This is widely considered the most important level-up in the game
4. **Random events**: Some events offer enchantment choices
5. **PvE monster drops**: Certain monsters can drop enchantments
6. **Haddy encounter**: A special encounter that can award enchantments
7. **Fate's Crossroads (Resurrection)**: When you hit 0 Prestige, one of the three options is a random enchantment

## Enchantment Mechanics

### One Per Item
Each item holds exactly one enchantment. You must choose carefully -- applying a second enchantment replaces the first permanently.

### Upgrade Interaction
When you upgrade an item by acquiring a duplicate, the **newer copy's enchantment** is retained on the upgraded item. Plan accordingly:
- If your existing item has a great enchantment, be aware that buying a duplicate with a different (or no) enchantment will overwrite it
- Conversely, you can use this to "fix" an item's enchantment by upgrading with a better-enchanted copy

### Transform Interaction
When an item is Transformed (randomly changed to another item of the same size), the new item **retains the former enchantment IF applicable** to the new item.

## Strategic Enchantment Guide

### Offensive Enchantments
- **Obsidian**: Best on high-damage, fast-cooldown items. Doubles damage output.
- **Fiery**: Best on items that already deal Burn or trigger frequently. Stacks Burn fast.
- **Toxic**: Best on Poison-synergy items. Poison bypasses shields, making this strong against defensive builds.
- **Deadly**: Best on items with high base effects. +50% crit means more consistent doubled output.
- **Shiny**: Best on items with powerful per-activation effects. Extra multicast = extra triggers per activation.

### Defensive Enchantments
- **Shielded**: Adds Shield generation. Strong against Burn builds. Useless against Poison.
- **Restorative**: Adds healing. Counters Poison and Burn through cleansing.
- **Radiant**: Makes an item immune to Freeze, Slow, and Destroy. Critical for your key items against control builds.

### Utility Enchantments
- **Turbo**: Grants Haste to 2 items, effectively doubling their charge speed. Extremely strong on boards with slow, powerful items.
- **Heavy**: Slows 2 enemy items. Disrupts opponent's timing and reduces their DPS.
- **Icy**: Freezes enemy items. Completely shuts down an enemy item temporarily.
- **Golden**: Increases item Value (sell price and gold generation). Economy-focused, not for combat builds.

## Key Enchantment Moments

- **Level 10**: The defining enchantment moment. Two free enchantments can transform a decent build into a dominant one. Plan your build to maximize this spike.
- **Fate's Crossroads**: If your run is dying at 0 Prestige, the enchantment option can sometimes salvage it. Heavy and Icy are noted as particularly powerful resurrection enchantments.
- **Enchanted Start**: If you choose this start-of-run option, you get one enchanted item immediately. High variance -- could be amazing or useless depending on the random item and enchantment.
