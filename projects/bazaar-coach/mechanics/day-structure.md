# Day Structure, Prestige, and PvE

## Day/Hour System

Each day in The Bazaar consists of **6 hours** (Hours 0-5). Each hour, you choose from available options (merchants, events, free rewards). The final hour is always a PvP fight.

### Hour-by-Hour Breakdown

| Hour | Phase          | What Happens                                                  |
|------|----------------|---------------------------------------------------------------|
| 0    | Vendor/Event   | First shopping opportunity. One of two chances to buy before PvE. |
| 1    | Vendor/Event   | Second shopping opportunity. Skip if nothing useful.          |
| 2    | PvE Encounter  | Fight a monster. Choose from 3 encounters of varying difficulty. |
| 3    | Vendor/Event   | Post-PvE shopping. You now have monster gold to spend.        |
| 4    | Vendor/Event   | Last chance to buy/sell before PvP.                           |
| 5    | PvP Fight      | Mandatory battle against another player's ghost. Win or lose Prestige. |

### What Appears Each Hour

Each hour typically offers a choice between:
- **A merchant** (item shop)
- **An event** (random beneficial/risky choice)
- **A free reward** (item, gold, XP, Max Health, Regen, etc.)

Rarely, two merchants or two events may appear simultaneously. The types and quality of options vary by day.

### XP Per Hour

You gain **1 XP per hour automatically** as the clock advances. With 6 hours per day, that's 6 XP per day baseline. Additional XP comes from PvE encounters (1-4 XP based on performance) and events.

Since leveling requires 8 XP, you typically level up roughly every 1-1.5 days.

## Prestige System

Prestige is your "lives" or "run health." It determines how many losses you can survive.

### Core Rules

- **Starting Prestige**: 20
- **Losing a PvP fight costs Prestige equal to the current day number**
  - Lose on Day 1 = -1 Prestige
  - Lose on Day 3 = -3 Prestige
  - Lose on Day 5 = -5 Prestige
  - Lose on Day 10 = -10 Prestige
- Early losses are cheap; late losses are devastating
- When Prestige reaches **0**, the run enters its final phase

### Win Condition

Achieve **10 PvP victories** to complete a successful run with maximum rewards.

### Reward Tiers Based on Wins

| Wins | Chests |
|------|--------|
| 4    | 2      |
| 7    | 4      |
| 10   | 6      |

Chests contain cosmetics: hero skins, card backs, rugs, and more.

### Resurrection: Fate's Crossroads

When Prestige hits 0, you don't immediately lose. Instead you get **one final opportunity** called Fate's Crossroads. Choose one of three options:

1. **Random Diamond-tier item** -- potentially build-defining
2. **Random enchantment** -- can dramatically strengthen a key item (Heavy and Icy are noted as especially powerful here)
3. **20 Gold + 5 XP** -- safe, flexible option

After this event, you continue playing until your next PvP loss ends the run.

### Prestige Recovery

Prestige recovery is extremely rare:
- **Arken's Ring**: Dropped by Lord Arken PvE encounter. Selling it recovers 5 Prestige.
- Most runs have no way to recover Prestige -- treat every point as precious.

## PvE Encounters

PvE fights happen at **Hour 2** of each day. You choose one of **3 encounters** with different difficulty levels and rewards.

### Monster Difficulty Tiers

Encounters range across 8 difficulty levels, scaling with the day:

| Monster Tier | Gold Reward | XP Reward | HP Range        |
|--------------|-------------|-----------|-----------------|
| Bronze       | 2 gold      | 3 XP      | Low             |
| Silver       | 3 gold      | 3 XP      | Low-Medium      |
| Gold         | 4 gold      | 3 XP      | Medium          |
| Diamond      | 5 gold      | 4 XP      | Medium-High     |
| Legendary    | 6 gold      | 4 XP      | High            |
| Higher tiers | More        | More      | 900-1700+ HP    |

### Notable PvE Encounters

**Easy encounters** (early game):
- Banannabal, Pyro, Fanged Inglet -- essentially free wins that introduce basic item types

**Medium encounters** (mid game):
- Boarrior -- aggressive weapon scaling, requires damage output
- Giant Mosquito -- very tough, demands sustained offense or defense
- Viper -- one of the few early encounters that can actually threaten you

**Hard encounters** (late game):
- Trashtown Mayor -- drops "Augmented Weaponry," one of the best weapon-scaling skills
- Late encounters have 900-1700+ HP with complex mechanics

### Notable PvE Drops

- **Proboscis** (Giant Mosquito): One of the best items in the game
- **Diamond Fangs**: Exceptional damage scaling
- **Augmented Weaponry** (Trashtown Mayor): Top-tier weapon-scaling skill
- **Petrifying Gaze** skill: Enables board-wide enemy freezing
- **Arken's Ring** (Lord Arken): Sell for 5 Prestige recovery

### PvE Strategy

- Higher-tier encounters offer better rewards but carry greater risk
- If your board can't handle a tough encounter, take the easier option for safe gold/XP
- Passive Shield builds struggle against damage-scaling monsters
- Burst-focused builds counter monsters with regeneration
- You gain XP from PvE encounters based on **damage dealt** (1-4 XP)
- Expedition tickets (unlocked at higher progression) give access to bonus encounters

## Run Pacing

### Early Game (Days 1-3)
- Focus on acquiring items and building a board identity
- Losses are cheap (1-3 Prestige)
- Bronze and Silver merchants
- Establish your build direction

### Mid Game (Days 4-7)
- Refine your strategy, start upgrading key items
- Gold merchants appear on Day 7
- Level 10 enchantments are a huge power spike
- Losses start getting expensive (4-7 Prestige)

### Late Game (Days 8-10+)
- Diamond merchants appear on Day 9
- Endgame optimization -- every slot matters
- Losses are devastating (8-10+ Prestige)
- Opponents at this stage have accumulated many items and enchantments
- Aim to close out 10 wins as soon as possible

### Run Length

- Minimum run: ~10 days (if you win almost every PvP fight)
- Typical run: 10-15 days depending on win/loss record
- Longer runs mean facing stronger opponents with more accumulated power
- Aggressive builds (like Vanessa) want to win fast before getting outscaled
