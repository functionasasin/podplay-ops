# Progression System

## XP and Leveling

### XP Sources

- **Hourly XP**: 1 XP gained automatically each hour (6 XP per day baseline)
- **PvE encounters**: 1-4 XP based on damage dealt to the monster
- **PvE encounter tier bonus**: Higher-tier monsters grant more base XP (3-4 XP)
- **Events**: Some events grant bonus XP

### XP Per Level

Every level requires **8 XP** to advance. With ~6 XP per day from hours + 3-4 XP from PvE, you typically level up every 1-1.5 days.

### Critical Level Milestones

**Level 4** -- First upgrade opportunity. Have a strong Bronze item ready to upgrade to Silver.

**Level 8 (Orlin)** -- Gold-tier skills are a significant power jump. This is when your build starts to solidify.

**Level 10** -- **The most important level-up**. Two free enchantments can "turn a good build into an insane build" and potentially salvage underperforming runs. Plan your entire run around reaching this level with enchantable items ready.

**Level 13** -- Upgrade any item (can push a Gold item to Diamond). Save this for your most impactful item.

**Level 14 (Adira)** -- Diamond-tier skills. These are build-defining and represent a major endgame spike.

## Start-of-Run Options

At the beginning of every run, you choose one of three starting bonuses. Each is added on top of the base starting resources of **8 Gold and 5 Income**.

### Option 1: Income Start
- **Bonus**: +12 Gold and +2 Income (totaling 20 Gold and 7 Income)
- **Strengths**: Most predictable. Lots of early spending power. Reroll freely if needed.
- **Weaknesses**: No items or skills to start. Limited upside potential.
- **Best for**: Consistent play, any hero, beginners

### Option 2: Enchanted Start
- **Bonus**: One enchanted Small Bronze item (random from hero's pool)
- **Strengths**: Can provide a powerful early-game carry. Enables unusual build paths.
- **Weaknesses**: High variance. Random item might not synergize. Can be salvaged via Transform events.
- **Best for**: Players comfortable with adapting to random starts

### Option 3: Skill Start
- **Bonus**: One monster skill (potentially rare)
- **Strengths**: Strongest early-game potential. Extra Damage/Burn/Poison gives a huge early edge.
- **Weaknesses**: No extra gold. Forces specific strategic direction. Less flexibility.
- **Best for**: Experienced players who can build around a specific skill

## Heroes

The Bazaar has six playable heroes (three base, two DLC, one additional):

| Hero       | Theme                        | Playstyle                                     |
|------------|------------------------------|-----------------------------------------------|
| Vanessa    | Sea captain / Pirate         | Aggressive weapons, aquatic items, ammo-based  |
| Dooley     | Robot builder                | Single-item focus, tech synergies, flexible    |
| Pygmalien  | Economist                    | Value stacking, economy scaling, late-game     |
| Mak        | Alchemist                    | Potions, reagents, ancient relics              |
| Stelle     | Mechanic                     | Machines, weapons, vehicles                    |
| Jules      | (Additional hero)            | Various specialized builds                     |

### Hero Selection Tips

- Master one hero before learning others
- Each hero has a unique item pool and skill set
- Hero choice determines your available build archetypes
- Vanessa is the starter/default hero and is good for learning fundamentals

## Modes

- **Ranked**: Standard competitive mode against matched opponents
- **Normal**: Features easier opponents, good for beginners learning mechanics
