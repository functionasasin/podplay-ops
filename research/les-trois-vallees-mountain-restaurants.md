# Les Trois Vallées - On-Mountain Restaurant Guide

Budget-friendly to mid-range lunch spots across all three valleys.
Focus on local favorites, not tourist traps.

## Price Key

| Symbol | Meaning |
|--------|---------|
| $ | Budget: under ~EUR 15 for a main |
| $$ | Mid-range: EUR 15-25 for a main |
| $$$ | Upper mid-range: EUR 25-40 per person (with drink) |
| $$$$ | Splurge: EUR 40+ per person |

**Pro tip:** Almost every restaurant offers a **plat du jour** (dish of the day) at lunchtime -- typically EUR 13-22 for a main with salad. This is always the best value option.

**Timing tip:** Break for lunch at 12:15-12:30. The rush hits at 1pm. You get served faster, full dessert selection, and the slopes are empty when you head back out.

## 1. Meribel Valley

Accessible from Brides-les-Bains via the Olympe gondola, plus Meribel Centre, Altitude 1600, and Mottaret.

### 1a. Le Rond Point ("The Ronnie")

| Detail | Info |
|--------|------|
| **Location** | Junction of Foret and Rhodos green runs, just above Meribel Centre at ~2,000m. Below the Saulire Express mid-station. |
| **Nearest lift** | Adret chairlift, Rhodos gondola top station |
| **Price range** | $$ |
| **Sun terrace** | Yes -- huge south-facing terrace with panoramic valley views. One of the best terraces in the 3 Valleys. |
| **Known for** | The iconic Meribel meeting point. Two food options: an a la carte restaurant (fusion of local and pan-European dishes) and a **self-service snack bar** (burgers, Thai woks, locally sourced ingredients). The snack bar is one of the most popular and affordable mountainside food outlets in the 3 Valleys. Also legendary for apres-ski (live music daily from 3:30pm). |
| **Budget lunch** | Snack bar: ~EUR 12-18 (burger/wok). A la carte mains: ~EUR 18-28. |
| **Booking** | Snack bar is first-come-first-served (expect queues at peak). Restaurant bookable. |
| **Season card** | EUR 10 discount card valid at Rond Point, Taverne, Jacks, Sullys, The Den. |
| **Hours** | 12:00-14:30 (restaurant), 12:00-17:00 (snack bar) |
| **Contact** | lerondpointmeribel.com |

## 2. Courchevel Valley

Including La Tania, Le Praz (1300), Courchevel Village (1550), Moriond (1650), and 1850.

**General rule for Courchevel:** The higher you go, the more you pay. La Tania and Le Praz are the best value. Moriond is the sweet spot. 1850 is expensive but Polar Cafe is the exception.

### 2a. Le Bouc Blanc

| Detail | Info |
|--------|------|
| **Location** | Top of the La Tania gondola, on the La Tania side of the mountain. |
| **Nearest lift** | La Tania gondola (top station) |
| **Price range** | $$ |
| **Sun terrace** | Yes -- large terrace, comes alive in spring with music. |
| **Known for** | Family-run by locals Christiane and Fabrice. Legendary for no-nonsense lunchtime food with efficient service, even when packed. Grilled chicken, burgers, grilled salmon, vegan bowls, pasta, plat du jour -- all at sensible prices. This is "extraordinarily rare in the Courchevel ski area." A true local favorite. Book in busy weeks. |
| **Budget lunch** | Mains from ~EUR 15-22. Plat du jour excellent value. |
| **Booking** | Strongly recommended in peak weeks. |

### 2b. Bistrot Machet

| Detail | Info |
|--------|------|
| **Location** | La Tania snow front (front de neige), 94 Esplanade des Tornets. Ski-to-the-door access. |

| **Sun terrace** | Yes -- south-facing terrace with panoramic views of Grand Bec. |

Small seasonal menu with dishes like lapin a la moutarde and creative pasta. La Cocotte du Jour (daily casserole) is EUR 14. Children's menu EUR 12. | **Budget lunch** | Cocotte du Jour EUR 14. Children's EUR 12. Mains ~EUR 17-25. |

| Detail | Info |

## 3. Belleville Valley

Val Thorens, Les Menuires, and Saint-Martin-de-Belleville.

### 3a. Le Grand Lac

| Detail | Info |

| **Sun terrace** | Yes -- large terrace with incredible mountain views. Cosy chalet interior with big central fireplace. |

Homely mountain cuisine: onion soup, tartiflette, croziflette (pasta-based alternative), grilled steak, omelettes, pasta. Mighty portions. Warm, friendly welcome. Children's 2-course menu available. Afternoon waffles and ice cream. |

## 5. Top Picks by Situation

### Best overall value (plat du jour under EUR 15)
- **Le Grand Lac** (Les Menuires) -- EUR 13 plat du jour, huge portions

### Best terrace for a long lazy lunch
- **Le Rond Point** (Meribel) -- the iconic south-facing panoramic terrace
- **Le Bel Air** (Courchevel Moriond) -- south-facing valley views with indoor fire

### Best "local secret" everyone should know
- **La Loy** (St Martin/Biolley piste) -- passionate chef, EUR 11-21 dishes
- **Le Bouc Blanc** (La Tania) -- family-run, sensible prices, rare for Courchevel

### Best Michelin-quality on a budget
- **Bistrot Machet** (La Tania) -- Michelin-starred chef's casual bistrot, EUR 14 cocotte

### Best self-service for budget days
- **Chalet-Refuge** (Orelle) -- under EUR 20, often overlooked
- **360** (Val Thorens) -- multi-station self-service, great terrace

### Best for families/beginners
- **Les Rhodos** (Meribel Altiport) -- right at beginner slopes, affordable
- **Chalet Tonia** (Mottaret) -- snack bar for kids, restaurant for adults

### Best for the Brides-les-Bains skier
Take the Olympe gondola up and ski to:
1. **Le Rond Point snack bar** -- accessible via green/blue runs from Saulire mid-station
2. **Les Rhodos** -- near the Altiport, easy skiing
3. **L'Adray Telebar** -- Doron piste, the classic
4. **Cafe del Mott** -- Mottaret, easy access from Plattieres

## 6. Ones to Avoid (Tourist Trap Warnings)

- Anything in Courchevel 1850 that isn't Polar Cafe will be eye-wateringly expensive
- The main strip restaurants in Val Thorens marked up for the party crowd
- Generic self-service at lift stations (poor quality/price ratio) -- the specific ones listed above are exceptions

## 7. Practical Notes

- **Booking:** Mountain restaurants get FULL by 12:45 in peak weeks. Book ahead for table-service spots or arrive by 12:15.
- **Payment:** Most accept cards. Some smaller spots are cash-preferred.
- **Last lifts:** Always check last lift times before settling in for a long lunch, especially if you're returning to Brides-les-Bains (Olympe gondola closes at ~17:00).
- **3 Valleys App:** Has a complete restaurant directory with real-time routing to help you find the way to any restaurant on skis.
- **Dog legs:** Some restaurants require short walks or traverses off-piste. La Loy and Le Grand Lac are easy to ski past if you're not looking.
