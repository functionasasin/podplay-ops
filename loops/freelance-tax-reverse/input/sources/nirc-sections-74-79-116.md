# NIRC Sections 74-79 (Quarterly Declarations) and Section 116 (Percentage Tax)
## As Amended by TRAIN Law (RA 10963) and CREATE Law (RA 11534)

Source: National Internal Revenue Code of the Philippines (RA 8424), as amended
Fetched: 2026-02-28

## Section 74 — Declaration of Income Tax for Individuals

### 74(A) — In General

Except as otherwise provided in this Code, every individual subject to income tax under Section 25(A)(1) hereof, who is receiving self-employment income, shall make and file a declaration of his estimated income for the current taxable year on or before April 15 of the same taxable year.

### 74(B) — Return and Payment of Estimated Income Tax

It shall be the duty of every individual specified in Subsection (A) hereof to pay to the BIR his quarterly income tax.

**Quarterly payment schedule:**
- 1st Quarter: on or before **May 15** of the current year
- 2nd Quarter: on or before **August 15** of the current year
- 3rd Quarter: on or before **November 15** of the current year
- Annual: on or before **April 15** of the following year (BIR Form 1701 or 1701A)

Note: There is no separate 4th Quarter return filing. The annual return (BIR Form 1701/1701A) serves as the final/4th quarter declaration.

### 74(C) — Quarterly Income Tax Computation Method

The income tax due for each quarter shall be computed based on the **cumulative taxable income** for that quarter and all preceding quarters within the same taxable year.

**Cumulative Method Formula:**

Q1 Tax Due = Tax on (Q1 cumulative taxable income) − 0 − CWT credits Q1

Q2 Tax Due = Tax on (Q1+Q2 cumulative taxable income) − Q1 tax paid − CWT credits Q1+Q2

Q3 Tax Due = Tax on (Q1+Q2+Q3 cumulative taxable income) − Q1 tax paid − Q2 tax paid − CWT credits Q1+Q2+Q3

Annual Tax Due = Tax on (Full Year taxable income) − Q1 paid − Q2 paid − Q3 paid − Total CWT credits

**Key implication:** Each quarter builds on the previous. This means effective tax rates shift as cumulative income changes brackets.

## Section 75 — Payment of Estimated Income Tax

The quarterly income tax payment computed in accordance with Section 74 shall be paid to the BIR at the time the quarterly return is due.

## Section 76 — Final Adjustment Return

Every corporation liable to tax under Section 27 shall file a final adjustment return covering the total taxable income for the preceding calendar or fiscal year.

(Note: For individuals, this provision is implemented through the BIR Form 1701 and 1701A annual returns, not a separate "adjustment return" for individuals per se.)

## Section 77 — Place and Time of Filing and Payment of Quarterly Corporate Income Tax

Applicable primarily to corporations. For individuals, filing is with the Revenue District Office (RDO) of principal place of business, or via eFPS/eBIRForms system.

## Section 78 — Withholding Tax on Compensation

(Relates to employer withholding obligations — not directly applicable to self-employed taxpayers filing their own returns. Self-employed are subject to Expanded Withholding Tax (EWT) when their clients are withholding agents.)

## Section 79 — Returns and Payment of Taxes Withheld at Source

Governs the filing of withholding tax returns (BIR Form 1601-E, etc.) by withholding agents. Self-employed individuals as PAYEES receive BIR Form 2307 (Certificate of Creditable Withholding Tax) from their clients who act as withholding agents.

## Section 116 — Tax on Persons Exempt from Value-Added Tax

### Current Text

Except as otherwise provided in this Code, any person whose sales or receipts are exempt under Section 109(CC) of this Code from the payment of value-added tax and who is not a VAT-registered person shall pay a tax equivalent to **three percent (3%)** of his gross quarterly sales or receipts; Provided, That cooperatives shall be exempt from the three percent (3%) gross receipts tax herein imposed.

### CREATE Law Amendment (RA 11534)

The CREATE Law temporarily reduced the Section 116 rate:

| Period | Rate |
|--------|------|
| Before July 1, 2020 | 3% |
| July 1, 2020 to June 30, 2023 | 1% (temporary COVID-era relief) |
| July 1, 2023 onwards (current) | 3% (reverted) |

**Current rate (as of 2024 and 2026): 3%**

### Who is Subject to Section 116 Percentage Tax?

Self-employed individuals and professionals who:
1. Have annual gross sales/receipts NOT exceeding ₱3,000,000 (VAT threshold)
2. Are NOT VAT-registered
3. Have NOT elected the 8% income tax rate option

The 8% income tax rate option is **in lieu of** both the graduated income tax AND the Section 116 percentage tax. So:
- If taxpayer elects 8% income tax → NO Section 116 percentage tax
- If taxpayer uses graduated rates (with OSD or itemized deductions) → Section 116 percentage tax APPLIES (at 3%)

### Filing Form for Section 116 Percentage Tax

- **BIR Form 2551Q** — Quarterly Percentage Tax Return
- Filed quarterly: on or before 25th day after end of each quarter
  - Q1: April 25
  - Q2: July 25
  - Q3: October 25
  - Q4: January 25 of following year

### Section 116 Computation

Percentage Tax = Gross Quarterly Sales or Receipts × 0.03

- Applied to GROSS receipts (no deduction allowed before computing)
- Quarterly filing and payment
- Is an ADDITIONAL tax obligation on top of income tax (for non-8% filers)
- Paid before or simultaneously with income tax

## BIR Form 1701Q — Quarterly Income Tax Return Key Provisions

### Filers

All self-employed individuals, professionals, and mixed-income earners who are:
- Registered as self-employed with the BIR
- Earning income from business or practice of profession

**Note:** Even if there is zero income for the quarter, a NIL return must still be filed.

### Filing Deadlines

| Quarter | Period Covered | Deadline |
|---------|---------------|----------|
| Q1 | January 1 – March 31 | May 15 |
| Q2 | January 1 – June 30 (cumulative) | August 15 |
| Q3 | January 1 – September 30 (cumulative) | November 15 |
| Annual | January 1 – December 31 | April 15 of following year |

### Tax Rate Election in 1701Q

The 8% income tax rate option is elected on the **first quarterly return (Q1)**:
- BIR Form 1701Q Item No. 16: tick box for "8% income tax rate"
- This election is irrevocable for the entire taxable year
- Q1 filing deadline is May 15 — the last chance to elect 8% for the year

If Q1 1701Q is filed without the 8% election, the taxpayer defaults to graduated rates for the year.
