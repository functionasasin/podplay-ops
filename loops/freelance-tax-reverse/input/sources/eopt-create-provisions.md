# RA 11976 — Ease of Paying Taxes (EOPT) Act

**Full Title:** "An Act Introducing Administrative Tax Reforms, Amending Sections 21, 22, 51, 56, 57, 58, 76, 77, 81, 90, 91, 103, 106, 108, 109, 110, 112, 113, 114, 115, 116, 117, 118, 119, 120, 128, 200, 204, 229, 235, 236, 237, 238, 241, 243, 245, 248, and 169; and Repealing Section 34(K) of the National Revenue Code of 1997, as Amended, and for Other Purposes"

**Signed:** January 5, 2024
**Published:** Official Gazette, January 7, 2024
**Effectivity:** January 22, 2024 (15 days after publication)

---

## 1. Taxpayer Classification (New NIRC Provision via RA 11976)

RA 11976 introduces a formal four-tier classification system based on **gross sales** (net of VAT):

| Classification | Gross Sales |
|---|---|
| Micro | Less than ₱3,000,000 |
| Small | ₱3,000,000 to less than ₱20,000,000 |
| Medium | ₱20,000,000 to less than ₱1,000,000,000 |
| Large | ₱1,000,000,000 and above |

**Notes per RR 8-2024:**
- "Gross sales" = total sales revenue, net of VAT, without other deductions, covering only business income (excludes compensation income under employer-employee relationship).
- Taxpayers registered in 2022 and prior are classified based on gross sales for taxable year 2022.
- Reclassification by BIR requires prior written notification to the taxpayer.
- A self-employed individual with gross receipts < ₱3,000,000 is classified as **Micro**.
- A self-employed professional with gross receipts ₱3,000,000–₱19,999,999 is classified as **Small**.

---

## 2. Individual Returns — NIRC Section 51 as Amended by EOPT

**SEC. 51(B) Where to File (as amended):**

"Except in cases where the Commissioner otherwise permits, the return shall be filed with **any authorized agent bank, Revenue District Office through Revenue Collection Officer, or authorized tax software provider.**"

**Effect:** File-and-pay-anywhere provision. Pre-EOPT, returns had to be filed at the RDO of registration; taxes paid only at AABs within that RDO's jurisdiction. This restriction is now eliminated for all taxpayers.

**Simplified ITR (Micro and Small Taxpayers):**
- ITR for micro and small taxpayers = maximum **two (2) pages** in paper or electronic form.
- This corresponds to BIR's existing Form 1701A (2-page) for eligible filers.

**Joint Filing for Self-Employed Spouses (per RR 4-2024):**
- Married individuals who are both self-employed shall file the ITR jointly.
- EXCEPTION: If businesses registered under two different RDOs, each spouse files separately.

**Individuals NOT required to file an ITR (per RR 4-2024):**
1. Individuals earning purely compensation income whose annual taxable income does not exceed ₱250,000
2. Individuals whose income tax has been correctly withheld by employer (substituted filing)
3. Individuals whose sole income has been subjected to final withholding tax
4. Minimum wage earners
5. OFWs/OCWs earning income solely from abroad (new exemption added by EOPT)

---

## 3. Payment — NIRC Section 56 as Amended by EOPT

**SEC. 56(A)(1) (as amended):**
"The total amount of tax imposed by this Title shall be paid, **either electronically or manually**, by the person subject thereto at the time the return is filed."

Payment channels: any authorized agent bank (AAB), revenue collection officer (RCO), or electronic payment platforms (GCash, Maya, Landbank e-Link, DBP, etc.).

---

## 4. Withholding Tax Timing — NIRC Section 58(C), New Provision Added by EOPT

**SEC. 58(C):** *"The obligation to deduct and withhold the tax arises at the time the income has become payable."*

**RR 4-2024 implementing definition (Section 2.57.4 of RR 2-98, as amended):**
*"The obligation of the payor to deduct and withhold the tax arises at the time an income payment is accrued or recorded as an expense or asset, whichever is applicable, in the payor's books, or at the issuance by the seller of the sales invoice or other adequate document to support such payable, whichever comes first."*

**Key change:** The "upon actual payment" trigger was removed. Only accrual/recording or invoice issuance remains as trigger.

---

## 5. Repeal of Section 34(K) — Withholding as Deductibility Prerequisite

**REPEALED PROVISION:** Former NIRC Section 34(K) required that expenses subject to creditable withholding tax be deductible only if the withholding tax had been withheld and remitted to BIR.

**Effect of the repeal (effective January 22, 2024):**
An income payment where tax is required to be withheld can now be **allowed as a deduction from gross income even if no tax was withheld**, provided the expense is:
- Necessary and ordinary
- Related to the registered business of the taxpayer
- Duly substantiated

**Critical caveat (RMC 60-2024):**
- Repeal applies **only to taxable years starting January 1, 2024 onwards**.
- For all ongoing audits and assessments covering taxable periods **prior to January 1, 2024**, the old rule applies — expenses are allowed as deductions only if the corresponding withholding tax was paid.
- The **obligation to withhold and remit remains**. Only the deductibility prerequisite was repealed; failure to withhold is still an offense.

---

## 6. Micro Taxpayer Withholding Exemption — VETOED Provision

**VETOED PROVISO (stricken by presidential veto):**
*"Provided, That micro taxpayers shall not be required to withhold taxes under Subsection (B) of this Section."*

**Reason for veto:** Estimated revenue loss of ₱40.4 billion in 2024 alone; ₱206.4 billion over 2024–2028. 50% non-compliance rate among micro taxpayers per World Bank data.

**Result:** Micro taxpayers remain fully subject to creditable withholding tax obligations. The withholding exemption was NOT enacted.

---

## 7. Elimination of Wrong-Venue Penalty

**Pre-EOPT:** 25% civil surcharge applied when return was filed with an internal revenue officer outside their jurisdiction.

**Post-EOPT (effective January 22, 2024):** The 25% wrong-venue surcharge is **entirely eliminated**. No longer a penalty for filing at an incorrect RDO or AAB.

---

## 8. Civil Penalties — NIRC Section 248 as Amended by EOPT

| Taxpayer Type | Standard Civil Penalty for Failure to File/Pay/Underpayment |
|---|---|
| Large and Medium taxpayers | 25% of amount due |
| **Small and Micro taxpayers** | **10% of amount due** (reduced from 25%) |

---

## 9. Interest on Unpaid Taxes — NIRC Section 249 as Amended by EOPT

| Taxpayer Type | Annual Interest Rate on Unpaid Taxes |
|---|---|
| Large and Medium taxpayers | 12% per annum |
| **Small and Micro taxpayers** | **6% per annum** (50% reduction) |

---

## 10. Penalties for Failure to File Information Returns — NIRC Section 250 as Amended by EOPT

| Taxpayer Type | Penalty Per Failure | Annual Aggregate Cap |
|---|---|---|
| Large and Medium taxpayers | ₱1,000 per failure | ₱25,000 |
| **Small and Micro taxpayers** | **₱500 per failure** | **₱12,500** |

---

## 11. Compromise Penalties for Invoicing Violations (Sections 113, 237, 238)

| Taxpayer Type | Penalty |
|---|---|
| Large and Medium taxpayers | Standard rate (set by BIR schedule) |
| **Small and Micro taxpayers** | **50% reduction** on compromise penalties |

---

## 12. Invoice System Reform (Replaces Official Receipts)

**Change (NIRC Sections 106, 108, 113, 237, 238 as amended by EOPT):**
- "Official Receipt" is no longer the primary sales document for services.
- All transactions (goods AND services) now require an **Invoice** as the primary document.
- "Official Receipt" reclassified as a supplementary document.
- Mandatory invoicing threshold increased from ₱100 to **₱500** per transaction (adjusted every 3 years via CPI).
- VAT-registered sellers must issue invoice for every transaction regardless of amount.

**Practical impact on self-employed:**
- Must issue Sales Invoice (not Official Receipt) for every client payment.
- OR Revenue Official Receipt (BIR-registered) — both are acceptable.
- Electronic invoices are now explicitly authorized without special BIR approval.

---

## 13. Gross Sales vs. Gross Receipts — Basis Shift (EOPT)

**Pre-EOPT:** Self-employed service providers computed percentage tax on **gross receipts** (cash method — amounts actually received).

**Post-EOPT (effective via RR 3-2024):** Basis shifted to **gross sales** (accrual method — amounts earned/invoiced, whether collected or not).

**Practical impact for freelancers:**
- If a freelancer invoices in December but receives payment in January, the amount is counted in December's gross sales for percentage tax purposes.
- This aligns with how VAT is computed (output VAT is due upon issuance of invoice).
- **For income tax purposes (BIR Form 1701/1701A/1701Q):** The basis remains "gross sales or gross receipts" — both terms are used in the form. The shift is primarily for percentage tax.

**Transition Period:** BIR allowed a 6-month transition period (through approximately October 27, 2024) for taxpayers to adjust systems.

---

## 14. Books of Accounts Retention — NIRC Section 235 as Amended by EOPT

| Rule | Old Period | New Period (Post-EOPT) |
|---|---|---|
| Retention of books and accounting records | 10 years | **5 years** |

**Start of retention period:** From the day following the deadline in filing a return, or if filed after the deadline, from the date of actual filing, for the taxable year when the last entry was made.

---

## 15. Annual Registration Fee (ARF) — Abolished by EOPT

**Effective January 22, 2024 (per RMC 14-2024):**
- BIR ceased collecting the Annual Registration Fee (ARF).
- No longer required to file BIR Form 0605 or pay the ₱500 ARF for new or annual renewal.
- Applies to ALL business taxpayers, including self-employed individuals and freelancers.

---

## 16. Digitalization Mandate

**Section 43 of RA 11976:** BIR must adopt integrated digitalization strategy including:
- Automated end-to-end solutions for registration, TIN issuance, filing, payments
- Streamlined procedures through automation
- Secure electronic data exchange systems

**Section 44:** "Ease of Paying Taxes and Digitalization Roadmap" must be issued within 90 days of effectivity, prioritizing micro and small taxpayers.

# RA 11534 — Corporate Recovery and Tax Incentives for Enterprises (CREATE) Act

**Full Title:** "An Act Reforming the Corporate Income Tax and Incentives System, Amending for the Purpose Sections 20, 22, 25, 27, 28, 29, 34, 40, 57, 109, 116, 204 and 290 of the National Internal Revenue Code of 1997, as Amended, and Creating Therein New Title XIII, and for Other Purposes"

**Signed:** March 26, 2021
**Published:** Business Mirror, March 27, 2021
**Effectivity:** April 11, 2021 (15 days after publication)

---

## 1. Section 13 — Amendment to NIRC Section 116 (Percentage Tax on Non-VAT Persons)

**Full Statutory Text of Amended Section 116 (per CREATE):**

*"SEC. 116. Tax on Persons Exempt from Value-Added Tax (VAT). – Any person whose sales or receipts are exempt under Section 109(CC) of this Code from the payment of value-added tax and who is not a VAT-registered person shall pay a tax equivalent to **three percent (3%)** of his gross quarterly sales or receipts: Provided, That cooperatives shall be exempt from the three percent (3%) gross receipts tax herein imposed: Provided, further, That **effective July 1, 2020 until June 30, 2023, the rate shall be one percent (1%).**"*

**Rate Timeline:**

| Period | Rate | Authority |
|---|---|---|
| Pre-January 1, 2018 | 3% | Pre-TRAIN NIRC Section 116 |
| January 1, 2018 – June 30, 2020 | 3% | RA 10963 (TRAIN Law) — no change |
| July 1, 2020 – June 30, 2023 | **1%** | RA 11534 (CREATE Act), Section 13 — retroactive |
| July 1, 2023 onwards | **3%** | Reversion per CREATE temporary provision |
| Current (post-EOPT, 2024+) | **3%** | NIRC as amended (basis changed to gross sales) |

**Retroactivity note:** CREATE Act was signed March 26, 2021 and took effect April 11, 2021, but the 1% rate was made **retroactive to July 1, 2020**. Taxpayers who paid 3% from Q3 2020 through Q1 2021 overpaid and were entitled to amend returns or carry over.

**Cooperatives exemption:** Cooperatives remain exempt from Section 116 percentage tax entirely — not just the rate, but the entire obligation.

---

## 2. Who Is Subject to Section 116 — Self-Employed Applicability

Section 116 applies to **any person** (individual or corporate) who:
1. Has gross sales or receipts **exempt under Section 109(CC)** — i.e., annual gross sales/receipts do not exceed the VAT threshold (₱3,000,000), AND
2. Is **not VAT-registered**

**Who is NOT subject to Section 116:**
- Cooperatives (expressly exempted by statute)
- Self-employed individuals and professionals who elected the **8% income tax rate option** under Section 24(A)(2)(b) — the 8% rate is "in lieu of the graduated rates of income tax AND the percentage tax under Section 116"
- VAT-registered taxpayers (they pay VAT instead)
- Taxpayers with gross sales/receipts exceeding ₱3,000,000 (they must register for VAT)

---

## 3. CREATE Act — Section 34 Labor Training Enhancement Deduction

**Provision:** An additional deduction of one-half (1/2) of the value of labor training expenses incurred for skills development of enterprise-based trainees enrolled in senior high schools, higher education institutions, or technical-vocational institutions under apprenticeship agreements, **not exceeding 10% of direct labor wage**.

**Applies to:** Individual taxpayers in trade/business AND corporations.

**Practical impact for most freelancers:** Minimal. Most solo practitioners do not employ registered apprentices. This deduction is primarily relevant to micro-enterprises with employees.

---

## 4. What CREATE Did NOT Change (for Individual Self-Employed)

- Section 24 (individual income tax rates including the 8% option): **NOT amended by CREATE**. Individual income tax rates and the 8% flat rate option remain as established under TRAIN (RA 10963, 2017).
- MCIT (Minimum Corporate Income Tax) reduction from 2% to 1% (effective July 1, 2020 – June 30, 2023): applies **only to corporations**, NOT to sole proprietors or individual taxpayers.
- Individual income tax NOLCO rules: not amended by CREATE.
- Section 34(K) withholding-as-deductibility prerequisite: NOT changed by CREATE (that change came under EOPT in 2024).

---

## 5. Implementing Regulations — CREATE Percentage Tax (Section 116)

**RR 4-2021** (April 8, 2021):
- Only the **rate** was reduced (3% to 1%); all other Section 116 rules remain unchanged.
- Taxpayers who filed Q3 and Q4 2020 percentage tax returns at the 3% rate must **amend** their returns; no penalty for carry-over amendments.
- Overpaid amounts can be:
  - Carried forward to succeeding quarters (for continuing non-VAT filers)
  - Applied for refund/Tax Credit Certificate (if shifted to VAT or to 8% flat rate)
- If the 3% percentage tax had already been claimed as a deductible expense in the 2020 Annual ITR, the taxpayer is **not eligible** for carry-over or refund of the overpaid portion.
- Government withholding agents must withhold **1% (not 3%)** on payments to Section 116 taxpayers for the July 1, 2020 – June 30, 2023 period.

**RMC 67-2021** (May 24, 2021):
*"The decrease of the Percentage Tax rate from 3% to 1%, effective July 1, 2020 until June 30, 2023, applies to both corporate taxpayers and self-employed individuals and professionals whose gross sales or gross receipts are not exceeding Three Million Pesos (₱3,000,000.00) threshold, except for cooperatives and self-employed individuals and professionals availing the 8% Income Tax rate."*

**Quarterly Percentage Tax Filing Deadlines (BIR Form 2551Q) — confirmed by RMC 67-2021:**
- Q1: on or before **April 25**
- Q2: on or before **July 25**
- Q3: on or before **October 25**
- Q4: on or before **January 25** (of the following year)

---

## 6. CREATE + EOPT Interaction on Section 116

The EOPT Act (RA 11976) also amended Section 116, with a key change: the computation basis shifted from **gross receipts** (cash method) to **gross sales** (accrual method) per RR 3-2024. The 3% rate itself was not changed by EOPT — it had already reverted from CREATE's 1% temporary reduction as of July 1, 2023.

**Current rule (2024 onwards):**
- Rate: 3%
- Basis: Gross Sales (accrual — invoice date, not receipt date)
- Applies to: Non-VAT self-employed with gross sales ≤ ₱3,000,000, NOT on 8% option
- Filed via: BIR Form 2551Q, quarterly

---

## 7. Complete Section 116 Percentage Tax Rate History

| Period | Rate | Basis | Law |
|---|---|---|---|
| Pre-January 1, 2018 | 3% | Gross receipts | Pre-TRAIN NIRC |
| Jan 1, 2018 – Jun 30, 2020 | 3% | Gross receipts | RA 10963 (TRAIN) |
| Jul 1, 2020 – Jun 30, 2023 | **1%** | Gross receipts | RA 11534 (CREATE) retroactive |
| Jul 1, 2023 – Oct 27, 2024 | 3% | Gross receipts (transitioning) | CREATE reversion |
| Oct 27, 2024 onwards | **3%** | **Gross sales** | RA 11976 (EOPT) via RR 3-2024 |
