# NIRC Section 34 — Deductions from Gross Income
## For Individuals Engaged in Trade, Business, or Practice of Profession

Source: National Internal Revenue Code of the Philippines (RA 8424), as amended
Fetched: 2026-02-28

---

## Section 34(A) — Expenses

### 34(A)(1) — Ordinary and Necessary Trade, Business, or Professional Expenses

In general, all ordinary and necessary expenses paid or incurred during the taxable year in carrying on or which are directly attributable to the development, management, operation and/or conduct of the trade, business or exercise of a profession, including a reasonable allowance for salaries, wages, and other forms of compensation for personal services actually rendered; traveling expenses here and abroad while away from home in the pursuit of trade, business or profession; rentals and/or other payments which are required as a condition for the continued use or possession, for purposes of the trade, business or profession, of property to which the taxpayer has not taken or is not taking title or in which he has no equity other than that of a lessee, user or possessor; and entertainment, amusement and recreation expenses.

**General criteria for deductibility:**
1. Must be ordinary and necessary in carrying on the business/profession
2. Must be reasonable in amount
3. Must be adequately substantiated with official receipts or other valid documents
4. Must have been paid or incurred during the taxable year
5. Must be directly connected to or related to the taxpayer's business

**Entertainment, Amusement, and Recreation (EAR) expense limits:**
- Sellers of goods: Maximum EAR deduction = 0.5% of net sales
- Service providers: Maximum EAR deduction = 1% of net revenue
- Source: RR No. 10-2002

**Examples of allowable expenses:**
- Salaries and wages of employees
- SSS, PhilHealth, Pag-IBIG contributions (employer share)
- Rental of office/workspace
- Communication expenses (phone, internet — business portion)
- Office supplies and materials
- Travel and transportation expenses for business
- Professional fees paid to other professionals
- Advertising and marketing expenses
- Repairs and maintenance of business assets
- Utilities (electricity, water — business portion)
- Insurance premiums on business property
- Research and development expenses
- Employee training expenses

**Home office deduction:**
- Applicable only to the portion of the residence exclusively used for business
- Must be clearly substantiated (floor plan, billing statements showing business portion)
- BIR requires consistent application

### 34(A)(2) — Withholding Tax as Prerequisite (REPEALED BY EOPT ACT, RA 11976)

Prior to RA 11976 (EOPT Act, 2024): Deductions were disallowed if the taxpayer failed to withhold and remit the required withholding taxes.

**As amended by RA 11976 (EOPT Act):** The requirement to withhold as a prerequisite for deductibility has been repealed. Expenses are now deductible if ordinary, necessary, and duly substantiated, regardless of withholding status. However, non-withholding of required amounts still carries separate penalties.

## Section 34(B) — Interest

Allowable: Interest expense paid or incurred within the taxable year in connection with the taxpayer's profession, trade, or business.

**Limitations:**
- Interest income subject to final tax creates an "interest expense cap": deductible interest = gross interest expense − (33% of interest income subjected to final withholding tax)
- Interest paid on loans between related parties may be disallowed if the arrangement lacks commercial reality
- No deduction for interest on loans used to acquire capital assets (must be capitalized)

---

## Section 34(C) — Taxes

Allowable: Taxes paid or incurred within the taxable year in connection with taxpayer's business, trade, or profession.

**Allowable taxes:**
- Business/privilege tax
- Real property tax on business property
- Import duties
- Professional tax (annual professional fees to PRC/local government)
- Vehicle taxes on business vehicles

**Not allowable:**
- Philippine income tax (being the tax being computed)
- Estate and donor's taxes
- Final taxes (already withheld at source)

---

## Section 34(D) — Losses

Allowable: Losses actually sustained during the taxable year, not compensated for by insurance or otherwise, arising from:
- Fires, storms, shipwrecks, or other casualties
- Robbery, theft, or embezzlement
- Business losses (inventory losses, abandonment losses)

**Net Operating Loss Carry-Over (NOLCO):**
- Net operating loss (excess of allowable deductions over gross income) may be carried over as deduction from gross income for the next THREE consecutive taxable years
- Condition: loss not incurred during a year when the taxpayer was exempt from income tax
- NOLCO is not available to taxpayers using the OSD or 8% option
- NOLCO from COVID-affected years (2020): extended carry-over period per BIR issuances

---

## Section 34(E) — Bad Debts

Allowable: Debts due to the taxpayer actually ascertained to be worthless and charged off within the taxable year.

**Requirements:**
- Must be actually ascertained to be worthless (not merely doubtful)
- Must have been previously included in gross income (no deduction for non-income-related receivables)
- Must be charged off in the books as a loss

---

## Section 34(F) — Depreciation

Allowable: A reasonable allowance for the exhaustion, wear and tear (including reasonable allowance for obsolescence) of property used in the trade or profession.

**Depreciation methods:**
- Straight-line method (most common for BIR purposes)
- Declining balance (up to 2x the straight-line rate)
- Sum of years' digits
- Any other method approved by the BIR

**Rules:**
- Must be applied consistently
- Cannot depreciate land
- For vehicles: only the cost of the vehicle is depreciable; luxury vehicles have cost ceilings under BIR rules (generally ₱2.4M for sedans, per RR guidance)

---

## Section 34(G) — Depletion of Oil and Gas Wells and Mines

Allowable for: taxpayers engaged in oil, gas, mineral extraction.
Not relevant for most self-employed professionals/freelancers.

---

## Section 34(H) — Charitable and Other Contributions

Allowable: Contributions or gifts to qualifying institutions.

**Deductible contributions:**
- To Government agencies for priority activities: fully deductible
- To accredited nonprofit organizations: deductible up to 10% of taxable income (before this deduction, for individuals); 5% for corporations

**Not deductible:**
- Contributions to non-accredited organizations
- Political contributions
- Foreign charities

---

## Section 34(I) — Research and Development

Allowable: Expenditures paid or incurred during the taxable year in connection with the taxpayer's trade, profession, or business.

**Options:**
1. Deduct in full in year incurred
2. Defer and amortize over 60 months from first benefit

---

## Section 34(J) — Pension Trusts

Allowable: Employer contributions to a reasonable pension plan for employees.

---

## Section 34(K) — Additional Requirements for Deductibility

As further clarified by the EOPT Act (RA 11976):
- Expenses must be duly substantiated with official receipts/invoices or other valid documents
- The EOPT Act simplified substantiation: removed the previous requirement to withhold as a condition for deductibility
- BIR Form 2307 (Certificate of Creditable Tax Withheld at Source) is still relevant as an INPUT for the payor's withholding tax obligations, but payor's failure to withhold no longer disqualifies the payee's expense deduction

---

## Section 34(L) — Optional Standard Deduction (OSD)

In lieu of the deductions allowed under Subsections (A) through (K):

**For individuals (self-employed, professionals):**
- OSD = **40%** of **gross sales or gross receipts**
- No itemized expense substantiation required
- Financial statements NOT required to be attached to annual ITR (must be maintained and available upon BIR demand if annual gross > ₱3M per RR 4-2019)

**For corporations:**
- OSD = 40% of **gross income** (different base than individuals)
- Note: gross income for corporations = gross sales minus cost of sales

**Election rules:**
- Must signify intent in the FIRST QUARTERLY return of the taxable year (BIR Form 1701Q Q1)
- Election is IRREVOCABLE for the entire taxable year
- Cannot switch to itemized deductions mid-year once OSD is elected
- Must re-elect each taxable year

**Passive income excluded:**
- Passive income already subjected to final withholding tax is NOT included in the gross receipts base for OSD computation

**When OSD is beneficial:**
- When actual business expenses < 40% of gross receipts
- When taxpayer wants to minimize compliance burden (no substantiation needed)
- When taxpayer has poor or incomplete record-keeping

**When itemized deductions are beneficial:**
- When actual allowable expenses > 40% of gross receipts
- Common for high-expense businesses (e.g., businesses with significant labor costs, rent)

**Net Taxable Income under OSD:**
Net Taxable Income = Gross Receipts − OSD (40% of Gross Receipts)
Net Taxable Income = Gross Receipts × 60%

## Section 36 — Items Not Deductible

The following shall NOT be deducted from gross income:

1. Personal, living, or family expenses
2. Amounts paid for new buildings, permanent improvements, or betterments (must be capitalized)
3. Amounts expended in restoring property when a reserve was maintained
4. Premiums paid on life insurance where taxpayer is directly or indirectly a beneficiary
5. Losses from sales between related parties (family members, controlled corporations)
6. Bribes, kickbacks, and other illegal payments
7. Fines and penalties for violations of law
8. Political campaign contributions and lobbying expenses
