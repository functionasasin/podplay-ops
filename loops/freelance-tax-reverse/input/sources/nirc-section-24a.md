Source: National Internal Revenue Code of the Philippines (RA 8424), as amended by RA 10963 (TRAIN Law)

## Section 24(A) — Rates of Income Tax on Individual Citizen and Individual Resident Alien of the Philippines

### Section 24(A)(1) — In General

Except as otherwise provided in this Code, an income tax is hereby imposed:

(A) On the taxable income defined in Section 31 of this Code, other than income subject to tax under Subsections (B), (C) and (D) of this Section, derived for each taxable year from all sources within and without the Philippines by every individual citizen of the Philippines residing therein.

### Section 24(A)(2)(a) — General Tax Rate for Individuals (Employees and Self-Employed)

For individuals earning purely compensation income and for individuals engaged in business or practice of profession whose gross sales/receipts DO NOT EXCEED ₱3,000,000:

**Schedule 1: Effective January 1, 2018 to December 31, 2022**

| Bracket | Annual Taxable Income | Base Tax | Marginal Rate | Excess Over |
|---------|----------------------|----------|---------------|-------------|
| 1 | Not over ₱250,000 | ₱0 | 0% | — |
| 2 | Over ₱250,000 but not over ₱400,000 | ₱0 | 20% | ₱250,000 |
| 3 | Over ₱400,000 but not over ₱800,000 | ₱30,000 | 25% | ₱400,000 |
| 4 | Over ₱800,000 but not over ₱2,000,000 | ₱130,000 | 30% | ₱800,000 |
| 5 | Over ₱2,000,000 but not over ₱8,000,000 | ₱490,000 | 32% | ₱2,000,000 |
| 6 | Over ₱8,000,000 | ₱2,410,000 | 35% | ₱8,000,000 |

**Schedule 2: Effective January 1, 2023 and onwards (current)**

| Bracket | Annual Taxable Income | Base Tax | Marginal Rate | Excess Over |
|---------|----------------------|----------|---------------|-------------|
| 1 | Not over ₱250,000 | ₱0 | 0% | — |
| 2 | Over ₱250,000 but not over ₱400,000 | ₱0 | 15% | ₱250,000 |
| 3 | Over ₱400,000 but not over ₱800,000 | ₱22,500 | 20% | ₱400,000 |
| 4 | Over ₱800,000 but not over ₱2,000,000 | ₱102,500 | 25% | ₱800,000 |
| 5 | Over ₱2,000,000 but not over ₱8,000,000 | ₱402,500 | 30% | ₱2,000,000 |
| 6 | Over ₱8,000,000 | ₱2,202,500 | 35% | ₱8,000,000 |

**Formula:**
Tax Due = Base Tax + (Marginal Rate × (Taxable Income − Lower Bracket Limit))

### Section 24(A)(2)(b) — 8% Income Tax Rate Option

In lieu of the graduated income tax rates under Section 24(A)(2)(a) and the percentage tax under Section 116, a qualified individual may elect to be taxed at **eight percent (8%)** on gross sales or receipts and other non-operating income in excess of **₱250,000**.

**Formula:**
Tax Due = (Gross Receipts + Other Non-Operating Income − ₱250,000) × 0.08

**Eligibility conditions:**
- Gross sales/receipts and other non-operating income must NOT exceed ₱3,000,000 during the taxable year
- Must NOT be VAT-registered
- Must NOT be a partner in a General Professional Partnership
- Must NOT be subject to other percentage taxes (except Sec. 116)
- Must NOT be a BMBE (Barangay Micro Business Enterprise) tax-exempt

**Key rules:**
- In lieu of BOTH graduated income tax AND percentage tax under Section 116
- For mixed-income earners: ₱250,000 deduction does NOT apply (already in the compensation income bracket)
- Election is IRREVOCABLE for the taxable year once made
- Must re-elect each taxable year (does not carry over automatically)
- If gross receipts exceed ₱3,000,000 mid-year: revert to graduated rates, tax credit given for 8% payments made
- No deductions (itemized or OSD) are allowed under this option
- Financial statements NOT required to attach to annual ITR

### Section 24(A)(2)(c) — Applicability to Mixed-Income Earners

For mixed-income earners (compensation income + business/professional income):
- Compensation income is ALWAYS taxed under the graduated rates
- Business/professional income portion MAY elect 8% rate (if gross receipts ≤ ₱3M)
- Under 8% option for mixed-income: no ₱250,000 deduction from business income (used for compensation)
- Under graduated rates for mixed-income: business taxable income is added to compensation income, total is subject to graduated rates
