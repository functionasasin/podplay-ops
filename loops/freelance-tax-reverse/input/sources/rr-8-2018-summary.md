# Revenue Regulations No. 8-2018 — TRAIN Law Implementing Rules for Individual Income Tax
## Including 8% Income Tax Rate Option for Self-Employed Individuals and Professionals

Source: BIR Revenue Regulations No. 8-2018, issued February 20, 2018
Fetched: 2026-02-28

---

## Overview

RR No. 8-2018 implements the income tax provisions of RA 10963 (TRAIN Law) for individual taxpayers. It clarifies:
- The new graduated income tax rate tables (Schedule 1 and Schedule 2)
- The 8% income tax rate option — eligibility, election procedure, filing obligations
- Treatment of mixed-income earners
- New rules on itemized deductions and OSD under TRAIN

---

## Part I — The 8% Income Tax Rate Option

### Eligible Taxpayers

The 8% income tax rate option is available to:
1. Purely self-employed individuals (sole proprietors in trade or business)
2. Professionals practicing their profession (lawyers, doctors, CPAs, engineers, consultants, etc.)
3. Mixed-income earners (for their BUSINESS INCOME PORTION ONLY) — see separate rules

**Gross receipts/sales threshold:** Must NOT exceed ₱3,000,000 during the taxable year (the VAT registration threshold).

### Ineligible Taxpayers

The 8% option is NOT available to:
1. Purely compensation income earners (employees)
2. VAT-registered taxpayers (regardless of income level)
3. Partners in General Professional Partnerships (GPPs) — the GPP itself is not subject to income tax; partners are taxed on their distributive share, which is subject to graduated rates
4. Individuals exempt from income tax (e.g., BMBEs under RA 9178)
5. Individuals subject to OTHER percentage taxes under the NIRC (Sections 117-128)

### Effect of the 8% Election

The 8% income tax rate is **in lieu of BOTH:**
1. The graduated income tax rates under Section 24(A)(2)(a)
2. The percentage tax under Section 116

**Therefore:** No separate BIR Form 2551Q (Quarterly Percentage Tax Return) is required when the 8% option is elected.

### How to Elect the 8% Option

**New business registrants:**
- At time of registration using BIR Form 1901
- OR on the initial quarterly return (BIR Form 1701Q or 2551Q) of the taxable year after commencement of business

**Existing taxpayers:**
- By filing BIR Form 1905 (Application for Registration Information Update) before the first quarterly return deadline
- OR by ticking/clicking Item No. 16 (8% income tax rate option) in BIR Form 1701Q for Q1
- OR by filing a NIL BIR Form 2551Q with notation "8% income tax rate elected"

### Irrevocability

Once elected, the 8% option is **irrevocable for the entire taxable year.** The taxpayer cannot switch back to graduated rates for that year. If Q1 1701Q is filed with the 8% election ticked, this cannot be undone.

At the start of the next taxable year, the election resets — the taxpayer must again choose between 8% and graduated.

### Annual Re-Election Required

The 8% option does NOT automatically carry over to the next year. It must be elected anew each taxable year:
- Failure to elect defaults to graduated rates
- Election is still via BIR Form 1701Q Q1 or Form 2551Q Q1

### Deregistration from Percentage Tax

If a previously percentage-tax-paying taxpayer elects the 8% option, they should:
1. File BIR Form 1905 to update registration and deregister from percentage tax
2. File a NIL BIR Form 2551Q for Q1 with the annotation that they elected the 8% income tax rate

## Part II — Mixed-Income Earners

### Definition

A mixed-income earner is a taxpayer who derives income from BOTH:
- Employment (compensation income) — mandatory graduated rates
- Business or practice of profession (self-employment income) — can choose 8% or graduated

### How Mixed-Income is Treated

**Compensation income:**
- Always subject to graduated income tax rates under Section 24(A)
- Cannot be taxed at 8%

**Business/professional income:**
- CAN be taxed at 8% if gross receipts ≤ ₱3,000,000

**The ₱250,000 exemption for mixed-income earners:**
- The ₱250,000 threshold deduction is NOT applied to the business income portion of a mixed-income earner
- Reason: the ₱250,000 exemption is already factored into Bracket 1 of the graduated rate table for compensation income
- Formula for mixed-income earner business income under 8% option:
  8% Tax on Business Income = Gross Business Receipts × 0.08 (NO ₱250,000 deduction)

**Purely self-employed (no compensation income):**
  8% Tax = (Gross Receipts − ₱250,000) × 0.08

### Annual Income Tax Filing for Mixed-Income Earners

Mixed-income earners file BIR Form 1701 (Annual ITR) — NOT Form 1701A. Form 1701A is only for:
- Purely self-employed under graduated rates OR
- Purely self-employed under 8% option
- NOT for mixed-income earners

## Part III — Mid-Year Breach of the ₱3M Threshold

### When a Taxpayer Exceeds ₱3,000,000 Mid-Year

If a taxpayer who elected the 8% option finds that their cumulative gross receipts have exceeded ₱3,000,000 during the year:

1. The 8% option is **immediately disqualified** from that point forward
2. The taxpayer must recompute income tax under graduated rates for the ENTIRE year
3. All 8% quarterly payments already made are credited against the graduated-rate annual tax due
4. The taxpayer must update BIR registration to VAT-registered status within the month following the month of breach
5. Going forward, the taxpayer is subject to 12% VAT (not 3% percentage tax) on gross receipts above ₱3M

### BIR Registration Update

Once ₱3M is exceeded, the taxpayer must:
- File BIR Form 1905 to update registration from non-VAT to VAT
- Register for 12% VAT and file BIR Form 2550Q/2550M going forward
- Annual ITR for that year will be filed on BIR Form 1701 (not 1701A)

## Part IV — Filing Obligations Summary Under 8% Option

| Obligation | Required? |
|-----------|-----------|
| BIR Form 1701Q (Quarterly ITR) | YES — Q1, Q2, Q3 |
| BIR Form 1701A (Annual ITR) | YES (for purely self-employed) |
| BIR Form 2551Q (Quarterly % Tax) | NO — waived under 8% option |
| Audited Financial Statements | NOT REQUIRED to attach (but maintain books) |
| Books of Accounts | YES — required |
| Official Receipts/Invoices | YES — required to issue |

---

## Part V — 8% Option vs Graduated Rates: Which Form to File?

| Scenario | Annual ITR Form |
|----------|----------------|
| Purely self-employed, graduated rates (OSD or itemized) | BIR Form 1701A |
| Purely self-employed, 8% flat rate | BIR Form 1701A |
| Mixed-income earner (any combination) | BIR Form 1701 |
| Purely compensation income (employee only) | BIR Form 1700 (employer usually substitutes) |
