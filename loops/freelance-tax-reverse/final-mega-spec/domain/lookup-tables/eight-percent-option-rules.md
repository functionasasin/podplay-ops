## Philippine Freelance & Self-Employed Income Tax Optimizer

**Legal basis:** NIRC Sec. 24(A)(2)(b) as amended by RA 10963 (TRAIN Law); RR No. 8-2018; RMO No. 23-2018; RMC No. 32-2018; RMC No. 50-2018
**Status:** COMPLETE
**Last updated:** 2026-03-01

## Part 1 — Eligibility Matrix

All conditions below must be satisfied simultaneously. If ANY condition is FALSE, the 8% option is NOT available.

| # | Condition | Satisfied When | Violation Outcome |
|---|-----------|---------------|-------------------|
| E-01 | Taxpayer is an individual | Taxpayer is a natural person (not corporation, partnership, cooperative) | Out of scope — entity not supported |
| E-02 | Income source | Taxpayer earns from self-employment (sole proprietorship, practice of profession) OR mixed income (compensation + self-employment) | Pure compensation earners: not eligible |
| E-03 | Gross receipts threshold | Gross sales/receipts + other non-operating income for the taxable year do NOT exceed ₱3,000,000 | Exceeds ₱3M: 8% option unavailable; graduated + OSD or itemized only |
| E-04 | VAT registration status | Taxpayer is NOT VAT-registered (Certificate of Registration does not bear VAT) | VAT-registered: not eligible regardless of income level |
| E-05 | GPP partner exclusion | Taxpayer is NOT a partner in a General Professional Partnership (GPP) — OR — for GPP partners, the 8% election applies only to their OTHER self-employment income not derived from the GPP | Distributive GPP share: cannot use 8% option for that income stream |
| E-06 | Other percentage tax | Taxpayer is subject ONLY to Section 116 percentage tax (3% OPT), or is exempt from percentage tax; NOT subject to Sections 117–128 other percentage taxes | Subject to other % taxes (e.g., Sec. 120 carriers, Sec. 125 amusement): not eligible |
| E-07 | BMBE exclusion | Taxpayer is NOT a BMBE (Barangay Micro Business Enterprise) already exempt from income tax under RA 9178 | BMBE tax-exempt: income already exempt; no 8% needed |
| E-08 | Timely election | Taxpayer has signified intent to avail 8% at the proper time (see Part 3) | Late election (Q1 already filed under graduated): election window closed for that year |

**Summary:** The 8% option is available to INDIVIDUAL self-employed persons (sole proprietors, professionals) or mixed-income earners whose gross sales/receipts ≤ ₱3M, who are not VAT-registered, not GPP partners (for that income stream), not subject to other % taxes, and not BMBE-exempt — who timely elect.

## Part 3 — Election Procedure by Taxpayer Type

### 3A — New Business Registrant (First-Time Registration)

A new business registrant is a taxpayer filing BIR Form 1901 (Application for Registration) for the first time, or registering within the current taxable year after commencement of business.

**Election Method A — At Registration:**
1. Complete BIR Form 1901 (Application for Registration for Self-Employed Individuals, Estates, and Trusts)
2. In the Tax Type section, check the box for 8% income tax option
3. Submit Form 1901 to the RDO (Revenue District Office) where registered
4. Certificate of Registration (COR) issued will reflect non-VAT status and 8% election
5. No BIR Form 2551Q (Quarterly Percentage Tax Return) is required going forward
6. Effect: 8% option is elected for the current taxable year from the date of registration

**Election Method B — Initial Quarterly Return:**
1. Register via Form 1901 WITHOUT specifying 8% election
2. File BIR Form 1701Q for Q1 (the first quarterly income tax return after commencement of business)
3. On Item 16 ("Tax Regime"), select Option B: 8% Income Tax Rate
4. This Q1 election serves as the signification of intent
5. No BIR Form 2551Q required going forward

**Election Method C — Initial Percentage Tax Return:**
1. Register via Form 1901 without specifying 8% election
2. File BIR Form 2551Q for Q1 with:
   - Tax due: ₱0.00
   - Notation in remarks/memo field: "Availing of 8% Income Tax Rate Option for Taxable Year [YEAR]"
3. The zero-amount filing with notation signals the election
4. Also file BIR Form 1701Q for Q1 with Item 16 set to 8%

### 3B — Existing Taxpayer Electing 8% for a New Tax Year

An existing taxpayer is one already registered with the BIR who wishes to elect 8% at the beginning of a new taxable year.

**Election Method D — Update Registration (Preferred):**
1. File BIR Form 1905 (Application for Registration Information Update / Correction / Cancellation) at the RDO where registered
2. Timing: Must be filed BEFORE the Q1 percentage tax return deadline (April 25 for Q1, which covers January–March)
3. Purpose: Request end-dating of the quarterly percentage tax form type (2551Q); this signals election of 8%
4. Submit original Certificate of Registration (COR) for updating (first-time 8% electors)
5. After BIR processes Form 1905: taxpayer's registration is updated to reflect 8% election; COR may be updated
6. Then file BIR Form 1701Q for Q1 with Item 16 checked as 8%
7. If BIR processes the Form 1905 slowly: proceed to Method E while waiting

**Election Method E — Q1 Income Tax Return:**
1. File BIR Form 1701Q for Q1 with Item 16 selected as Option B: 8% Income Tax Rate
2. Timing: Must be the Q1 return; cannot elect on Q2 or Q3
3. Q1 1701Q deadline: May 15 (for Jan–Mar quarter; note: under EOPT all taxpayers file by May 15)
4. This Q1 election is irrevocable for the rest of the taxable year

**Election Method F — Zero-Amount Q1 Percentage Tax Return with Notation:**
1. File BIR Form 2551Q for Q1 with Tax Due = ₱0.00
2. Add notation in remarks: "Availing of 8% Income Tax Rate Option for Taxable Year [YEAR]"
3. This notation alone constitutes a valid election signification
4. Also file BIR Form 1701Q for Q1 with Item 16 marked as 8%
5. No further BIR Form 2551Q required for Q2 and Q3 of that year

**Default (no election made):** If the taxpayer does not perform any of the election procedures above by the Q1 1701Q filing deadline, the taxpayer is automatically considered to have availed of the GRADUATED RATES for that taxable year. No retroactive election is permitted.

## Part 4 — Irrevocability Rules

| Rule | Description |
|------|-------------|
| R-IRREV-01 | Once elected on Q1 1701Q (or at registration), the 8% option is IRREVOCABLE for the ENTIRE taxable year |
| R-IRREV-02 | Cannot switch from 8% to graduated mid-year (except via automatic disqualification — threshold breach) |
| R-IRREV-03 | Cannot switch from graduated to 8% mid-year after Q1 |
| R-IRREV-04 | Deduction method under graduated rates (OSD vs. itemized) is also irrevocable once elected on Q1 (RR 8-2018, Part VI) |
| R-IRREV-05 | The 8% election does NOT carry over to the next taxable year; must be re-elected each year |
| R-IRREV-06 | Annual re-election: if taxpayer fails to elect 8% on Q1 of the new year, they default to graduated rates for that year |
| R-IRREV-07 | Exception to irrevocability: Mid-year breach of ₱3M threshold AUTOMATICALLY AND RETROACTIVELY disqualifies the 8% option for the entire year (not the taxpayer's choice — it is mandatory) |

## Part 5 — The ₱250,000 Deduction — Rules and Application

The ₱250,000 deduction under the 8% option is not a personal exemption or a business expense; it is a statutory adjustment built into the formula to make the 8% option equivalent in concept to the zero-bracket in the graduated rate table.

### 5A — When the ₱250,000 Deduction APPLIES

**Condition:** Taxpayer has NO compensation income for the year (purely self-employed).

**Formula:**
```
8%_tax_due = MAX(0, gross_receipts + non_operating_income - 250,000) × 0.08
```

Where:
- `gross_receipts` = total professional fees / service revenues received / accrued for the year, LESS: sales returns, allowances, discounts (net figure)
- `non_operating_income` = see Part 6 for full definition
- `250,000` = the statutory deduction (always exactly ₱250,000; not prorated for partial years)
- `MAX(0, ...)` = if gross_receipts + non_operating_income < ₱250,000, the tax is ₱0 (cannot be negative)

**At quarterly level (cumulative):**
```
8%_tax_due_cumulative_QN = MAX(0, cumulative_gross_QN + cumulative_non_op_QN - 250,000) × 0.08
8%_tax_payable_QN = 8%_tax_due_cumulative_QN - cumulative_cwt_QN - prior_quarterly_payments_QN
```

The ₱250,000 deduction is applied against the CUMULATIVE total at each quarterly filing, not freshly each quarter. This means:
- Q1: deducted once from Q1 cumulative
- Q2: deducted once from Q1+Q2 cumulative (effective deduction already absorbed in Q1 computation)
- Q3: deducted once from Q1+Q2+Q3 cumulative
- Annual: deducted once from full-year cumulative

This is equivalent to deducting ₱250,000 once per year, because each quarterly tax due is computed on the cumulative basis and prior payments are subtracted.

**Example of quarterly ₱250K application (purely self-employed, 8% elected):**
- Q1 cumulative gross: ₱200,000 → Tax = MAX(0, 200,000 − 250,000) × 8% = ₱0
- Q2 cumulative gross: ₱500,000 → Tax = MAX(0, 500,000 − 250,000) × 8% = 250,000 × 8% = ₱20,000. Pay: ₱20,000 − CWT − ₱0 (paid Q1)
- Q3 cumulative gross: ₱900,000 → Tax = MAX(0, 900,000 − 250,000) × 8% = 650,000 × 8% = ₱52,000. Pay: ₱52,000 − CWT − ₱20,000 paid Q2 net
- Annual cumulative gross: ₱1,200,000 → Tax = MAX(0, 1,200,000 − 250,000) × 8% = 950,000 × 8% = ₱76,000. Balance = ₱76,000 − CWT − all quarterly payments

### 5B — When the ₱250,000 Deduction does NOT APPLY

**Condition:** Taxpayer has compensation income from employment (mixed income earner).

**Reason:** The ₱250,000 is the zero-bracket of the graduated rate table applied to compensation income. When compensation income is taxed under graduated rates, the ₱250,000 bracket is already applied to that compensation. To apply it again to the business income would be double-counting.

**Formula for mixed-income earner (business portion):**
```
8%_business_tax = (gross_business_receipts + non_operating_income) × 0.08
```

**Critical RMC 50-2018 rule — no transfer of unused ₱250,000:**
If a mixed-income earner's TAXABLE compensation income is below ₱250,000 (e.g., employee earns ₱150,000 taxable compensation), the unused portion of the ₱250,000 bracket (₱100,000 in this example) is NOT transferable to the business income side. The business income is still taxed at 8% of the full amount with no deduction.

**Example:**
- Compensation income: ₱150,000 taxable (after employer withholding allowances). Graduated tax = ₱0 (in zero bracket).
- Business income: ₱800,000 gross.
- 8% Business Tax = ₱800,000 × 0.08 = ₱64,000 (NOT ₱(800,000 − 100,000) × 8% = ₱56,000)
- The ₱100,000 "leftover" from the ₱250,000 bracket is permanently lost.

## Part 6 — Gross Receipts and Non-Operating Income Definitions

### 6A — Gross Sales/Receipts (for 8% threshold and computation)

"Gross sales/receipts" under the 8% option means:

| Component | Include? | Notes |
|-----------|---------|-------|
| Professional service fees billed and received | YES | Core revenue |
| Service fees accrued but not yet collected | YES, if accrual method; NO, if cash method | Depends on accounting method on COR |
| Sales of goods (if trade business) | YES | Gross sales |
| Sales returns | DEDUCT | Net of returns/allowances |
| Sales allowances | DEDUCT | Net of returns/allowances |
| Sales discounts | DEDUCT | Net figure used |
| VAT portion of invoices (if VAT-registered) | NOT applicable | 8% option unavailable to VAT-registered; moot |
| Gross receipts from sub-contracting | YES | All service revenues |
| Retainer fees, consultation fees, honoraria | YES | All professional compensation |
| Advances from clients (if already earned) | YES | If earned; not if merely deposits |
| Reimbursements billed to clients (e.g., travel) | DEPENDS | If billed on cost-plus basis with markup: include. If mere pass-through at cost with no markup: exclude (RMC guidance) |

**For threshold determination (is taxpayer below ₱3M?):**
```
threshold_base = gross_receipts_net_of_returns + other_non_operating_income
eight_pct_eligible = threshold_base <= 3_000_000
```

**For 8% tax computation base (purely self-employed):**
```
eight_pct_tax_base = MAX(0, threshold_base - 250_000)
eight_pct_tax_due = eight_pct_tax_base × 0.08
```

### 6B — Other Non-Operating Income

"Other non-operating income" is included in BOTH the threshold determination AND the 8% tax computation base.

| Income Type | Include in Non-Operating? | Notes |
|------------|--------------------------|-------|
| Interest income from business bank accounts (savings/checking used for business) | YES | Business-related interest |
| Rental income from business property used in trade/profession | YES | If the property is registered as a business asset |
| Gain on sale of business assets (e.g., laptop, office equipment) | YES | Capital gains from business assets |
| Royalty income from professional works | YES | If related to practice of profession |
| Interest from personal savings accounts | NO | Subject to 20% FWT at source; already final-taxed |
| Dividends from domestic corporations | NO | Subject to 10% FDT; already final-taxed |
| Lottery winnings | NO | Subject to 20% FWT; already final-taxed |
| Prize money (above ₱10,000) | NO | Subject to 20% FWT; already final-taxed |
| Passbook interest, time deposit interest | NO | Final-taxed at source |
| Capital gains from sale of real property (at 6% CGT) | NO | Subject to Capital Gains Tax; not part of income tax base |
| Capital gains from sale of shares not listed on PSE | NO | Subject to 15% CGT |

**Key rule:** Income already subject to FINAL WITHHOLDING TAX is excluded from the 8% computation base and threshold. This is because final-taxed income is already fully taxed and not included in annual ITR income.

## Part 7 — Mid-Year Threshold Breach Procedure

This applies when a taxpayer who elected the 8% option finds that cumulative gross receipts + non-operating income EXCEEDS ₱3,000,000 at some point during the taxable year.

### Step-by-Step Breach Procedure

**Step 1: Detection — Determine the breach month**
- Monitor cumulative gross receipts + non-operating income each month
- The breach month is the calendar month in which cumulative receipts first exceed ₱3,000,000
- Example: January cumulative = ₱2,800,000; February = ₱3,100,000 → Breach month = February

**Step 2: 8% Option Auto-Disqualification**
- The 8% option is AUTOMATICALLY disqualified from the beginning of the breach year
- This is retroactive — the taxpayer is treated as if they were always on graduated rates for the entire year
- No partial 8% computation for the months before breach

**Step 3: BIR Registration Update (File Form 1905)**
- File BIR Form 1905 within the month FOLLOWING the breach month
- Example: Breach in February → Form 1905 filed in March
- Purposes of Form 1905:
  - Update tax type from non-VAT / Section 116 percentage tax to VAT-registered
  - End-date the percentage tax form type (2551Q)
  - Add VAT form type (2550M / 2550Q)
  - Add 12% VAT to Certificate of Registration

**Step 4: Retroactive Percentage Tax Liability**
- Percentage tax under Section 116 (3%) is due from January 1 through the last full month BEFORE the month of VAT registration
- Example: Breach in February, VAT registration effective March 1 → Percentage tax due for January only (one month)
- This retroactive percentage tax must be paid via amended/late BIR Form 2551Q
- Rate: 3% (not 1%; CREATE 1% rate expired June 30, 2023)

**Step 5: Annual Income Tax Recomputation**
- At year-end, file BIR Form 1701 (NOT Form 1701A — breach year must use 1701)
- Compute income tax under GRADUATED RATES for the full year
- Under either Path A (itemized) or Path B (OSD) — taxpayer selects deduction method
- If taxpayer had originally elected OSD in Q1 (for graduated rate path before 8%): OSD election from Q1 applies to the full year
- If taxpayer had elected 8% in Q1 and never made a deduction method selection: Must choose OSD or itemized at annual filing

**Step 6: Credit Prior 8% Quarterly Payments**
- All 8% quarterly tax payments made before the breach are treated as TAX CREDITS (not deductions) on the annual return
- These credits offset the graduated-rate annual tax due
- If credits exceed the annual tax due: excess is refundable or can be carried over to the next taxable year

**Step 7: VAT Going Forward**
- From the month of breach (the month cumulative gross first exceeded ₱3M): file VAT returns
- BIR Form 2550M (monthly VAT declaration) or BIR Form 2550Q (quarterly VAT return, if on quarterly VAT)
- 12% VAT applies on all sales/receipts from the breach month onwards
- Input VAT credits available for VAT-registered purchases from breach month onwards

### Breach Computation Pseudocode

```
function handle_mid_year_breach(
  monthly_receipts: array[12] of decimal,
  cwt_paid: decimal,
  quarterly_8pct_payments: decimal,
  non_operating_income: decimal
) -> BreachResult:

  cumulative = 0
  breach_month = null

  for month in 1..12:
    cumulative += monthly_receipts[month]
    if cumulative + non_operating_income > 3_000_000 and breach_month is null:
      breach_month = month

  if breach_month is null:
    return BreachResult { breached: false }  // No breach

  // Retroactive percentage tax: 3% for months Jan through (breach_month - 1)
  receipts_before_breach = sum(monthly_receipts[1..breach_month-1])
  retroactive_pct_tax = receipts_before_breach * 0.03

  // Annual income tax under graduated rates for full year
  full_year_gross = sum(monthly_receipts[1..12]) + non_operating_income
  // Taxpayer must elect OSD or itemized — get net taxable income
  // (OSD path shown; itemized path requires expense input)
  osd_deduction = full_year_gross * 0.40
  net_taxable_osd = full_year_gross - osd_deduction
  annual_it_osd = compute_graduated_tax(net_taxable_osd, tax_year)

  // Credit prior 8% quarterly payments
  annual_it_payable = annual_it_osd - cwt_paid - quarterly_8pct_payments

  vat_registration_month = breach_month + 1  // Register for VAT next month

  return BreachResult {
    breached: true,
    breach_month: breach_month,
    vat_registration_month: vat_registration_month,
    retroactive_pct_tax: retroactive_pct_tax,
    annual_it_due_graduated: annual_it_osd,
    prior_8pct_payments_credited: quarterly_8pct_payments,
    annual_it_payable: annual_it_payable,
    form_for_annual_itr: "1701",  // NOT 1701A in breach year
    pct_tax_due_months: "Jan through month " + (breach_month - 1)
  }
```

## Part 8 — VAT Reversion Rules (Return to Non-VAT After Breach)

A taxpayer who exceeded ₱3M and became VAT-registered may revert to NON-VAT status under the following conditions:

| Condition | Rule |
|-----------|------|
| Duration | Gross sales/receipts must remain BELOW ₱3,000,000 for THREE (3) CONSECUTIVE taxable years following the year of breach |
| Example | Breached in TY2024 → Non-VAT reversion possible if gross < ₱3M in TY2025, TY2026, AND TY2027 |
| Procedure | File BIR Form 1905 to cancel VAT registration after completing the three-year period |
| Effect | Once non-VAT again, taxpayer is subject to Section 116 percentage tax (3%) |
| 8% re-eligibility | Once non-VAT and gross ≤ ₱3M in subsequent year, may elect 8% option again for that new year |
| Verification | BIR may audit the three-year period before approving VAT cancellation |
| Continued obligation | During the three-year waiting period, taxpayer is still VAT-registered and must file VAT returns even if no VAT-taxable sales |

## Part 10 — Quarterly 8% Computation Mechanics (Detailed)

### For Purely Self-Employed (No Compensation)

**Form 1701Q Schedule II fields relevant to 8%:**

| Item | Label | Value |
|------|-------|-------|
| 47 | Gross Sales/Receipts and Other Non-Operating Income (cumulative) | Sum from January 1 to end of quarter |
| 48 | Less: ₱250,000 allowance | ₱250,000 (ONLY for purely self-employed; blank for mixed) |
| 49 | Net Taxable Income (8%) [Item 47 − Item 48] | Item 47 − ₱250,000 (or ₱0 if negative) |
| 50 | Tax Due (8%) [Item 49 × 8%] | Item 49 × 0.08 |
| 51 | Less: Tax Credits (CWT from Form 2307, year-to-date) | Total CWT credits received Jan to end of quarter |
| 52 | Less: Prior Quarter Income Tax Payments | Q1 actual payment (for Q2); Q1+Q2 payments (for Q3) |
| 53 | Balance Tax Still Due (or Overpayment) | Item 50 − Item 51 − Item 52 |

**Q1 Computation:**
```
q1_gross = gross_receipts_jan_to_mar + non_op_income_jan_to_mar
q1_taxable = MAX(0, q1_gross - 250_000)
q1_tax_due = q1_taxable * 0.08
q1_cwt = sum(form_2307_credits_jan_to_mar)
q1_payable = MAX(0, q1_tax_due - q1_cwt)
// Note: if q1_payable < 0, the overpayment is carried forward; cannot claim refund at quarterly level
```

**Q2 Computation:**
```
q2_gross = gross_receipts_jan_to_jun + non_op_income_jan_to_jun  // CUMULATIVE
q2_taxable = MAX(0, q2_gross - 250_000)
q2_tax_due_cumulative = q2_taxable * 0.08
q2_cwt = sum(form_2307_credits_jan_to_jun)  // CUMULATIVE
q2_payable = MAX(0, q2_tax_due_cumulative - q2_cwt - q1_actual_payment)
// q1_actual_payment = what was actually remitted for Q1 (not just computed)
```

**Q3 Computation:**
```
q3_gross = gross_receipts_jan_to_sep + non_op_income_jan_to_sep  // CUMULATIVE
q3_taxable = MAX(0, q3_gross - 250_000)
q3_tax_due_cumulative = q3_taxable * 0.08
q3_cwt = sum(form_2307_credits_jan_to_sep)  // CUMULATIVE
q3_payable = MAX(0, q3_tax_due_cumulative - q3_cwt - q1_actual_payment - q2_actual_payment)
```

**Annual (1701A under 8%):**
```
annual_gross = gross_receipts_jan_to_dec + non_op_income_jan_to_dec
annual_taxable = MAX(0, annual_gross - 250_000)
annual_tax_due = annual_taxable * 0.08
annual_cwt = sum(form_2307_credits_jan_to_dec)  // Full year CWT
annual_payable = annual_tax_due - annual_cwt - q1_actual_payment - q2_actual_payment - q3_actual_payment
// If annual_payable > 0: taxpayer pays balance with annual return (or in installments if > ₱2,000)
// If annual_payable < 0: taxpayer may claim refund or carry over to next year
```

### For Mixed-Income Earner (Compensation + Business)

**Key difference:** No ₱250,000 deduction on the business side.

**Q1 Business Income Computation:**
```
q1_biz_gross = biz_receipts_jan_to_mar + non_op_income_jan_to_mar
q1_biz_tax_due = q1_biz_gross * 0.08  // No ₱250K deduction
q1_comp_tax = graduated_tax(taxable_compensation_q1)  // Separate computation
q1_total_tax_due = q1_biz_tax_due + q1_comp_tax
q1_cwt = sum(all_cwt_credits_jan_to_mar)  // Includes both biz and compensation CWT
q1_payable = MAX(0, q1_total_tax_due - q1_cwt)
```

**Annual (Form 1701 for mixed income, 8% on business):**
```
annual_biz_tax = annual_biz_gross * 0.08
annual_comp_tax = graduated_tax(annual_taxable_comp)
annual_total_tax_due = annual_biz_tax + annual_comp_tax
annual_payable = annual_total_tax_due - annual_cwt - all_quarterly_payments
```

## Part 11 — Worked Examples

### Example 8E-01: Purely Self-Employed, Low Income (Below ₱250K Total)

**Profile:** Beginner VA. Gross receipts Jan–Dec = ₱220,000. No non-operating income. No CWT credits.

**8% Tax Computation:**
- Gross receipts: ₱220,000
- Less: ₱250,000 exemption: ₱250,000
- Tax base: MAX(0, 220,000 − 250,000) = MAX(0, −30,000) = ₱0
- 8% Tax Due: ₱0 × 8% = **₱0**
- Percentage Tax: ₱0 (waived under 8%)
- **Total Tax Burden: ₱0**

**Note:** Taxpayer STILL MUST FILE quarterly 1701Q and annual 1701A. Zero-tax return must be filed to avoid late-filing penalties. Failure to file = ₱1,000 compromise penalty (first offense NIL return).

**Graduated comparison:**
- Path B (OSD): Net taxable = 60% × ₱220,000 = ₱132,000 → Tax = ₱0 (within zero bracket). Percentage tax = 3% × ₱220,000 = ₱6,600. Total = ₱6,600.
- **Winner: 8% option saves ₱6,600** (₱6,600 percentage tax avoided)

### Example 8E-02: Purely Self-Employed, ₱1,000,000 Gross, No Expenses

**Profile:** Freelance developer. Gross receipts = ₱1,000,000. No other income. No CWT.

**8% Tax Computation:**
- Gross: ₱1,000,000
- Less: ₱250,000
- Tax base: ₱750,000
- 8% Tax Due: ₱750,000 × 0.08 = **₱60,000**
- Percentage Tax: ₱0 (waived)
- **Total: ₱60,000**

**Graduated comparison:**
- Path B (OSD): Net taxable = 60% × ₱1,000,000 = ₱600,000. Tax = ₱22,500 + 20% × (₱600,000 − ₱400,000) = ₱22,500 + ₱40,000 = ₱62,500. Pct tax = ₱30,000. Total = ₱92,500.
- **8% saves ₱32,500 vs Path B**

### Example 8E-03: Purely Self-Employed, ₱1,000,000 Gross, High Expenses (70%)

**Profile:** Photographer with studio. Gross = ₱1,000,000. Expenses = ₱700,000.

**8% Tax:**
- Tax base: ₱750,000. Tax = ₱60,000. Total = ₱60,000.

**Path A (Itemized):**
- Net taxable = ₱1,000,000 − ₱700,000 = ₱300,000
- Graduated tax = 15% × (₱300,000 − ₱250,000) = ₱7,500
- Percentage tax = 3% × ₱1,000,000 = ₱30,000
- **Total: ₱37,500**
- **Itemized WINS: saves ₱22,500 vs 8%**

**Breakeven:** At 70% expense ratio, itemized is superior. 8% wins when expense ratio < 68% for ₱1M gross.

### Example 8E-04: Mixed-Income Earner, 8% on Business Side

**Profile:** Employee (₱600,000 compensation) + Freelance designer (₱800,000 gross business receipts).
CWT from compensation: ₱42,500 (withheld by employer). CWT from business (2307): ₱40,000.

**Compensation Tax:**
- Taxable compensation: ₱600,000
- Graduated tax: ₱22,500 + 20% × (₱600,000 − ₱400,000) = ₱22,500 + ₱40,000 = ₱62,500
- Less: CWT from employer: ₱42,500
- Compensation IT balance: ₱62,500 − ₱42,500 = ₱20,000

**Business Tax (8%, no ₱250K deduction for mixed income):**
- 8% Tax: ₱800,000 × 0.08 = ₱64,000
- Less: CWT from business (2307s): ₱40,000
- Business IT balance: ₱64,000 − ₱40,000 = ₱24,000

**Total Annual IT Payable:**
- ₱20,000 (comp) + ₱24,000 (biz) = **₱44,000**
- No separate percentage tax (waived under 8%)

### Example 8E-05: Mid-Year Threshold Breach

**Profile:** Freelancer. Elected 8% in Q1. Q1 gross = ₱1,200,000 (tax = (1.2M − 250K) × 8% = ₱76,000, paid ₱70,000 after CWT). Q2 gross (additional) = ₱1,900,000; cumulative = ₱3,100,000 → BREACH in Q2.

**Detection:** Q2 cumulative ₱3,100,000 > ₱3,000,000. Breach month = month 4 (April, earliest month in Q2 where cumulative exceeded ₱3M — assume April is breach month for this example).

**Breach Actions:**
1. 8% option auto-disqualified for entire year
2. File Form 1905 in May (month following April breach) — register for VAT
3. Retroactive percentage tax: 3% on Jan−Mar receipts = 3% × ₱1,200,000 = ₱36,000

**Annual Income Tax (Graduated):**
- Assume full-year gross (Q1+Q2+Q3+Q4 additional): ₱3,500,000 total; no expenses (OSD path)
- Net taxable OSD: 60% × ₱3,500,000 = ₱2,100,000
- Graduated tax: ₱402,500 + 30% × (₱2,100,000 − ₱2,000,000) = ₱402,500 + ₱30,000 = ₱432,500
- Less: CWT credits (full year): ₱175,000
- Less: Prior 8% quarterly payment: ₱70,000
- Annual IT payable: ₱432,500 − ₱175,000 − ₱70,000 = **₱187,500**

## Part 12 — Interaction with Percentage Tax (Section 116)

**Under 8% option:**
- Income Tax rate = 8% of gross (less ₱250K if purely self-employed)
- Percentage Tax (Section 116, 3%) = WAIVED / SUBSUMED
- The 8% rate is described in the statute as "in lieu of" BOTH the graduated income tax AND the percentage tax
- No BIR Form 2551Q required for Q2 and Q3 of the elected year
- For Q1: File NIL 2551Q with notation (existing taxpayer electing) OR simply skip Q1 2551Q (new registrant)

**Under Graduated Rates (Path A or B):**
- Income Tax = graduated rate applied to net taxable (after itemized or OSD)
- Percentage Tax = 3% of gross receipts (Section 116), filed via Form 2551Q quarterly
- Both apply separately; total burden = IT + PT

**Why 8% almost always wins vs Path B (OSD) for service businesses:**
- Path B total = graduated IT on 60% of gross + 3% PT on gross
- At ₱1M gross: Path B total = ₱62,500 IT + ₱30,000 PT = ₱92,500
- Path C (8%) total = ₱60,000 IT + ₱0 PT = ₱60,000
- Savings = ₱32,500 (35% reduction)
- The elimination of the 3% percentage tax is the primary driver of 8% superiority at low expense ratios

## Part 13 — Sales Returns, Allowances, and Discounts

The 8% tax base uses GROSS RECEIPTS net of the following deductions:

| Deduction | Allowed? | Documentation Required |
|-----------|---------|----------------------|
| Sales returns (goods returned by client, reversed revenue) | YES | Credit memo or refund documentation |
| Sales allowances (price reductions after the fact) | YES | Billing adjustment documentation |
| Trade discounts (discounts given at time of billing, shown on invoice) | YES | Visible on original invoice |
| Cash discounts (discounts for early payment) | YES (if availed and recorded) | Separate documentation if not on invoice |
| Volume discounts | YES | Per contract or discount policy |
| Professional fee reductions agreed post-contract | YES | Written agreement or email |
| VAT portion of invoice | NOT APPLICABLE (8% taxpayer is non-VAT) | — |

**Net Gross Receipts Formula:**
```
net_gross_receipts = gross_billings - sales_returns - sales_allowances - discounts_granted
eight_pct_base = MAX(0, net_gross_receipts + non_op_income - 250_000)  // purely self-employed
eight_pct_base_mixed = net_gross_receipts + non_op_income  // mixed income earner
```

## Part 14 — Validation Invariants

The following invariants MUST hold for any correct 8% computation. If any invariant is violated, the engine has a bug.

| Code | Invariant | Test |
|------|-----------|------|
| 8PCT-V01 | 8% tax due is never negative | `assert eight_pct_tax_due >= 0` |
| 8PCT-V02 | If gross + non-op ≤ ₱250,000 (purely self-employed), tax due = ₱0 | `if pure_se and gross <= 250000: assert tax == 0` |
| 8PCT-V03 | 8% option not available when gross > ₱3,000,000 | `if gross > 3_000_000: assert regime != EIGHT_PCT` |
| 8PCT-V04 | Mixed-income 8% tax base = gross (no ₱250K deduction) | `if mixed_income and regime == EIGHT_PCT: assert tax_base == gross` |
| 8PCT-V05 | No percentage tax when 8% is elected | `if regime == EIGHT_PCT: assert pct_tax == 0` |
| 8PCT-V06 | 8% annual tax due = (annual_gross − 250K) × 0.08 for purely SE | `assert abs(annual_tax - MAX(0, annual_gross - 250000) * 0.08) < 1.0` (within ₱1 for rounding) |
| 8PCT-V07 | 8% never applies to VAT-registered taxpayer | `if vat_registered: assert regime != EIGHT_PCT` |
| 8PCT-V08 | Form 1701A used for 8% purely SE; Form 1701 used for 8% mixed income | `if mixed_income and regime == EIGHT_PCT: assert annual_form == "1701"` |
| 8PCT-V09 | 8% quarterly computations are cumulative (each quarter uses YTD gross) | `assert q2_gross >= q1_gross` (monotonically increasing) |
| 8PCT-V10 | Unused ₱250K from compensation cannot reduce business 8% base | `if mixed_income: assert business_tax_base == business_gross` (no partial ₱250K applied) |
