## 1. Primary Statutes

### 1.1 National Internal Revenue Code of 1997 (NIRC, RA 8424)

The foundational tax code of the Philippines. All income tax, percentage tax, and withholding tax rules derive from this code, as amended.

**Relevant sections:**

| Section | Title | Relevance |
|---------|-------|-----------|
| Sec. 24(A) | Income Tax Rates on Individual Citizens and Resident Aliens | Core graduated rate tables; 8% flat rate option |
| Sec. 24(A)(1) | General applicability | Who is subject to individual income tax |
| Sec. 24(A)(2)(a) | Graduated rate table | All individual taxpayers not on 8% option |
| Sec. 24(A)(2)(b) | 8% income tax rate option | Self-employed, professionals, gross ≤ ₱3M |
| Sec. 24(A)(2)(c) | Mixed-income earner rules | Compensation + business income treatment |
| Sec. 34(A) | Ordinary and Necessary Business Expenses | Itemized deduction category |
| Sec. 34(B) | Interest Expense | Itemized deduction: interest on business loans |
| Sec. 34(C) | Taxes | Itemized deduction: taxes paid in conduct of business |
| Sec. 34(D) | Losses | Itemized deduction: business losses, NOLCO |
| Sec. 34(E) | Bad Debts | Itemized deduction: uncollectible accounts |
| Sec. 34(F) | Depreciation | Itemized deduction: capital asset wear and tear |
| Sec. 34(G) | Depletion | Oil/gas/mines only — not relevant for most freelancers |
| Sec. 34(H) | Charitable Contributions | Itemized deduction: donations to qualified entities |
| Sec. 34(I) | Research and Development | Itemized deduction: R&D expenses |
| Sec. 34(J) | Pension Trusts | Itemized deduction: employee pension contributions |
| Sec. 34(K) | Additional Deductibility Requirements | Substantiation, EOPT Act repeal of withholding prerequisite |
| Sec. 34(L) | Optional Standard Deduction (OSD) | 40% of gross receipts/sales, in lieu of Sec. 34(A)-(K) |
| Sec. 36 | Items Not Deductible | Personal expenses, capital improvements, illegal payments |
| Sec. 74 | Declaration of Income for Self-Employed | Quarterly declaration obligation |
| Sec. 74(A) | Who must declare | Self-employed individuals receiving self-employment income |
| Sec. 74(B) | Return and payment schedule | Q1: May 15, Q2: Aug 15, Q3: Nov 15, Annual: Apr 15 |
| Sec. 74(C) | Cumulative method for quarterly computation | Each quarter uses cumulative income year-to-date |
| Sec. 75 | Payment of quarterly income tax | Due with quarterly return |
| Sec. 116 | Percentage Tax on Non-VAT Persons | 3% of gross quarterly receipts (for non-8% filers) |

### 1.2 RA 10963 — Tax Reform for Acceleration and Inclusion (TRAIN Law)
- **Enacted:** December 19, 2017
- **Effective:** January 1, 2018
- **Key changes:**
  - Introduced revised graduated income tax rate table (Schedule 1: 2018-2022; Schedule 2: 2023+)
  - Introduced 8% income tax rate option (new Section 24(A)(2)(b))
  - Raised VAT threshold from ₱1.9M to ₱3,000,000
  - Repealed personal and additional exemptions (replaced by ₱250,000 exempt bracket)
  - Updated OSD rules

### 1.3 RA 11534 — Corporate Recovery and Tax Incentives for Enterprises (CREATE Act)
- **Enacted:** March 26, 2021
- **Effective:** Various dates
- **Key change affecting self-employed:**
  - Temporarily reduced Section 116 percentage tax rate from 3% to 1%
  - Effective period: July 1, 2020 to June 30, 2023
  - Rate reverted to 3% as of July 1, 2023

### 1.4 RA 11976 — Ease of Paying Taxes (EOPT Act)
- **Enacted:** January 5, 2024
- **Key changes affecting self-employed:**
  - Repealed the requirement to withhold taxes as a prerequisite for claiming expense deductions
  - Simplified filing procedures and venue rules
  - Authorized BIR to classify taxpayers by size (Micro, Small, Medium, Large) with corresponding compliance simplifications
  - Authorized electronic and online filing/payment without regard to RDO limitations

## 2. Revenue Regulations

### 2.1 RR No. 8-2018 (February 20, 2018)
- **Title:** Implementing the Income Tax Provisions of the TRAIN Law
- **Key provisions:**
  - Implements both graduated rate tables (Schedules 1 and 2)
  - Details the 8% income tax rate option: who can avail, election procedure, filing obligations
  - Clarifies mixed-income earner treatment (compensation always at graduated rates; business income can elect 8%)
  - Specifies that ₱250,000 threshold under 8% option does NOT apply to mixed-income earners
  - Clarifies OSD election irrevocability
  - Specifies BIR Form 1701A for purely self-employed; BIR Form 1701 for mixed-income earners
- **Source:** BIR Revenue Regulations, issuance date February 20, 2018

### 2.2 RR No. 16-2008 (October 27, 2008)
- **Title:** Implementing the OSD provisions of Section 34(L) NIRC, as amended by RA 9504
- **Key provisions:**
  - OSD for individuals = 40% of GROSS SALES OR GROSS RECEIPTS (not gross income)
  - Corporations use 40% of GROSS INCOME (different base)
  - Cost of sales/cost of services NOT deducted before computing OSD for individuals
  - Irrevocability of election

### 2.3 RR No. 10-2002
- **Title:** Entertainment, Amusement, and Recreation (EAR) expense deduction limits
- **Key provision:**
  - EAR deduction limited to 0.5% of net sales for goods sellers
  - EAR deduction limited to 1% of net revenue for service providers

### 2.4 RR No. 2-98 (February 17, 1998)
- **Title:** Consolidated implementing regulations for income tax
- **Key provision:**
  - General rules for itemized deductions, depreciation methods, bad debt treatment

### 2.5 RR No. 4-2019
- **Title:** Rules on submission of Financial Statements (FS)
- **Key provision:**
  - Audited FS required for individuals/corporations with annual gross sales/receipts > ₱3M
  - But if OSD is elected, FS need NOT be attached to the return — must be available upon BIR demand

## 5. BIR Forms — Overview

| Form | Purpose | Filer |
|------|---------|-------|
| BIR Form 1701 | Annual Income Tax Return | Mixed-income earners; self-employed with Itemized deductions and AFS |
| BIR Form 1701A | Annual ITR (Simplified) | Purely self-employed under graduated rates with OSD; and purely self-employed under 8% option |
| BIR Form 1701Q | Quarterly Income Tax Return | All self-employed, professionals, mixed-income earners |
| BIR Form 2551Q | Quarterly Percentage Tax Return | Non-VAT self-employed under graduated rates (not on 8% option) |
| BIR Form 2307 | Certificate of Creditable Tax Withheld at Source | Issued by clients to self-employed payees; used to claim CWT credit |
| BIR Form 1700 | Annual ITR for Compensation Income | Purely employed individuals (usually employer-substituted) |
| BIR Form 1901 | Registration of Self-Employed | New registrant declaration — includes 8% option election for new businesses |
| BIR Form 1905 | Application for Registration Information Update | Updates BIR records when taxpayer elects/cancels 8% option, changes RDO, etc. |

## 6. VAT Threshold and Its Significance

**Current VAT registration threshold (as of 2024-2026): ₱3,000,000 annual gross sales/receipts**

- Set by RA 10963 (TRAIN Law), effective January 1, 2018
- Taxpayers with gross receipts BELOW ₱3M: non-VAT; subject to Section 116 percentage tax (3%) OR 8% option
- Taxpayers with gross receipts AT OR ABOVE ₱3M: MUST register for VAT; subject to 12% VAT; NOT eligible for 8% income tax option
- Source: NIRC Section 109(CC), Section 116, as amended by TRAIN Law

## 7. Withholding Tax on Professional Fees (Creditable Withholding Tax)

**BIR clients who are withholding agents (corporations, partnerships, government agencies, large taxpayers) are required to withhold income tax from payments to self-employed individuals:**

| Type of Income | EWT Rate | Reference |
|----------------|----------|-----------|
| Professional fees to individuals | 5% (if gross receipts ≤ ₱3M registered) OR 10% (if gross receipts > ₱3M or registered VAT) | RR No. 2-98 as amended |
| Income payments to self-employed | Same as above | RR 2-98 |

Note: These are CREDITABLE withholding taxes — they are not final taxes. The withheld amount is a credit against the taxpayer's income tax due, not a final settlement.

**The withheld amounts are evidenced by BIR Form 2307**, which the client issues to the self-employed individual. This becomes the input for computing tax credits.

## 9. Items Outside This Tool's Scope (for Legal Disclosure)

The tool does NOT cover:
- Corporations and partnerships (only individual self-employed)
- Estate and trust income
- Passive income subject to final withholding tax (dividends, royalties, interest)
- Capital gains tax (shares of stock, real property)
- Foreign-sourced income for non-resident citizens
- Estate tax, donor's tax
- VAT computation (only determines whether taxpayer is VAT-liable)
- Specific industry taxes (banking, insurance, transport, amusement)
- BMBE (Barangay Micro Business Enterprise) exemptions
- Special economic zone incentives (PEZA, BOI)
- Minimum corporate income tax
- Fringe benefits tax

See [legal/limitations.md](../legal/limitations.md) for the full disclaimer scope.
