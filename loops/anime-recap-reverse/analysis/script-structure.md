# Script Structure Analysis

## Summary

The 75-minute recap follows an **8-part macro structure**: Hook (0.8%) → Act 1: Premise (15.8%) → Act 2: Escalation (22.9%) → Act 3: Transformation (22.9%) → Act 4: Conspiracy (16.2%) → Act 5: Climax (17.0%) → Resolution (3.6%) → Outro (0.6%). The structure mirrors the source anime's narrative arc, not an arbitrary episode-by-episode retelling. The middle three acts (Escalation, Transformation, Conspiracy) absorb ~62% of runtime, while the hook+outro bookends are under 2% combined. Episode transitions use explicit markers ("In the Nth episode") for ~83% of episodes, with the remainder arc-merged into continuous narration.

## Patterns

### 1. Story-Arc Structure, Not Episode-Sequential

The recap is NOT "episode 1, then episode 2, then episode 3." Instead, it follows the **narrative arc** of the source material. Episodes are grouped into acts based on plot function:
- **Premise** (world-building) → **Escalation** (rising stakes) → **Transformation** (character change) → **Conspiracy** (expanded scope) → **Climax** (payoff) → **Resolution** (denouement)
- This means the forward engine's script generator must understand the anime's plot structure, not just summarize episodes in order

### 2. Inverted Triangle Duration Allocation

Early episodes get disproportionate time, creating an inverted triangle:
- Eps 1-2 (Act 1): 5.5 min/episode average
- Eps 3-7 (Act 2): 3.4 min/episode average
- Eps 8-12 (Act 3): 3.5 min/episode average
- Eps 13-17 (Act 4): 2.4 min/episode average (arc-merged)
- Eps 18-23 (Act 5): 2.1 min/episode average
- Ep 24 (Resolution): 2.7 min (finale bump)

### 3. The 80/20 Commentary Rule

~96% of narration is pure recap (third-person story retelling). Only ~4% is narrator commentary (first/second person). But these commentary moments are disproportionately impactful:
- They appear at **transitions** (welcoming viewers, bridging episodes)
- They appear at **emotional peaks** (reactions to plot twists)
- They appear at **humor beats** (pop culture references, slang)
- They NEVER interrupt mid-scene — they occur at natural break points

### 4. Arc Merging for Compression

When episode boundaries would break narrative flow, the narrator drops per-episode framing and tells a continuous story. This happens in Act 4 (eps 13-17) where 5 episodes are narrated as one continuous arc. Criteria for arc merging:
- Episodes share the same primary plotline (conspiracy arc)
- No major character introductions at episode boundaries
- The per-episode framing would create artificial stopping points

### 5. Consistent Word Density Despite Variable Pacing

Despite covering episodes at different depths, the word density per 5-minute block stays remarkably consistent (142-160 WPM). This means the narrator maintains the SAME speaking pace throughout — what changes is the compression of plot content, not the delivery speed.

### 6. Bookend Brevity

Hook (36s) + Outro (28s) = 64 seconds total, or 1.4% of the video. The JarAnime style wastes almost zero time on non-content framing. The hook is tight and purposeful (4 beats in 36 seconds), and the outro is minimal (reflection + plug + sign-off in 28 seconds).

### 7. Resolution Gets Special Treatment

The season finale episode (24) gets its own distinct section with a slightly different tone:
- **No explicit episode marker** — instead: "In the season finale, things start to change for the better"
- **Reflective tone** — summarizes consequences rather than blow-by-blow
- **Time skip handling** — "A year passes" compressed into a single sentence
- **Emotional closure** — Migi's farewell, Shinichi's growth
- **Zero anime dialogue moments** — pure narration, letting the narrator control the emotional landing

## Spec Implications

### Script Generation Pipeline

The forward engine must generate scripts following this structure:

```
1. HOOK (target: 30-45 seconds, ~55-80 words)
   - Beat 1: Rhetorical question connecting anime premise to universal experience
   - Beat 2: 2 anime dialogue clips as teaser (emotional/action moments)
   - Beat 3: Narrator reaction (short, enthusiastic)
   - Beat 4: Transition phrase ("Let's get into it")

2. BODY (target: 95% of runtime)
   - Group episodes into 4-6 narrative acts based on plot structure
   - Act 1 (Premise): 15-16% — Extended coverage of first 1-2 episodes
   - Acts 2-3 (Rising Action): 45-46% — Per-episode coverage with decreasing time
   - Acts 4-5 (Climax Build): 33-34% — Arc-merged where appropriate, compressed
   - Use explicit episode markers for 80%+ of episodes
   - Arc-merge episodes that share continuous plotlines
   - Insert narrator commentary at transitions, emotional peaks, and humor beats
   - Target 4% commentary ratio

3. RESOLUTION (target: 3-4% of runtime)
   - Cover season finale with reflective tone
   - Summarize consequences rather than blow-by-blow
   - Use "In the season finale..." marker

4. OUTRO (target: 25-35 seconds, ~50-90 words)
   - Brief series reflection (1-2 sentences of personal opinion)
   - Channel plug
   - Sign-off catchphrase
```

### Episode Duration Allocation Formula

For an N-episode season with total body duration T:

```python
def episode_duration(ep_num, total_eps, total_body_duration):
    # Compression curve: early episodes get more time
    if ep_num <= 2:
        weight = 1.7  # Premise episodes get 1.7x average
    elif ep_num == total_eps:
        weight = 1.1  # Finale gets a bump
    elif ep_num <= total_eps * 0.3:
        weight = 1.2  # Early body
    elif ep_num <= total_eps * 0.7:
        weight = 0.9  # Mid body (may arc-merge)
    else:
        weight = 0.7  # Late body (compressed)

    avg_duration = total_body_duration / total_eps
    return avg_duration * weight
```

### Act Detection Algorithm

The script generator needs to identify narrative acts from episode summaries. Heuristic:
1. **Act boundaries** occur when there's a major shift in: protagonist's status, antagonist type, stakes level, or setting
2. **Each act should contain 3-7 episodes** (too few = not enough content, too many = no structural variety)
3. **First act** always covers eps 1-2 (premise establishment)
4. **Final act** always starts when the main conflict enters resolution phase
5. **Arc merging** applies to acts where 3+ consecutive episodes share a plotline without major character introductions

### Commentary Insertion Points

Insert narrator commentary (1st/2nd person) at these structural positions:
- After the hook transition
- At episode boundaries (1-2 sentences of reaction)
- After emotional peaks (death, transformation, betrayal)
- After absurd/funny moments (pop culture comparisons)
- Never mid-scene — always at natural narrative breaks

### Word Budget Calculator

```python
def word_budget(target_duration_minutes, wpm=144):
    total_words = target_duration_minutes * wpm
    hook_words = 65        # ~30-40s
    outro_words = 70       # ~25-35s
    body_words = total_words - hook_words - outro_words
    resolution_words = int(body_words * 0.035)  # 3.5% of body
    main_body_words = body_words - resolution_words
    return {
        "total": total_words,
        "hook": hook_words,
        "body": main_body_words,
        "resolution": resolution_words,
```
