# BIR-Approved Retirement Plans — Tax-Qualified Plan (TQP) Requirements

**Source type**: Synthesized from RR No. 15-2025 (BIR official), Ocampo & Suralvo Law Offices, Conventus Law, Grant Thornton Philippines, PwC Philippines
**Cached**: 2026-03-06
**Primary references**: RA 4917 (1967); RR No. 1-68 (1968); RR No. 1-83; RR No. 15-2025 (April 29, 2025); NIRC Sec. 32(B)(6)(a); RMC No. 13-2024

---

## 1. Overview: What Is a Tax-Qualified Plan (TQP)?

A Tax-Qualified Plan (TQP) is a private retirement benefit plan registered with the Bureau of Internal Revenue (BIR) and declared as "reasonable" within the contemplation of the NIRC. Only TQPs are entitled to the full tax incentives under RA 4917 and NIRC Sec. 32(B)(6)(a).

**Governing regulations**:
- RA 4917 (1967) — original authorizing law for private retirement plan tax exemptions
- RR No. 1-68 (March 25, 1968) — original implementing rules, in effect for 57 years
- RR No. 15-2025 (April 29, 2025) — revised and updated rules, effective May 15, 2025, supersedes RR 1-68

---

## 2. Plan-Level Qualification Requirements

For a private retirement benefit plan to be certified as a TQP, the plan itself must satisfy ALL of the following:

### 2.1 Written and Permanent Program
- Must be a **definite written program** (formal plan document)
- Must be **permanent and continuing** (not a temporary or one-time arrangement)

### 2.2 Coverage Requirements
- Plan must cover **at least 70% of all officials and employees**
- Of those eligible to participate, **at least 80%** must actually be covered (from RR 1-68, continued under RR 15-2025)
- **Permissible exclusions** from coverage:
  - Employees who have not completed the plan's minimum service requirement
  - Employees working **20 hours per week or less** (part-time)
  - Seasonal workers employed **5 months per year or less**
- Classification-based coverage (e.g., covering only certain job grades) is permitted **only if non-discriminatory**

### 2.3 Non-Discrimination Rule
- **No discrimination** in contributions or benefits in favor of:
  - Officers
  - Shareholders
  - Supervisors
  - Highly compensated employees
- Classification plans allowed only if coverage does not disproportionately favor these groups
- This is the BIR's primary "reasonableness" test

### 2.4 Employer Contributions
- Employer must make contributions to the plan (employee contributions alone are insufficient)
- Employer and employee may both contribute
- Contribution amounts must be actuarially determined for defined-benefit plans

### 2.5 Exclusivity / Non-Diversion Rule
- No part of the corpus or income of the retirement fund may be **used for or diverted to any purpose** other than the exclusive benefit of employees
- The fund **cannot be invested in the employer's own business ventures** (Section 5(f) of RR 1-68, continued in RR 15-2025)

### 2.6 Non-Forfeitable Rights
- Rights of each official or employee to benefits accrued are **non-forfeitable**
- Forfeitures upon severance of employment, death, or other causes **cannot increase benefits** for other employees prior to plan termination
- Plan must expressly provide for forfeitures in case of severance, death, or other causes (but only to the extent permitted)

## 3. Individual-Level Qualification Conditions (Track B)

Even if the plan is TQP-certified, the **individual employee** must meet ALL four conditions for their retirement benefit to be tax-exempt:

| # | Condition | Requirement |
|---|-----------|-------------|
| 1 | Age | **At least 50 years old** at time of retirement |
| 2 | Service | **At least 10 years** of continuous service with the **same employer** |
| 3 | Plan status | Employer holds a BIR-issued **Certificate of Qualification** |
| 4 | Frequency | **First time only** — no prior exempt retirement benefit from any employer (once-in-a-lifetime) |

**Note**: This is distinct from Track A (RA 7641 statutory, age 60-65, 5+ years), which does not require a BIR-approved plan.

## 4. Trust vs. Non-Trusteed Plans (Key Change in RR 15-2025)

### Under RR 1-68 (Old Rule, pre-May 2025)
- Retirement fund **must be administered by a trust**
- Only trusteed plans were fully entitled to tax benefits
- Insured/deposit administration plans had more limited benefits

### Under RR 15-2025 (New Rule, effective May 15, 2025)
- **Trust requirement removed** — non-trusteed plans are now officially recognized
- Two plan types now have distinct tax treatment:

| Plan Type | Trust Income Tax | Annual Filing |
|-----------|-----------------|---------------|
| **Trusteed** | Income earned by employees' trust is **exempt** from income and withholding tax (except stock transaction tax on listed shares) | Trustee files annual information return by **April 15** each year |
| **Non-trusteed / Insured** | Insurer/custodian files **regular income tax returns** for investment earnings (earnings taxable) | No separate annual return requirement |

## 5. Certificate of Qualification

### How to Apply
- Employer applies with the **BIR Legal and Legislative Division, National Office** (not regional offices)
- Must be filed within **30 days** from the date of effectivity of the retirement benefit plan
  - Late filing triggers a penalty
- Certificate is **valid until revoked** by the BIR
- **Pending approval**: tax incentives may be availed of during the pendency, but the employer bears **sole and direct liability** for any deficiency income taxes if the application is ultimately denied

### Documentary Requirements (Trusteed Plans)
1. **Certified True Copy of the latest Actuarial Valuation Report** (must not be more than 3 years old as of application date)
2. **Duly accomplished BIR Form 17.60** (Retirement Benefit Plan Information Sheet), signed by an authorized officer
3. **Certified True Copy of the Retirement Plan Rules and Regulations**, containing all required provisions

### Documentary Requirements (Non-Trusteed / Insured Plans)
1. **Duly accomplished BIR Form 17.60**
2. **Copy of the written program constituting the Plan**
3. (No actuarial report required)

### Fee Schedule (RR 15-2025)

| Number of Employees | Initial Certificate | Amendatory Certificate |
|---------------------|--------------------|-----------------------|
| 5 or fewer | **Exempt (no fee)** | **Exempt (no fee)** |
| 6–50 | PHP 2,000 | PHP 2,000 |
| 51–100 | PHP 3,000 | PHP 3,500 |
| More than 100 | PHP 5,000 | PHP 5,000 |

### Plan Amendments
- Any amendment to an approved retirement plan must be submitted to BIR for certification that the amendment **does not affect the plan's qualification** (per RR 1-83, continued in RR 15-2025)
- Failure to certify amendments can result in disqualification

## 6. Tax Incentives for Approved Plans

When a plan holds a Certificate of Qualification:

### For Employees
1. **Retirement benefits tax-exempt** — no income tax, no withholding tax (subject to individual conditions: age 50+, 10+ years, first time)
2. **Once-in-a-lifetime** — applies across all employers, not just current one (RMC 13-2024)

### For the Employer
3. **Employer contributions deductible** from taxable income
4. **Excess contributions** (above Normal Cost) deductible, but **amortized over 10 consecutive years** beginning with the year of payment

### For the Trust (Trusteed Plans Only)
5. **Trust investment income exempt** from income tax and withholding tax
6. Exception: stock transaction tax on sales of listed shares still applies

## 7. Multi-Employer Plan Provisions (Service Tacking)

- Service years at a **previous employer may be counted** toward the 10-year requirement ONLY when:
  1. Transfer was due to a **valid corporate merger** (not voluntary resignation)
  2. Employee received **no separation pay** from the previous employer
  3. Both/all employers participate in the **same multi-employer plan**
- Without meeting all three conditions, only service with the **current employer** counts
- This rule was clarified in **RMC No. 13-2024** (January 22, 2024)

---

## 8. Annual Compliance Requirements

| Plan Type | Filing | Deadline | Form |
|-----------|--------|----------|------|
| Trusteed | Annual information return | April 15 of each year | BIR-prescribed form |
| Trusteed | Annual report with Certificate of Qualification copy | April 15 | Attach to return |
| Non-trusteed | Regular income tax return for investment income | Standard income tax deadline | Regular BIR forms |

---

## 9. Investment Restrictions

For both trusteed and non-trusteed plans:
- Retirement fund **may not be invested in the employer's own business ventures**
- This is explicitly required to maintain separation of the employees' trust fund from the employer's operations
- Violation is grounds for disqualification

---

## 10. Consequences of Non-Compliance / Disqualification

| Scenario | Consequence |
|----------|-------------|
| No application within 30 days of plan effectivity | Penalty imposed on employer |
| Application denied | Tax incentives previously availed become taxable; employer pays deficiency taxes + penalties |
| Plan disqualified | All retirement benefits paid under the plan become taxable compensation |
| Fund diverted to employer's business | Grounds for disqualification |
| Unamended/uncertified plan amendment | Potential disqualification |
| Trustee fails to file annual return | Administrative penalties |

## 11. Distinction: Track A vs. Track B

| Feature | Track A (RA 7641 Statutory) | Track B (RA 4917 BIR-Approved) |
|---------|---------------------------|-------------------------------|
| Legal basis | Labor Code Art. 302 (RA 7641) | NIRC Sec. 32(B)(6)(a) + RA 4917 |
| Minimum age | **60** (optional) / **65** (compulsory) | **50** |
| Minimum service | **5 years** | **10 years** |
| BIR plan required | **No** | **Yes** — Certificate of Qualification |
| Tax exemption | Yes — automatic | Yes — conditional on plan status + individual conditions |
| Re-employment caveat | Exempt revoked if re-employed within 12 months | Same |
| Once-in-a-lifetime | Yes | Yes |

**Key implication for the calculator**: Most employees retiring at age 60-65 under RA 7641 statutory pay (Track A) do **not** require their employer to have a BIR-approved plan for tax exemption. The BIR plan matters primarily for:
- Early retirees (age 50-59) wanting tax exemption
- Employees with company plans paying more than the RA 7641 minimum
- The gap analysis feature: comparing company plan amount vs. statutory minimum

## 12. Product Design Implications

1. **Input field needed**: `employerHasBirApprovedPlan: boolean` — determines Track B eligibility for early retirees
2. **Input field needed**: `employeeHasUsedRetirementExemption: boolean` — once-in-a-lifetime check
3. **Tax determination logic**:
   - Age 60-65 + service ≥ 5 years → Track A exempt (no plan needed)
   - Age 50-59 + service ≥ 10 years + employer has TQP + first time → Track B exempt
   - Age 50-59 + service ≥ 10 years + employer has no TQP → taxable
   - Age < 50 → taxable regardless
4. **Warning for HR users**: If company has no BIR-approved plan, early retirees (age 50-59) cannot receive tax-exempt retirement benefits under Track B. The calculator should surface this as an actionable flag.
5. **NLRC worksheet**: Must note tax treatment basis (Track A or Track B or taxable) with statutory citation.
