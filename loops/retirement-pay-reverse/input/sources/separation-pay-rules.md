# Separation Pay Rules — Philippine Labor Code Art. 298-299

## Source
Compiled from: laborlaw.ph, library.laborlaw.ph, respicio.ph, ndvlaw.com
Date cached: 2026-03-06

---

## Article 298 [formerly Art. 283] — Closure of Establishment and Reduction of Personnel

**Full Text (key excerpt):**
> The employer may also terminate the employment of any employee due to the installation of labor-saving devices, redundancy, retrenchment to prevent losses or the closing or cessation of operation of the establishment or undertaking unless the closing is for the purpose of circumventing the provisions of this Title, by serving a written notice on the workers and the Ministry of Labor and Employment at least one (1) month before the intended date thereof.
>
> In case of termination due to the installation of labor-saving devices or redundancy, the worker affected thereby shall be entitled to a separation pay equivalent to at least his one (1) month pay or to at least one (1) month pay for every year of service, whichever is higher.
>
> In case of retrenchment to prevent losses and in cases of closures or cessation of operations of establishment or undertaking not due to serious business losses or financial reverses, the separation pay shall be equivalent to one (1) month pay or at least one-half month pay for every year of service, whichever is higher.

## Article 299 [formerly Art. 284] — Disease as Ground for Termination

**Full Text (key excerpt):**
> An employer may terminate the services of an employee who has been found to be suffering from any disease and whose continued employment is prohibited by law or is prejudicial to his health as well as to the health of his co-employees: Provided, That he is paid separation pay equivalent to at least one (1) month salary or to one-half (1/2) month salary for every year of service, whichever is greater, **a fraction of at least six (6) months being considered as one (1) whole year.**

## Separation Pay Rates Summary

| Authorized Cause | Rate |
|---|---|
| Installation of labor-saving devices | 1 month pay OR 1 month/year of service, whichever is higher |
| Redundancy | 1 month pay OR 1 month/year of service, whichever is higher |
| Retrenchment to prevent losses | 1 month pay OR 1/2 month/year of service, whichever is higher |
| Closure not due to serious losses | 1 month pay OR 1/2 month/year of service, whichever is higher |
| Closure due to serious business losses | NOT required |
| Disease/illness (Art. 299) | 1 month salary OR 1/2 month/year of service, whichever is greater |

**Note:** Separation pay uses "1/2 month salary" = 15 days basic pay only. This is NOT the same as RA 7641's "1/2 month salary" which = 22.5 days (15 + 5 SIL + 1/12 of 13th month).

## Fraction of Year Rule (Art. 299 explicit, Art. 298 by practice)

- Fraction of at least 6 months = 1 whole year
- Fraction of less than 6 months = dropped

Same 6-month rounding rule as RA 7641.

---

## Notice Requirements

- Written notice to both employee AND DOLE at least 30 days before intended date of separation
- Notice must identify the authorized cause and effective date
- Failure to comply does not void termination but entitles employee to nominal damages (Agabon doctrine)

---

## Tax Treatment of Separation Pay

- Separation pay due to authorized causes beyond employee's control (redundancy, retrenchment, closure, disease) is **tax-exempt** under NIRC Sec. 32(B)(6)(b)
- "Causes beyond the control of the employee" = authorized causes under Art. 298-299
- This tax exemption is SEPARATE from the retirement pay tax exemption under Sec. 32(B)(6)(a)

## Retirement Pay vs. Separation Pay — Interaction

### Distinct Benefits, Different Triggers

- **Retirement pay (RA 7641/Art. 302):** Triggered by retirement at qualifying age (60-65) + 5 years service. Formula: 22.5 days/year.
- **Separation pay (Art. 298-299):** Triggered by authorized cause termination (redundancy, retrenchment, closure, disease). Formula: 15 or 30 days/year.

### Dual Entitlement — Supreme Court Jurisprudence

**Aquino v. NLRC, G.R. No. 87653 (February 11, 1992):**
- Court held separation pay and retirement benefits are NOT alternative but can be cumulative
- "Any doubt concerning the rights of labor should be resolved in its favor"
- If the employer did not include a provision crediting separation pay against retirement benefits, both must be paid
- Employee may receive both if eligibility criteria for each are independently met

**Goodyear Philippines, Inc. v. Angus, G.R. No. 185449 (November 12, 2014):**
- Court awarded both separation pay AND retirement pay to qualified employees in a retrenchment scenario
- Labor Code does not prohibit dual recovery

### Practical Scenario: Employee Retrenched at Age 62 with 15 Years Service

This employee qualifies for:
1. **Separation pay (Art. 298 retrenchment):** 1/2 month x 15 years = 7.5 months pay (using 15-day half month = 112.5 days basic pay)
2. **Retirement pay (RA 7641):** 22.5 days x 15 years = 337.5 days pay

In this scenario:
- If company policy/CBA explicitly credits separation pay against retirement pay: pay only the higher (retirement pay wins at 337.5 days vs. 112.5 days)
- If no such crediting provision: employee may claim BOTH under *Aquino v. NLRC*

## Who Is Eligible for Separation Pay

- Regular, probationary, casual, project, seasonal, fixed-term employees
- All are entitled to separation pay if terminated for authorized causes
- Employees dismissed for just causes (Art. 297) are NOT entitled to separation pay
- Employees who voluntarily resign are NOT entitled to separation pay
