# NIRC Section 32(B)(6)(a) + Revenue Regulations — Retirement Pay Tax Exemption

**Source type**: Synthesized from BIR official documents, PwC Philippines, DivinaLaw, Grant Thornton, Respicio & Co.
**Cached**: 2026-03-06
**Primary reference**: NIRC Section 32(B)(6)(a); RA 4917 (1967); RR 1-68 (1968); RR 15-2025 (2025)

---

## 1. NIRC Section 32(B) — Exclusions from Gross Income

Section 32(B) of the National Internal Revenue Code (NIRC), as amended, lists items excluded from gross income (i.e., not subject to income tax). Subsection (6)(a) covers retirement benefits.

### Section 32(B)(6) — Retirement Benefits, Pensions, Gratuities, etc.

**(a) Retirement benefits received under RA 7638, now under the mandatory provisions of the Labor Code, and those received by officials and employees of private firms, whether individual or corporate, in accordance with a reasonable private benefit plan maintained by the employer:**

*Provided,* That the retiring official or employee has been in the service of the same employer for at least **ten (10) years** and is **not less than fifty (50) years of age** at the time of retirement: *Provided, further,* That the benefits granted under this subparagraph shall be **availed of by an official or employee only once.**

For purposes of this subsection, the term "reasonable private benefit plan" means a pension, gratuity, stock bonus or profit-sharing plan maintained by an employer for the benefit of some or all of his officials or employees, wherein contributions are made by such employer for the officials or employees, or both, for the purpose of distributing to such officials and employees the earnings and principal of the fund thus accumulated, and wherein it is provided in said plan that at no time shall any part of the corpus or income of the fund be used for, or be diverted to, any purpose other than for the exclusive benefit of the said officials and employees.

## 2. Two Tracks for Tax Exemption

### Track A: RA 7641 Statutory Retirement (Mandatory Labor Code)

Retirement benefits received under the **mandatory provisions of the Labor Code** (as amended by RA 7641) are tax-exempt when:

| Condition | Requirement |
|-----------|-------------|
| Age | At least **60 years old** but **not more than 65** at time of retirement |
| Service | At least **5 years** of service with the employer |
| Benefit limitation | Availed of **only once** |
| Plan requirement | **None** — no BIR-registered plan required |

**Key point**: Statutory retirement pay under RA 7641 at age 60-65 with 5+ years service is tax-exempt WITHOUT needing a BIR-approved plan. The exemption flows directly from the "mandatory provisions of the Labor Code" language.

**Re-employment caveat**: If the retiree is re-employed by the same employer or any related party within the succeeding **12-month period** from the date of retirement, the exemption is disallowed and income tax becomes due.

### Track B: RA 4917 Private Benefit Plan (BIR-Approved)

Retirement benefits under a **reasonable private benefit plan** approved by the BIR are tax-exempt when ALL four conditions are met:

| # | Condition | Requirement |
|---|-----------|-------------|
| 1 | Age | At least **50 years old** at time of retirement |
| 2 | Service | At least **10 years** of continuous service with the **same employer** |
| 3 | Plan registration | BIR-issued **Certificate of Qualification** as a Reasonable Employee's Retirement Benefit Plan |
| 4 | Frequency | Availed of **only once** — no prior exempt retirement benefit from any employer |

**Note on "same employer"**: The 10-year service requirement is counted in one company only. For multi-employer plans, RMC 13-2024 (January 2024) clarified that service tacking from a previous employer is allowed only for mergers where no separation pay was received and the previous employer participates in the same plan.

## 3. Revenue Regulations No. 1-68 (Original Framework, 1968)

Issued March 25, 1968 as implementing rules for RA 4917. Established:

### Coverage Requirement
- Written, permanent program
- Must cover at least **70%** of the company's employees
- Of those eligible, at least **80%** must actually be covered
- May exclude: employees who haven't completed minimum service, part-time employees, seasonal employees

### Exclusive Benefit Rule
No part of the corpus or income of the retirement fund may be used for or diverted to any purpose other than the **exclusive benefit of employees**.

### Trust Administration
- Retirement fund must be **administered by a trust**
- Fund must be invested in proper and sound investments
- May **not** be invested in the employer's business ventures (Section 5(f) of RR 1-68)
- Only trusteed plans are fully entitled to tax benefits (BIR has ruled that insured/deposit administration plans have more limited benefits)

### Registration
- Employer must apply for **Certificate of Qualification** within **30 days** from effectivity of the retirement plan
- Certificate is **valid until revoked**
- RR 1-83 (amendment) added requirement for annual information return filed by trustees/fiduciaries

### Actuarial Valuation
- Required for fixed-benefit plans at registration
- Must be certified by a **Fellow of the Actuarial Society of the Philippines**
- Re-certification required upon plan amendment

## 4. Revenue Regulations No. 15-2025 (Updated Framework, April 29, 2025)

The BIR issued RR 15-2025 on April 29, 2025, superseding/updating RR 1-68. Key changes:

- Provides updated clarity on tax incentives under RA 4917
- Confirms that income earned by trust fund investments of a tax-qualified plan is exempt from income tax (Section 60(B) of NIRC)
- Specifies investment limitations for retirement trust funds
- Updates registration procedures and annual reporting requirements

## 5. The "No BIR-Registered Plan" Situation

**Critical rule**: If an employer has **no BIR-registered retirement plan**, retirement benefits paid to retiring employees are **subject to income tax**, even if:
- The employee meets the age and service conditions
- The employer is paying the RA 7641 statutory minimum

**Exception**: Benefits received under the **mandatory Labor Code provisions** (Track A, above) — i.e., at age 60-65 with 5+ years service — remain tax-exempt even without a registered plan, because the exemption flows from the Labor Code itself, not from a plan.

**Summary table**:

| Scenario | Age | Service | BIR Plan | Tax-Exempt? |
|----------|-----|---------|----------|-------------|
| RA 7641 statutory (optional) | 60-64 | ≥5 yrs | Not required | **YES** |
| RA 7641 statutory (compulsory) | 65 | ≥5 yrs | Not required | **YES** |
| Early retirement, BIR plan | 50-59 | ≥10 yrs | Required | **YES** |
| Early retirement, no BIR plan | 50-59 | ≥10 yrs | None | **NO** |
| Early retirement, BIR plan | 50-59 | <10 yrs | Required | **NO** |
| Early retirement, BIR plan | <50 | any | Required | **NO** |
| Re-employed within 12 months | any | any | any | **NO** (retroactively) |
| Second-time retiree | any | any | any | **NO** |

## 6. BIR Revenue Memorandum Circular No. 13-2024

Issued January 2024. Key clarifications:
- Reinforced the "same employer rule" for the 10-year service requirement
- Clarified computation in multi-employer plan contexts (tacking allowed only for mergers, no separation pay received)
- Emphasized that the once-in-a-lifetime rule applies across all employers, not just the current one

---

## 7. Interaction with RR 2-98 (Withholding Tax on Compensation)

Revenue Regulations No. 2-98 governs withholding tax on compensation. Under this regulation:
- RA 7641 retirement pay (Track A) is listed among exclusions from compensation subject to withholding
- Employers with BIR-approved plans (Track B) are not required to withhold on qualifying retirement benefits
- Benefits that fail to meet exemption conditions are treated as additional compensation and subject to withholding

---

## 8. Separation Pay Distinction (Section 32(B)(6)(b))

Separation pay (under Art. 298-299 Labor Code, authorized causes) is exempt under **Section 32(B)(6)(b)** — a different provision:
- Exempt "regardless of age or length of service"
- Only for separation caused by circumstances **beyond the control of the employee** (retrenchment, redundancy, closure, disease)
- Does NOT apply to voluntary resignation

This is distinct from retirement pay. Both may apply simultaneously (pay-the-higher rule), but they are governed by different subsections.

## 9. Practical Impact on Product Design

For the RA 7641 calculator:

1. **Standard Track A case** (age 60-65, 5+ years service): Retirement pay is tax-exempt regardless of whether employer has a BIR-registered plan. No withholding required.

2. **Early retirement case** (age 50-59, employer has BIR-approved plan): Exempt if employee has 10+ years with same employer and hasn't previously claimed the exemption.

3. **Early retirement, no BIR plan** (age 50-59, no registered plan): Taxable. Add to gross income, compute income tax per graduated rates.

4. **Tax flag fields needed** in computation output:
   - `taxExemptionTrack`: `"labor_code_mandatory"` | `"bir_approved_plan"` | `"none"`
   - `isTaxExempt`: `boolean`
   - `taxExemptReason`: `string` (human-readable explanation)
   - `birApprovedPlanRequired`: `boolean` (whether employer needs BIR plan for exemption to apply)

5. **NLRC worksheet implication**: Tax treatment must be noted in the worksheet (exempt or taxable, with statutory basis).
