## REPUBLIC ACT NO. 7641

**AN ACT AMENDING ARTICLE 287 OF PRESIDENTIAL DECREE NO. 442, AS AMENDED, OTHERWISE KNOWN AS THE LABOR CODE OF THE PHILIPPINES, BY PROVIDING FOR RETIREMENT PAY TO QUALIFIED PRIVATE SECTOR EMPLOYEES IN THE ABSENCE OF ANY RETIREMENT PLAN IN THE ESTABLISHMENT**

Enacted: December 9, 1992
Effective: January 7, 1993 (15 days after publication)

---

### SECTION 1

Article 287 of Presidential Decree No. 442, as amended, otherwise known as the Labor Code of the Philippines, is hereby amended to read as follows:

> "ART. 287. *Retirement.* — Any employee may be retired upon reaching the retirement age established in the collective bargaining agreement or other applicable employment contract.
>
> In case of retirement, the employee shall be entitled to receive such retirement benefits as he may have earned under existing laws and any collective bargaining agreement and other agreements: *Provided, however*, That an employee's retirement benefits under any collective bargaining and other agreements shall not be less than those provided herein.
>
> In the absence of a retirement plan or agreement providing for retirement benefits of employees in the establishment, an employee upon reaching the age of sixty (60) years or more, but not beyond sixty-five (65) years which is hereby declared the compulsory retirement age, who has served at least five (5) years in the said establishment, may retire and shall be entitled to retirement pay equivalent to at least one-half (1/2) month salary for every year of service, a fraction of at least six (6) months being considered as one whole year.
>
> Unless the parties provide for broader inclusions, the term 'one-half (1/2) month salary' shall mean fifteen (15) days plus one-twelfth (1/12) of the 13th month pay and the cash equivalent of not more than five (5) days of service incentive leaves.
>
> Retail, service and agricultural establishments or operations employing not more than ten (10) employees or workers are exempted from the coverage of this provision.
>
> Violation of this provision is unlawful and subject to the penal provisions provided under Article 288 of this Code."

### SECTION 2

Nothing in this Act shall deprive any employee of any benefit, supplement or retirement pay to which he may be entitled under existing laws or any collective bargaining or other agreement, or company policy or practice.

### SECTION 3

This Act shall take effect fifteen (15) days after its publication in the Official Gazette or in at least two (2) national newspapers of general circulation, whichever comes earlier.

## IMPLEMENTING RULES AND REGULATIONS (IRR)

**Rules Implementing the New Retirement Law (R.A. No. 7641)**
Issued pursuant to Article 287 of the Labor Code as amended by RA 7641, in relation to Article 5 of the Labor Code.
Effective: April 1, 1993

### RULE II: Retirement Benefits

#### Section 1 — Coverage
This Rule shall apply to all employees in the private sector, regardless of their position, designation or status, and irrespective of the method by which their wages are paid.

#### Section 2 — Exemptions

The following are NOT covered:
1. National government employees and employees of government-owned or controlled corporations and their subsidiaries (covered by Civil Service Law and/or GSIS)
2. Retail, service, and agricultural establishments or operations employing not more than ten (10) employees (exempted from RA 7641)

**Note:** Domestic helpers and persons in personal service were initially excluded but the exclusion was deleted in 1994 by RA 7655.

#### Section 3 — Contractual Retirement Plan
Employees may be retired under collective bargaining agreements or employment contracts. Retirement benefits under any such agreement shall NOT be less than the statutory minimum under RA 7641.

#### Section 4 — Retirement Ages
- **Optional Retirement:** Employee who is 60 years of age or more, with at least 5 years of service in the establishment, may retire at his/her option.
- **Compulsory Retirement:** Employee who reaches 65 years of age is compulsorily retired.
- The period of employment before the effectivity of RA 7641 (January 7, 1993) is counted in computing years of service.
- "Service" includes authorized absences, vacations, regular holidays and other leaves.

#### Section 5 — Retirement Benefits

Minimum entitlement: **at least one-half (1/2) month salary for every year of service**, a fraction of at least six (6) months being considered as one whole year.

**Components of "one-half (1/2) month salary":**
1. Fifteen (15) days salary based on the latest salary rate
2. Cash equivalent of not more than five (5) days of service incentive leaves (SIL)
3. One-twelfth (1/12) of the 13th month pay

**Total effective days: 22.5 days** (15 + 5 + 2.5 [which is 1/12 × 30 days])

**Formula:**
> Minimum Retirement Pay = Daily Rate × 22.5 × Credited Years of Service

**Daily Rate calculation:**
- For monthly-paid employees: Daily Rate = Monthly Basic Salary / 26 working days (or 22 if the applicable divisor under company practice)
- For piece-rate or those paid by results: Daily Rate = Average Daily Salary (ADS) for the last 12 months / actual working days in that period

**Credited Years of Service (rounding rule):**
- A fraction of at least six (6) months is considered one whole year
- A fraction of less than six (6) months is disregarded

#### Section 6 — Tax Treatment
Retirement benefits are exempt from income tax if they satisfy the conditions under Section 32(B)(6)(a) of the NIRC (formerly Section 28(b)(7)(A)):
1. Employee is at least fifty (50) years old at time of retirement
2. Employee has served for at least ten (10) years in the same establishment
3. This is the first time the employee availed of the tax exemption privilege
4. The retirement plan is registered and approved by the BIR

If any condition is not met, the retirement pay is treated as gross income subject to income tax.

#### Section 7 — Penalties
Violations of RA 7641 are unlawful and subject to the penal provisions of Article 288 of the Labor Code.

#### Section 9 — Effectivity
These rules took effect on January 7, 1993, concurrent with RA 7641.

## DOLE LABOR ADVISORY
**Guidelines for the Effective Implementation of R.A. 7641**
Issued: October 24, 1996

### Key Clarifications

1. **Coverage:** All employees in the private sector regardless of position, designation or status — including part-time workers.

2. **Exemptions:** Retail, service, and agricultural establishments employing not more than ten (10) employees are exempt. Government workers under Civil Service Law are not covered.

3. **Retirement Pay Formula:**
   > Retirement Pay = (Daily Rate × 22.5 days) × Credited Years of Service

   Where "one-half month salary" = 15 days salary + 5 days SIL equivalent + 1/12 of 13th month pay.

4. **Service period before Jan 7, 1993:** Counted toward total service years for purposes of retirement pay computation.

5. **Substitute Retirement Plans:** Where employer has a retirement plan (via CBA, individual contract, or company policy) providing benefits equal to or superior to RA 7641 minimum, that plan prevails. Where benefits are inferior to the statutory minimum, the employer must pay the difference.

6. **PAG-IBIG Fund as Substitute:** PAG-IBIG Fund coverage may substitute for RA 7641 compliance only if the benefits provided meet or exceed RA 7641 requirements. If insufficient, the employer pays the shortfall. Only employer contributions (not employee contributions) count toward the employer's RA 7641 obligation.

7. **COLA Exclusion:** The cost-of-living allowance (COLA) is NOT included in the salary basis for retirement pay computation.

## RA 8558 — Amendment to RA 7641

RA 8558 clarified regularization rules affecting worker eligibility for retirement benefits. It indirectly supports RA 7641 by ensuring more employees achieve permanent/regular status and thus qualify for the required 5-year service period.

---

## RA 10757 — Surface Mine Workers

Republic Act No. 10757 reduced the **optional retirement age for surface mine workers from 60 to 50 years**. This industry-specific modification recognizes the occupational health risks of surface mining. All other RA 7641 provisions (including the 22.5-day formula) apply unchanged.

## LABOR CODE RENUMBERING

Note: Article 287 of the original Labor Code (PD 442) was renumbered to **Article 302** by RA 10151 (June 21, 2011). References to "Article 287" and "Article 302" are to the same provision. Modern citations use Article 302.

## KEY STATUTORY DEFINITIONS (for precision)

| Term | Statutory Definition |
|------|----------------------|
| One-half month salary | 15 days + 1/12 of 13th month pay + cash equivalent of up to 5 days SIL |
| Credited year of service | Actual years served; fraction ≥ 6 months = 1 year; fraction < 6 months = dropped |
| Optional retirement age | 60 years (private sector general rule); 50 years (surface mine workers per RA 10757) |
| Compulsory retirement age | 65 years |
| Minimum service for eligibility | 5 years with the same establishment |
| Covered employees | All private sector employees regardless of position, designation, or status |
| Exempt establishments | Retail, service, agricultural with ≤ 10 employees |

## COMPUTATION EXAMPLE (from DOLE Guidelines)

**Employee data:**
- Monthly basic salary: PHP 20,000
- Daily rate: PHP 20,000 / 26 = PHP 769.23
- Years of service: 20 years 8 months → 21 credited years (8 months ≥ 6 months → rounds up)

**Retirement Pay:**
- Daily Rate × 22.5 × Credited Years
- PHP 769.23 × 22.5 × 21 = PHP 363,461.54

**Common employer error (15-day computation):**
- PHP 769.23 × 15 × 21 = PHP 242,307.69 ← WRONG (underpays by PHP 121,153.85 = 33%)

The 33% underpayment arises because most employers use 15 days instead of the legally required 22.5 days.
