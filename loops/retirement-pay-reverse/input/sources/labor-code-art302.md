## Article 302 [287] — Retirement (Current Consolidated Text)

### Paragraph 1: CBA/Contract Retirement Age
> "Any employee may be retired upon reaching the retirement age established in the collective bargaining agreement or other applicable employment contract."

### Paragraph 2: CBA Benefits Floor
> "In case of retirement, the employee shall be entitled to receive such retirement benefits as he may have earned under existing laws and any collective bargaining agreement and other agreements: *Provided, however*, That an employee's retirement benefits under any collective bargaining and other agreements shall not be less than those provided herein."

### Paragraph 3: Statutory Retirement (No Plan)
> "In the absence of a retirement plan or agreement providing for retirement benefits of employees in the establishment, an employee upon reaching the age of sixty (60) years or more, but not beyond sixty-five (65) years which is hereby declared the compulsory retirement age, who has served at least five (5) years in the said establishment, may retire and shall be entitled to retirement pay equivalent to at least one-half (1/2) month salary for every year of service, a fraction of at least six (6) months being considered as one whole year."

### Paragraph 4: Definition of "One-Half Month Salary"
> "Unless the parties provide for broader inclusions, the term 'one-half (1/2) month salary' shall mean fifteen (15) days plus one-twelfth (1/12) of the 13th month pay and the cash equivalent of not more than five (5) days of service incentive leaves."

### Paragraph 5: Underground Mine Workers (as amended by RA 8558)
> "An underground mining employee upon reaching the age of fifty (50) years or more, but not beyond sixty (60) years [who has served at least five (5) years] may retire and shall be entitled to retirement pay equivalent to at least one-half (1/2) month salary for every year of service."

### Paragraph 6: Surface Mine Workers (as amended by RA 10757)
> "Surface mine workers shall only include mill plant workers, electrical, mechanical and tailings pond personnel."
> Surface mine workers may retire upon reaching age fifty (50), with sixty (60) as compulsory retirement age, with at least five (5) years of service.

### Paragraph 7: Small Employer Exemption
> "Retail, service and agricultural establishments or operations employing not more than ten (10) employees or workers are exempted from the coverage of this provision."

## 22.5-Day Breakdown — Statutory Authority

The term "one-half (1/2) month salary" = **15 + 5 + 1/12(13th month)**

Mathematically:
- 15 days salary = 15/26 × monthly basic salary (for daily-rate employees: 15 × daily rate)
- 5 days SIL cash equivalent = 5 × daily rate
- 1/12 of 13th month pay = (annual basic salary / 12) / 12 = monthly basic / 12

Total days equivalent per year of service: 15 + 5 + (30/12) = 15 + 5 + 2.5 = **22.5 days**

**Important:** The 5 days SIL component says "not more than five (5) days" — but it means the employer must provide at minimum 5 days if the employee has SIL entitlement. The floor is 5 days per the DOLE implementing rules.

## DOLE Rule II, Book VI — Implementing Rules

### Coverage
All employees in the private sector regardless of position, designation, or status, including:
- Part-time employees
- Employees of service and job contractors
- Domestic helpers (with qualifications)

### Exemptions
1. Government employees covered by Civil Service Law
2. Employees of retail, service, or agricultural establishments employing **not more than 10 employees or workers**
3. [Note: Domestic helpers later removed from exemption by subsequent regulations]

### Retirement Age Schedule

| Employee Category | Optional Retirement | Compulsory Retirement |
|---|---|---|
| General private sector | Age 60 + 5 years service | Age 65 |
| Underground mine workers (RA 8558) | Age 50 + 5 years service | Age 60 |
| Surface mine workers (RA 10757) | Age 50 + 5 years service | Age 60 |

### Salary Basis for Computation
The "latest salary rate" includes:
- All remunerations paid during normal working days and normal working hours
- Fixed, time-based, task, piece, or commission-based wages
- Fair value of board, lodging, or other facilities customarily furnished by employer

For results-based/piece-rate workers: **average daily salary over the preceding 12 months** is used.

### Credited Years of Service
- A fraction of at least **six (6) months** is considered **one whole year**
- Service before January 7, 1993 (law's effectivity) **is counted** toward total credited years
- Continuous or broken service counts

### Non-Diminution
Employers may NOT reduce retirement benefits already being enjoyed by employees under:
- Individual employment agreements
- Collective bargaining agreements
- Established employer practices

### PAG-IBIG Substitution
An employer may use PAG-IBIG Fund contributions to substitute for RA 7641 retirement pay if:
- The benefit under PAG-IBIG is **equal to or superior** to what RA 7641 requires
- Only employer contributions count toward compliance
- If PAG-IBIG benefit is less, employer pays the difference

## DOLE Labor Advisory on RA 7641 (October 24, 1996)

**Secretary:** Leonardo A. Quisumbing

Key clarifications:
1. "One-half month salary" = 15 days + 1/12 of 13th month pay + cash equivalent of up to 5 days SIL
2. This **expanded the concept** of "one-half month salary" beyond the simple calculation of one month's salary divided by two
3. Employment periods **before January 7, 1993** count toward total service length
4. PAG-IBIG may substitute only if benefit is equal or superior to statutory minimum

## RA 8558 — Underground Mine Workers (February 26, 1998)

- Approved: February 26, 1998
- Signed by: President Fidel V. Ramos
- Amends: Art. 287 (now Art. 302) of the Labor Code
- Effect: Reduces retirement age for underground mine workers from 60 to 50 (optional) and from 65 to 60 (compulsory)
- Minimum service: 5 years
- Benefit rate: Same — at least one-half (1/2) month salary for every year of service

---

## RA 10757 — Surface Mine Workers (April 8, 2016)

- Approved: April 8, 2016
- Amends: Art. 302 of the Labor Code (already renumbered)
- Coverage: "Surface mine workers shall only include mill plant workers, electrical, mechanical and tailings pond personnel"
- Effect: Reduces retirement age for surface mine workers from 60 to 50 (optional) and from 65 to 60 (compulsory)
- Policy basis: Recognizes "innumerable harmful elements" in mining profession causing "more serious health problems"
- Also amends Social Security Act to align SSS benefits with new age 50 retirement threshold
- Effective: 15 days after publication in official government media

## Key Judicial Interpretations Touching Art. 287/302

### *Santo v. University of Cebu* (G.R. No. 232522)
- Compared employer's retirement plan (15 days/year) vs. Art. 287 (22.5 days/year)
- Held: Art. 287 applies as it is more beneficial to the employee
- Confirms: "22.5 days" is the correct statutory formula

### *Beltran v. AMA Computer College-Binan*
- "While the employer is free to grant retirement benefits and impose different age or service requirements, the benefits should not be lesser than those provided in Article 287 of the Labor Code"

### *Grace Christian High School v. Lavandera*
- Affirmed 22.5-day breakdown
- Rejected pro-rating the SIL component
- SIL must be included at its full 5-day cash equivalent

## Engineering-Relevant Takeaways

1. **Art. 302 is the renaming of Art. 287** — same substantive content; DOLE Advisory No. 1, Series of 2015 did the renumbering
2. **The product primarily implements the general rule** (not mine worker variants) — but mine worker edge cases should be documented
3. **"At least" language** means company plans can exceed but not undercut the statutory formula
4. **Retroactivity**: Service before January 7, 1993 counts — users who input hire dates before 1993 are valid
5. **The 5-day SIL component is a floor**: "not more than five (5) days" means 5 days is the statutory minimum included, not a cap on what parties can agree
6. **Small employer exemption (≤10 workers)** is a hard cut-off — the product should flag this and compute nothing (or show "exempt, RA 7641 does not apply")
7. **No floating-point arithmetic**: 1/12 of 13th month = exact rational, compute as integer centavos
