# DOLE Labor Advisory No. 06, Series of 2020
## Guidelines on the Payment of Final Pay and Issuance of Certificate of Employment

## Source
Compiled from: dole.gov.ph, emplawphil.wordpress.com, platonmartinez.com, laborlaw.ph, everythingatwork.com
Date cached: 2026-03-06
Official issuance date: January 31, 2020

---

## Overview

DOLE Labor Advisory No. 06-20 was issued on January 31, 2020 to establish uniform guidelines on:
1. The payment and/or release of Final Pay to separated employees
2. The issuance of Certificate of Employment (COE)

Prior to this advisory, there was no specific period prescribed for the release of final pay. Employees often waited months or years. The 30-day rule remedied this.

---

## Section 1 — Definition of Final Pay

**Final Pay** (also referred to as "back pay" or "last pay") is defined as:

> "The sum or totality of all the wages or monetary benefits due the employee regardless of the cause of the termination of employment."

---

## Section 2 — Components of Final Pay

Final pay includes, but is not limited to, the following:

| Item | Component | Legal Basis |
|------|-----------|-------------|
| (a) | Unpaid earned salary | Labor Code (wages) |
| (b) | Cash conversion of unused Service Incentive Leave (SIL) | Art. 95, Labor Code |
| (c) | Cash conversions of remaining unused vacation, sick, or other leaves (if applicable) | Company policy / CBA |
| (d) | Pro-rated 13th month pay | Presidential Decree No. 851 |
| (e) | Separation pay (if applicable) | Arts. 298-299, Labor Code / company policy / CBA |
| (f) | Retirement pay (if applicable) | Art. 302, Labor Code |
| (g) | Income tax claim for excess taxes withheld (if applicable) | NIRC |
| (h) | Other compensation per individual or collective agreement | CBA / employment contract |
| (i) | Cash bonds or any kind of deposits due for return to the employee | — |

**Notes on item (f) — Retirement Pay:**
The advisory cross-references Art. 302 of the Labor Code (RA 7641). When an employee separates by reason of retirement (voluntary at age 60, or compulsory at age 65 with 5 years service), retirement pay is part of their final pay. The advisory does not recompute the RA 7641 formula — it incorporates it by reference.

---

## Section 3 — Release Period

**Final Pay:** Must be released **within thirty (30) calendar days** from the date of separation or termination of employment.

Exception: If a company policy, individual agreement, or collective bargaining agreement prescribes a **more favorable** (shorter) period, that shorter period governs.

The 30-day period begins at the date of separation, **not** after the clearance process is completed. Employers may use clearance procedures to verify liabilities, but cannot use the clearance process as an indefinite delay mechanism.

**Certificate of Employment (COE):** Must be issued within **three (3) days** from the date of the employee's request. The COE must contain:
- Dates of engagement
- Date of termination (if terminated)
- Type(s) of work performed

The COE must NOT include infractions, disciplinary records, or negative assessments.

---

## Section 4 — Employer Rights: Clearance and Offsets

Employers retain the right to:
1. Establish reasonable clearance procedures (return of equipment, etc.)
2. Withhold or offset final pay to recover legitimate employee debts or accountabilities already due

**Case law basis:** Milan v. NLRC, G.R. No. 202961, February 04, 2015

However, the clearance process cannot be used to indefinitely postpone final pay beyond 30 days. The 30-day deadline is firm.

---

## Section 5 — Enforcement

- Non-compliance may result in a DOLE complaint
- Disputes are filed before the nearest **DOLE Regional/Provincial/Field Office** having jurisdiction over the workplace
- Subject to existing enforcement and conciliation mechanisms

## Computation Formulas for Final Pay Components

### (a) Unpaid Earned Salary

```
Daily Rate = Monthly Basic Salary / Working Days per Month
Working days per month = 22 (standard) or actual calendar days depending on pay basis
Unpaid Salary = Daily Rate × Number of days actually worked in final pay period
```

### (b) SIL Cash Conversion

```
SIL Days Earned = 5 days per year (for employees with >= 1 year service)
Pro-rated SIL = (Months worked in current year / 12) × 5 days
Cash Value = Daily Rate × Unused SIL days
```

Note: The 5-day SIL mandated by Art. 95 is the minimum. If the company provides more leave days, unused leaves in excess of 5 days are computed per company policy, not RA 7641.

### (d) Pro-Rated 13th Month Pay

```
13th Month Pay = (Total Basic Salary Earned in Calendar Year) / 12
Pro-rated for partial year = Total Basic Salary Earned from Jan 1 to separation date / 12
```

Example: Employee separated in July, monthly basic = PHP 30,000, worked Jan–July (7 months):
- Total basic earned = PHP 30,000 × 7 = PHP 210,000
- Pro-rated 13th month = PHP 210,000 / 12 = PHP 17,500

### (f) Retirement Pay (RA 7641 formula — incorporated by reference)

When separation reason is retirement under Art. 302:

```
Retirement Pay = (Monthly Basic Salary / 2) × Credited Years of Service × (22.5/15)

Equivalent formula:
= Daily Rate × 22.5 × Credited Years of Service

Where:
  22.5 days = 15 days salary + 5 days SIL + (1/12 × 13th month pay expressed as days)
  Daily Rate = Monthly Basic Salary / 26 (per SC interpretation)
  Credited Years = actual years served, fraction ≥ 6 months rounds up
```

Note: The 5 SIL days and 1/12 of 13th month in the RA 7641 formula are specifically for the retirement pay computation — they are NOT double-counted with the SIL cash conversion and pro-rated 13th month pay also listed as final pay components (b) and (d). In practice, when retirement pay is paid under RA 7641, items (b) and (d) may or may not be separately paid depending on the employer; the employer must separately pay unused leave and pro-rated 13th month in addition to retirement pay since the RA 7641 retirement pay formula already incorporates SIL and 13th month as a legal minimum standard, not as a credit against other obligations.

---

## Section 6 — Relationship to Retirement Pay Under RA 7641

LA 06-20 does NOT modify the RA 7641 formula. Key relationship:

1. When an employee retires under Art. 302 (RA 7641), retirement pay becomes part of the final pay package
2. The 30-day release rule applies to retirement pay just as to any other final pay component
3. The RA 7641 formula (22.5 days × credited years × daily rate) is computed separately
4. Pro-rated 13th month pay (item d) and unused SIL (item b) are still due as separate components of final pay in addition to retirement pay — the 22.5-day formula already incorporates SIL and 13th month as the minimum legal standard for retirement pay, but does not extinguish independent claims for those components

**NLRC Position:** In money claim cases, the final pay components are itemized separately:
- Retirement pay (RA 7641, 22.5-day formula)
- SIL cash conversion (Art. 95)
- Pro-rated 13th month (PD 851)
- Unpaid salary
- Other earned but unpaid benefits

## Section 7 — Relevance to RA 7641 Calculator Product

For the retirement pay calculator:

1. **30-day rule** — Should be surfaced in the results display: employer must pay within 30 days of retirement date
2. **Final pay components** — The NLRC worksheet should enumerate ALL final pay components, not just retirement pay
3. **Double-counting warning** — The calculator should note that the 5 SIL days and 1/12 13th month in the RA 7641 formula are the minimum retirement pay components; unused leave beyond 5 days and actual 13th month entitlement may be owed separately
4. **Certificate of Employment** — An employee separating by retirement is entitled to a COE within 3 days of request; this can be surfaced as a checklist item in the results view
