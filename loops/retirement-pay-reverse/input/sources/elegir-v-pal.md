## Factual Background

Bibiano C. Elegir was hired by Philippine Airlines as a commercial pilot on **March 16, 1971**.

In 1995, PAL offered eight B747-400 Captain positions under a refleeting program. Elegir won the bid and underwent training in Seattle, Washington, from May 8, 1995, to September 19, 1995.

On **November 5, 1996**, after 25 years, 8 months, and 20 days of service, Elegir applied for optional retirement under the CBA between PAL and the Airline Pilots Association of the Philippines (ALPAP). PAL warned that training costs would be deducted from retirement pay if he retired within three years of post-training service.

Elegir retired on **November 6, 1996**. PAL deducted training costs and computed retirement at ₱5,000 per year of service. Elegir's counsel disputed this, arguing retirement benefits should be based on Article 287 of the Labor Code (as amended by RA 7641).

Elegir filed a complaint with the Labor Arbiter on August 27, 1997, for non-payment of retirement pay, moral and exemplary damages, and attorney's fees.

---

## Legal Issues

1. Should Elegir's retirement benefits be computed under Article 287 of the Labor Code (RA 7641) or under PAL's retirement plans?
2. Should Elegir reimburse PAL for training costs?
3. Should legal interest be imposed on the monetary award?

---

## Supreme Court Ruling

### Issue 1: Which Retirement Scheme Applies

**Held:** PAL's retirement plans govern (not Article 287 / RA 7641), because PAL's plans provide **substantially higher benefits** than the statutory minimum.

**Key doctrine:**

> "The determining factor in choosing which retirement scheme to apply is still superiority in terms of benefits provided. Thus, even if there is an existing CBA but the same does not provide for retirement benefits equal or superior to that which is provided under Article 287 of the Labor Code, the latter will apply — to assure the employee a reasonable amount of retirement pay for his sustenance."

PAL-ALPAP Retirement Plan and PAL Pilots' Retirement Benefit Plan both offered benefits exceeding RA 7641 statutory minimum (PAL plans provided approximately 240% of monthly salary per year of service). RA 7641 applies only when no superior plan exists.

### Issue 2: Training Cost Reimbursement

**Held:** Reimbursement upheld. Elegir served only 1 year post-training (CBA required 3 years). Deduction justified under:
- CBA Section 1, Article XXIII
- Civil Code Article 22 (unjust enrichment)
- Elegir received salary increases (₱125,692 → ₱137,977) and enhanced career prospects from the training

### Issue 3: Interest

**Held:** Standard legal interest applies after judgment finality; no exceptional rate imposed.

## The 22.5-Day Doctrine — Key Passage

The decision is the principal modern authority for the 22.5-day interpretation of "one-half month salary." The exact statutory text from RA 7641 cited in the decision:

> "one-half (1/2) month salary shall mean fifteen (15) days plus one-twelfth (1/12) of the 13th month pay and the cash equivalent of not more than five (5) days of service incentive leaves"

The Court confirmed this formula computes to **22.5 days**:

| Component | Days |
|---|---|
| 15 days salary (base) | 15.0 |
| Cash equivalent of 5 days SIL | 5.0 |
| 1/12 of 13th month pay | 2.5 |
| **Total** | **22.5** |

**Derivation of 2.5 days for 1/12 of 13th month pay:**
- 13th month pay = 1 month = 30 days of salary
- 1/12 × 30 days = **2.5 days**

This formula was first established in ***Capitol Wireless, Inc. v. Hon. Secretary Ma. Nieves R. Confesor*, G.R. No. 117174, November 13, 1996**, and *Elegir* is the more recent, controlling affirmation.

---

## Doctrines Established / Affirmed

### Doctrine 1: Superior Benefits Rule
When multiple retirement schemes exist (statute + company plan + CBA), the scheme providing the **greatest benefit** to the employee controls. RA 7641 is a floor, not a ceiling.

### Doctrine 2: RA 7641 as Safety Net
RA 7641 applies when:
(a) No retirement plan exists in the establishment, OR
(b) An existing plan provides benefits **below** the RA 7641 minimum.

If a CBA/company plan provides equal or better benefits, RA 7641's specific formula does not apply (though the superior plan does).

### Doctrine 3: 22.5-Day Formula Confirmed
"One-half (1/2) month salary" under RA 7641 = 22.5 days. The phrase "one-half month" is a legal term of art, not a literal half-month computation. It includes SIL and 1/12 of 13th month pay.

### Doctrine 4: SIL Inclusion Regardless of Use
The 5 days SIL is part of the formula whether or not the employee used SIL during service.

### Doctrine 5: Retroactivity of Service
Service before RA 7641's effectivity (January 7, 1993) counts toward credited years of service.

## Significance for RA 7641 Calculator

1. **22.5 days is not negotiable** — it is the confirmed statutory minimum multiplier; any employer using 15 days underpays by exactly 33.3%.
2. **Compliance gap**: "one-half month" is widely misread as 15 days (literally half a 30-day month). *Elegir* removes any ambiguity.
3. **Superior plan prevails** — the calculator's plan comparison feature must check whether company plan > RA 7641 minimum and display which applies.
4. **SIL is unconditional** — the 5 days SIL component is always included, regardless of whether the employee actually has SIL entitlement or used SIL.

## Additional Authority: Capitol Wireless v. Confesor (G.R. No. 117174, Nov. 13, 1996)

This is the earlier landmark case establishing the 22.5-day computation. Key holding:

> "Unless the parties provide for broader inclusions, the term one-half (1/2) month salary shall mean fifteen (15) days plus one-twelfth (1/12) of the 13th month pay and the cash equivalent of not more than five (5) days of service incentive leaves."

The Court "simplified the computation of 'one-half month salary' by equating it to '22.5 days'" = 15 + 2.5 + 5.
