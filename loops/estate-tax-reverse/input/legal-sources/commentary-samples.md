## Sample 1: TRAIN-Era Simple Case (Single, No Spouse)

**Source**: respicio.ph

**Facts**:
- Filipino citizen, resident
- Date of death: 2022
- Married status: Single (no surviving spouse)
- Gross estate: ₱20,000,000 total
  - Real property: ₱15,000,000
  - Personal property (bank, shares): ₱5,000,000
- Family home FMV: ₱10,000,000 (part of real property)
- Medical expenses (within 1 year): ₱500,000 (actual, documented)

**Computation**:
```
Gross Estate:                           ₱20,000,000
Less: Ordinary Deductions:
  - None assumed                               ₱0
Estate After Ordinary Deductions:       ₱20,000,000

Less: Special Deductions:
  - Standard Deduction:                -₱5,000,000
  - Family Home (FMV = ₱10M, cap ₱10M): -₱10,000,000
  - Medical Expenses (≤₱500K):           -₱500,000
  Total Special Deductions:            -₱15,500,000

Net Estate:                             ₱4,500,000
Less: Surviving Spouse Share:                  ₱0 (single)
Net Taxable Estate:                     ₱4,500,000

Estate Tax: 6% × ₱4,500,000 =            ₱270,000
```

## Sample 2: TRAIN-Era Married (ACP), Debts, No Family Home Deduction Used

**Source**: asglawpartners.com (adapted)

**Facts**:
- Filipino citizen, resident
- Date of death: 2023
- Married under ACP (post-1988 marriage)
- Gross estate breakdown:
  - All property is community (ACP): ₱18,000,000
  - Community liabilities (debts): ₱500,000
- No family home separately identified (or family home value already excluded from real property)

**Computation**:
```
Gross Estate (all community):           ₱18,000,000

Less: Ordinary Deductions:
  - Claims Against Estate (debts):        -₱500,000
  Total Ordinary Deductions:              -₱500,000

Estate After Ordinary Deductions:        ₱17,500,000

Less: Special Deductions:
  - Standard Deduction:                  -₱5,000,000
  Total Special Deductions:              -₱5,000,000

Net Estate:                              ₱12,500,000

Less: Surviving Spouse Share:
  Total Community Assets:               ₱18,000,000
  Less: Community Liabilities:            -₱500,000
  Net Community Property:               ₱17,500,000
  Spouse Share (50%):                    -₱8,750,000

Net Taxable Estate:                      ₱3,750,000

Estate Tax: 6% × ₱3,750,000 =            ₱225,000
```

*Note: There is an important question about how surviving spouse share interacts with deductions. The standard approach: compute net estate first (gross estate − all deductions), then subtract spouse share. Confirmed by Form 1801 Item 38 (Net Estate) → Item 39 (Spouse Share) → Item 40 (Net Taxable Estate).*

## Sample 3: TRAIN-Era Standard Case (Married, All Major Deductions)

**Source**: respicio.ph, ralblaw.com (composite)

**Facts**:
- Filipino citizen, resident
- Date of death: 2021
- Married under ACP
- All property is community (ACP)
- Gross estate: ₱15,000,000 total
  - Real property (family home): ₱8,000,000 (family home only)
  - Real property (other): ₱3,000,000
  - Personal property: ₱4,000,000
- Community liabilities: ₱1,000,000 (documented debts)
- Medical expenses: ₱400,000 (within 1 year, receipts available)
- No vanishing deduction

**Computation**:
```
GROSS ESTATE BREAKDOWN:
  Real Property (other):               ₱3,000,000
  Family Home:                         ₱8,000,000
  Personal Property:                   ₱4,000,000
TOTAL GROSS ESTATE:                   ₱15,000,000

ORDINARY DEDUCTIONS (Schedule 5):
  Claims Against Estate (debts):       -₱1,000,000
TOTAL ORDINARY DEDUCTIONS:            -₱1,000,000

ESTATE AFTER ORDINARY DEDUCTIONS:    ₱14,000,000

SPECIAL DEDUCTIONS (Schedule 6):
  Standard Deduction:                  -₱5,000,000
  Family Home (FMV ₱8M, cap ₱10M):    -₱8,000,000
  Medical Expenses:                      -₱400,000
TOTAL SPECIAL DEDUCTIONS:            -₱13,400,000

NET ESTATE:                              ₱600,000

SURVIVING SPOUSE SHARE:
  Community Assets:                   ₱15,000,000
  Community Liabilities:              -₱1,000,000
  Net Community:                      ₱14,000,000
  Spouse Share (50%):                  -₱7,000,000

NOTE: Spouse share (₱7,000,000) > Net Estate (₱600,000)
→ Net Taxable Estate = max(0, ₱600,000 − ₱7,000,000) = ₱0

ESTATE TAX DUE: ₱0
```

**Commentary**: When deductions (including spouse share) exceed gross estate, the estate tax is zero. There is no minimum tax under regular TRAIN rules (unlike amnesty which has ₱5,000 minimum).

## Sample 4: Zero Tax Case (Deductions Exceed Gross Estate)

**Facts**:
- Single decedent, Filipino citizen
- Date of death: 2020
- Gross estate: ₱4,000,000
- No debts or other ordinary deductions

```
Gross Estate:                         ₱4,000,000
Less: Standard Deduction:            -₱5,000,000
Net Estate: max(0, -₱1,000,000) =          ₱0

Estate Tax Due:                              ₱0
```

**Commentary**: The standard deduction alone (₱5,000,000) covers any estate ≤₱5,000,000. No tax applies.

## Sample 5: Pre-TRAIN Case (Death in 2015, Married CPG)

**Source**: ralblaw.com (adapted)

**Facts**:
- Filipino citizen, resident
- Date of death: March 2015 (pre-TRAIN)
- Married under CPG (marriage before 1988)
- All property is conjugal
- Gross estate: ₱8,000,000
- Conjugal liabilities: ₱400,000
- Funeral expenses (actual): ₱350,000; 5% of ₱8,000,000 = ₱400,000 → deductible: ₱350,000
- Family home FMV: ₱1,500,000 → deductible: ₱1,000,000 (pre-TRAIN cap)

**Computation**:
```
Gross Estate (all conjugal):          ₱8,000,000

ORDINARY DEDUCTIONS:
  Claims Against Estate (debts):        -₱400,000
  Funeral Expenses (lower of actual/5%): -₱350,000
TOTAL ORDINARY DEDUCTIONS:              -₱750,000

ESTATE AFTER ORDINARY DEDUCTIONS:    ₱7,250,000

SPECIAL DEDUCTIONS:
  Standard Deduction (pre-TRAIN):     -₱1,000,000
  Family Home (pre-TRAIN cap):        -₱1,000,000
TOTAL SPECIAL DEDUCTIONS:            -₱2,000,000

NET ESTATE:                           ₱5,250,000

SURVIVING SPOUSE SHARE (CPG):
  Conjugal Assets:                    ₱8,000,000
  Conjugal Liabilities:                -₱400,000
  Net Conjugal Property:              ₱7,600,000
  Spouse Share (50%):                 -₱3,800,000

NET TAXABLE ESTATE:                   ₱1,450,000

TAX COMPUTATION (graduated):
  On first ₱500,000 exempt:                  ₱0
  Wait — net taxable estate is ₱1,450,000, which is > ₱500,000 but ≤ ₱2,000,000:
  Tax = ₱15,000 + 8% × (₱1,450,000 − ₱500,000)
  Tax = ₱15,000 + 8% × ₱950,000
  Tax = ₱15,000 + ₱76,000
  ESTATE TAX DUE: ₱91,000
```

## Sample 6: Estate Tax Amnesty Case (Death in 2012)

**Facts**:
- Filipino citizen
- Date of death: May 2012
- Gross estate: ₱6,000,000 (real property)
- No prior estate tax return filed
- Chose to avail of amnesty under RA 11213/11956 (filed before June 14, 2025)
- No surviving spouse (single)

**Amnesty Computation (applying pre-TRAIN deductions at time of death)**:
```
Gross Estate:                         ₱6,000,000
Less: Standard Deduction (2012, pre-TRAIN): -₱1,000,000
Less: Funeral Expenses (assumed 5% × ₱6M = ₱300K): -₱300,000
Less: Family Home (if applicable, ≤₱1M):  -₱1,000,000
Net Estate:                           ₱3,700,000

Amnesty Tax: 6% × ₱3,700,000 =        ₱222,000
(Minimum ₱5,000 applies if net estate ≤ 0; here it does not)
```

**Versus regular pre-TRAIN tax**:
```
Net Taxable Estate: ₱3,700,000
Tax (₱135,000 + 11% × (₱3,700,000 − ₱2,000,000)):
Tax = ₱135,000 + 11% × ₱1,700,000
Tax = ₱135,000 + ₱187,000 = ₱322,000
Plus surcharges/interest for 13 years of non-payment → substantial additional amount

Amnesty savings: Large (₱222,000 vs. ₱322,000 base + years of penalties)
```

## Sample 7: Vanishing Deduction (100% — Property Within 1 Year)

**Facts**:
- Filipino citizen, single
- Date of death: January 2020
- Gross estate: ₱10,000,000
  - Inherited property (received October 2019, within 1 year): FMV at inheritance = ₱3,000,000; FMV at current death = ₱3,200,000
  - Other property: ₱6,800,000
- Prior estate tax on the inherited property: duly paid
- No debts or other deductions

**Vanishing Deduction Computation**:
```
Initial Value = min(₱3,000,000, ₱3,200,000) = ₱3,000,000
Time elapsed: < 1 year → 100% deductible

Vanishing Deduction = 100% × ₱3,000,000 = ₱3,000,000
```

**Full Computation**:
```
Gross Estate:                        ₱10,000,000

Ordinary Deductions:
  Vanishing Deduction (100%):         -₱3,000,000
TOTAL ORDINARY DEDUCTIONS:           -₱3,000,000

Estate After Ordinary Deductions:    ₱7,000,000

Special Deductions:
  Standard Deduction:                -₱5,000,000
TOTAL SPECIAL DEDUCTIONS:           -₱5,000,000

Net Taxable Estate:                   ₱2,000,000

Estate Tax: 6% × ₱2,000,000 =         ₱120,000
```

## Key Takeaways for Engine Development

1. **Net estate can be zero**: When deductions exceed gross estate, the result is ₱0, not negative. No minimum tax under regular TRAIN rules.

2. **Surviving spouse share sequence matters**: Form 1801 deducts spouse share AFTER standard/special deductions (Net Estate → less Spouse Share → Net Taxable Estate).

3. **Deductions can interact**: e.g., debts and funeral expenses reduce conjugal/community property before computing spouse share (in some interpretations) — needs clarification in detailed analysis.

4. **Family home cap**:
   - Pre-TRAIN: ₱1,000,000
   - TRAIN: ₱10,000,000
   - For conjugal/community: only ½ of FMV used (decedent's share)

5. **Vanishing deduction**: Use the **lower** of prior FMV vs. current FMV as the "initial value."

6. **Medical expense cap**: Maximum ₱500,000, requires documentation; within 1 year of death.

7. **Graduated rates** (pre-TRAIN): The first ₱200,000 of net estate is exempt — this is the key threshold for the 0% bracket.
