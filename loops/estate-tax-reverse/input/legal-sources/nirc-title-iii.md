# NIRC Title III, Chapter I — Estate Tax (Sections 84–97)
## As Amended by RA 10963 (TRAIN Law, effective January 1, 2018)

**Source**: taxacctgcenter.ph, chanrobles.com, BIR official resources
**Note**: All provisions below reflect the TRAIN-amended (current) version unless explicitly noted as pre-TRAIN.

---

## SEC. 84. Rate of Estate Tax

*As amended by Section 22, RA 10963 (TRAIN Law)*

"There shall be levied, assessed, collected and paid upon the transfer of the net estate as determined in accordance with Sections 85 and 86 of every decedent, whether resident or nonresident of the Philippines, a tax at the rate of **six percent (6%)** based on the value of such net estate."

**TRAIN Change**: Prior to January 1, 2018, the rate was graduated from 5% to 20%. See `pre-train-rates.md` for old schedule.

---

## SEC. 85. Gross Estate

The value of the gross estate of the decedent shall be determined by including the value at the time of his death of all property, real or personal, tangible or intangible, wherever situated.

**For Nonresident Non-Citizens**: Only property **situated in the Philippines** is included in the gross estate. Intangible personal property of a non-citizen nonresident is excluded unless the country of their domicile imposes a similar tax on Filipino citizens not residing there (reciprocity rule).

### Subsection A: Decedent's Interest
All property to the extent of the interest therein of the decedent at the time of death.

### Subsection B: Transfers in Contemplation of Death
All transfers made in contemplation of death, or intended to take effect in possession or enjoyment at or after death. Any transfer made without adequate consideration within 3 years of death is presumed to have been made in contemplation of death.

### Subsection C: Revocable Transfers
The value of any interest in property transferred (except bona fide sales for full consideration) where the decedent retained the power to alter, amend, revoke, or terminate, or where such power was relinquished within 3 years of death.

### Subsection D: Property Under General Power of Appointment
All property passing under a general power of appointment exercised by the decedent by will or by deed in contemplation of death.

### Subsection E: Life Insurance Proceeds
Proceeds of life insurance under policies taken out by the decedent upon his own life:
- Receivable by the **estate** of the decedent: **INCLUDED** in gross estate
- Receivable by other **beneficiaries** designated **irrevocably**: **EXCLUDED** from gross estate
- Receivable by other beneficiaries designated **revocably**: **INCLUDED** in gross estate

### Subsection F: Prior Interests
Applies to the extent of any interest held by the decedent in transfers described in subsections B through E, whether the transfer was made before or after the effectivity of this Code.

### Subsection G: Transfers for Insufficient Consideration
Where property was transferred for less than adequate consideration, the amount included in the gross estate is the **excess** of the fair market value at death over the consideration received.

## SEC. 86. Computation of Net Estate

### SUBSECTION A: Citizens and Residents

For the purpose of the tax imposed in Section 84, the value of the net estate of a citizen or resident of the Philippines shall be determined by deducting from the value of the gross estate:

#### (1) Expenses, Losses, Indebtedness, and Taxes (ELIT)

The following are deductible:

**(a) Claims Against the Estate**
Claims against the estate at the time of death, subject to the following conditions:
- The liability represents a personal obligation of the deceased existing at the time of death
- The liability was contracted in good faith and for adequate and full consideration
- The claim must be a debt or claim which is **duly substantiated** (supported by a notarized instrument or contract)
- If the claim was incurred after the death of the decedent, it cannot be deducted

**(b) Claims of the Deceased Against Insolvent Persons**
To the extent that the claim is uncollectible.

**(c) Unpaid Mortgages Upon, or Any Indebtedness in Respect to, Property**
Included in the value of the gross estate to the extent of only the value of the decedent's interest therein. If the property is community/conjugal, only the decedent's share of the mortgage is deductible.

**(d) Unpaid Taxes**
Taxes which have accrued as of the death of the decedent which were unpaid as of the time of death.

**(e) Casualty Losses**
Losses incurred during the settlement of the estate arising from fires, storms, shipwreck, or other casualties, or from robbery, theft, or embezzlement, when such losses are not compensated for by insurance or otherwise, and if at the time of the filing of the return such losses have not been claimed as a deduction for income tax purposes in an income tax return.

**TRAIN Change**: Funeral expenses and judicial/administrative expenses are **NO LONGER DEDUCTIBLE** under TRAIN. These were deductible pre-TRAIN.

#### (2) Property Previously Taxed (Vanishing Deduction)

*Also known as the "vanishing deduction."*

Deductible when:
- Property formed part of the taxable estate of a prior decedent who died within 5 years before death of the current decedent, OR
- The property was transferred to the current decedent by gift within 5 years before death, AND
- The prior estate tax or donor's tax was finally determined and paid

**Deductible Percentage Table (based on time elapsed since prior transfer):**

| Time Since Prior Transfer | Deductible Percentage |
|---|---|
| Within 1 year | 100% |
| More than 1 year but not more than 2 years | 80% |
| More than 2 years but not more than 3 years | 60% |
| More than 3 years but not more than 4 years | 40% |
| More than 4 years but not more than 5 years | 20% |

**Formula**:
```
Initial Value = lower of (FMV at prior transfer, FMV at current decedent's death)
Proportional Deduction Ratio = (Initial Value - Proportional Mortgage) / Gross Estate
Vanishing Deduction = Applicable Percentage × Initial Value × [(Gross Estate - ELIT Deductions) / Gross Estate]
```
*Note: This formula requires detailed elaboration in aspect analysis `deduction-vanishing`.*

#### (3) Transfers for Public Use

The amount of all bequests, legacies, devises, or transfers to or for the use of the **Government of the Philippines**, or any political subdivision thereof, for exclusively public purposes.

#### (4) Standard Deduction

**₱5,000,000** (Five million pesos) — no documentation required; automatic deduction.

#### (5) Family Home

Current fair market value of the family home to the extent of **up to ₱10,000,000** (Ten million pesos).

Conditions:
- Certified by the **Barangay Captain** of the locality where the family home is situated
- The family home must be the **actual residential home** of the decedent and their family at the time of death
- For **exclusive property**: full FMV deductible (up to ₱10M)
- For **conjugal/communal property**: ½ of FMV deductible (up to ₱10M)
- Available only for **citizens and residents**; not available for non-resident aliens

#### (6) Medical Expenses

Medical expenses incurred within **1 year before death**, deductible up to **₱500,000** (Five hundred thousand pesos).

Requirements:
- Must be duly substantiated with official receipts
- Must have been incurred within the 1-year period immediately preceding death

#### (7) Amounts Received Under RA 4917

Any amount received by the heirs from the decedent's employer as a consequence of the death of the decedent-employee in accordance with **Republic Act No. 4917** (providing for the tax exemption of retirement benefits). This amount is deductible from the gross estate.

---

### SUBSECTION B: Nonresident Non-Citizens

For nonresident aliens, the net estate is determined by deducting:

**(1) Standard Deduction**: ₱500,000 (Five hundred thousand pesos)

**(2) Proportional Deduction for Claims/Expenses/Losses/Taxes**:
```
PH Value of Property
---------------------------------  ×  Total Claims/Expenses/Losses/Taxes
Total Gross Estate (worldwide)
```
Only the proportionate amount (Philippine property / total worldwide estate) is deductible.

**(3) Property Previously Taxed**: Same graduated percentage table as Section 86(A)(2), but limited to Philippine-situated property.

**(4) Transfers for Public Use**: Same as Section 86(A)(3), limited to Philippine government.

---

### SUBSECTION C: Share of the Surviving Spouse

The net share of the **surviving spouse** in the conjugal/community partnership property shall be deducted from the net estate of the decedent.

**Computation**:
- Determine total community/conjugal property
- Deduct liabilities chargeable against community/conjugal property
- Net conjugal/community assets / 2 = surviving spouse's share
- This amount is deducted from the gross estate (since it is not the decedent's property)

**Applicable Property Regimes**:
- **Absolute Community of Property (ACP)**: Default for marriages contracted on or after August 3, 1988 (effectivity of Family Code)
- **Conjugal Partnership of Gains (CPG)**: Default for marriages contracted before August 3, 1988
- **Complete Separation**: By prenuptial agreement

*For separation of property regime: no community/conjugal property exists; surviving spouse share = 0.*

---

### SUBSECTION D: Foreign Estate Tax Credit

Estate taxes paid to a foreign country on property of a citizen or resident of the Philippines located in that foreign country are allowed as a credit against the Philippine estate tax.

**Limitations**:
- Maximum credit = (Philippine estate tax) × (Foreign country estate / Total gross estate)
- Cannot exceed total Philippine estate tax
- Must actually be paid and documented

## SEC. 87. Exemptions

The following shall not be taxed:

**(a) Merger of usufruct** — transmission on the death of the usufructuary

**(b) Fiduciary or fideicommissary transmission** — transmission from the fiduciary heir or legatee to the fideicommissary

**(c) Transmission in virtue of a fideicommissary substitution** — made under a will

**(d) Charitable bequests** — all bequests, devises, legacies, or transfers to social welfare, cultural and charitable institutions, **provided that**:
- No part of the net income of the institution inures to the benefit of any individual; AND
- Not more than **30%** of the amount transferred shall be used by the institution for administration purposes

---

## SEC. 88. Determination of the Value of the Estate

**(A) Usufruct and Annuities**
The value of any right of usufruct or of an annuity shall be determined by taking into account the probable life of the beneficiary in accordance with the latest Basic Standard Mortality Table.

**(B) Property**
Property shall be appraised at its **fair market value as of the time of death**.

For real property:
- The value shall be the **higher** of:
  1. The fair market value as determined by the **Commissioner of Internal Revenue** (zonal value); or
  2. The fair market value as shown in the schedule of values fixed by the **Provincial or City Assessors** (assessed value)

**Note for Engine**: The engine takes FMV as a user-provided input. The engine does NOT determine FMV (no zonal value lookup required).

## SEC. 90. Estate Tax Returns

### (A) Requirements

The executor, or the administrator, or any of the legal heirs of the decedent shall file in all cases of transfer subject to the tax imposed by Section 84, or where though exempt from tax, the gross estate of the decedent consists of registered or registrable property (real property, motor vehicle, shares of stock, etc.) for which BIR clearance is required for transfer.

**CPA Certification**: If gross estate **exceeds ₱5,000,000**, the return shall be accompanied by a statement duly certified to by a Certified Public Accountant (CPA) containing a detailed listing of assets, deductions, and tax due.

**Contents of Return**:
1. The value of the gross estate of the decedent at the time of his death, or in case of a nonresident, of that part of his gross estate situated in the Philippines
2. The deductions allowed from gross estate
3. Such part of such information as may at the time be ascertainable and such supplemental data as may be necessary to establish the correct taxes

### (B) Time for Filing

Within **one (1) year** from the decedent's date of death.

### (C) Extension

The Commissioner of Internal Revenue may grant a **reasonable extension**, not exceeding **30 days**, for filing the return in meritorious cases.

### (D) Place of Filing

With any **Authorized Agent Bank (AAB)** of the Revenue District Office (RDO) having jurisdiction over the place of domicile of the decedent at the time of his death.

If the decedent has no legal residence in the Philippines: file with the Office of the Commissioner (Revenue District Office No. 39, South Quezon City).

---

## SEC. 91. Payment of Tax

### (A) Time of Payment

The estate tax imposed shall be paid **at the time the return is filed**.

### (B) Extension of Time

The Commissioner may extend the time for payment of the estate tax, subject to interest, if:
- The estate is **settled through the courts (judicial settlement)**: up to **5 years**
- The estate is settled **extrajudicially**: up to **2 years**

### (C) Installment Payment

Payment by installment shall be allowed within **2 years** from the statutory date of its due date **without civil penalty and interest**. However, if any installment is not paid on the due date, the whole unpaid balance shall become due and payable together with penalty.

### (D) Liability for Payment

The executor or administrator is primarily liable. Beneficiaries are secondarily liable to the extent of their distributive share.

---

## SEC. 92. Discharge of Executor or Administrator from Personal Liability

After payment of the tax, the executor or administrator, upon application, shall be discharged from personal liability for any deficiency in the estate tax thereafter found to be due, if the Commissioner had been notified within the required period.

---

## SEC. 93. Definition of "Deficiency"

"Deficiency" means the amount by which the tax imposed by this chapter exceeds the amount shown as the tax by the taxpayer upon his return.

---

## SEC. 94. Requirement of Judicial Authorization

No judge shall authorize the executor or judicial administrator to deliver any distributive share to any party interested in the estate unless a certification from the Commissioner of Internal Revenue that the estate tax has been paid is shown.

---

## SEC. 95. Duties of Certain Officers and Debtors

Registers of Deeds shall not transfer properties without BIR clearance (Certificate Authorizing Registration). Banks and other institutions holding assets of the decedent shall not allow withdrawal or transfer without BIR authorization (except where subject to final withholding tax).

---

## SEC. 96. Restitution of Tax Upon Satisfaction of Outstanding Obligations

Where a claim against the estate is subsequently found to have been overstated, the Commissioner may allow restitution.

---

## SEC. 97. Payment of Tax Antecedent to the Transfer of Shares, Bonds, or Rights

No corporation shall register any transfer of shares of stock, bonds, or rights unless a certification has been secured from the BIR that the tax has been paid.
