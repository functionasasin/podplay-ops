# LTO/LTFRB Route Rationalization Studies — Planned vs Actual Route Changes

**Source type**: Official government documents, news reporting, academic analysis
**Retrieved**: 2026-02-25
**Confidence**: Medium — study documents not publicly released; planned vs actual gap is well-documented but specific route lists require eFOI

---

## Summary

The LTFRB route rationalization program is the regulatory backbone of PUV Modernization. It restructures Metro Manila's route network from a historically overcrowded, overlapping, supply-led system into a planned, demand-driven network. As of early 2026:

- **Bus rationalization**: Fully implemented (MC 2020-019), then progressively expanded; 31 planned routes grew to 68 local bus routes by Nov 2024 due to demand pressure
- **Jeepney/UV Express rationalization**: Planned but not complete — the Greater Metro Manila (MUCEP area) route rationalization study was commissioned but remains unpublished; deadline pushed to end-2026
- **LPTRP status**: Only ~9.82% of LGUs nationwide have approved Local Public Transport Route Plans (LPTRPs); NCR is particularly behind as route planning was devolved to LGUs who lack capacity
- **Gap**: The formal route rationalization plan for NCR jeepney and UV Express routes does not exist as a public document

---

## 1. Background: What Route Rationalization Means

Before the pandemic, Metro Manila had:
- **900+ public transport routes** (all modes combined)
- **830+ bus franchises** competing on overlapping routes
- **43,000+ jeepney franchises** with no coordinated scheduling
- Result: severe congestion, route duplication, and inadequate coverage in low-density areas

Route rationalization aims to:
1. **Reduce route overlap** — eliminate redundant parallel routes on the same corridor
2. **Optimize mode by corridor** — BRT/bus on high-demand corridors; jeepneys as feeders
3. **Fix authorized unit counts** — determine how many vehicles each route actually needs
4. **Align with mass transit** — design routes to feed LRT/MRT/PNR stations

- AC: ₱13 for first 5 km + ₱2.20/km

### 2.2 August 2020 Expansion

On August 17, 2020, 4 more city bus routes added — bringing total to 35 rationalized routes. Specific routes not documented in available sources.

### 2.3 2022 Rollback of Rationalization (Demand Pressure)

LTFRB **Memorandum Circular No. 67, series of 2022** (August 19, 2022) reopened many pre-pandemic route franchises in response to the post-pandemic transport shortage. Key changes:

- 39 existing rationalized routes **modified** to include 32 pre-pandemic bus routes
- Only routes "found to be responsive to passenger demand" were retained as rationalized
- Effectively: rationalization was partially reversed because the 31-route network had coverage gaps

Additional changes in 2022:
- **MC 2022-073** (Sep 29): Opened 43 pre-pandemic routes for PUB, PUJ, UV Express in MUCEP area (20 bus, 16 jeepney, 7 UV Express)
- **MC 2022-082** (Dec 15): Modified 7 existing bus routes (effective Dec 26, 2022)
- **MC 2022-083** (Dec 15): Opened 6 new bus routes (effective Dec 26, 2022)

### 2.4 2023 New Routes

January 1, 2023: **9 new bus routes** opened:
- Antipolo–McKinley Hill via FVR Road/C-5
- Antipolo–McKinley Hill via C-6
- McKinley Hill–Southwoods Mall
- Ayala–Southwoods Mall
- Alabang–Naic via Governor's Drive
- Cubao–Dasmariñas via Governor's Drive
- 3 others not documented in available sources

**June 2023**: New PNR augmentation routes announced for areas affected by North-South Commuter Railway construction:
- Route 1: Bus route serving affected PNR stations (Alabang corridor)
- Route 2: Bus route serving affected PNR stations (FTI/Taguig corridor)
- Plus 1 jeepney augmentation route

**August 2023**: Modifications to pre-pandemic routes via Board Resolution No. 55 and No. 57, series of 2023.

### 2.5 Current State (as of Nov 2024)

**68 local bus routes** franchised in Metro Manila — more than double the 31 planned routes from 2020. This reflects:
- Demand pressure overriding rationalization goals
- Coverage gaps in the 31-route plan exposed by actual commuter needs
- Restoration of many pre-pandemic routes as provisional authority

## 3. Jeepney/UV Express Rationalization — Incomplete

### 3.1 The MUCEP Route Rationalization Study

The formal route rationalization study for jeepney and UV Express routes in the MUCEP area (Greater Metro Manila) was commissioned to **Transport and Traffic Planners Inc. (TTPI)** — but has **not been publicly released** as of early 2026.

The study scope:
- **Area**: NCR + adjacent provinces (Bulacan, Rizal, Laguna, Cavite)
- **Purpose**: Identify rationalized routes by priority — Immediate, Medium Term, Long Term
- **Method**: Transport modeling + public transport surveys + capacity building
- **Status**: Ongoing/incomplete as of 2024; new deadline for route rationalization set at **end of 2026**

### 3.2 LPTRP (Local Public Transport Route Plan) Process

Under the PUVMP regulatory framework, **route planning was devolved to LGUs**. Each LGU must submit an LPTRP to LTFRB:

| LPTRP Metric | Value | Source |
|---|---|---|
| LGUs nationwide with approved LPTRP | ~9.82% of 1,600 LGUs | LTFRB data, Jan 2024 |
| NCR-specific LPTRP completions | Unknown (not publicly listed) | — |
| Approval deadline | Ongoing; route rationalization target end-2026 | DOTr announcement |
| Consequence of no LPTRP | Routes remain under franchise-as-applied system | — |

**NCR-specific challenge**: Metro Manila is governed by 17 LGUs (16 cities + Pateros). Inter-municipal/inter-city routes require DOTr coordination, not just individual LGU plans. The DOTr has not completed the overarching NCR route rationalization plan as of 2024.

### 3.3 Planned vs Actual — The Gap

| Dimension | Planned | Actual |
|-----------|---------|--------|
| Bus routes (city) | 31 (MC 2020-019) | 68 (Nov 2024) |
| Jeepney routes (NCR) | Not yet finalized | ~875–950 active routes (555 consolidated + provisional) |
| UV Express routes (NCR) | Not yet finalized | ~250 active routes (142 consolidated + provisional) |
| LPTRP completion (NCR LGUs) | 17 LGUs by end-2026 | Unknown; most likely incomplete |
| Route rationalization finalization | End-2025 (50%), End-2026 (100%) | Target; actual progress unclear |

### 3.4 Known Outcomes of Rationalization (Jeepney)

What is clear from available data:
- **555 jeepney routes** in NCR had consolidated transport entities by Dec 31, 2023
- **395 jeepney routes** in NCR had NO consolidated entity at that date
- Routes with no TSE received **special permits or provisional authority** to continue operating
- The government effectively allows unconsolidated routes to continue while rationalization catches up
- **28 additional jeepney routes** in Manila, Parañaque, Makati, Pasay, Marikina, Pasig, QC opened in Dec 2022 (MC 2022-084)

## 5. Structural Gap Analysis

### Why Rationalization Is Incomplete

1. **NCR inter-city coordination**: No single LGU controls inter-municipal jeepney routes; DOTr hasn't finalized the overarching plan
2. **LTFRB capacity**: Reviewing ~900+ NCR jeepney routes plus ~250 UV Express routes requires significant resources
3. **Legal challenges**: PISTON and other transport groups have filed multiple SC petitions; SC has not yet issued TRO but is monitoring
4. **Political opposition**: Senate Resolution 1096 (July 2024) — 22 of 23 senators signed call for suspension
5. **COVID disruption**: Pandemic delayed studies that were underway in 2019-2020
6. **Financing gap**: ₱5B in outstanding DBP PASADA loans; operators can't buy modern vehicles, hampering TSE formation
7. **LGU capacity**: Many NCR LGUs do not have the technical staff to write LPTRPs; only 9.82% nationwide have completed plans

### Corridors Most Affected by Planning Gap

| Corridor | Issue |
|----------|-------|
| Commonwealth Ave (QC) | High route density; rationalization underway but incomplete |
| España-Sampaloc (Manila) | University belt routes: multiple overlapping jeepney routes |
| EDSA (full) | Bus rationalization complete (carousel); but feeder jeepneys unclear |
| C5 Road | Some rationalized P2P/bus; most jeepney routes unratified |
| Quezon City inner streets | Many local jeepney routes with no LPTRP approval |

## 6. GTFS Implications

| Finding | GTFS Impact |
|---------|------------|
| Bus routes documented (31+32 pre-pandemic+others) | Can produce routes.txt for ~68 bus routes with medium confidence |
| Rationalized bus route boundaries well-defined | Can estimate stop locations and route shapes |
| Jeepney rationalization incomplete | Cannot rely on official rationalized route list; must use pre-pandemic routes |
| UV Express rationalization incomplete | Same as jeepney — use franchise data from earlier analysis |
| LPTRP data not public | No official route geometry or stop data available from rationalization study |
| MUCEP study not released | Cannot access planned route network data |

**Recommended approach for GTFS**: Use the franchise/franchise data already collected (ltfrb-jeepney, ltfrb-bus, ltfrb-uv-express raw files) rather than waiting for rationalization study to be published. Cross-reference with Sakay.ph and OSM.
