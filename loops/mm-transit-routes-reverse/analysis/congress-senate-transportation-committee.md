# Congress/Senate Transportation Committee — Metro Manila Transit Routes

**Source type**: Government legislative records
**Date analyzed**: 2026-02-25
**Data quality**: Medium — aggregate statistics from hearings; no official route list published

---

## Key Committees

### Senate
- **Senate Committee on Public Services** — primary committee for LTFRB/DOTr oversight; chaired historically by Sen. Grace Poe, then Sen. Robin Padilla
- Conducts hearings on PUVMP/PTMP implementation, route rationalization, fare adjustments

### House of Representatives
- **House Committee on Transportation** — primary House committee for transportation legislation; conducted inquiry into LTFRB corruption (Jan 2024)
- **House Committee on Metro Manila Development** — secondary, handles MMDA-related transport matters

### Route Rationalization Plans (National)
- LGUs required to submit Local Public Transport Route Plans (LPTRP)
- Only **139 of 1,575 LGU route plans** had been approved as of 2024 (8.8% completion)
- NCR LGU plans: very few approved; DOTr targeting completion by end-2026

### LTFRB Franchise Scale (often cited in hearings)
- Over **43,000 jeepney franchises** issued in Metro Manila across 900+ routes
- Over **830 bus franchises** in Metro Manila prior to pandemic consolidation (reduced to ~68 city bus routes post-rationalization)
- ~9,700 jeepneys in NCR without valid franchise operating as colorum (as of 2018 data cited in 2020 policy brief)

## Key Legislative Actions Affecting Routes

### Senate Resolution 1096 (July 2024)
- Signed by **22 of 23 senators**
- Called for temporary suspension of the Public Transport Modernization Program (PTMP)
- Key concerns: high cost of modern PUVs, inadequate route plans, financial burden on operators
- DOTr/LTFRB declined to halt program ("as long as no presidential directive")
- In response, LTFRB reopened consolidation applications until Nov 29, 2024

### House Committee Inquiry (January 2024)
- Investigated corruption allegations in LTFRB route franchise issuance
- LTFRB Chair Teofilo Guadiz III was subsequently suspended over franchise-related corruption
- Hearing surfaced evidence that LTFRB "was not prepared for PUVMP implementation"

### COA Findings Presented to Congress
The Commission on Audit flagged LTFRB for three incomplete PUVMP projects:
1. **P319-million public information center** — not completed
2. **P45-million Metro Manila route rationalization study** — not completed (the MUCEP/TTPI study)
3. **P28-million inter-provincial route rationalization study** — not completed

These COA findings confirm that the comprehensive Metro Manila route rationalization study was commissioned but **never delivered**, which is why no official, complete route map exists for NCR jeepneys.

### Senate Bill on Jeepney Modernization (18th Congress)
- Jeepneys + buses account for **70% of all person-trips in Metro Manila**
- Proposed engine-upgrade-only approach (Euro 4 compliance) vs full vehicle replacement
- Government assistance: ≥10% of unit price + ≤4% annual interest over ≥15 years

## Relevant Published Research (CPBRD)

The Congressional Policy and Budget Research Department published:

### Policy Brief 2020-02: PUV Modernization
- 180,000 PUJ units nationwide; 90% are 15+ years old (2018 data)
- Jeepneys contribute 7% of all transport-related GHG emissions in Metro Manila
- Large gap between valid franchises and actual operating units

### Facts & Figures 2024-23: Traffic Congestion in Metro Manila
- Metro Manila has longest average travel time for 10km trip globally: 25m30s (Cubao-Makati benchmark)
- 387 cities in 55 countries ranked; Manila is worst performer
- New vehicle registrations fell 11.5% in 2023 to 613,100 units

### Discussion Paper 2025-06: Philippine Rail Transport System
- Metro Manila ranked 60th of 65 economies in transit quality (5th worst globally)
- JICA estimate: traffic congestion costs ₱3.5B/day now → ₱5.4B/day (₱1.97T/year) by 2035 if unaddressed
- Rail passenger recovery: 100.4M (2020) → 216.7M (2022) → 296.7M (2023)

## Data Utility for GTFS

**Useful for**:
- Confirming total route counts (NCR: ~950 jeepney routes, ~250 UV Express routes, ~68 city bus routes)
- Understanding policy gaps that explain why route data is fragmented
- Cross-referencing that the P45M Metro Manila route rationalization study exists but is unpublished

**Not useful for**:
- Specific route names, endpoints, or codes (none published)
- Route geometry, stop lists, or fare structure
- Any machine-readable data

**Key gap explained**: The COA-flagged P45M Metro Manila route rationalization study would have been the authoritative route database. Its non-delivery is the root cause of the data vacuum this loop is trying to fill.
