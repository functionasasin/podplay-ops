**Aspect**: Specialized Data — PNR commuter rail feeder routes
**Date analyzed**: 2026-02-28
**Method**: Web research via search and article scraping

---

## Key Finding: PNR Metro Commuter Line is SUSPENDED

The **PNR Metro Commuter Line** suspended all Metro Manila operations on **March 28, 2024** to give way for the North–South Commuter Railway (NSCR) construction. Suspension expected to last ~5 years (until ~2028–2029). An estimated **30,000 daily commuters** were affected.

This means feeder routes are primarily documented as:
1. **Historical feeder routes** (pre-March 2024) — which routes connected to which PNR stations
2. **LTFRB replacement routes** (post-July 2023) — formal augmentation routes mandated to replace PNR service

## LTFRB Replacement Routes (Official, Post-Closure)

Per **LTFRB Memorandum Circular 2023-020**, three replacement routes were opened to serve commuters displaced by PNR station closures:

### Route 1: FTI–Divisoria via East Service Road
- **Mode**: Public Utility Bus (PUB)
- **Units allocated**: 30
- **Path**: FTI → East Service Road → SLEX → Bicutan → Nichols → Osmeña Highway → Quirino Avenue → Legarda Street → Recto Avenue → Abad Santos Avenue → Mayhaligue Street → Divisoria (Tutuban)
- **Coverage**: Serves former FTI, Nichols, EDSA, Buendia/Dela Rosa, Vito Cruz, Paco areas
- **Confidence**: High (official LTFRB announcement)

### Route 2: Alabang (Starmall)–Divisoria via SLEX
- **Mode**: Public Utility Bus (PUB)
- **Units allocated**: 25
- **Path**: Starmall Alabang → Manila South Road → East Service Road → SLEX → Bicutan → same path as Route 1 → Divisoria
- **Coverage**: Serves former Alabang, Sucat, Bicutan areas; longest replacement corridor
- **Confidence**: High (official LTFRB announcement)

### Route 3: Malabon–Divisoria
- **Mode**: Modern Public Utility Jeepney (MPUJ)
- **Units allocated**: 5
- **Path**: Malabon → (via Malabon-Caloocan area) → Divisoria (Tutuban)
- **Coverage**: Serves former Gov. Pascual, Sangandaan, Asistio north section
- **Notes**: Only 5 units, likely severely under-serving the ~5,000 north section commuters; likely using Rizal Avenue extension or Abad Santos corridor
- **Confidence**: High (official LTFRB announcement)

## Pre-Existing Feeder Routes (Historically Documented)

### North Section Feeders
- **Balut–Divisoria** via Abad Santos — passes through Tondo/Malabon corridor near Solis/5th Ave stations
- **Sangandaan–Divisoria** via Abad Santos — direct feeder for north section stations
- **Gasak–Divisoria** via Abad Santos — Caloocan-to-Manila feeder

### Central Section (España/Sampaloc) Feeders
- **Quiapo–Divisoria–North Harbor** — lateral connector near Tutuban
- **Baclaran–Divisoria** via Taft Avenue — long north-south spine
- **Cubao–Divisoria** via Aurora Blvd/Ramon Magsaysay/Legarda/CM Recto — key Cubao connector
- **Gastambide–Divisoria** via Morayta/CM Recto/Soler — University Belt connector
- Multiple university-area jeepneys from España, UST, FEU areas feeding España station
- **Florida/Victory Liner/Sampaloc Bus Terminal** buses at España station

### Vito Cruz / Paco / San Andres Feeders
- **CCP Complex–Taft Ave via Vito Cruz** — connects Manila Bay area
- **Monumento–Vito Cruz via Rizal Ave/Mabini** — long-haul north-south feeder
- **Monumento–Vito Cruz via Rizal Ave/Taft Ave** — alternate path
- **Route 309: Nichols–Vito Cruz LRT** — South to Vito Cruz corridor
- **T3111: Kamuning–Vito Cruz via E. Rodriguez/A. Mabini** — QC to Manila
- Buses on Osmeña Highway near Vito Cruz station

### FTI/Taguig Feeders
- **Route 410: Guadalupe–FTI via JP Rizal** — Makati to FTI
- **Guadalupe–FTI via JP Rizal Ext. C5** — Makati/Mandaluyong to FTI
- Jeepneys serving: Arca South, Bayani Road, FTI complex, Heritage Park, Libingan ng mga Bayani, Merville, Sunshine Mall Plaza

### Alabang/South Feeders
- **Pasay–Alabang** jeepneys via service road
- **Sucat–Alabang** jeepneys
- **Bicutan–Alabang** jeepneys
- **Zapote–Alabang** to Las Piñas
- **Baclaran–Alabang** via Coastal/Roxas
- **Moonwalk–Alabang**
- **SM Southmall–Alabang**
- UV Express and P2P from Alabang terminal to various QC/Makati/BGC destinations

## Data Quality Assessment

- **Completeness**: Medium — LTFRB replacement routes well-documented; pre-existing feeders documented at major stations but thin for intermediate stations (Laon Laan, Paco, San Andres, Pandacan, Nichols, Sucat)
- **Currency**: High — PNR status (suspended March 28, 2024) is current and confirmed
- **Sources**: GMA News, Inquirer, PNA, LTFRB MC 2023-020, commutetour.com, Wikipedia
- **Geometry**: None available for feeder routes specifically; would need to be derived from corridor research

---

## Key Gaps

1. **No specific stop lists** for LTFRB replacement routes (Alabang-Divisoria, FTI-Divisoria)
2. **North section feeders** are poorly documented beyond the 5-MPUJ Malabon-Divisoria route — likely severely under-served
3. **Intermediate stations** (Laon Laan, San Andres, Nichols) have minimal documented feeder data
4. **Post-suspension demand shifts** — which jeepney routes saw ridership spikes after PNR closure?
5. **NSCR station locations** — the future NSCR will have different station locations than PNR, affecting future feeder planning
6. PNR Buendia was **already closed in 2017** before the full 2024 suspension; Dela Rosa station replaced it

---

## Implications for GTFS

- PNR itself is **not operational** and should not be included in the current GTFS as an active service
- The 3 LTFRB replacement routes should be flagged as "PNR replacement" and included in the bus/jeepney route databases
- The PNR alignment geometry (south line: Tutuban–Alabang, ~35 km) should be retained for NSCR planning context
- Feeder routes documented above should inform the Wave 2 corridor validation (especially EDSA, Taft/Osmeña, East Service Road corridors)
