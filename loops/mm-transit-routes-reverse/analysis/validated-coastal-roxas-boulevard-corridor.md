## Corridor Geography

The **Coastal/Roxas Boulevard corridor** is Metro Manila's western seafront axis, running ~22 km from Navotas/Caloocan in the north to PITX/Parañaque in the south. Unlike the EDSA or Commonwealth corridors, it is not a single continuous road but a chain of aligned arterials:

```
Navotas / Caloocan (North Harbor, R-10)
        ↓ Radial Road 10 (R-10) / Bonifacio Drive / North Harbor
Divisoria / Port Area / Intramuros
        ↓ Roxas Blvd begins at Rizal Park (Luneta)
Luneta / Rizal Park (Manila)
        ↓ Roxas Boulevard (Manila Bay seafront)
Malate / Ermita (tourist belt; US Embassy, Manila Hotel area)
        ↓
Quirino Grandstand / CCP Complex
        ↓ Roxas Blvd continues south-southwest
CCP Complex / Roxas Blvd junction with Buendia (Taft Ave begins)
        ↓ Road bends toward Pasay; meets EDSA at Heritage Hotel
Heritage Hotel / Roxas Blvd Pasay (EDSA Carousel stop)
        ↓ Becomes Diosdado Macapagal Boulevard
SM Mall of Asia (MOA) / Bay City, Pasay
        ↓
DFA – Office of Consular Affairs, Pasay
        ↓
Ayala Malls Manila Bay / ASEANA, Parañaque
        ↓
City of Dreams / Entertainment City, Parañaque
        ↓ Coastal Road (R-1 Expressway)
PITX (Parañaque Integrated Terminal Exchange)
        ↓ Coastal Road continues south to Cavite
```

**Key sub-segments:**
- **Roxas Blvd North (Luneta → CCP)**: ~3.5 km; tourism-oriented; Manila Hotel, US Embassy, Manila Bay Rehabilitation waterfront promenade (2019–present); no dedicated bus lanes
- **Roxas Blvd South (CCP → Heritage Hotel/Taft)**: ~2.5 km; connects Malate/Ermita to EDSA junction; EDSA Carousel's Heritage Hotel stop is at this junction
- **Diosdado Macapagal Blvd (Heritage → DFA)**: ~4 km; Bay City development; SM MOA (major transit attractor)
- **Coastal Road / R-1 Expressway (DFA → PITX)**: ~5 km; Parañaque; limited-access elevated expressway sections; PITX as southern terminus

**Key transit nodes:**
1. **Luneta / Rizal Park** — nominal northern terminus of Roxas Blvd; LRT-1 United Nations Ave Station ~600 m east
2. **CCP Complex** — Cultural Center; junction with Buendia/Taft Ave; small jeepney turnaround
3. **Heritage Hotel / Roxas Blvd** — EDSA Carousel curbside stop (northbound/southbound); crossroads between Roxas Blvd, EDSA, and Taft Ave; EDSA-Taft/Pasay Rotonda ~500 m east
4. **SM Mall of Asia (MOA)** — largest destination on Diosdado Macapagal Blvd; major jeepney and UV Express hub; coordinates: 14.5356°N, 120.9832°E
5. **DFA / Macapagal Blvd** — government services node; EDSA Carousel stop
6. **Ayala Malls Manila Bay / ASEANA** — new retail district; EDSA Carousel stop; LRT-1 Redemptorist-Aseana feeder
7. **City of Dreams** — entertainment complex; EDSA Carousel stop
8. **PITX** — Parañaque Integrated Terminal Exchange; LRT-1 PITX Station (Cavite Extension, Nov 2024); EDSA Carousel southern terminus; provincial bus hub

**Critical observation on Route 1 (EDSA Carousel)**: The southernmost segment of the EDSA Carousel — from Taft/Pasay Rotonda to PITX — uses **Roxas Boulevard / Diosdado Macapagal Blvd / Coastal Road**, not EDSA. The southbound stops Heritage Hotel/Roxas Blvd Eastbound → SM MOA → DFA → Ayala Malls/ASEANA → City of Dreams → PITX are all on this coastal alignment. This makes the EDSA Carousel both an EDSA corridor route AND a key coastal corridor route. Frequency: peak 2–5 min headway; off-peak 5–15 min. Fare: ₱15 minimum, ₱75.50 maximum.

**Route 23 vs Route 24 conflict**: Route 23 uses Coastal Road for the Lawton→Alabang segment; Route 24 (Lawton→Alabang via SLEx) uses the expressway instead. Both are confirmed HIGH confidence with distinct alignments. Route 23 is the only bus route that uses Roxas Blvd proper as a through-route (not just the MOA/DFA section).

**Route 44 alignment uncertainty**: OSM and ltoportal confirm Navotas→Alabang "via Sucat Road, Roxas Blvd" but the exact Manila routing is unclear. Likely: Navotas → North Harbor / Divisoria → Roxas Blvd → Ermita → Pasay → Coastal Road → Sucat → Alabang. The route is the inverse of T429 (Alabang→Baclaran via Coastal Road) extended to Navotas. The Roxas Blvd segment confirms this corridor is used by a long-distance bus.

## Key Gaps

1. **Jeepney routes along Roxas Blvd north (Luneta → CCP section)**: Sparse data. The tourist belt section of Roxas Blvd (Manila Hotel, US Embassy area) has informal jeepney service, but no T-codes or confirmed LTFRB routes were found specifically on this stretch. The "Quiapo–Pier" loop route (other-major-terminals analysis) passes near Luneta but is primarily an Intramuros/harbor-focused route.

2. **CCP Complex feeder**: A jeepney reportedly connects the CCP Complex to Vito Cruz LRT-1 station, documented in lrt1-feeder-routes analysis, but no T-code, LTFRB franchise, or Sakay ID was found for this specific route.

3. **Route 44 exact alignment**: While Route 44 (Navotas→Alabang) is confirmed to use "Sucat Road, Roxas Blvd," the specific Manila-side routing — whether it uses Roxas Blvd proper or an adjacent parallel road — is not confirmed in source data. Could be Roxas Blvd, Taft Ave, or A. Mabini.

4. **Coastal Road expressway segment**: The R-1 Expressway from MOA to PITX has limited-access elevated sections. Jeepney service on the expressway proper is restricted; buses (Route 23, EDSA Carousel) use it but jeepneys must use surface roads. The distinction matters for stop placement.

5. **Night service**: No documented jeepney or city bus service specifically on Roxas Blvd north section (Luneta→CCP) after 10 PM. The EDSA Carousel (limited overnight) serves the southern Coastal Road (PITX–Taft section) but not the Luneta/Malate waterfront section.

6. **MOA internal circulation**: SM Mall of Asia has a large complex with multiple entrances and an internal bus bay. The exact stops for jeepney routes within MOA (vs. at the perimeter roads) are not documented.

7. **Bay City development transit needs**: Multiple ongoing reclamation and development projects in Bay City (Aseana, Ayala Manila Bay, Entertainment City) generate new transit demand corridors not yet served by documented LTFRB routes.

8. **Baclaran-UV-Alabang stop list**: While confirmed as operating via Coastal Road, intermediate stops (if any) between Baclaran and Alabang are unknown. Described as non-stop express but this is from a single Moovit source.

**Total routes identified**: 22 (11 HIGH + 8 MEDIUM + 3 LOW/ORPHAN)

**Corridor character**: This corridor is **less dense in traditional jeepney service** compared to EDSA, Taft Ave, or Commonwealth, for several structural reasons:
- Roxas Blvd north (Luneta→CCP) is a scenic boulevard with limited residential density; most commuter O/D pairs bypass it via Taft Ave (one block east)
- The Diosdado Macapagal / Coastal Road section (MOA→PITX) is a relatively new urban area (Bay City development) with fewer traditional jeepney routes; served primarily by BRT (EDSA Carousel) and UV Express
- PITX absorbs terminal functions that formerly scattered across Pasay/Baclaran, consolidating demand onto express services (P2P, UV Express) rather than traditional jeepneys
- The Coastal Road (R-1) has expressway restrictions on part of its length, limiting traditional PUJ access

**Primary transit function**: Long-distance bypass for north-south travel avoiding EDSA congestion (Route 23, Route 44, Monumento–PITX via R-10), plus MOA/Bay City access feeder (Baclaran–MOA jeepney, Pasay Rotonda–MOA, UV Express Festival Alabang→MOA).
