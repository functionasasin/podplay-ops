# LTFRB Modernization Program — Modern Jeepney Routes, Consolidated Routes, New Franchises

**Source type**: Official government + news reporting
**Retrieved**: 2026-02-25
**Confidence**: Medium (program is active/evolving; route rationalization not yet complete; full modern PUJ route list not publicly available as single document)

---

## Summary

The **Public Utility Vehicle Modernization Program (PUVMP)**, launched in 2017 and renamed **Public Transport Modernization Program (PTMP)** in 2024, is a comprehensive overhaul of Metro Manila's jeepney and UV Express fleet and route network. It has two components:

1. **Fleet modernization**: Replace traditional jeepneys with Euro 4+ compliant modern PUVs operated by cooperatives/corporations.
2. **Route rationalization**: Redesign the entire route network via LGU-submitted Local Public Transport Route Plans (LPTRPs) and LTFRB route rationalization studies.

As of early 2026:
- **~97% of registered PUJ units in NCR** have consolidated under a Transport Service Entity (TSE) — cooperative or corporation
- **Route rationalization is ongoing** — target 50% of routes rationalized by end-2025, remainder by end-2026
- **48 modern PUJ routes** were authorized by late 2020 (845 units); growth after 2020 is not well-documented publicly
- The complete current list of modern PUJ routes is **not available in a single public document** — full data requires LTFRB eFOI request

## Vehicle Standards (Modern PUJ Requirements)

Modern PUVs must comply with the **Omnibus Franchising Guidelines (OFG)** and **Philippine National Standards (PNS)**:

- **Engine**: Euro 4 minimum (or fully electric); some units Euro 5
- **Capacity**: Higher than traditional jeepney; can accommodate standing passengers
- **Safety**: CCTV cameras, dashcam, speed limiter
- **Navigation**: GPS fleet management system
- **Payment**: Automatic Fare Collection System (AFCS) — cashless capability required
- **Connectivity**: Wi-Fi (many routes)
- **Accessibility**: Higher ceiling; handrails

Traditional jeepneys are NOT required to be replaced immediately but must be operated under a TSE with a valid franchise.

| National PUV consolidation rate (by routes) | 73.71% (7,019 routes) | Apr 2024 |

**Key implication**: While nearly all *active* PUJ units in NCR consolidated, many routes (395 out of ~950) lacked a TSE — meaning those routes were served by provisional authority or special permits only.

**TSE minimum threshold**: 15 franchises required to form a cooperative or corporation.

**CPC validity**: 7 years for consolidated TSEs deploying brand-new compliant vehicles.

## Route Rationalization

### Framework

Route rationalization is driven by **LGUs** through their **Local Public Transport Route Plan (LPTRP)**. LGUs submit route plans to the LTFRB, which then franchises TSEs on those routes.

- **MUCEP Area**: Metro Manila + adjacent areas of Region 3 (Central Luzon) and Region 4A (CALABARZON) — the study area for route rationalization
- **Rationalization study**: Determines appropriate mode, vehicle count, and service characteristics per corridor
- **Target**: 50% of routes rationalized by end-2025; remainder by end-2026

### Status (as of late 2025 / early 2026)

Route rationalization is slow due to:
- Limited DOTr/LTFRB resources
- Required coordination with 17 LGUs in NCR
- Outstanding loan issues (~₱5B still outstanding from DBP PASADA program)
- Legal challenges from PISTON and other transport groups (SC not yet issued TRO)
- Senate Resolution 1096 (Jul 2024) calling for program suspension (22 of 23 senators signed)

A **special government committee formed in April 2025** is reviewing the PTMP to expedite implementation.
