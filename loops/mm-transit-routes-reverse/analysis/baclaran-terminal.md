## Terminal Overview

Baclaran is one of Metro Manila's highest-volume transit hubs, straddling the border of **Pasay City** and **Parañaque City** at the southern end of **Taft Avenue**. The terminal cluster anchors the southwestern axis of NCR, connecting Manila's inner districts with Las Piñas, Parañaque, Muntinlupa, and Cavite province.

**Historical significance**: Baclaran LRT-1 Station was the line's **southern terminus from 1984 until November 16, 2024**, when Phase 1 of the LRT-1 Cavite Extension opened. The 5 new stations — Redemptorist-Aseana, Ninoy Aquino Avenue, PITX, MIA Road, and Dr. Santos — extended the line further south into Parañaque. The current southern terminus is **Dr. Santos Station**. Baclaran is no longer a terminus station but remains a major ridership node.

**Key crowd generators**:
- **Baclaran Church** (National Shrine of Our Mother of Perpetual Help / Redemptorist) — Wednesday devotions draw hundreds of thousands, creating one of Metro Manila's highest single-day transit demand spikes.
- **Baclaran Market** (tiangge / dry goods) — one of the largest street markets in NCR, anchoring daytime jeepney demand.
- **NAIA proximity** — ~2 km from the terminal cluster; significant feeder traffic.

**Distinct terminal zones at Baclaran:**
1. **Baclaran LRT-1 Station area** — Taft Avenue, Pasay side; main multi-modal hub for northbound routes (Taft Ave–Divisoria, Taft Ave–Blumentritt, Taft Ave–Navotas)
2. **Baclaran Church / Redemptorist area** — near Lydia's Lechon and Max's Baclaran; new Redemptorist-Aseana LRT station adjacent
3. **Roxas Boulevard / MIA Road corner** — departure point for Cavite-bound jeepneys; also MOA-bound routes via Coastal Road
4. **Street-side terminals on F.B. Harrison St. / Quirino Ave** — southbound Parañaque suburban routes (Sucat, Moonwalk, Multinational Village)

**Rail connections**:
- **LRT-1 Baclaran Station** (Line 1, non-terminus since Nov 2024) — connects to all 25 LRT-1 stations from FPJ/Roosevelt to Dr. Santos; new fare ₱15 min / ₱43 max (stored value), ₱15–₱45 single journey; schedule 4:30 AM–10:00 PM (Mon–Fri)
- **LRT-1 Redemptorist-Aseana Station** (adjacent, new as of Nov 2024) — next stop southward on Cavite Extension; free modern jeepney rides to Ayala Malls Manila Bay from here
- **EDSA Carousel**: nearest stop is **Roxas Blvd (Heritage Hotel)**, approximately 1 km from Baclaran LRT; not directly at Baclaran but within walking distance

## PITX Relationship

The **Parañaque Integrated Terminal Exchange (PITX)** opened November 5, 2018, approximately **4 km south** of Baclaran along the Cavite-bound corridor. PITX absorbed most provincial bus terminals formerly at Baclaran/Pasay for Cavite and Batangas routes. Operators now at PITX include **DLTB, Ceres Liner, JAM, JAC Liner, and other Cavite/Batangas operators** that previously stopped at or near Baclaran.

Several jeepneys now operate as **feeders between Baclaran and PITX**, and the LRT-1 Cavite Extension now has a PITX station (one stop beyond MIA Road), further integrating the corridor.

## Geographic Notes

**Key road corridors from Baclaran:**
- **Taft Avenue** (northbound): Main trunk to Pasay, Gil Puyat, Vito Cruz, Buendia, Manila (Lawton, Divisoria, Blumentritt, Navotas)
- **A. Mabini / M.H. del Pilar** (northbound): Alternate Manila routes (Ermita, Quiapo, Blumentritt variants)
- **Roxas Boulevard** (northbound): Route to SM MOA, then continuing to Manila Bay, Luneta, Intramuros
- **Ninoy Aquino / MIA Road** (southbound): To NAIA, PITX, southern Parañaque
- **Quirino Avenue** (southbound/inland): To Sucat, Moonwalk, Multinational Village
- **Coastal Road / R-1 Expressway** (south): To MOA/Bay City, then Cavite via Coastal Road
- **Aguinaldo Highway** (south via Zapote): To Bacoor, Imus, Dasmariñas, Tagaytay, Trece Martires

**Key destinations served from Baclaran:**
- **Northbound** (city-bound): Divisoria, Blumentritt, Recto, Quiapo, Dapitan, Retiro, Navotas, P. Campa
- **Inner south**: Sucat (SSH), Moonwalk, Multinational Village, NAIA/Baltao
- **Southwest**: SM MOA/Bay City (Coastal Rd)
- **South (Cavite)**: Zapote, Bacoor, Imus, Dasmariñas, Paliparan, Tagaytay, Trece Martires
- **Major hubs**: PITX (~4 km), MRT Taft (~1 km via EDSA Carousel), Pasay Rotonda (~0.5 km)
