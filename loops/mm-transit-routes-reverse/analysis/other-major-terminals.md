## Terminal Overview

This aspect covers five major transit nodes in Manila's inner districts — the dense historic core north and east of Intramuros. These terminals collectively serve as the primary commute spine between the northern districts (Tondo, Caloocan, Valenzuela) and the southern (Pasay, Parañaque) and eastern (Cubao, Pasig, Marikina) parts of Metro Manila.

| Terminal | District | Type | Dominant corridors |
|----------|----------|------|--------------------|
| Divisoria | Tondo/Binondo/San Nicolas | Street-level jeepney node | North Harbor, Navotas, Valenzuela, Baclaran |
| Quiapo | Quiapo | Quezon Blvd jeepney hub | Cubao, Fairview, Pasig, Pier/Intramuros |
| Lawton | Ermita/Paco | Bus terminal (southbound) | Alabang, Dasmariñas, Biñan, Calamba |
| Santa Cruz | Santa Cruz/Binondo | Through-corridor (Rizal Ave) | Valenzuela, Caloocan, Baclaran-bound |
| Blumentritt | Binondo/Caloocan border | Street-level jeepney + LRT-1 | Baclaran, Pasay, Navotas, Novaliches |

## Divisoria Terminal

### Location & Character
Divisoria is the largest dry-goods market district in Metro Manila, centered on **Tutuban Center** (former PNR terminus), along Claro M. Recto Avenue, Juan Luna Street, and Divisoria Market proper. It straddles the boundaries of Tondo, Binondo, and San Nicolas. The jeepney terminal is primarily on **A. Rivera Street** (Antonio Rivera St.), with loading zones on adjacent streets.

**Key crowd generators**: Divisoria Market, Tutuban Center Mall, Tutuban PNR station (18 min walk from Divisoria Market), DLTB Bus Co. ticketing office.

**Rail access**: PNR Tutuban Station (~18 min walk); LRT-1 Doroteo Jose Station (accessible by jeepney from Divisoria via CM Recto).

## Quiapo Terminal

### Location & Character
Quiapo is one of Manila's most religiously and commercially significant districts. The jeepney terminal is clustered along **Quezon Boulevard** (southbound, near Quiapo Church) and extends to streets like Evangelista, Hidalgo, Sales, and C.M. Recto. Quezon Boulevard is a major thoroughfare connecting Quiapo northward through the University Belt to Quezon Avenue.

**Key crowd generators**: Quiapo Church (Feast of the Black Nazarene — January 9, largest street gathering in PH), Quiapo Market, Golden Mosque.

**Rail access**: LRT-1 Carriedo Station (~19 min walk); LRT-1 Doroteo Jose / Recto Station (nearer for connecting northbound jeepneys).

## Lawton Bus Terminal

### Location & Character
Lawton (officially **Plaza Lawton / Liwasang Bonifacio**) in Ermita is Metro Manila's primary southbound bus terminal for Laguna, Batangas, and Cavite province destinations. The **Park N Ride Terminal** is no longer operational; the current staging area is beside the **Philippine Postal Corporation (Manila Central Post Office)** on Liwasang Bonifacio. A separate **Multi-Modal Terminal** at the Pasig River Ferry Terminal handles the Calamba P2P bus and ferry connections.

**UBE Express** terminal is located at **Robinsons Place Manila** in Ermita (~5 min walk from Lawton).

**Rail access**: LRT-1 UN Avenue Station (~6 min walk); LRT-1 Central Station (~8 min).

**Operating hours**: 5:00 AM – 10:00 PM (buses/vans); some jeepneys operate 24h.

**Notes**:
- Lawton is primarily a **southbound bus hub** — the dominant flow is Manila–South Luzon. Northbound commuters use Lawton more as a drop-off point.
- For Lucena/Quezon destinations: no direct buses from Lawton — commuters must take LRT-1 to Gil Puyat then board JAC Liner or DLTB from the Buendia terminal.
- Several operators (DLTB, BBL, JAM) serve the Laguna/Quezon corridor from the Buendia terminal; Lawton focuses on Cavite and Laguna near-province routes.

## Santa Cruz (Transit Corridor)

### Location & Character
**Santa Cruz** is a district in Manila along Rizal Avenue (the main northbound arterial). It is not a single terminal but a dense transit corridor. The key intersection is **Rizal Ave / New Antipolo / Leonor Rivera area**, near the **LRT-1 Bambang and Doroteo Jose stations**. The **JAC Liner provincial terminal** is at **Doroteo Jose St., Santa Cruz**.

Routes that "go via Sta. Cruz" are using Rizal Avenue as the Manila inner-city spine, connecting Quiapo/Divisoria area northward toward Caloocan (Monumento) and Valenzuela.

## Blumentritt Terminal/Node

### Location & Character
Blumentritt is the area around the **LRT-1 Blumentritt Station**, near Manila North Cemetery and the Caloocan–Manila boundary. It serves as a junction between Manila inner-city routes (Divisoria, Quiapo, Binondo) and the northern Manila fringe (Novaliches, Balintawak, Caloocan via Rizal Ave). The district name comes from Blumentritt Road/Street in Sampaloc.

**E-tricycle (e-trike)** services operate as feeders from the Blumentritt area to Divisoria (short-distance).

**Modern jeepney activity**: Nova-Blumentritt Cooperative deployed **15 modern jeepneys** (Euro 4+) on the Novaliches–Balintawak–Blumentritt route under PUVMP.

## Cross-Terminal Observations

### The Taft–Rizal–Recto Spine
The majority of inner-Manila routes use one of two N–S spines:
1. **Taft Avenue / L. Guinto / A. Mabini** (western) — connects Baclaran ↔ Quiapo ↔ Blumentritt ↔ Navotas
2. **Rizal Avenue** (central) — connects Divisoria ↔ Sta. Cruz ↔ Blumentritt ↔ Monumento ↔ Caloocan

East–west connectors cross at:
- **CM Recto Avenue** (Quiapo–Divisoria)
- **Legarda/Espana** (Quiapo–University Belt)
- **J.A. Santos / Juan Luna** (Divisoria–North Harbor)

### Quezon Blvd — Radial Road 8 (R-8)
Quezon Boulevard is LTFRB National Route 170 / Radial Road 8, serving as the primary jeepney corridor from Quiapo northward through the University Belt toward Quezon City. Routes using this axis typically originate at Quiapo and terminate at various QC points (Proj. 2&3, Proj. 4, Cubao, Fairview).

### PNR Tutuban — Divisoria Connection
PNR Tutuban Station in Divisoria provides a limited commuter rail option, with PNR augmentation routes (Alabang–Divisoria, FTI–Divisoria) documented in the DOTr plans analysis. Tutuban is ~18 min walk from the Divisoria Market terminal.
