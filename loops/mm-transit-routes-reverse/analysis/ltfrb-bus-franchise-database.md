## Summary

As of November 2024, there are **68 local bus routes** in Metro Manila franchised to private operators by the LTFRB. Pre-pandemic there were 830+ bus franchises; the 2020 Bus Rationalization Program drastically cut this. The system has since partially reopened via LTFRB MC No. 67 (series 2022) and Board Resolutions 55 and 57 (series 2023).

The full franchise database is not publicly available online. Access requires an eFOI request to LTFRB-NCR (ncr@ltfrb.gov.ph, B61 L8 Regalado Ave., Brgy. North Fairview, QC).

## Bus Categories in Metro Manila

### 1. EDSA Carousel (Bus Rapid Transit)

The Philippines' first BRT system. Operates on a dedicated median busway along EDSA.

- **Route**: Monumento, Caloocan ↔ PITX (Parañaque Integrated Terminal Exchange)
- **Length**: ~28 km
- **Stops**: 23 northbound / 24 southbound
- **Fare**: ₱15.00 base (first 5 km) + ₱2.65/km; end-to-end ₱74.50; 20% discount for students/seniors/PWDs
- **Payment**: Cash and GCash (as of Dec 2025; previously required smart card only)
- **Hours**: PITX–Monumento: 4:00 AM–11:00 PM; Taft–MCU: 24 hours; limited overnight service midnight–4 AM
- **Key stations** (south to north): PITX, SM MOA, Heritage Hotel/Roxas Blvd, MRT Taft/Pasay Rotonda, Ayala (One Ayala southbound), Buendia, Guadalupe, Ortigas, Santolan, Main Ave/Cubao, Nepa Q Mart/Kamuning, Quezon Avenue, North Avenue/Trinoma, Muñoz, Balintawak/Cloverleaf, Bagong Barrio, MCU/Monumento
- **Accessibility**: Elevators/escalators at Monumento, Bagong Barrio, Balintawak, SM North EDSA, Philam, Guadalupe, One Ayala
- **Operators**: Multiple private bus companies under EDSA Carousel contract; HM Transport Inc. is a key operator
- **Planned expansion**: 4 stations to be renovated; 3 new stations by 2026 (2 at PITX + 1 in Cubao)

### 3. Premium Point-to-Point (P2P) Bus Service

Launched March 2015. Express service with fixed schedules, fewer stops, amenities (AC, WiFi, reclining seats). Regulated by LTFRB but premium fares.

- South Park (Alabang) → Makati Greenbelt 1: ₱110/₱88; Mon–Fri 6–8 AM only
- Araneta City (Cubao) ↔ NAIA Terminals 1–4: ₱200; specified schedule

### 5. Provincial Bus Operators (NCR Terminals)

Provincial buses pass through Metro Manila terminals. Their NCR segments function as city bus alternatives for some corridors.

**Cubao Araneta Terminal** (primary northern/eastern hub):
- Genesis Transport: Baguio, Balanga (Bataan), Baler (Aurora), Cabanatuan, Dau, Mariveles, NAIA T3, New Clark Airport, Rosales, San Fernando (Pampanga), San Jose (NE), SM Clark, Tarlac, Trinoma
- DLTB: Manila–Magallanes Interchange (via SLEX), Mandaluyong EDSA, Laguna, Batangas, Quezon Province, Bicol Region, Eastern Visayas
- Victory Liner: Baguio, Pangasinan, Cagayan Valley, Central Luzon
- Also at Cubao: Partas, JoyBus, JAM Liner, Five Star, Baliwag Transit, Dominion Bus Lines, CODA Lines, Superlines, Raymond Transit, Bicol Isarog, RSL Bus, Penafrancia Tours, Pintados

**Pasay / PITX** (primary southern hub):
- Philtranco / Amihan Bus Lines: Bicol region (temporarily ceased operations, later resumed)
- DLTB, Victory Liner, Partas, CUL Transport, Ceres, Elvavil Transport, Bicol Isarog, Five Star, CISCO, Saulog Transit, and others

**Buendia / Taft Bus Terminal**:
- DLTB, JAM Liner, JAC Liner, Batangas Bus

**North Luzon Express Terminal (NLET)** (Caloocan/north Metro Manila):
- Northern Luzon operators; serves as gateway for passengers entering/exiting NCR from north

**Valenzuela Gateway Complex (VGC)**: newer terminal serving Valenzuela and northern routes
