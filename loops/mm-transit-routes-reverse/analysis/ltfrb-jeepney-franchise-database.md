# LTFRB Franchise Database — Jeepney Routes (Metro Manila)

**Source type**: Official government
**Retrieved**: 2026-02-25
**Confidence**: Medium (publicly accessible data is fragmentary; complete database requires FOI or direct LTFRB request)

---

## Summary

The LTFRB (Land Transportation Franchising and Regulatory Board) is the primary regulator and franchisor of all public utility vehicles in the Philippines, including jeepneys. They maintain the authoritative database of all jeepney franchise routes in Metro Manila (NCR). However, this database is **not publicly available as a downloadable file** — full access requires either an eFOI request or direct engagement with LTFRB NCR.

What is publicly known:

- Pre-pandemic: ~900 total public transport routes, 43,000+ jeepney franchises in NCR
- As of July 2020 (early post-pandemic reopening): ~387 traditional PUJ routes active, 33,979 TPUJs
- As of Dec 31, 2023: 555 jeepney routes in NCR had consolidated under PUV Modernization Program; 395 routes had **no consolidated entities** (but may still have individual franchise holders)
- Nationwide consolidation rate (April 2024): 78.33% by units, 73.71% by routes (7,019 routes total)

---

## Route Code System

LTFRB uses a **"T" prefix** numbering system for Traditional PUJ routes. The numbering appears to be loosely geographic:

| Code Series | Geographic Area |
|-------------|----------------|
| T1xx | Northern NCR — Caloocan, Malabon, Novaliches, Valenzuela |
| T2xx | Quezon City, Cubao, Marikina, Mandaluyong |
| T3xx (3-digit) | Manila city proper, Pasay, Makati |
| T3xxx (4-digit) | Manila city proper (overflow/sub-routes) |
| T4xx+ | Likely southern NCR — Parañaque, Las Piñas, Taguig |

Route codes are **not displayed on jeepney vehicles** (as of 2024) — they are administrative designations used by LTFRB for franchise tracking. Route names on vehicles follow the convention: **"Origin – Destination via [road/landmark]"**.

## Fare Structure (Jeepney)

| Effective | Base Fare | Base Distance | Per-km Rate |
|-----------|-----------|---------------|-------------|
| Pre-2023  | ₱9.00     | First 4 km    | ₱1.50/km    |
| 2023–2025 | ₱12.00    | First 4 km    | ₱1.50/km    |
| 2026 (current) | ₱13.00 | First 4 km | ₱1.80/km    |

- Students (elementary/high school) and senior citizens/PWDs: 20% discount
- Fare is a minimum/flat rate structure, not strictly metered
- Fare adjustments require LTFRB board approval

## Key Regulatory Milestones

| Date | Event |
|------|-------|
| 2017 | PUV Modernization Program (PUVMP) launched |
| 2020 (Mar) | COVID lockdown — nearly all routes suspended |
| 2020 (Jun–Jul) | Phased reopening: ~387 TPUJ routes, 33,979 units |
| 2020 (Oct) | MC 2020-062/063/064: additional 30 PUJ + 22 UV Express routes opened |
| 2022 (Sep) | MC 2022-073/074: pre-pandemic route franchises reopened |
| 2022 (Dec) | MC 2022-084: full reinstatement of pre-pandemic routes + 28 new routes |
| 2023 (Dec 31) | PUVMP consolidation deadline: 555 NCR routes consolidated, 395 not |
| 2024 (Apr 30) | Extended consolidation deadline — unconsolidated routes officially "colorum" |
| 2025 (Aug 31) | Phase-out deadline: jeepneys older than 15 years lose franchise eligibility |

## Access Path for Complete Data

1. **eFOI Request** (foi.gov.ph/agencies/ltfrb): Formal written request — precedent exists (FOI request LTFRB-054832698688 filed Feb 2025 for "jeepney routes in Metro Manila from 2010-2023")
2. **LTFRB NCR Portal** (ptops-ncr.ltfrb.gov.ph): Franchise transaction portal; may have route lookup
3. **LTFRB Memorandum Circulars** (ltfrb.gov.ph/issuances/memorandum-circulars/): PDFs listing route annexes; MC 2020-026, 2020-029, 2020-062, 2020-063, 2020-064, 2022-073, 2022-084 are key documents
4. **Rappler LTFRB database**: Published consolidation status data for all NCR routes as of Dec 2023 (interactive; could contain full route list)

---

## Gaps & Unknowns

- Routes T4xx through T6xx (likely southern NCR) not found in public sources
- Complete list of modern PUJ routes with LTFRB codes unknown
- Routes added 2023-2025 under PUVMP consolidation not documented
- No route shapes or stop sequences in any public LTFRB data
- Unknown: whether route codes changed during consolidation (individual → cooperative)
- Unknown: exact list of which 395 routes had no consolidated entities (named routes not all published)
