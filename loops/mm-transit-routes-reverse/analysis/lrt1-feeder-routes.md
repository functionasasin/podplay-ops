# LRT-1 Feeder Routes — Jeepney/Bus Connections per Station

**Aspect**: Specialized Data — LRT-1 feeder routes
**Date analyzed**: 2026-02-28
**Method**: Web research via search and article scraping

---

## Overview

LRT Line 1 (Green Line) runs north–south from **Fernando Poe Jr. (FPJ) station** in Quezon City to **Dr. Santos station** in Parañaque. As of November 16, 2024, the line has **25 stations** covering ~26 km, following Phase 1 of the Cavite Extension opening. Daily ridership ~440,000 passengers.

This document catalogs jeepney, bus, UV Express, and other road-based transport connecting to each station.

---

## Station-by-Station Feeder Inventory

### 1. Fernando Poe Jr. (FPJ) / Roosevelt Station
- **City**: Quezon City (boundary: Veterans Village / Ramon Magsaysay / Bago Bantay)
- **Major roads**: EDSA, Congressional Avenue, Fernando Poe Jr. Avenue
- **Feeder modes**: Buses, jeepneys, UV Express on EDSA; jeepneys on Congressional Ave and FPJ Ave
- **Key destinations reachable**: Northbound QC/Caloocan via EDSA; Congressional Ave corridor to QC interior
- **Landmarks**: Waltermart North EDSA (northbound side), Jackman Plaza Muñoz (southbound side)
- **Notes**: Northern terminus of LRT-1; no specific route codes documented
- **Confidence**: Medium

---

### 2. Balintawak Station
- **City**: Quezon City (boundary: Balingasa, Unang Sigaw, Apolonio Samson)
- **Major roads**: EDSA at QC–Caloocan boundary
- **Feeder modes**: Buses and jeepneys via Balintawak Bus Terminal (~160 m, ~3-min walk)
- **Key destinations reachable**: North Luzon Expressway (NLEX) connections; northern QC/Caloocan area; Balintawak Market
- **Landmarks**: Balintawak Cloverleaf, Balintawak Market
- **Notes**: Primarily a gateway to NLEX/north; provincial bus staging area for Manila-bound trips from north Luzon
- **Confidence**: Medium

---

### 3. Monumento Station
- **City**: Caloocan (southern Caloocan)
- **Major roads**: Rizal Avenue Extension, Samson Road, MacArthur Highway
- **Feeder modes**: Jeepneys, buses, UV Express
- **Key route connections**:
  - **Monumento → Malabon (via Letre/Samson Rd)**: Every 10 min, ~14 min; boards at Areneta Square Mall, Samson Rd, Caloocan; ends at Letre Rd / Sanciangco St, Malabon
  - **Monumento → Navotas via Letre (Lapu-Lapu/Samson Rd)**: Every 5 min, ~13 min; to Franco Clinic & Hospital, Letre Rd, Malabon
  - **Monumento → Navotas City Hall (Areneta Sq.)**: Every 15 min, ~23 min
  - **Monumento → Valenzuela (Karuhatan)**: From McDonald's Rizal Ave; every 5 min, ~20 min; via MacArthur Highway to Karuhatan
- **Key destinations reachable**: Malabon, Navotas, Valenzuela; Samson Road corridor; MacArthur Highway
- **Landmarks**: Bonifacio Monument / Monumento Circle; SM City Grand Central (northbound); LRT Caloocan Mall (southbound)
- **Provincial buses**: Victory Liner, First North Luzon Transit, RJ Express terminals near station
- **Notes**: Major hub for northwestern NCR cities (Malabon, Navotas, Valenzuela); provincial buses to Pangasinan, Ilocos via NLEX
- **Confidence**: High (multiple corroborating sources)

- **City**: Caloocan
- **Major roads**: 5th Avenue / C-3 road
- **Feeder modes**: Jeepneys and buses to Caloocan destinations
- **Key destinations reachable**: Interior Caloocan streets; Caloocan civic center area
- **Notes**: First Caloocan station heading north; minimal feeder documentation; general Caloocan coverage
- **Confidence**: Low (general description only)

### 5. R. Papa Station

- **Major roads**: Ricardo Papa Street / Rizal Avenue
- **Feeder modes**: Jeepneys available below station
- **Key destinations reachable**: Tondo and Caloocan interior roads; La Loma Cemetery (alight here)

- **Confidence**: Low

### 6. Abad Santos Station
- **City**: Manila (Tondo / Santa Cruz districts)
- **Major roads**: Abad Santos Avenue
- **Feeder modes**: Jeepneys and tricycles below station
- **Key destinations reachable**: Inner Tondo streets; inner Santa Cruz streets
- **Notes**: Tricycles serve last-mile within Tondo and Santa Cruz narrow roads
- **Confidence**: Medium

---

### 7. Blumentritt Station
- **City**: Manila (Santa Cruz / Tondo / Sampaloc)
- **Major roads**: Rizal Avenue at Blumentritt
- **Feeder modes**: Jeepneys, taxis; direct transfer to PNR Blumentritt (street-level, opposite side)
- **Key destinations reachable**: SM San Lazaro; Manila North Cemetery; Sampaloc, Tondo, Santa Cruz interior
- **Landmarks**: Chinese General Hospital, SM San Lazaro, Manila North Cemetery
- **Rail transfer**: PNR Blumentritt (SUSPENDED since March 2024 for NSCR construction)
- **Notes**: PNR transfer is currently not operational; banks, fast food, grocery, flea markets in area
- **Confidence**: Medium

---

### 8. Bambang Station
- **City**: Manila (Sampaloc / Santa Cruz)
- **Major roads**: Bambang Street / Rizal Avenue
- **Feeder modes**: Buses and jeepneys toward UST/hospital corridor
- **Key destinations reachable**: University of Santo Tomas (UST campus); Jose Reyes Memorial Medical Center; San Lazaro Hospital; medical supplies district
- **Notes**: Primarily a student/medical destination; more convenient than Doroteo Jose for UST-bound passengers
- **Confidence**: Medium

---

### 9. Doroteo Jose Station
- **City**: Manila (Santa Cruz district)
- **Major roads**: Rizal Avenue at Doroteo Jose Street
- **Feeder modes**: Provincial and city buses in adjacent terminals; LRT-2 transfer
- **Key route connections**:
  - **Doroteo Jose → Quezon City (via Rizal Ave)**: Jeepney, 11 stops
  - **Doroteo Jose → Malabon (via MacArthur Hwy)**: Jeepney, 38 stops
- **Rail transfer**: LRT-2 via walkway to Recto Station (cross-platform walkway ~200 m)
- **Provincial bus terminals**: Bataan Transit, Genesis Transport Service, Philippine Rabbit Bus Lines (within walking distance)
- **Key destinations reachable**: Northern Luzon via provincial buses; QC, Malabon via local jeepney; entire LRT-2 corridor via transfer
- **Notes**: Major intermodal hub; primary northbound transfer point for LRT-1 ↔ LRT-2 riders
- **Confidence**: High

---

### 10. Carriedo Station
- **City**: Manila (Quiapo / Santa Cruz)
- **Major roads**: Rizal Avenue at Carriedo Street
- **Feeder modes**: Jeepneys at street level; near Quiapo market transport
- **Key destinations reachable**: Quiapo Church (short walk); Chinatown/Binondo; Liwasang Bonifacio; SM Quiapo; Philippine Postal Corporation; FEATI University; Santa Cruz Church (short walk)
- **Notes**: Extremely busy pedestrian area; no specific feeder route codes documented; proximity to Quiapo market with extensive informal jeepney/FX services
- **Confidence**: Medium

### 11. Central Terminal Station
- **City**: Manila (Ermita)
- **Major roads**: Taft Avenue at P. Faura / Padre Burgos area
- **Feeder modes**: Extensive — buses, jeepneys, UV Express, Ferry
- **Key transport hubs nearby**:
  - **Lawton Bus Terminal / Park N' Ride**: Intercity and intracity buses, jeepneys, UV Express
  - **Manila Multimodal Terminal**: Near Pasig River, by Lawton area
  - **Lawton Ferry Station**: Pasig River Ferry Service
  - **Lawton UV Express stop**: ~278 m (4-min walk)
- **Key jeepney routes at Lawton**:
  - Quiapo–Pier
  - Doroteo Jose–Baclaran / Mabini
  - Quiapo–Lawton / SM City Hall
  - Divisoria–Lawton / SM City Hall
  - UST/Dapitan–Lawton / SM City Hall
  - Blumentritt–Lawton / SM City Hall
  - Cubao–Lawton / SM City Hall
  - Baclaran–Lawton / SM City Hall (via MOA variant)
  - Blumentritt–Pasay RTDA via Santa Cruz & L. Guinto
  - Divisoria–Lawton (multiple variants)
  - Dapitan–Pasay RTDA via L. Guinto
  - Arroceros–Roxas District via España
- **Key bus routes**: Dasmariñas Bayan–Lawton; Manuela/Alabang–Lawton/Taft/PGH; Ayala–Lawton/Taft/PGH
- **Landmarks**: Letran, PLM, Mapúa University, Lyceum, PNUl; near Intramuros
- **Notes**: One of the richest feeder hubs on LRT-1; ferry service provides unique waterway connection
- **Confidence**: High

- **Feeder modes**: Buses and jeepneys on Taft Ave and UN Ave
- **Key destinations reachable**: De La Salle University; De La Salle–CSB; Saint Scholastica's College; Intramuros; Rizal Park (Luneta)
- **Notes**: Tourism corridor station; student-heavy; near US Embassy area
- **Confidence**: Medium

### 13. Pedro Gil Station (also known as Herran Station)
- **City**: Manila (Malate)
- **Major roads**: Taft Avenue at Pedro Gil (Herran) Street
- **Feeder modes**: Buses, jeepneys, taxis
- **Key destinations reachable**: Philippine General Hospital (PGH); Robinsons Place Manila; Paco Park; Malate churches; Ermita shopping belt
- **Notes**: High foot traffic from hospital workers and patients; also serves as entry to Paco district; jeepneys and buses along Taft Ave continue to Pasay/EDSA
- **Confidence**: Medium

---

### 14. Quirino Station
- **City**: Manila (Malate)
- **Major roads**: Taft Avenue at Quirino Avenue / San Andres Street intersection
- **Feeder modes**: Jeepneys and buses; taxis readily available
- **Key destinations reachable**: Malate interior (via San Andres St, Adriatico St); Manila Zoo; Malate Church; Remedios Circle / Korea Town nightlife district; Roxas Boulevard via short connection
- **Notes**: Easy hailing of jeepneys/buses on Quirino Ave and San Andres for Malate interior; Korea Town destination for expats/tourists
- **Confidence**: Medium

---

### 15. Vito Cruz Station
- **City**: Manila (Malate / Singalong border)
- **Major roads**: Taft Avenue at Vito Cruz Street
- **Feeder modes**: Jeepneys and buses; PNR Vito Cruz (~1,084 m, 15-min walk — but PNR currently SUSPENDED)
- **Key jeepney routes**:
  - CCP Complex–Taft Ave via Vito Cruz (connects to Cultural Center, Manila Bay area)
  - Monumento–Vito Cruz via Rizal Ave/Mabini (long-haul north-south)
  - Monumento–Vito Cruz via Rizal Ave/Taft Ave (alternate)
  - Route 309: Nichols–Vito Cruz LRT (south to Vito Cruz)
  - T3111: Kamuning–Vito Cruz via E. Rodriguez / A. Mabini (QC to Manila)
- **Buses**: Osmeña Highway buses accessible from Vito Cruz
- **Key destinations reachable**: DLSU campus, De La Salle–CSB; CCP Complex, Manila Bay; Makati (via Osmeña Highway / EDSA)
- **Notes**: Student-dominated; Vito Cruz is also a southern Manila–Makati corridor interchange point
- **Confidence**: High (multiple route codes confirmed)

---

### 16. Gil Puyat Station (Buendia)
- **City**: Pasay
- **Major roads**: Taft Avenue at Gil Puyat Avenue (Buendia)
- **Feeder modes**: Buses (provincial + city), jeepneys, UV Express, taxis
- **Key transport hubs nearby**:
  - **Buendia Bus Terminal** (beside station): JAM Liner / Atrium; buses to Batangas, Laguna, Lucena, Marinduque (DLTB, JAM)
  - **Buendia UV Express Terminal**: ~418 m (6-min walk)
- **Key jeepney routes**:
  - Buendia (Crispa)–PRC via P. Tamo
  - Taft Ave buses (various south-bound)
- **Key bus routes**: Provincial services to Laguna, Batangas, Quezon, Marinduque; city bus routes along Buendia/Taft
- **Key destinations reachable**: Makati CBD (walking/short jeep); SM Mall of Asia (multicab/jeep); World Trade Center; CCP; PICC; southern provinces
- **Notes**: Important provincial connection hub for Laguna/Batangas passengers; also Makati CBD access; multicabs serve MOA/CCP area
- **Confidence**: High

### 17. Libertad Station (Arnaiz / Herran)
- **City**: Pasay
- **Major roads**: Taft Avenue at Antonio S. Arnaiz Avenue (formerly Vito Cruz Extension)
- **Feeder modes**: Jeepneys along Arnaiz Ave (primarily to Makati), buses on Taft Ave
- **Key jeepney routes**:
  - Jeepneys along Arnaiz Ave to Makati CBD
  - Divisoria–Libertad via L. Guinto
  - Baclaran–Blumentritt via L. Guinto / Santa Cruz
  - Dapitan–Pasay RTDA via L. Guinto
- **Key destinations reachable**: Makati (Arnaiz Ave direction); Pasay City interior; US Embassy Annex and Japanese Embassy (Roxas Blvd)
- **Notes**: No escalators or elevators at any entrance; must cross street to access opposite platform; Victory Pasay Mall adjacent
- **Confidence**: High

---

### 18. EDSA Station (Taft-EDSA)
- **City**: Pasay (Pasay Rotonda)
- **Major roads**: Taft Avenue at EDSA (one of the busiest intersections in Metro Manila)
- **Feeder modes**: Jeepneys, buses, UV Express, EDSA Carousel BRT
- **Key transport connections**:
  - **MRT-3 Taft Avenue Station**: Direct transfer via elevated walkway + Metro Point Mall
  - **EDSA Carousel BRT**: Dedicated stop; routes north to Monumento and south to PITX; fare ₱15–₱74.50
  - **Jeepneys**: To Makati, Taguig, Parañaque, Muntinlupa, and all areas along EDSA
  - **Provincial buses**: Victory Liner, Philtranco terminals nearby
- **Key destinations reachable**: Any point on EDSA via Carousel; all MRT-3 stations; SM Mall of Asia (one jeepney ride); Makati CBD; NAIA area
- **Notes**: Critical intermodal hub — LRT-1, MRT-3, and EDSA Carousel all converge here; also called Taft-EDSA or Pasay Rotonda
- **Confidence**: High

---

### 19. Baclaran Station
- **City**: Pasay / Parañaque (boundary)
- **Major roads**: Taft Avenue southern terminus; near Parañaque/Pasay boundary
- **Feeder modes**: Jeepneys (extensive), buses, taxis, pedicabs/tricycles
- **Key destinations from jeepney terminal**:
  - Alabang, Blumentritt, Dapitan, Dasmariñas Bayan, Divisoria, Imus, MOA (LRT EDSA via jeep), MRT Taft
  - Nichols, Paliparan, PITX, SM Southmall, Sucat, Tagaytay, Trece Martires, Zapote Talaba
  - Baclaran–Sucat route (via Parañaque)
  - Sucat–Lawton (via Mall of Asia)
  - NAIA terminal access (~2 km; taxis dedicated for NAIA transfers)
- **Key destinations reachable**: Las Piñas, Parañaque, Muntinlupa to the south; Manila, Pasay, QC, Caloocan to the north; Cavite province (Imus, Dasmariñas, Tagaytay direction)
- **Landmarks**: Baclaran Church (famous shopping/flea market district); Redemptorist-Aseana station is just across Baclaran Church (new Cavite Extension station)
- **Notes**: One of Manila's largest jeepney staging areas; historically the southern terminus until 2024; PNR Nichols nearby
- **Confidence**: High

---

### 20. Redemptorist-Aseana Station *(Cavite Extension Phase 1 — opened Nov 16, 2024)*
- **City**: Parañaque
- **Major roads**: Redemptorist Road / Aseana Avenue (near Baclaran Church)
- **Feeder modes**: Free modern jeepney shuttle
- **Key route connections**:
  - **Free modern jeepney**: Redemptorist-Aseana ↔ Ayala Malls Manila Bay (daily 9am–9pm, every 30 min)
  - Same free jeepney also connects MIA Road station to Ayala Malls Manila Bay
- **Notes**: Named after adjacent Redemptorist Road and Baclaran Church; northernmost station of the Cavite Extension; free shuttle is temporary promotion
- **Confidence**: High

---

### 21. MIA Road Station *(Cavite Extension Phase 1)*
- **City**: Parañaque
- **Major roads**: MIA (Manila International Airport) Road; near Roxas Boulevard
- **Feeder modes**: Buses, jeepneys, UV Express
- **Key route connections**:
  - Buses, jeepneys, UV Express on **Roxas Boulevard** route
  - **Baclaran–Sucat jeepney** (via Parañaque)
  - Free modern jeepney → Ayala Malls Manila Bay (same service as Redemptorist-Aseana, 9am–9pm, every 30 min)
- **Key destinations reachable**: NAIA airport terminals (very close); Roxas Boulevard; MOA area
- **Confidence**: High

---

### 22. PITX Station (Asia World Station) *(Cavite Extension Phase 1)*
- **City**: Parañaque
- **Major roads**: Parañaque Integrated Terminal Exchange / Ninoy Aquino Avenue
- **Feeder modes**: Comprehensive hub — all modes present
- **Key transport at PITX**:
  - **UBE Express Airport Shuttle**: NAIA Terminal 1, 2, 3, and 4
  - **EDSA Carousel Bus**: End-to-end to Monumento; fare ₱15–₱74.50
  - **Traditional PUJs and modern jeepneys**: To various Parañaque/Pasay/Las Piñas points
  - **City buses**: Multiple routes
  - **Provincial buses**: To Quezon, Laguna, Baguio, Occidental Mindoro, Bicol, Batangas, and beyond
- **Station access**: Direct escalator link from PITX terminal to station
- **Key destinations reachable**: Any NAIA terminal; all of EDSA via Carousel; all of southern Luzon via provincial buses
- **Notes**: The most important intermodal hub in southern Metro Manila; PITX replaced the old Parañaque integrated terminal; EDSA Carousel southern terminus
- **Confidence**: High

---

### 23. Ninoy Aquino Avenue Station *(Cavite Extension Phase 1)*

- **Feeder modes**: Jeepneys and buses on Ninoy Aquino Ave
- **Key destinations reachable**: BF Parañaque, SM BF Parañaque, Multinational Village

### 24. Dr. Santos Station (formerly Sucat) *(Cavite Extension Phase 1 — current southern terminus)*
- **City**: Parañaque
- **Major roads**: Dr. A. Santos Avenue (Parañaque–Sucat Rd) at C5 Extension / Cavitex-C5 Link
- **Feeder modes**: Jeepneys, buses
- **Key destinations reachable**: Makati, Taguig, Parañaque (south), Muntinlupa; SM City Sucat (~5-min walk)

## Data Quality Assessment

- **Completeness**: Medium — Major transfer stations (Monumento, Doroteo Jose, Central, EDSA, Gil Puyat, Baclaran, PITX) well-documented; intermediate stations (5th Avenue, R. Papa, Abad Santos, Bambang, Carriedo, UN Ave, Pedro Gil, Quirino, Ninoy Aquino Ave) have minimal feeder documentation
- **Currency**: High — LRT-1 system status (25 stations, Cavite Extension Phase 1) is current as of Nov 2024
- **Geometry**: None available specifically for feeder routes at LRT-1 stations
- **Route code specificity**: Only ~12 specific route codes documented (T3111, Route 309, Route 410, etc.); most feeder info is destination-based rather than LTFRB-coded

---

## Key Gaps

1. **5th Avenue, R. Papa, Abad Santos** — minimal feeder documentation; Caloocan/Tondo interior routes poorly mapped
2. **Carriedo, Bambang, Doroteo Jose** — only bus terminal info; no specific jeepney route codes
3. **UN Avenue, Pedro Gil, Quirino** — neighborhood-level feeder routes to Malate interior not catalogued
4. **Ninoy Aquino Avenue station** — almost no feeder data found; new station, minimal documentation online
5. **FPJ / Balintawak** — general EDSA bus/jeepney info only; specific route codes not documented
6. **Exact stop counts and GPS coordinates** for feeder routes not available from secondary sources

---

## Implications for GTFS

- LRT-1 itself has coverage in the TUMI Datahub GTFS (LRTA agency); rail stops have GPS coordinates
- Feeder routes documented here should be cross-referenced during Wave 2 (especially corridor validation for Taft Ave, Rizal Ave, EDSA, MacArthur Hwy, Lawton)
- Monumento, EDSA, Gil Puyat, and PITX are the most critical transfer nodes with highest feeder route density
- Baclaran–Sucat, Monumento–Valenzuela, and Doroteo Jose–Malabon corridors are important feeders with enough documentation to include in GTFS
- Terminal interchanges (Lawton, PITX) should be modeled as major stops in stop_times.txt
