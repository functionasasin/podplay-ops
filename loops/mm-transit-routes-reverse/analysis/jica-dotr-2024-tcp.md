## Project Overview

The Japan International Cooperation Agency (JICA) and the Department of Transportation (DOTr) signed the **Record of Discussions** for a three-year Technical Cooperation Project (TCP) titled **"Capacity Development of Public Utility Vehicles (PUV) in Metro Manila and its Adjoining Areas"** on **July 17, 2024** at the DOTr office in San Juan City.

- **Duration**: July 2024 – approximately mid-2027
- **Implementing agencies**: DOTr (lead), LTFRB, Office for Transportation Cooperatives (OTC), LGUs
- **JICA Chief Representative at signing**: Sakamoto Takema
- **DOTr Secretary at signing**: Jaime Bautista

The TCP directly follows from — and responds to — the **2022 JICA Data Collection Survey on Road-Based Public Transportation in Metro Manila** (analyzed in `analysis/jica-2022-survey.md`), which documented overlapping routes, franchising dysfunction, supply-demand mismatch, and colorum vehicle proliferation.

---

## Planned Deliverables

Based on press releases and news coverage, the TCP will produce:

1. **Route planning manuals** — methodology and guidelines for DOTr/LTFRB to evaluate and rationalize PUV routes
2. **Intermodal transport hub design guidelines** — standards for designing transfer points between buses, jeepneys, UV Express, and rail
3. **Service plans** — templates and frameworks for route service level planning and evaluation
4. **Training programs** — capacity building workshops for DOTr and LTFRB staff on route planning and management
5. **Service level monitoring framework** — metrics and tools for ongoing performance assessment of PUV services

The TCP explicitly supports these existing programs:
- Public Transport Modernization Program (PTMP) / PUVMP
- EDSA Busway system
- Active Transport Program
- EDSA Greenways Projects

## Companion TCP: JICA-MMDA ITS Project (Feb 2025)

A related TCP was signed on **February 21, 2025** between JICA and MMDA: **"Capacity Enhancement on Traffic Management with Improvement of Intelligent Transportation Systems in Metro Manila"** — a 3-year project building on the 2019–2022 CTMP TCP.

Key components:
1. Planning capacity enhancement for ITS development
2. ITS implementation improvements (procurement of equipment)
3. Traffic data management system establishment

This ITS TCP is complementary to the DOTr PUV TCP — the ITS project handles traffic signal optimization and monitoring infrastructure; the DOTr TCP handles route planning and intermodal design.

## Route Rationalization Context

The TCP operates against a severely behind-schedule PUVMP rationalization backdrop:

| Metric | Value |
|--------|-------|
| Target route rationalization by end-2025 | 50% |
| Actual completion as of Jan 2025 | ~15% |
| Target full rationalization | End-2026 |
| NCR LPTRP status | NOT YET PREPARED (DOTr responsibility per DILG MC 2018-60) |

**Critical regulatory fact**: Under DILG Memorandum Circular No. 2018-60, the NCR is **excluded from LGU-prepared LPTRPs** — it is DOTr's responsibility to prepare the Local Public Transport Route Plan for the MUCEP study area (16 NCR cities + Pateros + adjoining cities in Bulacan, Rizal, Laguna, Cavite). The DOTr has NOT yet published this plan.

Without a finalized NCR LPTRP, JICA's TCP route planning capacity development has no approved route rationalization framework to operationalize. This explains the disconnect between the ambitious TCP deliverables and the slow on-ground rationalization progress.

## Route Rationalization Progress (Nationwide, for context)

- As of April 23, 2024: 78.33% consolidation by units / 73.71% by routes (150,179 units, 7,019 routes nationwide)
- NCR consolidation: ~57% before April 30, 2024 deadline (per LTFRB Chairman Guadiz)
- Unconsolidated units (July 2024): ~31,818 (16.6% of total)
- Route rationalization vs. fleet consolidation: these are SEPARATE processes — high consolidation rates do NOT mean routes have been rationalized
- Senate Resolution No. 1096 (22 of 23 senators, July 2024): Called for PUVMP suspension
- Supreme Court (May 2024): Required DOTr/LTFRB status updates on route rationalization plans

## GTFS Value Assessment

**Direct value: ZERO**

This TCP will not produce route databases, GTFS files, stop coordinates, or any structured transit data that could be directly imported into a routing engine. The deliverables are institutional capacity documents — manuals, guidelines, training curricula.

**Indirect value: LOW-MEDIUM (future)**

If and when the JICA TCP produces a route rationalization framework adopted by DOTr/LTFRB, the resulting route decisions may:
1. Flow into LTFRB CPUVMS (internal system, not publicly accessible)
2. Be reflected in updated franchise decisions that could be extracted via LTFRB data if that becomes public
3. Inform intermodal hub designs at specific terminals (adding to Wave 3 stop placement accuracy)

For Wave 3 GTFS synthesis, the TCP's route rationalization study could eventually inform which routes persist, merge, or terminate — but this will not be available in the 2024–2027 TCP timeframe for this project.

## Summary

The JICA-DOTr 2024 TCP is a **capacity building project**, not a route data collection project. It will eventually produce planning methodology manuals and intermodal hub design guidelines. **No interim public outputs exist as of February 2026** (7 months into a 36-month project). The TCP responds directly to the systemic data vacuum documented in the 2022 JICA survey — but will not fill that vacuum in time for this GTFS project. Route data must come from the sources already analyzed (OSM, Sakay, LTFRB records, Moovit). 0 routes extracted.
