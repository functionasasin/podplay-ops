## Known Limitations

1. **No actual GPS tracks**: All road-based shapes are approximated from waypoints. Actual routes may deviate (e.g., local barangay streets, diversion routes, peak vs off-peak alignments).
2. **Directional shape simplification**: One shape per route (bidirectional). GTFS allows separate shapes per trip direction — a future improvement for routes with significantly different forward/return paths (e.g., one-way road systems in BGC, Makati CBD).
3. **Southbound-only stops**: EDSA Carousel stops One Ayala (SB) and Tramo (SB) are not in the main shape. Reverse-direction trips should use a slightly modified shape.
4. **PNR geometry**: Coordinates trace the approximate historical alignment through Manila, but the actual PNR track has curves not reflected in the point-to-point segments.
5. **Cavite extension (LRT-1)**: The 5 Cavite extension stations (Redemptorist → PITX) are based on announced station locations; exact alignment pending construction.
6. **Provincial terminuses**: For UV Express and P2P routes with terminuses outside NCR (Antipolo, Cavite, Laguna, Bulacan), coordinates are approximate town-center locations.

## How Shapes Relate to trips.txt

The `shape_id` values generated here follow the pattern `SHP_{route_id_normalized}`. When `trips.txt` is generated (next aspect), each trip record references its `shape_id`:

```
trip_id,route_id,service_id,shape_id,...
LRT1-T1,LRT1,WD_FULL,SHP_LRT1,...
BUS2-T1,BUS-2,WD_FULL,SHP_BUS_2,...
```

Reverse-direction trips can either reuse the same shape_id or reference a dedicated reverse shape (e.g., `SHP_LRT1_R`) if the route has significantly different SB and NB alignments.
