## Summary

WikiMili serves as a mirror of the Wikipedia article "List of bus routes in Metro Manila." The content is substantively identical to the Wikipedia source but accessible in environments where Wikipedia is blocked. As of November 2024, the article documents **68 total local bus routes** franchised to private operators; the numbered table runs through **Route 65**, with PNR augmentation routes and special programs accounting for the remainder.

**Key finding**: Routes 66, 67, and 68 are not individually listed in the WikiMili/Wikipedia source as of the fetch date. The count of 68 includes the rationalized numbered routes (1–65), 2 PNR augmentation routes, and possibly the Love Bus routes operating under Routes 51/52 and/or newer additions.

## Love Bus Program

Routes 51 and 52 have been noted as operating under the **Love Bus program** (special social equity routes). This is not a separate operator but a DOTr/LTFRB designation for subsidized/social routes. Route 51 was noted as having been "modified to VGC–DSWD Batasan under the Love Bus program" at some point.

---

## BGC Bus Integration

Historical BGC Bus routes have been integrated into the rationalized route system:
- **Route 62**: Originally Arca South–BGC route of BGC Bus
- **Route 63**: Originally pre-pandemic Ayala Express and East Express routes of BGC Bus

The former standalone BGC Bus concession is no longer operating as a separate brand per this source.

## Routes 66–68: Status Unknown

The article states 68 total franchised local bus routes as of November 2024, but only 65 numbered routes (plus PNR 1 and PNR 2 = 67) are explicitly listed. Possible explanations:
1. Routes 66–68 were added after the article's last edit and not yet reflected
2. The count includes QCityBus routes (which are city-government-operated, not LTFRB-franchised)
3. Love Bus routes under Routes 51/52 are double-counted
4. Three additional routes were approved under LTFRB Board Resolutions 55 and 57 (August 2023) but not yet numbered in this article

**Recommended action**: Cross-reference with ltoportal-ph analysis (which documents the same 65-route table) and search for Board Resolutions 55 and 57 from 2023 to identify the 3 missing routes.

## Regulatory Context

- Bus services regulated by DOTr + LTFRB; MMDA handles traffic management
- Routes subject to LTFRB Memorandum Circulars (most recent referenced: MC No. 55 and 57, series 2023)
- Pre-pandemic: 830+ bus routes; post-rationalization: 68 local routes (massive reduction)
- Operators include: Metrolink Bus Corp., Green Frog Hybrid Bus, Reinalyn Bus Line, JAC Liner, RRCG, HM Transport
