## Source Assessment

**LTOPortal.ph** is a third-party informational website (NOT the official LTO). It aggregates LTFRB route data into readable summaries. As of Nov 2024, it lists 68 local bus routes.

**WikiMili** mirrors Wikipedia's "List of bus routes in Metro Manila" — the most comprehensive publicly available route list. The Wikipedia article is regularly updated and cites LTFRB Memorandum Circulars directly.

**Limitation**: Routes 66, 67, 68 not found in any accessible mirror. These are the most recently added routes. Direct LTFRB resolution (RESO 178 S-2024) would be needed to confirm them.

## Key Observations

1. **Route numbering is not geographic** — numbers were assigned chronologically as routes were approved under the rationalization program.
2. **Many routes serve Cavite/Laguna** — the rationalization program includes provincial extensions, so "city bus routes" now include inter-regional routes.
3. **Fairview is a major origin** — Routes 36–41 all originate from Fairview, QC heading to southern Metro Manila.
4. **Navotas cluster** — Routes 44–47 all originate from Navotas, an underserved area in pre-pandemic routing.
5. **PITX is the dominant southern terminal** — at least 20+ routes terminate at PITX in Parañaque.
6. **QCityBus has the best stop-level data** — complete stop lists with landmark references, schedules by day type (weekday/Sat/Sun) and time period (peak/off-peak).
7. **Routes 66–68 remain unconfirmed** — likely added in 2023–2024 based on PNR service start or new corridors.
