**Source type**: Official government (supplemented by news reports and community sources)
**Retrieved**: 2026-02-25
**Confidence**: Medium (fragmentary public data; no bulk download of full franchise database; complete list requires eFOI or direct LTFRB NCR engagement)

---

## Summary

UV Express (UVE) services are air-conditioned vans (primarily Toyota Hi-Ace, 10–14 passenger capacity) operating on LTFRB-franchised terminal-to-terminal routes. They serve as a middle tier between jeepneys and city buses, covering radial routes from residential neighborhoods to CBD hubs (Ayala, Cubao, Ortigas, EDSA Central, SM Megamall, etc.).

Key scale figures:
- **~6,000 UV Express units** operating in Metro Manila (pre-pandemic peak)
- **~250 UV Express routes** in Metro Manila NCR, based on consolidation data:
  - 142 routes had consolidated entities as of Dec 31, 2023
  - 108 routes had **no consolidated entities** (at risk of cancellation)
- Nationwide: ~181 UV Express routes were unconsolidated (of ~1,948 total nationwide unconsolidated PUV routes)
- NCR consolidation rate for UV Express: ~59% as of early 2024 (slightly lower than the 82% national rate)

---

## Route Code System

LTFRB uses two route code prefixes for UV Express:

| Prefix | Meaning | Example |
|--------|---------|---------|
| **N-xxx** | NCR internal routes (both endpoints in Metro Manila) | N08, N25, N52, N55, N64, N69, N72 |
| **C-xxx** | Cross-region routes (one endpoint outside NCR — Bulacan, Cavite, Laguna, Rizal) | C65, C66, C67, C68, C69 |

Unlike jeepney route codes (T-prefix), the N/C numbering does **not** appear to follow a strict geographic ordering — route numbers are assigned sequentially as franchises are issued or re-numbered post-pandemic.

Route codes are **not typically displayed on vehicles** — vehicles display origin–destination text (e.g., "Antipolo – Ayala").

---

## Operational Rules (LTFRB-mandated)

- **Terminal-to-terminal operation**: UV Express units are technically authorized to load and unload only at terminals, not at arbitrary curbside stops
- **No EDSA/Commonwealth traversal**: UV Express units may **cross** EDSA and Commonwealth Avenue but may **not use them as route corridors** — a major distinction from city buses
- **Service design**: Intended for corridors with demand up to **1,000 passengers per hour per direction (pphpd)**
- **Marking requirements**: White body color; "UV Express Service" and "Authorized Route" on windshield and sides; rear must show plate number, operator name, case number, passenger capacity
- **Fare structure**: ₱2.00 per kilometer (distance-based), with no flat rate (fares are calculated per trip, not per segment)
- **Capacity**: 10–14 passengers; air-conditioned

## Fare Structure

- **Rate**: ₱2.00 per kilometer (distance-based)
- **No base minimum explicitly shown** in most listings (fares as low as ₱6 suggest short routes are pro-rated without a meaningful floor, or may predate fare rationalization)
- **No fare integration** with LRT/MRT (separate fares)
- **Cash only** (no Beep card compatibility as of data collection date)
- **No half-fare discount system** for students/seniors listed (though PWD/senior discounts mandated by law apply)
- Fare range in practice: ₱6 (very short, e.g., Market! Market! – Pasig via San Joaquin) to ₱100 (Calumpit, Bulacan – CIT)

## Gaps and Issues

1. **Full N/C-code index not available**: Only 7 N-codes and 5 C-codes confirmed from news articles; full index likely 100+ codes
2. **Post-PUVMP route changes unknown**: Routes from 2020 re-opening may not reflect current 2024/2025 operational reality; consolidation may have merged or retired some routes
3. **No geometry**: UV Express routes have no publicly available shape files
4. **No stop data**: Since UV Express are terminal-to-terminal, intermediate stops are not officially defined; however, in practice many routes pick up along corridors
5. **Makati CBD terminal reorganization (2023)**: The reorganization of Makati UV Express terminals (Dela Rosa, Valero, One Ayala) has altered route endpoints from pre-pandemic data
6. **108 NCR routes had no consolidated entity**: These routes may have been cancelled or are operating in legal grey area post-April 2024 deadline
