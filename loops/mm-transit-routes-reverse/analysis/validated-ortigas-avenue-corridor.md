# Ortigas Avenue Corridor — Cross-Reference & Validation

**Analysis date:** 2026-02-28
**Aspect:** Wave 2 — Ortigas Avenue corridor
**Scope:** All routes from Pasig to Manila via Ortigas Avenue, including Ortigas Center, Ortigas Avenue Extension (to Cainta/Taytay), and the western stub through San Juan

---

## Corridor Geography

Ortigas Avenue runs east–west for ~12.1 km within Metro Manila, then extends further into Rizal Province:

1. **Western terminus (NCR):** San Juan / Quezon City boundary (near N. Domingo / Gen. Romulo Ave)
2. **Ortigas Center:** Pasig / Mandaluyong border; major CBD; EDSA interchange with **MRT-3 Ortigas Station**
3. **Pasig East:** Meralco Avenue junction, Ugong, Tiendesitas, C5 crossing
4. **Cainta Junction (Rizal):** Where Felix Avenue (to Santolan LRT-2) and Manila East Road diverge
5. **Taytay:** ~7 km east of Cainta Junction
6. **Angono:** Eastern terminus for the main bus route (~20 km from Ortigas Center)

**Key cross-streets:** EDSA (Ortigas MRT-3), Meralco Ave, C5/Lozada Highway, Felix Ave (to Santolan LRT-2), Manila East Road

**Sister corridor note:** The Aurora Boulevard corridor handles parallel east–west traffic north of Ortigas Ave. Many routes originating from Cubao/Santolan use Aurora Blvd westward and Ortigas Ave Extension eastward — or vice versa — making terminal labeling ambiguous. City Bus Route 2 is the clearest example: it uses Magsaysay Blvd / Ortigas Ave for most of its alignment but is commonly also called an "Aurora Blvd" route because its western San Juan segment touches that corridor.

**Note on Route consolidation:** Under MC 2020-019 (Metro Manila Bus Rationalization Program), Routes 11 (Gilmore–Taytay) and 12 (Pasig–Taytay/Kalentong–Pasig) were consolidated into the new **Route 2 (Angono–Quiapo)**. De Guia Enterprises (under Mega Manila Consortium) is the documented OSM operator for Route 2.

Most Route 2 buses terminate at **Taytay** in practice; full Angono terminus is less frequent.

### Rail Transfer Point

**MRT-3 Ortigas Station** (Mandaluyong/Pasig, at EDSA–Ortigas Ave intersection)
- Median platform between northbound and southbound EDSA Busway
- Served by EDSA Carousel BRT and all EDSA city bus routes
- Connects to jeepney routes along Ortigas Ave heading east (Cainta/Taytay) or west (N. Domingo/Quiapo direction)
- ~8-minute walk to Ortigas Avenue proper from station exit

### Contested/Ambiguous Routes

1. **Route 11 (Gilmore–Taytay) vs Route 2 (Angono–Quiapo):** Multiple sources list Route 11 as still operating, while LTFRB rationalization documents show consolidation into Route 2. Most likely: Route 2 is the official designation for through-service; Route 11 may continue as the common shorthand name for the Gilmore–Taytay segment. **Resolution:** Track as Route 2 with Gilmore–Taytay as a short-turn variant.

2. **Ortigas Ave vs Aurora Blvd for Route 2 western segment:** Google Maps and most transit blogs route the bus via Aurora Blvd (San Juan City portion), while the route is officially named "via Ortigas Avenue." The actual routing appears to use Ortigas Avenue for the Pasig–Cainta–Taytay–Angono segment and Magsaysay Blvd / N. Domingo for the San Juan segment. **Resolution:** Route 2 is primarily an Ortigas Ave corridor route; the Aurora Blvd corridor analysis already cross-referenced this (see validated-aurora-boulevard-corridor.md).

3. **T2105 (Antipolo–Cubao via Felix Ave) vs Marcos Highway corridor:** Felix Avenue connects Santolan LRT-2 to Cainta Junction and is a spur off Ortigas Ave Extension — not the same as Marcos Highway. However the Marcos Highway analysis included this route. **Resolution:** Include in Ortigas corridor (Felix Ave is an extension of Ortigas Ave Extension), cross-listed in Marcos Highway.

## Coverage Gaps

1. **Intra-Ortigas Center shuttle:** No documented route covering the ~2 km between Robinsons Galleria / EDSA and the Tiendesitas / SM East Ortigas area along Ortigas Avenue within Pasig City. This gap is likely filled by multiple informal/unregistered jeepney operators.

2. **San Juan segment of Ortigas:** Specific stop sequence from N. Domingo through San Juan City to Ortigas Ave Pasig is not documented beyond Moovit's general listing.

3. **Cainta Junction UV Express:** The Ortigas-Antipolo UV Express Terminal mentioned in data is 793 m from the main Ortigas Center UV Express Terminal — suggesting two distinct terminals. The Antipolo-direction routes from this terminal are not fully itemized.

4. **Night service:** Most sources give daytime frequency; Route 2 reportedly runs until ~11 PM but off-peak evening headways are unknown.

5. **MRT-4 future impact:** MRT Line 4 is planned to run along Ortigas Avenue from Ortigas CBD toward Taytay — this will substantially restructure the corridor when operational.

## Key Findings

1. **Route 2 (Angono–Quiapo) is the spine of this corridor** — confirmed across 5+ sources with detailed stop sequence (26 stops documented). It is the post-rationalization designation for what was Routes 11 + 12 + Cainta/Taytay–Quiapo.

2. **Ortigas Center is a major multi-modal hub** with MRT-3, UV Express terminal, and P2P buses — but jeepney coverage at the center is fragmented into many short routes.

3. **The Cainta Junction is a critical transfer node** where Ortigas Ave Extension meets Felix Avenue (to Santolan LRT-2) and Manila East Road (to Taytay/Angono). Multiple routes converge here.

4. **Rizal-bound routes (Cainta, Taytay, Angono, Antipolo, Binangonan)** largely use Ortigas Ave Extension as their NCR exit corridor, making this a high-volume provincial gateway corridor.

5. **P2P penetration is high:** Multiple RRCG Transport P2P routes serve the Ortigas corridor (Cainta/Robinsons Cainta–Greenbelt 5, SM Fairview–UnionBank Plaza, Masinag–Megamall–Makati), indicating strong demand for premium express service.
