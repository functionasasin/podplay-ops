## Summary

The Makati Loop comprises two distinct jeepney-type services operating within the Makati Central Business District:

1. **Makati Loop E-Jeepney ("Love Bus")** — Modern electric jeepney launched May 2023 connecting One Ayala and Circuit Makati; LTFRB-franchised, operated in partnership with Ayala Land Inc.
2. **Makati PUJ Loop (Route 541)** — Traditional jeepney loop in the Makati CBD south area (Kalayaan/JP Rizal corridor), pre-existing route documented in Sakay.ph.

Both are **currently operating as of early 2025**. No discontinuation notices were found.

## Geographic Notes

- **One Ayala Terminal**: EDSA/Ayala Ave corner, adjacent to MRT-3 Ayala Station; three-floor intermodal hub
- **Circuit Makati**: Mixed-use development off Kalayaan Ave near EDSA South; Ayala Land property
- The Makati Loop E-Jeepney connects the northern CBD (Ayala/EDSA axis) to the southern CBD (Kalayaan/Circuit area) — functionally an intra-CBD shuttle reducing car use
- The traditional PUJ Loop covers the mid-to-south CBD (Makati Ave / JP Rizal / Kalayaan triangle)

## Relationship to Other Routes

- **BGC-Ayala Bus**: BGC Bus system includes "Ayala Express" route connecting Makati CBD (analyzed separately); different service
- **MRT-3 Ayala Station**: At One Ayala, directly connected to Makati Loop boarding
- **EDSA Busway**: One Ayala is a busway stop — commuters transfer here to/from Makati Loop
