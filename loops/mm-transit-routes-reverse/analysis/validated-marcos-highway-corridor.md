# Marcos Highway Corridor — Cross-Reference & Validation

**Analysis date:** 2026-02-28
**Aspect:** Wave 2 — Marcos Highway corridor
**Scope:** All routes using Marcos Highway from Masinag (Antipolo LRT-2 terminus) westward to Cubao/Santolan, plus Rizal province extensions through Rodriguez (Montalban) and Taytay/Binangonan

---

## Corridor Geography

Marcos Highway runs east–west through:
1. **Santolan LRT-2 Station** (Pasig City, near C5 junction) — western corridor anchor in the NCR rail network
2. **Marikina-Pasig LRT-2 Station** — mid-corridor rail stop (boundary area)
3. **Antipolo (Masinag) LRT-2 Station / SM City Masinag** — eastern LRT-2 terminus, main interchange for deep Antipolo and Rodriguez routes

The corridor bifurcates after SM Masinag:
- **Northeast fork:** Sumulong Highway → Rodriguez (Montalban), Rizal
- **East fork:** Marcos Highway continues → Tikling Junction → Antipolo city proper, Binangonan, Morong, Tanay

**Total NCR-to-Rizal corridor length:** ~22 km from Santolan LRT-2 to SM Masinag; further 10–30 km into Rizal province depending on destination.

## Contested Routes

**Issue 1: Cubao–Antipolo jeepney vs bus routing**
- Community data (blog-9) describes Cubao–Antipolo bus as running via Marcos Highway from Cubao
- Other data suggests Cubao–Antipolo can also run via Felix Ave / Ortigas Ave Extension (T238/T239 corridor)
- **Resolution:** Both routings exist as separate routes. The bus Route 9 likely uses Marcos Highway for the NCR segment. Felix Ave routes serve Cainta/Taytay, not directly Antipolo.

**Issue 2: Rodriguez (Montalban) route numbering**
- LTFRB bus data has "Route 7: Quezon Ave–Montalban" and "Route 8: Cubao–Montalban"
- OSM has "OSM-BUS-34: PITX–Rodriguez" (via Quezon Ave/Commonwealth)
- These appear to be three distinct routes; OSM-BUS-34 routing (PITX→QA→Commonwealth→Rodriguez) does NOT primarily use Marcos Highway
- **Resolution:** Route 8 is the primary Marcos Highway bus to Rodriguez; Route 7 and OSM-34 are different alignments.

**Issue 3: MPUJ-033 Cogeo–Cubao routing**
- LTFRB modernization data lists this modern jeepney as via Aurora Blvd/Katipunan
- Traditional T247 uses Marcos Highway
- **Resolution:** These are confirmed different routes for same O/D. Not a conflict — different path, same endpoints.

## Gap Analysis

### Critical Gaps

1. **No geometry data** for any Marcos Highway route. The highway's alignment is well-known but detailed stop-level geometry needs OSM extraction or field work.

2. **Frequency data sparse:**
 - Santolan–Masinag jeepney: ~5 min headway (confirmed)
 - All bus routes: no headway data
 - UV Express: no headway data
 - All P2P: schedule confirmed only for RRCG SM Masinag route

3. **Tikling Junction routes unrepresented:**
 Tikling is a major transfer point on Marcos Highway toward Binangonan. Routes beyond Tikling (Tanay, Morong, Binangonan) are listed only at origin/destination level, no intermediate stops.

4. **Weekend P2P coverage gap:**
 All P2P routes on this corridor are weekday-only. Weekend commuters from Antipolo/Rizal to Makati have no P2P option — only jeepney or UV Express.

5. **Barangay-level Antipolo access:**
 Routes to specific Antipolo barangays beyond Cogeo/Padilla/Masinag are not documented (e.g., Mayamot, Dalig, San Jose, San Luis, etc.).

6. **San Mateo routes:**
 San Mateo, Rizal is served by a Cubao-Silangan MPUJ (DOTR:R_SAKAY_MPUJ_1144) but the routing via Marcos Highway or an alternate is not confirmed.

### Moderate Gaps

7. **Route 3 (Antipolo–Quiapo) full stop list** — only start/end confirmed
8. **Route 55 (Antipolo–McKinley Hill) routing** — C5 vs Marcos Highway segment unclear
9. **Cainta-EDSA Central UV (UVE-C68) routing** — Felix Ave vs Marcos Highway ambiguous
