# University Shuttle Services — Metro Manila

**Source type**: University websites, transit apps (Sakay.ph, Moovit), news articles, official memoranda
**Retrieved**: 2026-02-28
**Coverage**: UP Diliman (Quezon City), Ateneo de Manila (Quezon City), DLSU Manila (Manila), UST, FEU/Mapua, UP Manila, PUP

---

## Overview

University campus shuttle services in Metro Manila exist on a spectrum: from fully LTFRB-franchised public jeepney routes that happen to loop inside a campus (UP Ikot/Toki), to private electric circulator networks open to visitors (Ateneo), to restricted employee/student-only inter-campus shuttles (DLSU Arrows Express). Only the UP Diliman routes are captured in transit apps or LTFRB route databases.

**Key finding**: The UP Ikot and Toki are the only campus-based services that qualify as de facto public transit — they carry LTFRB franchises, charge standard jeepney fares, appear in Sakay.ph and Moovit, and are open to anyone entering the campus (which has no public access restriction during daytime hours).

## 1. UP Diliman — Ikot Jeepney (LTFRB-Franchised)

**Sakay route ID**: `DOTR:R_SAKAY_PUJ_2156`
**Mode**: PUJ (jeepney)
**Direction**: Counterclockwise loop
**Campus**: University of the Philippines Diliman, Quezon City (~500 ha)

### Route
Closed loop entirely within UP Diliman campus. Stops include:
- CHK (Checkpoint / University Avenue Gate — entry from Commonwealth Ave)
- ISSI, College of Fine Arts, Centennial Dorms, Krus na Ligas
- NIGS / Engineering Library II / CS Library, EEEI, MSI
- AS-CAL, Sampaguita & Kamia Dorms, UPIS, PHAN
- College of Education, Vinzon's Hall, CBA, School of Economics
- College of Law, Asian Center, International Center
- Ilang-Ilang Dorm, UHS (University Health Service), SC (Student Center)
- Area 2, Kalayaan Dorm, Molave & Yakal Dorms
- Bahay ng Alumni, Alumni Center, CSWCD
- Back to CHK

**Route modification (September 2022)**: Jeepneys now exit at Magsaysay Portal and re-enter via Shuster Street, slightly altering the southern portion of the loop.

### Operations
- **Fare**: PHP 13 base (first 4 km), PHP 1.80/km thereafter (LTFRB rates). Older references cite PHP 12 or PHP 6.50 (pre-fare hike).
- **Hours**: 5:00 AM – ~9:00 PM on school days; some sources cite 5:00 AM – 5:00 PM (Sakay.ph listing)
- **Frequency**: High during class hours (multiple jeepneys on circuit); lower evenings/weekends
- **Identifier**: Yellow roof, windshield sign "IKOT"
- **Operator**: LTFRB-franchised PUJ, consolidated under Maroon Riders Transport Cooperative and North UP Campus Transport Cooperative under PUV Modernization Program
- **Access**: Open to all — students, staff, faculty, visitors. Campus accessible to general public during daytime hours.
- **Transit apps**: Listed on Sakay.ph, Moovit. Is GTFS-compatible per Sakay listing.

## 2. UP Diliman — Toki Jeepney (LTFRB-Franchised, Restored 2024)

**Mode**: PUJ (jeepney)
**Direction**: Clockwise loop (reverse of Ikot)
**Campus**: UP Diliman

### Route
Clockwise counterpart to the Ikot. Covers areas the Ikot does not serve well, including:
- CHK → CSWCD → College of Music → CMC → Quezon Hall → Vargas Museum
- CAL → FC → Freshie Walk → College of Engineering
- Molave & Yakal Dorms → Kalayaan Dorm → Area 2 → SC → UHS
- Ilang-Ilang Dorm → International Center → Asian Center → College of Law
- College of Engineering → Freshie Walk → AS → PHAN → College of Education
- UPIS → CHE → Institute of Biology/MBB → Institute of Chemistry → NIP
- C.P. Garcia → **Math** → **OUR (Office of University Registrar)** → NIGS → EEEI → MSI
- AS-CAL → Arki → Fine Arts → ISSI → CHK

Name "Toki" is the reverse of "Ikot" (both mean "loop/circle" in Filipino).

### Operations
- **Restoration**: February 5, 2024, per UP Diliman Office of the Vice Chancellor for Community Affairs (OVCCA) memorandum, following sustained appeals from students and faculty
- **Fare**: PHP 13 (LTFRB rates; historically PHP 6.50 before fare hike)
- **Hours**: Approximately 5:00 AM – 5:00 PM on school days
- **Identifier**: Yellow roof with red lining, windshield sign "TOKI"
- **Operator**: LTFRB-franchised PUJ, same cooperative framework as Ikot
- **Access**: Open to all
- **Transit apps**: Likely listed on Sakay.ph post-restoration (2024); official info at `pages.upd.edu.ph/ikotokipara/home`

## 3. UP Diliman — External Routes Entering Campus

Four LTFRB-franchised PUJ routes connect UP Diliman to surrounding Metro Manila. All are open to the public and appear in Sakay.ph.

| Route | Destination | Roof Color | Approx. Fare | Key Connection |
|-------|------------|------------|--------------|----------------|
| UP-Katipunan | Katipunan Ave / LRT-2 Katipunan Station | Red | ~PHP 8.50 | LRT-2, Ateneo, Miriam |
| UP-Philcoa | Philcoa junction (Commonwealth Ave) | Green | ~PHP 6.50 | Commonwealth Ave jeepneys/buses |
| UP-SM North / UP-Pantranco | SM City North EDSA / Trinoma / MRT-3 QA | Green | ~PHP 10.50 | MRT-3 Quezon Avenue Station |
| UP-MRT | Quezon Avenue MRT-3 Station | Green | ~PHP 10.50 | MRT-3 direct |

**Note**: These routes enter via the University Avenue gate (CHK), loop partially through campus, and exit toward their destinations. They carry separate LTFRB route IDs from the Ikot/Toki.

## 4. Ateneo de Manila University — E-Jeepney Campus Shuttle

**Mode**: Electric jeepney (private university service)
**Campus**: Ateneo de Manila University, Loyola Heights, Quezon City (~86 ha)

### Route
Two circulator lines (Line A and Line B) across campus, 11 stops total:

**Line A (as of February 2025)**:
- Express lane via Gate 1 → JSEC Mall → Old Rizal Library → Xavier Hall → Cervini Hall → Old Communications Building → Loyola House of Studies

**Line B**:
- Areté → Bellarmine Hall → Junior High School Inner Driveway → Formation and Learning Center → Social Developmental Complex

**Recent update**: Line A opened a new express lane on February 6, 2025, improving connection to Gate 1 area.

### Operations
- **Fare**: Free (no charge to any rider)
- **Fleet**: ~10 electric jeepneys, approximately 25 trips/day per line
- **Hours**: Aligned with class/business hours (not specified publicly)
- **Operator**: University-operated; launched in partnership with Meralco Energy Inc. (charging infrastructure). First academic institution in Philippines to operate e-jeepney.
- **Access**: Open to students, employees, staff, and visitors to campus
- **Transit apps**: Not listed in Sakay.ph, Google Maps, or Moovit. Private service, no LTFRB franchise.

**GTFS relevance**: Not capturable as LTFRB route; campus-internal only. Gate connections to Katipunan Avenue are made by public jeepneys/tricycles/Grab outside campus.

## 5. DLSU (De La Salle University) — Arrows Express

**Mode**: Bus/van shuttle (private, restricted)
**Route**: DLSU Manila (Taft Ave / Gil Puyat) ↔ DLSU Laguna Campus (via SLEX)

### Summary
The Arrows Express is an inter-campus shuttle strictly reserved for DLSU students, faculty, and staff with official business at either campus. Advance booking required via the Shuttle Service Reservation System (SSRS). **Not public transit**.

- **Embarkation point (Manila)**: Milagros del Rosario Building (MRR) East Canopy, DLSU Manila
- **Disembarkation (Manila)**: Petron Gasoline Station, Gil Puyat Avenue
- **DLSU Laguna**: E-Jeep shuttle from Laguna Central (near Phoenix Station, SLEX) to Laguna campus, ~30-min intervals from 6 AM
- **Fare**: Line 1 free for students; other lines restricted
- **Transit apps**: Not listed anywhere

**GTFS relevance**: Out of scope — private restricted service between NCR and Laguna. Not public transit.
