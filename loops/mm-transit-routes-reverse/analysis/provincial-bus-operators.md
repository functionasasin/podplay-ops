**Aspect**: Provincial bus operators — Victory Liner, Philtranco, DLTB, JAM, Five Star, Genesis, etc.

**Method**: WebSearch + WebFetch aggregation from operator websites, phbus.com, ph.commutetour.com, Wikipedia, PITX/ACBP terminal guides

---

## Overview

Provincial buses in Metro Manila serve two distinct functions:
1. **Long-haul provincial service** — passengers traveling to/from provinces outside NCR
2. **NCR transit role** — multiple drop/pick-up points within Metro Manila create de facto in-city transit segments

As of late 2024, the distinction between "provincial bus" and "city bus" is regulatory: provincial operators hold CPC from LTFRB for origin-destination pairs that cross NCR boundaries, while city bus operators hold rationalized routes strictly within NCR. However, provincial buses make in-city stops that commuters use routinely (e.g., Cubao↔Pasay via EDSA on a Victory Liner bus).

**LTFRB regulatory note**: MC 2019-031 rerouted northbound provincial buses away from EDSA terminals to the Valenzuela Interim Terminal (NLET) — but many exceptions remain, and EDSA-side terminals are still active as of 2026.

## Terminal Hubs (NCR)

### 1. Cubao Complex (Quezon City) — Primary Northern Hub

The densest provincial bus cluster in Metro Manila. Multiple sub-nodes:

| Sub-Terminal | Location | Primary Operators |
|---|---|---|
| EDSA Northbound strip | EDSA between Aurora Blvd and N. Domingo | Victory Liner, Five Star, Baliwag, Dominion, Viron, Superlines, German Espiritu |
| EDSA Southbound strip | EDSA between Aurora Blvd and N. Domingo | Genesis, DLTB, ALPS, JAC/JAM, Partas, Solid North, Daet Express |
| Araneta City Bus Port (ACBP) | Gen. Romulo Ave, Socorro, QC | Pangasinan Solid North, Philtranco, Amihan, Silver Star, JVH, Megabus, Raymond, RSL, ES Transport |
| Kamias terminal | EDSA cor. Mapagmahal St. | JAC Liner (main QC hub) |
| Dagupan Bus Terminal | New York Ave cor. EDSA | Dagupan Bus Co., Fariñas Transit |

EDSA Carousel: **Cubao - Main Ave** and **Cubao - Nepa Q-Mart** stops (2 stops) serve as de facto express connection to Pasay/PITX for provincial bus passengers.

### 3. PITX — Parañaque Integrated Terminal Exchange (Primary Southern Hub)

Opened November 2018; 4.5 hectares, largest bus terminal in Philippines. 50+ operators, 200+ destinations. Effectively replaced many Pasay EDSA-strip terminals for southbound provincial routes.

Key operators:
- DLTB Co. (Batangas, Laguna, Quezon, Bicol, Leyte)
- JAM Liner / JAC Liner (Laguna, Batangas, Lucena)
- Bicol Isarog Transport System (Bicol, Leyte, Palawan)
- Philtranco (Bicol, Visayas, Mindanao) — Amihan/subsidiary also here
- Saulog Transit (Cavite, Olongapo)
- Dela Rosa Transit (Batangas)
- ALPS Bus (P2P + provincial connections)
- Genesis Transport (Clark P2P, Baguio P2P via PITX)
- Solid North Transit (Pangasinan, Baguio)

PITX gate assignments: Gate 1 (Cavite), Gate 2 (Visayas/Mindanao/Mindoro), Gate 9 (North Metro Manila + Bulacan provincial), Ticket Booth 1 (Bicol/Visayas/Mindanao), Ticket Booth 6 (Quezon/Laguna/Pangasinan/Tuguegarao/Baguio).

## NCR Corridor Role of Provincial Buses

Provincial buses make multiple stops within NCR that commuters use for in-city travel. Key in-NCR segments:

| Corridor | Operators | Passenger Behavior |
|---|---|---|
| Monumento → Cubao → Pasay (EDSA) | Victory Liner, Five Star, DLTB, etc. | Regular commuters use for EDSA express |
| Cubao → Avenida/Quiapo | JAC, DLTB, Partas | Manila inner-city segment |
| Pasay → PITX (via EDSA/Seaside) | DLTB, JAC, Bicol Isarog | Last-mile to airport area |
| Sampaloc → Cubao (Aurora Blvd) | Partas, Fariñas, GV Florida | East-west Manila-QC link |

These in-NCR provincial bus stop pairs are **not systematically documented** in any existing source but are well-known commuter practices. LTFRB provincial CPCs do not list in-city stops — only origin/destination terminals.

## Data Quality Assessment

- **Best sources**: Operator websites (victoryliner.com, dltb.online, genesisjoybus.com, partasbus.com), phbus.com aggregator, ph.commutetour.com
- **Geometry**: NOT available for any provincial bus routes from open sources
- **Stops within NCR**: Only terminals known; intermediate NCR stops undocumented
- **Frequency**: Peak/off-peak not consistently reported; most operators run continuous service (provincial buses have scheduled departures, not headways)
- **GTFS applicability**: Provincial routes require separate GTFS agency entries + route files; in-NCR stops would need field survey to populate stop_times.txt accurately
