Pateros, the only municipality in NCR, is the smallest unit analyzed.

## City 1: Marikina City

### Geography

Marikina City (21.52 km², ~450,000 pop.) lies east of Quezon City, bounded by Rodriguez/Rizal province to the north, Antipolo to the east, Pasig to the south, and QC to the west. The Marikina River bisects the city north–south. Key districts: Parang, Calumpang, Fortune, SSS Village, Concepcion, Tumana, Industrial Valley Complex (IVC).

**Key roads:**
- **Marcos Highway / Sumulong Highway:** Primary E–W arterial connecting to Antipolo and Rizal province; LRT-2 elevated above this road
- **Aurora Boulevard (Calumpang segment):** Western approach from QC into Marikina; LRT-2 Marikina-Pasig Station
- **P. Tuazon Boulevard:** Secondary E–W corridor parallel to Aurora Blvd; used by intra-Marikina jeepney routes
- **Shoe Avenue / A. Bonifacio Ave. (Marikina):** Internal north-south artery through commercial center

**Rail in Marikina:**
- **LRT-2 (Purple Line):** Marikina-Pasig Station — on Aurora Blvd/Marcos Highway boundary; 5:30 AM–10:30 PM; ~3–5 min peak frequency

## City 2: San Juan City

### Geography

San Juan City (5.94 km², ~120,000 pop.) is one of NCR's smallest cities, bordered by Manila (west), QC (north, east), and Mandaluyong (south). Key areas: Greenhills Shopping Center, Pinaglabanan, Balong Bato, N. Domingo, Elliptical/Camp Crame area (north boundary), Agora Market.

**Key roads:**
- **Aurora Boulevard (R-6):** Northern boundary; primary jeepney/bus corridor; LRT-2 elevated above
- **N. Domingo Street:** Primary commercial north-south artery; Agora Market terminus
- **Ortigas Avenue:** Southern boundary shared with Mandaluyong/Pasig
- **F. Blumentritt Ave (QC portion):** Northern boundary near LRT-2 J. Ruiz Station
- **A. Blumentritt / N. Domingo extension:** Connection to Manila (Sta. Mesa direction)

**Rail in San Juan:**
- **LRT-2 (Purple Line):** J. Ruiz Station — on Aurora Blvd/F. Blumentritt Ave; northern San Juan boundary with QC
- **MRT-3 (Blue Line):** Santolan-Annapolis Station — at EDSA/Santolan; eastern boundary with QC, serves Greenhills access

## City 3: Pasay City

### Geography

Pasay City (13.97 km², ~360,000 pop.) is Metro Manila's airport gateway city, bounded by Manila (north), Makati (east), Parañaque (south), and Manila Bay (west). It contains NAIA Terminals 1, 2, 3 (the main airport areas), Baclaran (south), and the EDSA-Taft transit megahub.

**Key roads:**
- **EDSA (C-4):** Primary N-S arterial; carries MRT-3, EDSA Carousel; bisects Pasay from north to south
- **Taft Avenue / F.B. Harrison St:** Parallel to EDSA; LRT-1 spine; major jeepney corridor
- **Roxas Boulevard (R-2):** Coastal highway along Manila Bay; connects to PITX
- **Gil Puyat Avenue (Buendia):** E-W arterial at Manila/Makati border; LRT-1 Gil Puyat Station; provincial bus hub
- **Andrews Avenue / MIA Road:** Airport access roads in central Pasay
- **Quirino Avenue / Airport Road:** South-central Pasay connects to airport and Parañaque

**Rail in Pasay:**
- **LRT-1 (Green Line):** Gil Puyat, Libertad, EDSA, Baclaran, Redemptorist-Aseana (new, Nov 2024) — 5 stations
- **MRT-3 (Blue Line):** Taft Avenue Station (southern terminus) — co-located with LRT-1 EDSA Station

### Coverage Gaps — Pasay

1. **NAIA Terminal connectivity fragmented**: Nichols loop jeepney connects NAIA 3→MRT Taft but the airport area has no systematic feeder to LRT-1 stations (only EDSA via shuttle bus).
2. **F.B. Harrison / Malibay interior**: The Malibay barangay between NAIA and Taft Ave has limited documented regular transit; T3201 (Libertad–Malibay) is the only coded route.
3. **Roxas Boulevard feeder to Baclaran**: Multiple PITX-bound buses use Roxas Blvd but no jeepney route documented along the full Roxas Blvd Pasay segment.
4. **Night service gaps**: After LRT-1 ceases (~11PM), Baclaran area feeder routes are largely undocumented for late-night coverage.
5. **Route proliferation at Libertad**: 10+ distinct Libertad-terminus routes exist (427, 428, 431–438, etc.) but many have overlapping corridors; deduplication and current operational status unverified.

## City 4: Malabon City

### Geography

Malabon City (15.96 km², ~365,000 pop.) is in NCR's northwest, bounded by Navotas (west), Caloocan (east), and Valenzuela (north). No direct rail access. The city is traversed by **MacArthur Highway (C-3)** and **Gov. Pascual Avenue**, connecting it to Caloocan (south) and Valenzuela (north). The Malabon River and esteros create geographic constraints on road layout.

**Key roads:**
- **MacArthur Highway (C-3):** Primary N-S spine; connects Caloocan/Monumento (south) to Valenzuela (north) and Bulacan
- **Gov. Pascual Avenue:** Western connector toward Navotas
- **Letre Road:** E-W connector from MacArthur Hwy to Monumento (key jeepney corridor)
- **Acacia Road:** Parallel E-W connector to Monumento (alternate jeepney alignment)
- **Don Bastilio Bautista Blvd:** Northern Malabon, toward Obando/Bulacan

**No rail service in Malabon.** Nearest stations: LRT-1 Monumento (~3 km east via Letre Road).

## City 5: Navotas City

### Geography

Navotas City (10.77 km², ~250,000 pop.) is Metro Manila's smallest city, situated on Manila Bay's eastern edge, northwest of Manila, bounded by Malabon (east/north) and Manila Bay (west). It is a fishing/port city. The **MMDA Navotas Bus Terminal** (C-4 Road) is the city's primary transit anchor, serving long-haul routes.

**Key roads:**
- **H. Lopez Blvd (C-4 Road / Circumferential Road 4):** The primary N-S artery; main bus corridor; Navotas City Hall vicinity
- **M. Naval Street:** Internal E-W connector; terminal area
- **Gov. Pascual Avenue:** Connects south toward Malabon and Manila (Divisoria)

**No rail service in Navotas.** Nearest: LRT-1 Monumento (~3 km via MacArthur/Letre).

### Coverage Gaps — Navotas

1. **No rail access** to Navotas; the city is entirely dependent on buses and jeepneys.
2. **Long-haul bus dominance**: Navotas Bus Terminal routes run to Alabang, Taguig, Parañaque, Cavite — but no short-haul city bus to key Manila hubs (Quiapo, Divisoria) directly from the terminal.

## City 6: Valenzuela City

### Geography

Valenzuela City (44.59 km², ~620,000 pop.) is NCR's northernmost city, bordering Bulacan province (north/west). It is a major industrial city traversed by **MacArthur Highway** and accessed via the **North Luzon Expressway (NLEx)**. **Valenzuela Gateway Complex (VGC)** at Paso de Blas is the city's primary transit hub.

**Key roads:**
- **MacArthur Highway (C-3):** Primary N-S spine through the city; connects to Caloocan (south), Malabon, and Bulacan (north)
- **North Luzon Expressway (NLEx):** Toll expressway with Karuhatan, Malinta, and Paso de Blas exits
- **NLEx East Service Road (Paso de Blas):** Location of VGC
- **Mindanao Avenue:** Connects to Quezon City; used by VGC bus routes

**No rail service in Valenzuela.** Nearest: LRT-1 Monumento (~6 km from Karuhatan via MacArthur Hwy).

### Coverage Gaps — Valenzuela

## City 7: Pateros (Municipality)

### Geography

Pateros (2.10 km², ~65,000 pop.) is Metro Manila's only municipality — the smallest geographic unit in NCR. It is completely surrounded by Taguig City (south/west/east) and Pasig City (north), bisected by the **Pateros River**. Key streets: M. Almeda, B. Morcilla, Elizco Road. The **balut (duck egg) industry** is historically concentrated here.

**No rail service in Pateros.** Nearest: **MRT-3 Guadalupe Station** (~1 km from Pateros center, Taguig/Makati border).

## Key Findings

1. **CAMANAVA's rail gap is Metro Manila's worst transit equity problem.** Malabon (~365K), Navotas (~250K), and Valenzuela (~620K) — 1.2 million residents combined — have ZERO rail access. Their transit consists entirely of MacArthur Highway jeepneys, C-4 Road buses, and the VGC terminal. This is structurally inadequate given the population.

2. **Navotas is Metro Manila's most transit-isolated city relative to size.** Its only rail connection to the PNR (Blumentritt–Gov. Pascual) has been suspended since 2023. The MMDA Navotas Bus Terminal is the sole formal transit anchor. Commutes from Navotas to Makati/BGC require 2+ hours via bus.

3. **Valenzuela Gateway Complex (VGC) is critical northern infrastructure.** Routes 50, 51, and 52 originate at VGC — it functions as a provincial terminal equivalent for northern NCR. Its bus connections to Alabang, Cubao, and PITX make it the backbone of Valenzuela's transit.

4. **Pasay is Metro Manila's most transit-dense city.** With 13 confirmed city bus routes passing through, 2 LRT-1 + MRT-3 rail lines, EDSA Carousel BRT, 20+ jeepney routes, and all major provincial bus terminals, Pasay has the highest modal diversity in the database.

5. **San Juan and Pateros are transit-minimal cities.** Both rely on surrounding cities' hubs. San Juan's Agora Market terminal and Greenhills feeder are undersized for the commuter demand. Pateros has no bus terminal, no ferry, and only tricycle internal access.

6. **Marikina benefits from LRT-2 Antipolo extension.** The 2024 LRT-2 extension to Antipolo dramatically extended Marikina's rail coverage. Santolan–Masinag jeepney (5 min headway) acts as a surface shuttle extending LRT-2 further east.

7. **Route 3 (Antipolo–Quiapo) is the single backbone route serving both Marikina and San Juan.** It is the only city bus documented to continuously serve both cities. Reliance on a single route is a significant vulnerability.

8. **MacArthur Highway is Metro Manila's most transit-dense corridor outside EDSA.** Malabon, Valenzuela, and northern Caloocan all depend on this single arterial. Routes 13, 14, 35, 37, 42 and dozens of jeepney franchises use it. Any MacArthur Hwy disruption causes system-wide CAMANAVA transit failure.
