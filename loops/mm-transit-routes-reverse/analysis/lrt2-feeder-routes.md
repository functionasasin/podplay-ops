# LRT-2 Feeder Routes — Jeepney/Bus Connections per Station

**Aspect**: Specialized Data — LRT-2 feeder routes
**Date analyzed**: 2026-02-28
**Method**: Web research via search and article scraping

---

## Overview

LRT Line 2 (Purple Line / Megatren) runs east–west from **Antipolo (Masinag) station** in Rizal Province to **Recto station** in Manila. As of 2026, the line has **13 stations** covering ~17.6 km. The East Extension (Santolan→Marikina→Antipolo) opened July 2021. Daily ridership ~440,000.

Operating hours: 5:00 AM–9:30 PM (first train departs both termini at 5:00 AM; last from Antipolo 9:00 PM, from Recto 9:30 PM as of Jan 2026). Fare: ₱13 min / ₱33 max (beep card); ₱15 / ₱35 (SJT). 50% concession for students, seniors, PWDs.

This document catalogs jeepney, bus, UV Express, and other road-based transport connecting to each station.

## Station-by-Station Feeder Inventory

### 1. Antipolo Station (Eastern Terminus)
- **City**: Antipolo, Rizal Province (first LRT station outside NCR)
- **Major roads**: Marikina-Infanta Highway at Sumulong Highway intersection
- **Adjacent facilities**: SM City Masinag (connected), East Gate Business Center & Terminal Center (walkway)
- **Feeder modes**: Jeepney, e-jeep, tricycle, bus
- **Key feeder routes** (from SM Masinag Terminal):
  - Antipolo city proper ↔ Antipolo LRT (jeepney, ₱10–₱40 min/max)
  - Bagong Nayon ↔ Antipolo LRT
  - Buso Buso ↔ Antipolo LRT
  - **Cogeo** ↔ Antipolo LRT (main feeder for Upper Antipolo/Cogeo Village)
  - **Cubao** ↔ Antipolo (long-distance jeepney, via Marcos Hwy → Aurora Blvd)
  - Henan ↔ Antipolo LRT
  - Marikina ↔ Antipolo LRT
  - Padilla ↔ Antipolo LRT (also served by e-jeeps from East Gate Terminal)
- **Bus routes**: QCityBus Route 3 (Antipolo→Quiapo, free) originates near here; Philippine Rabbit and Genesis buses to/from Cubao
- **Notes**: Eastern terminus of LRT-2; sole rail access point for commuters from Cogeo, Padilla, upper Antipolo, Teresa, Morong, and Tanay. Future Cogeo extension planned (JICA Phase 2 proposal). Station has provisions for continuation at elevated height.
- **Confidence**: High

### 2. Marikina–Pasig Station
- **City**: San Roque, Marikina (near Pasig–Cainta tripoint boundary)
- **Major roads**: Marcos Highway / Felix Avenue (Imelda Ave) at Gil Fernando Ave intersection
- **Adjacent facilities**: Robinsons Metro East (jeepney terminal inside); Sta. Lucia East Grand Mall (separate jeepney terminal)
- **Feeder modes**: Jeepney, UV Express, bus
- **Key feeder routes**:
  - **T238**: Cainta, Rizal → Araneta Cubao (via Ortigas Ave. Ext.)
  - **T239**: Cubao → Taytay, Rizal
  - **T247**: Cogeo, Rizal → Araneta Cubao (via Marcos Hwy)
  - **T261**: Marikina–Pasig via Dela Paz
  - **T242**: Calumpang → LRT-2 Katipunan (via Aurora Blvd)
  - **T243**: Marikina → Pateros via Pasig, San Antonio
  - Cainta Junction → Cubao (via Felix Ave/Ortigas Ave. Ext.)
- **Bus routes**:
  - QCityBus Route 3 (Antipolo→Quiapo, free) — stops at Sta. Lucia East Grand Mall
- **Key destinations reachable**: Cainta, Taytay, Angono, Binangonan (Rizal), Pasig City (Ortigas Ext. corridor)
- **Notes**: Gateway station for Rizal Province commuters. Sta. Lucia East Grand Mall is a major jeepney hub in the area. T238 and T247 are the primary Rizal-to-Cubao routes passing through this station catchment.
- **Confidence**: High

### 3. Santolan Station
- **City**: Santolan, Pasig (near Calumpang, Marikina boundary)
- **Major roads**: Marikina–Infanta Highway / Marcos Highway
- **Adjacent facilities**: BFCT East Metro Manila Transport Terminal; SM City Marikina (footbridge to Riverbanks Center); Riverbanks Center
- **Feeder modes**: Jeepney, UV Express, taxi, Grab
- **Nearest stop**: College of Arts & Sciences of Asia & the Pacific, Marcos Hwy (~114 m, 2-min walk)
- **Key feeder routes**:
  - Cainta Junction → Cubao (via Ortigas Ave. Ext.)
  - Calumpang → Cubao (via 20th Ave.)
  - Calumpang → Stop & Shop (via Aurora Blvd.)
  - Antipolo City → General Romulo Ave./Aurora Blvd. (via Marcos Hwy)
  - From Cainta/Taytay/Angono/Binangonan: PUJ to Cubao, Sta. Lucia Mall, Q-Plaza/Tropical, or LRT Santolan via Manila East Rd. → Ortigas Ave. Ext. → Felix Ave.
  - From Cogeo/Padilla/Lower Antipolo: PUJ to Cubao, Sta. Mesa, Marikina–Gil Fernando, or LRT Santolan via Marcos Hwy/Felix Ave.
  - From Pasig City: PUJ via Ortigas Ave. to Cainta Junction → to Felix Ave.
- **Notes**: Major transfer point for eastern Rizal Province. First trip arrives ~6:00 AM, last ~1:26 AM. Riverbanks footbridge gives direct mall access. BFCT terminal serves as staging area.
- **Confidence**: High

### 4. Katipunan Station (Underground)
- **City**: Loyola Heights, Quezon City
- **Major roads**: Katipunan Avenue (C-5) / Aurora Boulevard
- **Special note**: Only underground station on LRT-2
- **Feeder modes**: Jeepney, tricycle, bus
- **Terminal location**: Under the Katipunan flyover (exit North, near Ministop)
- **Key feeder routes**:
  - **UP-Katipunan jeepney** → UP Diliman (enters campus; use queue near Ministop/closer queue)
  - **UP-Tandang Sora jeepney** → Does NOT enter UP campus (second queue, farther from Ministop)
  - **Katipunan → UP via Philcoa** → connects to Fairview-bound PUVs at Philcoa on Commonwealth Ave.
  - Internal UP Diliman campus routes (board inside campus after UP-Katipunan jeep):
    - **Ikot** — circles UP campus (yellow roof)
    - **Toki** — shorter campus circuit
    - **Katipunan** — to UP Town Center, Miriam College, Ateneo (red roof)
    - **SM North** — to SM City North EDSA / Trinoma (green w/ white lining)
    - **Philcoa** — to Philcoa on Commonwealth (connects to Fairview, Balara)
    - **MRT** — to Quezon Ave. MRT Station (green roof)
- **Bus routes**:
  - QCityBus Route 3 (Antipolo–Quiapo, free) — stops on Katipunan Ave.
  - QCityBus Route 7 (QC Hall–Fairview via Katipunan, free) — stops on Katipunan Ave.
- **Key destinations reachable**: UP Diliman, Ateneo de Manila University (walkable across Katipunan Ave.), Miriam College, Fairview (via UP Philcoa + Commonwealth), SM North EDSA, MRT Quezon Ave.
- **Notes**: Top feeder usage is UP Diliman access. Ateneo is walkable (left of Katipunan Ave. after exiting north). Tricycles available at Esteban Abada intersection. Two jeepney queues at terminal — ask dispatcher which enters campus.
- **Confidence**: High (multiple corroborating sources)

### 5. Anonas Station
- **City**: Quezon City (Project 3/Anonas area)
- **Major roads**: Anonas Street at Aurora Boulevard
- **Feeder modes**: Jeepney, tricycle, bus
- **Key feeder routes**:
  - Jeepney and tricycle terminals near station for QC interior destinations
  - Buses along Anonas Street (24/7)
- **Bus routes**:
  - QCityBus Route 3 (Antipolo–Quiapo, free) — stops on Anonas/Aurora
- **Key destinations reachable**: Sikatuna Village, Project 3/4, QC Memorial Circle area
- **Future connection**: Planned Metro Manila Subway Station 4 (Anonas) will intersect here
- **Notes**: Relatively low feeder documentation vs. other stations. Near several medical establishments and educational institutions. Buses on Anonas St. run around the clock.
- **Confidence**: Medium

### 6. Araneta Center–Cubao Station
- **City**: Quezon City (Cubao, Araneta City)
- **Major roads**: Aurora Boulevard / EDSA intersection; Gateway Mall
- **Rail transfer**: MRT-3 Cubao Station (via Gateway Mall → Farmer's Plaza walkway)
- **Feeder modes**: Jeepney, city bus, P2P bus, provincial bus, UV Express
- **Key feeder routes**:
  - QCityBus Route 1 (QC Hall → Cubao, free) — near Araneta Center
  - All routes from Cubao terminal complex (documented separately): Victory Liner, RRCG, Genesis provincial buses; dozens of jeepney routes
  - UBE Express P2P (NAIA 1/2/3 ↔ Araneta City Bus Port)
  - Jeepney routes from Cainta, Antipolo, Marikina via Marcos Hwy/Aurora Blvd.
  - QCityBus Routes (1, 2, 5) converge at or near Cubao
- **Key destinations reachable**: All Metro Manila destinations (major hub); Bulacan, Pampanga, Pangasinan, Ilocos via provincial buses
- **Notes**: One of the largest multimodal hubs in Metro Manila. Full Cubao terminal inventory documented in separate `cubao-terminal.md`. UBE Express Araneta bus port is a key P2P NAIA feeder.
- **Confidence**: High

### 7. Betty Go-Belmonte Station
- **City**: Mariana, Quezon City
- **Major roads**: Aurora Boulevard at Betty Go-Belmonte / N. Domingo intersection
- **Nearest stop**: Aurora Blvd / Betty Go-Belmonte Intersection (~2-min walk)
- **Feeder modes**: Jeepney
- **Key feeder routes**:
  - **Murphy → North Harbor** via Divisoria, Sta. Mesa (passes Aurora Blvd.)
  - **Divisoria → Sta. Mesa** via R. Magsaysay
  - **Cubao (Arayat) → V. Luna** via Ybardolaza
  - P.A. Bernardo, Quezon City routes
- **Key destinations reachable**: Murphy, Divisoria, Sta. Mesa, North Harbor; V. Luna area (Quezon Institute, Veterans Memorial)
- **Notes**: First trip at 7:00 AM; last at 1:27 AM. Formerly called Boston Station (before renaming). Less prominent as a feeder hub — mostly local QC catchment.
- **Confidence**: Medium

### 8. Gilmore Station
- **City**: Mariana, Quezon City
- **Major roads**: Aurora Boulevard at N. Domingo / Dona Juana Rodriguez Ave.
- **Nearest stops**: Dona Juana S. Rodriguez Ave. (5-min walk); Ramon Magsaysay Blvd intersection
- **Feeder modes**: Jeepney, tricycle
- **Key feeder routes**:
  - **Calumpang → Stop & Shop** via Aurora Blvd. (q5–15 min)
  - **Project 4 (Yale) → Stop & Shop**
  - Jeepney to **Cubao, Eastwood City, and Divisoria** (q5–15 min, fare ₱12 base 2025)
- **Bus routes**: QCityBus stops in vicinity
- **Key destinations reachable**: Eastwood City (Libis), Cubao, Divisoria; Calumpang–Marikina corridor via Aurora Blvd.
- **Notes**: First JEEP at 6:00 AM; last at 1:22 AM. ~1.5 km west of Cubao MRT-3/LRT-2 interchange. Modest feeder catchment; primarily local Aurora Blvd. corridor service.
- **Confidence**: Medium

### 11. Pureza Station
- **City**: Santa Mesa, Manila
- **Major roads**: Ramon Magsaysay Boulevard (near Pureza Street)
- **Adjacent transit**: PNR Sta. Mesa Station (~1 km walk, historically — PNR suspended since March 2024); PUP Pasig River Ferry Port (nearby)
- **Feeder modes**: Jeepney, tricycle
- **Key feeder routes**:
  - **Murphy → North Harbor** via Divisoria, Sta. Mesa (R. Magsaysay Blvd)
  - **Divisoria → Sta. Mesa** via R. Magsaysay
  - **Quiapo (Barbosa) → Santol** via Sta. Mesa
  - **Punta → Quiapo (Barbosa)** via Sta. Mesa
  - **Divisoria → Punta** via Sta. Mesa
  - Jeepney to Tayuman (alighting point for UST via tricycle)
- **Key destinations reachable**: Divisoria, Murphy, North Harbor, Quiapo; PUP (Polytechnic University of the Philippines — ~1.5 km, 19-min walk or short tricycle)
- **Notes**: Primary access point for PUP students. UST students also use this station (jeep/tricycle to Tayuman). Accessible to Pasig River Ferry at PUP. Historical PNR connection at Sta. Mesa Station (suspended 2024).
- **Confidence**: High (well-documented PUP/UST commuter use)

### 12. Legarda Station
- **City**: Sampaloc, Manila
- **Major roads**: Legarda Street / Ramon Magsaysay Boulevard
- **Feeder modes**: Jeepney, tricycle, UV Express, bus
- **Nearest stop**: Jollibee Figueras Legarda / Bustillos Intersection (~73 m, 2-min walk)
- **Key feeder routes**:
  - **Proj. 2 & 3 → Recto** via Kamuning, E. Rodriguez (every ~15 min)
  - **Divisoria → Murphy** via Legarda, Aurora Blvd.
  - Lacson Ave. jeepney to UST (alight before Lacson Ave.; short walk or tricycle)
  - Jeepney/bus along Mendiola Street, Legarda Street, Recto Avenue
  - UV Express along Recto Avenue
- **Key destinations reachable**: University Belt (UE, San Sebastian, CEU, La Consolacion, Holy Spirit, San Beda, UST); Sampaloc, Quiapo; Divisoria/R. Magsaysay corridor
- **Notes**: One of the busiest LRT-2 stations. San Beda University is walkable from south entrance. UST accessible via Lacson Ave. jeep. Seven university-belt schools cluster within 1 km. Near Doroteo Jose LRT-1 station (~113 m, 2-min walk).
- **Confidence**: High

### 13. Recto Station (Western Terminus)
- **City**: Santa Cruz / Quiapo boundary, Manila
- **Major roads**: Claro M. Recto Avenue
- **Rail transfer**: LRT-1 Doroteo Jose Station (covered walkway, ~113 m)
- **Feeder modes**: Jeepney, bus, UV Express, tricycle
- **Key feeder routes**:
  - **Murphy → North Harbor** via Divisoria, Sta. Mesa (passes Recto area)
  - **Blumentritt → Pasay RTDA** via Quiapo, L. Guinto
  - **Cubao → Divisoria** via Aurora Blvd., R. Magsaysay, Legarda, CM Recto
  - **Gastambide → Divisoria** via Morayta, CM Recto, Soler
  - **Navotas → Divisoria** via Monumento, Rizal Ave., Abad Santos, CM Recto
  - **T3169: Recto → Roxas District** via Quezon Ave., España Ave. (passes UST)
  - **T3166: Punta → Quiapo (Barbosa)** via Sta. Mesa
  - **T3119: Munoz → Remedios** via Quiapo, L. Guinto
  - **T3114: Divisoria → Navotas** via H. Lopez
  - **T3170: Divisoria → Gasak** via H. Lopez
- **Bus routes**: Avenida Bus Terminal accessible from Recto
- **Key destinations reachable**: Divisoria (Isetann/Tutuban, walkable or short jeep); Quiapo (~0.5 km walk via Evangelista St.); España/UST (T3169); Roxas District; Monumento/Navotas/Caloocan (via Rizal Ave. routes)
- **Future**: LRT-2 West Extension planned to Tutuban → Divisoria → Pier 4 (right-of-way acquisition underway 2025)
- **Notes**: Western terminus and highest daily footfall station. Direct covered walkway to LRT-1 Doroteo Jose. Isetann Cinerama exit nearest to Quiapo. Odeon Terminal Mall, QQ Mall Quiapo, Raon Shopping Center nearby.
- **Confidence**: High

## Cross-Station Patterns

### Aurora Boulevard / R. Magsaysay Corridor Routes
The following jeepney routes serve multiple LRT-2 stations along the Aurora Blvd. / R. Magsaysay corridor (Betty Go-Belmonte through Recto):
- **Murphy ↔ North Harbor** via Divisoria, Sta. Mesa
- **Divisoria ↔ Sta. Mesa** via R. Magsaysay
- **Divisoria ↔ Murphy** via Legarda, Aurora Blvd.
- **Calumpang ↔ Stop & Shop** via Aurora Blvd.
- **SSS Village ↔ Stop & Shop** via Aurora Blvd.

### Eastern Corridor (Santolan–Marikina–Antipolo)
QCityBus Route 3 (Antipolo→Anonas→Katipunan→Cubao→Quiapo) is the primary organized bus feeder for the eastern stations, operating for free. For east Rizal Province (Cainta, Taytay, Cogeo, Angono, Teresa), T238 and T247 are the primary jeepney feeders.

### Katipunan / UP Diliman Hub
Katipunan station has the highest feeder complexity due to UP Diliman's internal route network. Six distinct campus routes (Ikot, Toki, Katipunan, SM North, Philcoa, MRT) radiate from campus to LRT-2 Katipunan.
