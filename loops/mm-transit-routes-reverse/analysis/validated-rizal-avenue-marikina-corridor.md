# Rizal Avenue / Marikina Corridor — Cross-Reference & Validation

**Analysis date:** 2026-02-28
**Aspect:** Wave 2 — Rizal Avenue / Marikina corridor
**Scope:** All routes using Rizal Avenue or its extension (A. Bonifacio Ave) through Caloocan and Manila; Malabon/Navotas connections via Letre/Acacia; and Marikina city routes not captured in the Aurora Boulevard or Marcos Highway validated corridors

---

## Corridor Geography

The "Rizal Avenue / Marikina" label actually describes **two overlapping geographic contexts** that this analysis treats as one aspect:

### 1. Rizal Avenue Corridor (Core N–S Spine)
**Rizal Avenue** is the historic north–south arterial connecting Manila with Caloocan. It is also called **A. Bonifacio Avenue** in northern Caloocan / Monumento and continues south as **Rizal Avenue Extension** through Caloocan before entering Manila proper.

**North terminus:** Monumento Circle (Caloocan) — at LRT-1 Monumento Station, Victory Liner terminal, and several jeepney terminals
**South terminus:** Blumentritt (Manila) — at LRT-1 Blumentritt Station — and continues toward Divisoria, Quiapo, Taft
**Total length (Monumento to Quiapo):** ~8 km
**Key nodes:**
1. Monumento Circle — major LRT-1 northern terminus, bus/jeepney hub
2. MCU (Manila Central University, Caloocan)
3. Sangandaan — Caloocan intermediate hub
4. Blumentritt — LRT-1 station; junction with Dimasalang/Dangwa corridor
5. Chinese General Hospital (end of Blumentritt Rd)
6. Divisoria — major market and bus/jeepney transfer point
7. Recto / CM Recto Avenue — junction with Quiapo/Manila
8. Quiapo — southern anchor

**Major cross-streets:** Samson Road, 8th Ave West, Rizal Ave Extension, Felix Huertas, Oroquieta, Evangelista, Blumentritt Road, Dimasalang, R. Magsaysay Blvd, C.M. Recto

### 2. Malabon / Navotas Connections (CAMANAVA West)
Malabon City and Navotas City are immediately west of Caloocan and do not lie on Rizal Avenue but connect to the Monumento hub via **Letre Road** and **Acacia Road** (perpendicular to Rizal Ave Ext). The following T-series routes serve this sub-corridor.

### 3. Marikina Routes (Internal and Unassigned)
The Aurora Boulevard analysis (Run 70) covered major Marikina–Cubao jeepney and UV Express routes. The Marcos Highway analysis (Run 71) covered Marikina–Antipolo and Marikina–eastern Rizal routes. This aspect covers **Marikina internal routes** and any Cubao-direction routes not captured there, specifically: intra-Marikina circulation (T215, T217) and Cubao–Parang via P. Tuazon (T271), which use a different corridor than Aurora Blvd.

## Deduplication Notes

1. **T104 vs T115 (both Malabon–Monumento):** These are confirmed distinct routes with different alignments — T104 uses Letre Road, T115 uses Acacia Road. Both are HIGH confidence per LTFRB and terminal sources. Not duplicates.

2. **T217 (Calumpang–Cubao) vs T242 (Calumpang–Katipunan):** These are different routes. T217 uses P. Tuazon corridor (through Cubao interior); T242 uses Aurora Blvd westbound to LRT-2 Katipunan. Same origin, different paths and destinations.

3. **T286 (Parang–Stop & Shop via Aurora)** vs **T271 (Cubao–Parang via P. Tuazon):** Different corridors. T286/287 use Aurora Blvd; T271 uses P. Tuazon. Both serve Parang–Cubao O/D but via different routes. All three versions (T271, T286, MODERN-018) likely operate concurrently.

4. **Blumentritt–Baclaran routes (T378 vs BLUM-LIBERTAD variants):** T378 is confirmed with OSM geometry (via Mabini). The L. Guinto variants are documented in Sakay and community sources but lack geometry. These are separate routes — different street alignments.

5. **Route 17 (city bus) vs Monumento–Pasay jeepney:** Both use Rizal Ave but are distinct modes, separate LTFRB franchises, and complementary (bus serves EDSA Taft terminus; jeepney serves P. Villanueva terminus, further south).

## Key Gaps

1. **No GPS stop coordinates** for any route in this corridor. All stops are name-only references from Moovit/Sakay text data.

2. **No modern PUJ replacements confirmed** for core Rizal Ave jeepneys (Monumento–Pasay, Monumento–Vito Cruz, etc.). The Novaliches–Blumentritt modern PUJ (15 units, Nova-Blumentritt Coop) is the only confirmed modern jeepney on this corridor.

3. **BLUM-NOVALICHES-MODERN modernization status unclear:** The 15 units (PNA-confirmed) covers only the Novaliches–Blumentritt segment. The longer Monumento–Pasay corridor is not known to have been fully modernized.

4. **Malabon internal routes underdocumented:** Routes within Malabon city (not connecting to Monumento or Divisoria) are not captured. The CAMANAVA sub-area (Caloocan/Malabon/Navotas/Valenzuela) has many local short-haul jeepneys not in public databases.

5. **Frequency data absent:** Only the Monumento–Pasay jeepney has any frequency estimate ("every 5–10 min; high frequency corridor" from Moovit data label). All others are unknown.

6. **Night service:** The Ferdinand Blumentritt/Aurora–Andreas Bonifacio route (Moovit 18-stop route) runs until midnight — notable for this corridor. Others unknown.

7. **T271 (Cubao–Parang via P. Tuazon) vs T286 (Parang–Aurora Blvd):** Modernization has altered this. Under PUVMP, traditional T271 may have been supplanted by modern routes. Operational status uncertain.

8. **Sangandaan as transfer node underdocumented:** Sangandaan (northern Caloocan) has multiple jeepney routes converging but stop-level and frequency data are missing.
