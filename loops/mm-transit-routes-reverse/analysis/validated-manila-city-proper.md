## Geographic Overview

The City of Manila (population ~1.85M; 38.55 km²) is the densest city in the world by area. It comprises **16 districts** grouped into geographic clusters that determine transit patterns:

## Gaps Identified

### Critical Gaps

1. **No GPS stop coordinates for any inner-Manila jeepney route**: Zero routes in Manila proper have confirmed stop-level GPS coordinates. All stop names are verbal references only. This is the most significant gap for GTFS synthesis in Manila.

2. **Pandacan / Sta. Ana severely underdocumented**: The eastern Manila districts have almost no documented transit routes. The PNR suspension (March 2024, NSCR construction) removed the primary service. Replacement jeepneys or buses are not documented.

3. **No formal bus stops on España Blvd**: Buses (Routes 6, 7, 17, 34) and jeepneys load informally at España/Morayta footbridge and España/UST gates. No official stop designations or GPS data.

4. **Lawton terminal exact layout**: While Lawton is documented as a major terminal, the precise staging lanes for each operator/route within Liwasang Bonifacio are unknown. Needed for stop-level GTFS entries.

5. **T-code coverage for Tondo/Binondo routes**: Only 2 Tondo routes (T3114, T3170) have confirmed Sakay T-codes. The remaining ~25+ documented Tondo/Binondo routes have no official designators.

### Secondary Gaps

6. **Night service in Manila**: Only the Ferdinand Blumentritt/Aurora–Andreas Bonifacio route (Moovit, until midnight) has confirmed late-night service. Hospital routes (to PGH, Philippine Heart Center) have unknown overnight status.

7. **Intramuros internal transit**: Horse-drawn calesa (traditional) serves tourists within Intramuros walls. No motorized LTFRB routes operate inside Intramuros. The Quiapo–Pier loop passes the exterior (Port Area/Luneta side) but not through Intramuros gates.

8. **Roxas Blvd north (Luneta → CCP)**: The tourist belt of Roxas Blvd has informal service but no T-coded jeepney routes. The Quiapo–Pier loop is the closest documented route.

9. **Route frequency data**: Almost no Manila jeepney routes have confirmed frequency estimates. Only high-volume routes (Divisoria–Navotas every ~5 min; Monumento–Pasay high-frequency) have rough estimates.

10. **Modern jeepney consolidation status**: PUVMP modernization has replaced some traditional T-code routes. Which Manila routes have been consolidated into modern PUJ franchises is undocumented beyond the Novaliches–Blumentritt Modern PUJ (15 units, Nova-Blumentritt Coop).
