## Overview

Metro Manila's city bus network was radically restructured in May 2020 under **LTFRB Memorandum Circular No. 2020-019**, which rationalized ~830+ pre-pandemic franchise holders into a numbered route system. The original plan called for 31 routes; by **November 2024** the network had grown to **68 local bus routes** as demand pressure prompted LTFRB to restore pre-pandemic routes via subsequent board resolutions (notably MC 2022-067, BR 55 and 57 of 2023).

Additionally, three specialized non-LTFRB bus systems operate in parallel: BGC Bus, QCity Bus, and the new Love Bus (2025).

---

## Regulatory Structure

All city bus operators hold a **Certificate of Public Convenience (CPC)** from LTFRB. Routes are numbered (1–68+) with the EDSA Carousel designated as **Route E** (or simply "EDSA Carousel"). Operators bid for franchises per rationalized route; multiple operators may share a single route number.

Two major EDSA Carousel consortia dominate:
- **ES Transport Consortium** (includes Earth Star Express, Kellen Transport, and others)
- **Mega Manila Transport Consortium**

## Major City Bus Operator Profiles

### 1. HM Transport Inc.
**Status**: Active (P2P + city bus + airport routes)
**Contact**: 09778884684, info@hmtransportinc.com
**City bus routes**: Route 10 (One Ayala–Starmall Alabang), NAIA Loop/Route 18, PNR Augmentation routes (Alabang–Divisoria, FTI–Divisoria)
**EDSA Carousel**: Major operator (part of Carousel consortium)
**Notes**: Also the primary P2P operator; see p2p-bus-operators.md for P2P details

### 2. Metro Manila Bus Co. (MMBC)
**Status**: Active
**Parent**: JAC Liner Inc. (Philippines' largest bus company group)
**Routes**: Baclaran–SM Fairview via EDSA; Baclaran–SM Fairview via Quiapo
**Notes**: One of the few operators documented specifically as operating the full EDSA/Quiapo trunk; connected to JAC Liner group (also: JAM Liner, Solid North, Dagupan Bus, Lucena Lines, Saulog)

### 3. RRCG Transport System (also: Southern Carrier Co. Inc.)
**Status**: Active
**Terminal**: Buendia Street, Manila (between EDSA and MRT Taft); also Cainta Rizal base
**City bus routes**: Manila (Buendia)–Taytay, Rizal via Ortigas Ave.; Manila–Cainta; Manila–Eastern Rizal corridor
**P2P routes**: See p2p-bus-operators.md
**Notes**: Oldest P2P operator (since 2016); also operates inter-provincial routes to Batangas and Iloilo; sister company of Southern Carrier

### 4. Kellen Transport Incorporated (KTI)
**Status**: Active (EDSA Carousel via ES Transport Consortium)
**History**: Subsidiary of former Jell Transport, Inc. (JTI); incorporated 2007
**Routes**: Baclaran–Grotto/San Jose del Monte, Bulacan; EDSA Carousel (ES Consortium member)
**Notes**: KTI absorbed JTI's Baclaran–SM Fairview route; now Baclaran–SJDM only; JKJ Express (also KTI-related) absorbed by Earth Star Express

### 5. Earth Star Express
**Status**: Active
**Routes**: SM Fairview–Alabang via EDSA / Commonwealth Ave (absorbed from JKJ Express / Kellen Transport KTI)
**Notes**: ES Consortium member for EDSA Carousel

### 6. Jayross Lucky Seven Tours
**Status**: Active
**Routes**: Baclaran/Alabang–SM Fairview via EDSA; also Grotto/Tungko/Palmera variants
**Notes**: Also operates Marlea Transport (sub-operator)

### 7. Marikina Auto Line Transport Corporation (MALTC)
**Status**: Reportedly INACTIVE as of June 2025
**Routes** (historical): Montalban–Baclaran via EDSA/Ayala/Aurora Blvd; San Mateo–Baclaran via Commonwealth Ave/Batasan Rd; Navotas–Baclaran via EDSA/Ayala
**Notes**: Served eastern Metro Manila–Baclaran corridor; cessation creates gap in Marikina/Montalban–Baclaran direct service

### 8. Fairview Bus Inc.
**Status**: Active
**Routes**: Baclaran–SM Fairview
**Notes**: North–south EDSA corridor operator

### 9. Fermina Express
**Status**: Active
**Routes**: Baclaran–SM Fairview via Ayala; Lagro–NAIA
**Notes**: Serves northwest QC (Lagro) to NAIA airport corridor

### 10. Funride Transport (formerly WLH Trans / WLLH Liner / Maggi Liner)

## Specialized Bus Systems (Non-LTFRB or LGU-Operated)

### BGC Bus (Bonifacio Transport Corporation)
**Operator**: Bonifacio Transport Corporation (subsidiary of Fort Bonifacio Development Corporation / Ayala Corp.)
**Status**: Active
**Fare**: ₱13–₱15 per trip (Beep Card, GCash)
**Routes** (8 named services):
1. **East Express** — EDSA Ayala ↔ Market! Market! (direct, no intermediate stops; Mon–Fri 5AM–10PM)
2. **North Route** — EDSA Ayala → BGC Turf → Park Triangle → Globe Tower → Net One → Bonifacio Stopover → Crescent Park West → NutriAsia → Uptown Mall → Uptown Parade (10 stops; Mon–Fri 6AM–10PM)
3. **Upper West Express** — EDSA Ayala → Bonifacio Stopover → Crescent Park West (via Buendia Ave / Kalayaan Flyover)
4. **Lower West Express** — McKinley Exchange → RCBC → Picadilly Star → Fort Victoria (Mon–Fri 6–10AM, 5–10PM)
5. **Central Route** — One Parkade → RCBC → Net One → Bonifacio Stopover → Crescent West Park → Globe Tower → NutriAsia → University Parkway (8 stops; Mon–Fri 6AM–10PM)
6. **Weekend Route** — EDSA Ayala area; Sat–Sun only
7. **Arca South Express** — Arca South ↔ BGC (select peak times only; higher fare)
8. **Ayala Express** — EDSA Ayala → Ritz Tower → Makati Stock Exchange → PBCom → RCBC Plaza → The Columns → City Gate → Security Bank → SGV → Glorietta 5
**Extended (2024)**: Route extended to Gil Puyat Station (Pasay) effective May 20, 2024 (weekday mornings)
**Payment**: Beep contactless card, GCash

### QCity Bus (Quezon City Government)
**Operator**: Quezon City Traffic and Transport Management Department (TTMD)
**Status**: Active (permanent program per QC Ordinance SP-3184, S-2023)
**Fare**: FREE (fully subsidized)
**Fleet**: 8 electric low-floor buses added Oct 2024 (41 seats each, wheelchair ramps, CCTV); total fleet ~20+ buses
**Routes** (8 routes as of June 2024 update):
1. **Route 1** — QC Hall ↔ Cubao (Araneta)
2. **Route 2** — QC Hall ↔ Litex / IBP Road (farthest north endpoint)
3. **Route 3** — Welcome Rotonda ↔ Aurora–Katipunan (LRT-2 Anonas Station; only route NOT starting at QC Hall)
4. **Route 4** — QC Hall ↔ General Luis (Nova Bayan; farthest endpoint)
5. **Route 5** — QC Hall ↔ Mindanao Ave. via Visayas Ave.
6. **Route 6** — QC Hall ↔ Gilmore (via E. Rodriguez Sr. Ave)
7. **Route 7** — QC Hall ↔ C5 / Ortigas Ave. Extension (IPI; farthest east)
8. **Route 8** — QC Hall ↔ Muñoz (via D. Muñoz St.)
**Interchanges**: E. Rodriguez–Gilmore, Kalayaan–Kamias, Aurora–Katipunan, QC Hall

### Love Bus (Global Electric Transport / DOTr)
**Operator**: Global Electric Transport (GET Philippines) partnered with DOTr
**Status**: Active (launched September 13, 2025)
**Fleet**: 10 electric buses (initial; all-electric fleet)
**Fare**: Free for all (Sep 2025); from Oct 2025: free during peak hours (6–9AM, 5–8PM), fee otherwise; PWD/senior free always; students 50% discount
**Payment**: Love Bus mobile app (Android; iOS pending)
**Routes** (2 initial routes):
1. VGC (Valenzuela Gateway Complex) ↔ DSWD Batasan Hills Loop
2. VGC (Valenzuela Gateway Complex) ↔ PITX (Parañaque Integrated Terminal Exchange)
**Proposed expansion routes** (MMDA proposal, not confirmed):
- Robinsons Cainta ↔ Venice Mall (Taguig) via C-5
- Eastwood ↔ Ortigas Center
- Stops proposed at: Robinsons Galleria, MMDA Main Office, Arcovia City, Bridgetowne/Opus Mall, Parklinks, Eastwood City
**Notes**: Revival of iconic 1970s MMTC Love Bus; focused on PWD accessibility

### PNR Augmentation Bus Routes (June 2023)
**Background**: Launched when PNR Metro Commuter Line suspended operations for NSCR construction (June 2023)
**Operator**: HM Transport (confirmed via Sakay.ph and OSM data)
**Routes**:
1. **PNR-AUG Route 1** — Alabang ↔ Divisoria (southern PNR stations to Manila north)
2. **PNR-AUG Route 2** — FTI Complex (Taguig) ↔ Divisoria
**Fare**: Regular city bus fare (₱13 base + ₱2.25/km ordinary)
**Notes**: 3 augmentation routes total; 2 are bus (above), 1 is van/FX
