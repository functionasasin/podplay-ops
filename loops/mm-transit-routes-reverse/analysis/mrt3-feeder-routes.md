# MRT-3 Feeder Routes — Jeepney/Bus Connections per Station

**Aspect**: Specialized Data — MRT-3 feeder routes
**Date analyzed**: 2026-02-28
**Method**: Web research via search and article scraping

---

## Overview

MRT Line 3 (Blue Line / Metro Rail Transit 3) runs north–south along **EDSA** from **North Avenue** (Quezon City) to **Taft Avenue** (Pasay), covering 16.9 km with **13 stations**. Opened December 15, 1999 (initial Buendia–North Avenue section); extended to Taft in 2000. Daily ridership ~375,000 (2024). Beep Card accepted; fare ₱13 minimum / ₱28 maximum (Taft–North Avenue).

Operating hours: First trip at North Avenue 4:36 AM; from Taft 5:18 AM. Last trip from North Avenue 9:30 PM; from Taft 10:11 PM.

This document catalogs jeepney, bus, UV Express, and other road-based transport connecting to each MRT-3 station.

## Station-by-Station Feeder Inventory

### 1. Taft Avenue Station (Southern Terminus)
- **City**: Pasay City
- **Major roads**: EDSA at Taft Avenue (Pasay Rotonda / EDSA-Taft intersection)
- **Rail transfer**: LRT-1 EDSA Station via elevated footbridge/Metro Point Mall
- **EDSA Carousel**: Yes (southbound terminus stop)
- **Feeder modes**: Jeepney, e-tricycle, city bus, provincial bus
- **Key feeder routes** (from below station and Pasay Rotonda terminal):
  - Dapitan – Pasay RTDA via L. Guinto
  - Blumentritt – Pasay RTDA via Quiapo, L. Guinto
  - Baclaran – Blumentritt via L. Guinto (two variants: Quiapo-entry and Sta. Cruz-entry)
  - Baclaran – Retiro via L. Guinto, Quiapo
  - Divisoria – Pasay RTDA via L. Guinto
  - Baclaran – P. Campa via L. Guinto
  - Libertad – M. Reyes
  - **To Alabang (Starmall)** via Expressway
  - **To Bicutan / FTI** via C5 (jeepney/modern jeep)
  - **To MOA (SM Mall of Asia)** via Roxas Blvd (e-tricycle)
  - **To Manila City Hall / Lawton** via Taft Ave.
  - **To Monumento / Grace Park** via Rizal Ave.
  - **To Quiapo / Divisoria** via Taft Ave.
  - **UBE Express** to NAIA T1/T2/T3 (Victory Liner terminal walkable, ~₱150)
- **Provincial buses nearby**: Victory Liner (Pangasinan/Bulacan), Philtranco (Samar/Leyte/Davao), Five Star Bus (Northern Luzon), Genesis Transport (Bataan/La Union), JAM Transit (Southern Luzon)
- **Notes**: Busiest point of entry for south Manila commuters. Transfer to LRT-1 gives access to Caloocan, Manila, Cavite. E-tricycles serve short trips to MOA and World Trade Center area.
- **Confidence**: High

### 2. Magallanes Station
- **City**: Makati City (boundary with Taguig)
- **Major roads**: EDSA near Magallanes Interchange; Chino Roces Avenue (formerly Pasong Tamo Ext.)
- **Rail connection**: PNR EDSA Station (~walkable via Magallanes Interchange, but PNR suspended since March 28, 2024)
- **EDSA Carousel**: No dedicated stop at this station
- **Feeder modes**: Jeepney, city bus
- **Key feeder routes**:
  - **PRC sign jeepneys** (from San Lorenzo Place/Alphaland Southgate Mall side) → Chino Roces Ave., Pasong Tamo, PRC, Home Depot, Universal Motors
  - **FTI – Kayamanan C** → Innove Makati (Chino Roces Ave.) to DBP Ave., Taguig City (~first trip 4:08 PM noted; route may be peak-only)
  - **Ayala–Mantrade via Pasong Tamo** (Sabio St / Chino Roces intersection, q15 min)
  - **ECO Plaza sign jeepney** from Alphaland Southgate Mall (opposite lane, northbound along EDSA)
  - **Jeepneys at Magallanes Interchange** (underpass): to Pio del Pilar, Kayamanan-C, San Lorenzo, Dasmariñas, and Taguig destinations
  - **LTFRB jeepney to DBP Ave., Taguig** (~q10 min, 38 min journey)
- **Notes**: Magallanes Interchange is the primary feeder access point — passengers board jeepneys underpass-level for Chino Roces Ave. and Taguig. San Lorenzo Place mall on northbound side has Chino Roces-bound jeepney stops. PNR EDSA station historically offered Alabang–Tutuban connection (suspended). Walking distance to Paseo de Magallanes commercial area.
- **Confidence**: Medium

### 3. Ayala Station
- **City**: Makati City
- **Major roads**: EDSA at Ayala Avenue / Makati CBD
- **Adjacent facilities**: One Ayala transport hub; SM Makati, Landmark, Glorietta, Greenbelt (walkable)
- **EDSA Carousel**: Yes (major Ayala stop; northbound boarding on westside)
- **Rail connection**: Future MRT-7 interchange planned at Ayala-EDSA node
- **Feeder modes**: Jeepney, city bus, UV Express, BGC Bus
- **Key feeder routes**:
  - **BGC Bus EDSA Terminal** (Ayala side) — all BGC Bus routes (see bgc-bus.md aspect)
  - **EDSA Carousel Ayala stop** — direct BRT connection to PITX, Cubao, North Ave.
  - **One Ayala transport hub**: Buses, UV Express to all Metro Manila + provincial routes
  - **Makati CBD internal routes**: Ayala Loop jeepney, Salcedo E-Jeep, Legaspi E-Jeep
  - **UV Express**: Multiple routes toward Cavite, Parañaque, Pasig, QC
  - **City buses**: Route 5 (Malanday–Ayala) among others terminating at Ayala
- **Notes**: Busiest MRT-3 station by ridership. One Ayala (opened 2023) consolidates transport terminals: Grab zones, BGC Bus, provincial UV Express under one roof. EDSA Carousel northbound and southbound both stop at Ayala.
- **Confidence**: High

### 4. Buendia Station (Gil Puyat)
- **City**: Makati City
- **Major roads**: EDSA at Gil Puyat Avenue (Buendia)
- **EDSA Carousel**: Yes (Buendia stop adjacent to station)
- **Feeder modes**: Jeepney, e-jeepney, city bus
- **Key feeder routes**:
  - **Guadalupe–Cartimar jeepney** — along Gil Puyat Ave. from EDSA (Makati) to Taft Ave. (Manila/Pasay); direct east–west traverse of Buendia
  - **Jeepneys to SM Cybertwo terminal** — along Gil Puyat Ave. from EDSA to Chino Roces Ave. (Zodiac St. terminal between SM Cybertwo and Shell)
  - **Ayala Loop jeepney** — northbound only along Paseo de Roxas from Pasay Road to Ayala Ave.
  - **Salcedo E-Jeep** — electric jeepney serving Salcedo Village within Makati CBD
  - **Legaspi E-Jeep** — electric jeepney serving Legaspi Village within Makati CBD
  - **eSakay electric jeepney** → Circuit Makati and Mandaluyong City Hall
  - **Malanday–Ayala city bus** (Route 5 variant) — stops at western entrance (westbound)
- **Key destinations reachable**: Salcedo Village, Legaspi Village, Makati CBD interior; Cartimar/Pasay (via Buendia-Taft jeep); Sta. Cruz/Quiapo/Manila (bus); Circuit Makati, Mandaluyong City Hall (eSakay)
- **Notes**: Three Makati CBD electric jeepney routes (Salcedo, Legaspi, eSakay) provide last-mile access to Makati CBD interior — otherwise reachable only by walking. EDSA Carousel stop is immediately beside the station entrance.
- **Confidence**: High

### 5. Guadalupe Station
- **City**: Makati City (Guadalupe Nuevo; boundary with Mandaluyong)
- **Major roads**: EDSA at Rockwell Drive / Guadalupe area; near C5 overpass
- **Adjacent facilities**: Guadalupe Jeep Terminal; ferry terminal (Pasig River)
- **EDSA Carousel**: No dedicated stop
- **Feeder modes**: Jeepney, city bus, ferry
- **Key feeder routes**:
  - **Route 202: Fort Bonifacio Gate 3 – Guadalupe/Market Market (ABC)** — main BGC-access route from Guadalupe; boards at Jollibee area jeepney terminal near station (q10 min, 38-min journey)
  - **T266: AFP/PNP Taguig – Guadalupe ABC** via M. Asuncion — connects Taguig military/police housing area to Guadalupe MRT
  - **FTI – Kayamanan C** via C5 and Diego Silang — to Tenement FTI (PNR FTI terminal), q peak/off-peak; 4 AM–11 PM (regular jeepneys 24 hours)
  - **M. Almeda, Pateros → Market Market C-5 Driveway** — via Buting C5, Comembo (J.P. Rizal Ext.)
  - **M. Almeda, Pateros → EDSA Central Jeepney Terminal** (Mandaluyong) — via Shaw Blvd, Capitol Commons, C5 Bagong Ilog, Pasig Rotonda, San Joaquin Intersection
  - **Pasig River Ferry** — Guadalupe dock serves Manila Bay ↔ Marikina route (service availability 2025 not confirmed)
- **Key destinations reachable**: BGC/Fort Bonifacio (Gate 3), FTI (Taguig), Pateros, Bicutan area (transfer at Market Market), eastern Makati (Rockwell area walkable ~500m)
- **Notes**: Primary MRT station for Fort Bonifacio / BGC access from EDSA. Guadalupe–FTI jeepney terminal is the main jump-off. No direct Bicutan/Alabang bus — transfer at Market Market BGC.
- **Confidence**: High

- **Adjacent facilities**: SM Light Mall (connected); Robinson's Cybergate (nearby); GA Twin Towers; TV5 Media Center
- **EDSA Carousel**: No stop
- **Feeder modes**: Jeepney
- **Key feeder routes**:

  - **Jeepneys toward Kapitolyo** (Pasig City) — via Boni Ave. eastbound

### 7. Shaw Boulevard Station
- **City**: Mandaluyong City
- **Major roads**: EDSA at Shaw Boulevard
- **Adjacent facilities**: Shangri-La Plaza (directly connected); Starmall Shaw; Greenfield District; Parklea Centre
- **EDSA Carousel**: No stop
- **Feeder modes**: Jeepney
- **Key feeder routes**:
  - **Jeepney to Pasig** — terminal on Parklea Centre Side exit (4th exit); jeepneys plying Shaw Blvd eastbound toward Pasig City
  - **Shaw Blvd corridor routes** — jeepneys along Shaw Blvd connecting Mandaluyong (EDSA) to Pasig (Ortigas area, Caruncho Ave.)

- **Notes**: Four exits: Shangri-La Plaza Side, Starmall Shaw Side, Greenfield District Side, Parklea Centre Side. Only Parklea Centre exit leads to jeepney terminal toward Pasig. Ortigas Center also accessible from Shaw (walk or short jeepney hop). No EDSA Carousel means MRT is the only trunk line service here.

### 8. Ortigas Station
- **City**: Mandaluyong City / Pasig City border
- **Major roads**: EDSA at Ortigas Avenue
- **Adjacent facilities**: SM Megamall (walking distance); Robinson's Galleria (walking distance)
- **EDSA Carousel**: Yes (Ortigas stop)
- **Feeder modes**: Jeepney, city bus, UV Express

  - **Jeepneys along Ortigas Ave.** — eastbound to Pasig, Cainta, Taytay; westbound to Mandaluyong/San Juan
  - **City buses along EDSA** — through-running buses (multiple routes)
  - **UV Express routes** from Ortigas Business District to south Metro Manila destinations

- **Notes**: Gateway station for Ortigas Central Business District. Both SM Megamall and Robinson's Galleria are within walking distance (~400-600m). Ortigas Ave. east provides access to Pasig, Cainta, Rizal Province jeepneys.
- **Confidence**: Medium

### 9. Santolan–Annapolis Station
- **City**: Quezon City (boundary with San Juan)
- **Major roads**: EDSA at Santolan Road (Bonny Serrano Ave.) / Annapolis Street
- **Adjacent facilities**: Camp Crame; Camp Aguinaldo; Greenhills Shopping Center (nearby)
- **EDSA Carousel**: Yes (Santolan stop)
- **Feeder modes**: Jeepney, tricycle
- **Key feeder routes**:
  - **Annapolis–Greenhills Shopping Center** — jeepney terminal at EDSA-Annapolis intersection, southbound; ~q15 min, ~4-min journey; fare ~₱12 min
  - **Gate 5 – Greenhills Shopping Center Loop** — dedicated jeepney loop serving Greenhills development from MRT access
  - **Camp Aguinaldo–Greenhills** — jeepney route serving military camp residents
  - **San Juan–Greenhills–Robinson's Galleria–Rosario** — cross-city route via Annapolis-area
  - **Gate5–Unimart route** (LTFRB) — Columbia St/Florida, San Juan to Greenhills Shopping Centre
  - **Tricycles** — near Camp Crame and Camp Aguinaldo for inner barangay streets
  - **Greenhills Wilson E-Trike** (launched December 2024) — 10 e-trikes serving Unimart, Cardinal Santos Medical Center, G Square, PUP San Juan; special franchise (City Ordinance No. 32, s-2024)
- **Key destinations reachable**: Greenhills Shopping Center (4-14 min); Camp Crame/Aguinaldo (walkable); San Juan interior; Robinsons Galleria via jeepney
- **Notes**: Annapolis Street is the primary feeder axis from this station. Past P2P buses to Antipolo (ceased 2023) and Alabang Town Center (ceased 2024) — now only tricycle/jeepney feeders to Greenhills. Future MRT-4 will add Greenhills and Bonny Serrano stations nearby.
- **Confidence**: High

### 10. Araneta Center–Cubao Station
- **City**: Quezon City (Araneta City / Cubao)
- **Major roads**: EDSA at Aurora Boulevard
- **Rail transfer**: LRT-2 Araneta Center–Cubao Station (via walkway through Gateway Mall → Farmer's Plaza; ~400m covered walk)
- **EDSA Carousel**: Yes (Cubao stop; among the busiest Carousel stations)
- **Adjacent facilities**: Araneta Center Bus Terminal; Araneta City (Gateway, Farmers Plaza, Ali Mall, Coronet); Aurora Blvd. terminal complex
- **Feeder modes**: Jeepney, city bus, P2P bus, provincial bus, UV Express
- **Key feeder routes**:
  - **All Cubao terminal complex routes** (see `cubao-terminal.md`): Victory Liner to Luzon, Genesis (Bataan/La Union), Philippine Rabbit, RRCG, etc.
  - **Araneta Center Bus Terminal (ACBT)** — provincial buses to Central/Northern Luzon
  - **UBE Express P2P**: Araneta City Bus Port ↔ NAIA T1/T2/T3 (₱150, q30 min peak)
  - **Dozens of jeepney routes** from Cubao to all Metro Manila destinations (QC, Manila, Rizal Province, Pasig, Mandaluyong, Caloocan)
  - **QCityBus Routes 1, 2, 5** converge at or near Cubao (all free)
  - **Route 1**: QC Hall → Cubao (free e-bus)
  - **Route 2**: QC Hall → EDSA Farmers Market (free e-bus)
  - **Route 5**: SM North EDSA → Cubao via East Ave. (free e-bus)
  - **UV Express**: Cubao to Rizal Province (Cainta, Taytay, Antipolo), Marikina, Montalban
- **Key destinations reachable**: All Metro Manila (primary hub); Bulacan, Pampanga, Pangasinan, Ilocos Norte/Sur, Cagayan, Isabela (provincial buses); Rizal Province (UV Express/jeep); NAIA terminals (UBE Express P2P)
- **Notes**: Second busiest MRT-3 station. One of Metro Manila's largest multimodal hubs. Full Cubao terminal inventory documented in `cubao-terminal.md`. LRT-2 transfer is the most significant rail-to-rail interchange in the system after Doroteo Jose/Recto.
- **Confidence**: High

### 11. GMA–Kamuning Station
- **City**: Quezon City (boundary of South Triangle and Pinyahan; Scout Area of Diliman)
- **Major roads**: EDSA at East Avenue / Timog Avenue intersection
- **Adjacent facilities**: GMA Network Center (Channel 7); Manuel L. Quezon University; Victoria Sports Tower; DILG-NAPOLCOM Center
- **EDSA Carousel**: Yes — Kamuning EDSA Carousel stop opened **July 15, 2024** (accessible exclusively via MRT station)
- **Feeder modes**: Jeepney, bus, tricycle
- **Key feeder routes**:
  - **EDSA Carousel Kamuning stop** (new July 2024) — northbound/southbound BRT via station access only
  - **Jeepneys along Timog Ave.** — "Cubao via Timog" sign; turns left on Ybardolaza St. toward Cubao
  - **España Rotonda–Proj. 2 & 3 via Timog** — Kamias Rd/Col. Salgado intersection to Tomas Morato Ave/Scout De Guia intersection (~q5 min, ~9-min journey)
  - **"Project 2-3" sign jeepney** — from Boy Scout Circle; traverses Tomas Morato Ave.
  - **"Welcome Rotonda," "Pantranco," "Roces" signs** — along EDSA-Kamuning northbound; routes passing through Tomas Morato / Timog
  - **JAM Liner and JAC Liner bus terminals** — located just south of EDSA at East Ave./Timog junction; provincial buses to Quezon Province, Laguna (JAM); Batangas, Quezon (JAC)
  - **Tricycles** — inner roads of Scout Area, East Triangle barangay
- **Key destinations reachable**: Timog Ave. entertainment district (walkable to GMA Network); Tomas Morato Ave. (jeepney ~9 min); ABS-CBN/Eugenio Lopez (short walk from Boy Scout Circle); East Triangle/QC interior; provincial buses to Southern Luzon
- **Notes**: The new EDSA Carousel Kamuning stop (July 2024) is exclusive to MRT station users — no separate street-level bus stop. This makes MRT-GMA Kamuning the primary access point for EDSA Carousel in this segment. JAM/JAC terminals just south offer affordable routes to Laguna and Batangas.
- **Confidence**: High

- **Adjacent facilities**: Eton Centris / Centris Mall (directly linked via walkway); ABS-CBN Broadcasting Center (nearby); Bantayog ng mga Bayani; DILG-NAPOLCOM Center
- **EDSA Carousel**: Yes (Quezon Avenue stop)
- **Feeder modes**: Jeepney, city bus

  - **Jeepney terminal inside Eton Centris** — to Fairview (Commonwealth Ave. direction via various routes)
  - **Jeepneys toward Tomas Morato / Timog** — same routes as GMA-Kamuning catchment (short overlap)

- **Notes**: The internal UP Diliman campus route explicitly labeled "MRT" terminates here, providing a direct one-seat ride from campus. Eton Centris mall jeepney terminal is the key feeder hub for Fairview-bound commuters who would otherwise need to travel to North Avenue or SM North EDSA.
- **Confidence**: High

## Cross-Station Patterns

### EDSA Carousel Integration
Six MRT-3 stations have co-located EDSA Carousel stops: **Taft (south terminus), Buendia, Ortigas, Santolan-Annapolis, GMA-Kamuning** (new July 2024, exclusive), **Quezon Ave., and North Ave. (north terminus)**. Stations **without** EDSA Carousel stops: Magallanes, Ayala (Carousel stop is nearby but at One Ayala side), Guadalupe, Boni, Shaw Blvd.

### BGC / Taguig Feeders (Mid-Line)
Three stations serve as BGC/Taguig access points:
- **Ayala**: BGC Bus EDSA Terminal (most prominent)
- **Buendia**: Short ride via Gil Puyat Ave jeepney → Cartimar → then transfer, or eSakay to Circuit
- **Guadalupe**: Route 202 jeepney directly to Fort Bonifacio Gate 3 / Market Market

### Makati CBD Internal Loop
At **Buendia** and **Ayala** stations, a series of e-jeepneys serve the Makati CBD interior where regular jeepneys cannot penetrate: Salcedo E-Jeep, Legaspi E-Jeep, Ayala Loop Jeep, eSakay (Circuit/Mandaluyong). These represent a distinct last-mile micro-transit layer.

### Provincial Bus Access Points
Key MRT-3 stations near provincial bus terminals:
- **Taft**: Victory Liner, Philtranco, Five Star, Genesis (Luzon-wide)
- **GMA-Kamuning**: JAM Liner, JAC Liner (Southern Luzon: Quezon, Laguna, Batangas)
- **Cubao**: Araneta Center Bus Terminal (Central/Northern Luzon)
