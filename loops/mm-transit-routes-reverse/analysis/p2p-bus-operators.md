## Overview

The Premium Point-to-Point (P2P) Bus Service launched March 2015, initially called "Express Connect," is administered by DOTr/LTFRB and operated by franchised private bus companies. Key features:
- Fixed departure schedules (not on-demand)
- Fewer stops than regular buses (2–5 stops total)
- AC coaches with restrooms and Wi-Fi (premium amenities)
- Flat-rate fares higher than regular buses
- 20% SC/PWD/student discount applies (RA 9994/10754/11314)
- Payment: cash, Beep Card, GCash (operator-dependent)

As of the most recent public data (2025), approximately **20–30 distinct P2P routes** operate in NCR and GMA. Froehlich Tours was the most prominent casualty (franchise expired 2020). Route count expanded from ~11 in 2019 to 31 routes at peak, then contracted during COVID; as of 2024 approximately 20+ routes active.

## Data Quality Assessment

| Aspect | Assessment |
|--------|-----------|
| Route coverage | HIGH — all major P2P operators documented |
| Fare accuracy | MEDIUM — fares current as of 2024–2025; subject to change |
| Schedule accuracy | MEDIUM — schedules change frequently; treat as estimates |
| Stop coordinates | LOW — no GPS coords for P2P stops; must derive from mall/terminal nodes |
| Geometry | NONE — no route shapes available from public sources |
| LTFRB franchise codes | NONE — no official P2P franchise numbers found publicly |

**Key gaps**:
- No LTFRB franchise codes for P2P operators (P2P routes use different designation from city bus T/N-codes)
- P2P stops are all named places (malls, terminals); coordinates can be derived from OSM nodes
- Some smaller operators (Riding Public First JV, San Agustin Circuit routes) lack complete details
- Suspended UBE Express routes may or may not resume
- Froehlich closure creates northern QC → CBD corridor gap (no direct P2P now)
