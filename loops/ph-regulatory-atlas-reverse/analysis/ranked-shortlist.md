## Executive Summary

This atlas enumerated every Philippine regulatory body with citizen-facing compliance requirements — 20 agencies across 107 distinct automatable domains. The domains span social insurance, maritime certification, property transfer, OFW labor rights, professional licensing, customs, fire safety, intellectual property, energy, and business compliance. Every domain identified here involves computation that Philippine statute defines precisely, yet Filipinos routinely pay ₱5,000–₱200,000+ for professionals to perform arithmetic the law already specifies.

**Three elite-tier opportunities emerge at score ≥ 4.50:**

1. **ONETT Deadline & Late Penalty Calculator** (4.55) — property transfer with confirmed ₱20K–₱50K professional fees per transaction
2. **SSS Contribution Computation & Remittance** (4.50) — 42M members, criminal liability for non-remittance, no free comprehensive tool
3. **OFW Placement Fee Legality Checker** (4.50) — pure information asymmetry exploiting 2.19M workers; illegal fees documented at ₱45K–₱200K/victim

**The highest-value cluster is the ONETT/property transfer domain.** Professional moat confirmed: lawyers charge 0.5%–1.5% of property price (₱50K–₱150K on a ₱10M property); processing services charge ₱20K–₱50K per transaction. The multi-agency sequencing (BIR → LGU → LRA → Registry of Deeds), dual deadline clocks (30-day CGT + 5-day DST), and late-penalty escalation (25% surcharge + 12%/year compounded interest) create a domain that is simultaneously high-pain, high-moat, and fully deterministic from statute.

**The most scalable mass-market opportunity is the OFW cluster.** 2.19M deployed OFWs, 500K+ new deployments per year, zero legitimate tools for verifying fee legality. RA 10022 Sec. 6 defines the fee cap as a binary decision tree — the entire logic fits on a single decision flowchart, yet recruitment agencies exploit the information gap to extract ₱45K–₱358K from workers whose entire job category may be zero-fee.

**The strongest untapped B2B cluster is maritime/seafarer certification.** 576K deployed seafarers, 115K+ annual revalidations, a 622-line fee schedule (MC GC-2026-01) that no seafarer can navigate without specialist help, and a missed deployment that translates directly into months of lost income. STCW 2024 amendments mandating SASH refreshers by 2026 will force mass revalidations, creating an acute product-market fit window.

## Top Domain Rationales

### Rank 1 — O-LRA-1: ONETT Deadline & Late Penalty Calculator
**Score: 4.55 | Law: NIRC Sec. 24(D)/196/248–250; PD 1529 Sec. 117 | Agency: BIR, LGU, LRA**
**Market: 400K–800K transactions/yr | Professional cost: ₱20K–₱50K/transaction (processing services) + 0.5%–1.5% of property price (notary/lawyer) | Computability: Fully deterministic**

This is the most underrated domain in the entire atlas. A property sale triggers two simultaneous deadline clocks: 30 days to file CGT with BIR (NIRC Sec. 24(D)/27(D)), 5 days to pay DST (NIRC Sec. 196 + RA 8424 deadline provision). Miss either and the 25% surcharge kicks in immediately; after that, 12% per year simple interest compounds on top of both the original tax and the 25% surcharge. Then the LRA's PD 1529 Sec. 117 adds a third trap: if more than 365 days pass since notarization of the deed, the registration fee doubles. Each computation is pure date arithmetic and statutory arithmetic — no judgment required, every formula is in the NIRC and PD 1529.

The professional moat is confirmed at the highest level found in this atlas. Notary/conveyancing lawyers charge 0.5%–1.5% of property price (₱50,000–₱150,000 on a ₱10M property); title transfer processing services charge ₱20,000–₱50,000 for full-service; the LRA's own ERCF calculator tool has been "under maintenance" for months with no alternative public tool. This is like the inheritance engine but for real property sales — where sellers and buyers alike cannot independently verify deadline counts, penalty amounts, or multi-agency sequencing (BIR → LGU → Assessor's Office → LRA → Registry of Deeds) without paying a specialist. The "ONETT Orchestrator" concept: input is transaction date + deed date + property value + zonal value → outputs are all five tax amounts (CGT, DST, LGU transfer tax, LRA registration fee, annotation fees), the exact deadline for each, penalty amounts on any given date, and a step-by-step agency sequence. Bundles with O-LRA-2 (pipeline calculator), O-LRA-3 (zonal value lookup), and O-LRA-4 (LRA fee schedule) into a complete Transfer PH product.

### Rank 2 — A-SSS-3: SSS Contribution Computation & Remittance
**Score: 4.50 | Law: RA 11199 Sec. 8(l)/18/22; RA 10361 (kasambahay special rules) | Agency: SSS**
**Market: ~42M members, ~1M+ employer accounts | Professional cost: bundled in ₱3K–₱15K/month payroll outsourcing | Computability: Fully deterministic**

SSS contribution computation is the compliance obligation every Philippine employer shares, regardless of industry, size, or location — from a household kasambahay employer with one domestic worker to a corporation with 10,000 employees. The monthly schedule computation (MSC table lookup by compensation range → employer share 14%/employee share 4%/self-employed 13%/OFW 13%/kasambahay special rules) is fully deterministic from RA 11199 and its implementing rules. The penalty for non-remittance is 3% per month compounded — and RA 11199 Sec. 28(f) imposes criminal liability on responsible corporate officers, not just administrative fines. The combination of universal applicability, criminal penalty exposure, and computation complexity (kasambahay rules differ from regular employee rules; OFW rates differ from domestic rates; voluntary member transition logic differs from mandatory) creates a domain where every Philippine employer effectively pays a monthly compliance tax in the form of bookkeeper/CPA fees.

The "inheritance engine equivalent" is a contribution compliance dashboard: inputs are each employee's compensation, employment type, and status (regular/kasambahay/voluntary/OFW) → outputs are the MSC bracket for each employee, the exact employer/employee contribution split to the peso, the total remittance amount, and the penalty escalation schedule for any outstanding delinquency. The tool eliminates the most common employer error: remitting at the wrong MSC bracket when compensation changes (the MSC table has 15+ compensation brackets). At 42M members and universal employer obligation, even a modestly distributed tool reaches millions of affected parties.

### Rank 3 — E-OFW-1: OFW Placement Fee Legality Checker
**Score: 4.50 | Law: RA 10022 Sec. 6; DMW-DC-01-2023 Sec. 49–54; RA 8042 Sec. 6(d) | Agency: DMW, NLRC**
**Market: 2.19M deployed OFWs, 500K+ new deployments/yr | Professional cost: illegal fees documented ₱45K–₱358K/victim | Computability: Fully deterministic**

This is the information asymmetry moat at its most extreme. RA 10022 Sec. 6 establishes a three-node decision tree: (1) Is the destination country on DMW's "no-fee" country list? If yes → total fee = ₱0. (2) Is the worker a household service worker, direct-hire, or seafarer? If yes → total fee = ₱0. (3) Otherwise → maximum allowable fee = 1× the worker's basic monthly salary. That is the entire legal framework. The binary tree fits on a single page. Yet recruitment agencies routinely charge ₱45,000–₱358,400 per worker even in zero-fee categories — exploiting the fact that the worker does not know whether their specific job category + destination country combination entitles them to zero payment.

Migrant Forum in Asia documented specific cases: a domestic worker heading to a zero-fee country paid ₱45,000 (the entire fee is illegal); a worker paid ₱358,400 in a 1×-salary-cap category where their salary was ₱20,000 (the legal maximum was ₱20,000; the excess ₱338,400 is illegal extortion). The "OFW Fee Shield" tool takes three inputs: job category, destination country, and offered basic salary → outputs are: fee category (zero-fee or capped), exact legal maximum in pesos, whether the offered fee is legal or illegal by how much, and the DMW complaint pathway. The entire computation is deterministic from the DMW advisory lists. The scale is 500K+ new deployments per year where a meaningful fraction are in illegal-fee categories; the consequence of ignorance is ₱50K–₱200K+ in extorted fees per worker.

### Rank 4 — O-LRA-2: ONETT Pipeline Calculator
**Score: 4.25 | Law: NIRC Sec. 24(D)/196/236; RA 7160 Sec. 135; PD 1529 Sec. 117/196 | Agency: BIR, LGU, LRA**
**Market: 400K–800K transactions/yr | Professional cost: ₱20K–₱50K/transaction (processing services) | Computability: Mostly deterministic**

The ONETT pipeline bundles five distinct taxes and fees, each with a different computation base: (1) Capital Gains Tax = 6% × MAX(zonal value, selling price, FMV) (NIRC Sec. 24(D)); (2) Documentary Stamp Tax = 1.5% × same base; (3) LGU Transfer Tax = 0.5%–0.75% × same base (RA 7160 Sec. 135, varies by LGU); (4) LRA Registration Fee = 17-tier graduated scale by assessed value; (5) PD 1529 Sec. 117 double-fee trigger if deed is more than 365 days old. Each formula is statute-defined, but the three different valuation bases (zonal value from BIR RDO bulletin, assessed value from LGU assessor, FMV from deed) create a coordination problem that buyers and sellers cannot independently resolve. The result: every title transfer requires a professional to aggregate multi-agency data inputs before computing the correct tax base.

This domain pairs naturally with O-LRA-1 (deadline/penalty tracking) into a unified "Transfer PH" product. While O-LRA-1 handles the timing and penalty dimensions, O-LRA-2 handles the total cost computation — answering "how much will this transfer cost in total?" before the transaction is committed, giving both buyer and seller the ability to negotiate with full cost information. Processing services charge ₱20K–₱50K precisely because no public tool exists to perform this aggregation. A product that makes zonal value lookup (O-LRA-3), fee computation (O-LRA-2/4), and deadline management (O-LRA-1) self-service effectively eliminates the processing service market's information moat.

### Rank 5–7 — A-SSS-1, I-MAR-1, I-MAR-2 (tied 4.30)

**A-SSS-1: SSS Monthly Retirement Pension (BMP)**
**Score: 4.30 | Law: RA 11199 Sec. 12–13; RA 11199 Sec. 12-C (MPF) | Agency: SSS**
**Market: 2.2M current pensioners + 42M future retirees | Professional cost: complexity moat, not fee moat | Computability: Fully deterministic**

The SSS BMP computation runs three formulas in parallel — (1) 0.025 × RAMC × PPP + ₱300; (2) 40% of AMSC + ₱300; (3) ₱1,000 floor — and takes the MAX(). SSS provides a free pension calculator, but it does not model the Option 1 vs. Option 2 NPV decision: Option 1 grants 60 months of BMP as a lump sum advance (foregoing future monthly pension); Option 2 grants 18 months lump sum + immediate monthly pension start. This is an irreversible, mortality-adjusted financial decision worth ₱500K–₱5M+ in differential lifetime income, and SSS branches do not provide comparative NPV analysis. Additionally, the interaction with the Mandatory Provident Fund (MPF/Pension Booster) contributions affects the RAMC computation — a recently enacted complexity that existing tools do not model. This is like inheritance but for the retirement decision that 42M Filipinos will eventually face.

**I-MAR-1: Total STCW Certification Cost Calculator**
**Score: 4.30 | Law: RA 9295; RA 12021; STCW Convention (2010 Manila Amendments); MC GC-2026-01 | Agency: MARINA**
**Market: 576K deployed seafarers, 115K+ revalidations/yr | Professional cost: ₱50K–₱200K total cert stack; missed deployment = months of lost income | Computability: Fully deterministic**

MARINA's MC GC-2026-01 is a 622-line fee schedule covering every COC, COP, documentary endorsement, and seaman's book fee. The total cost of a seafarer's STCW certification lifecycle — combining MARINA government fees with training center costs (BST ₱5K–₱8K, GMDSS ₱15K–₱25K, ECDIS ₱8K–₱15K, SASH ₱3K–₱8K with 2026 mandate) — cannot be computed without cross-referencing the 622-item schedule against individual training center price lists. No single public tool does this. The consequence of under-budgeting: deployment delay while scrambling for fees, or accepting a lower rank to avoid missing a departure. STCW 2024 amendments mandate a new SASH refresher by Jan 2026, forcing mass revalidations — a product-market fit window currently open.

**I-MAR-2: STCW Pathway & Sea Service Eligibility Calculator**
**Score: 4.30 | Law: STCW Tables A-II/1, A-III/1; MARINA MC GC-2024-07 | Agency: MARINA**
**Market: 576K seafarers; 50K+ promotion-seeking/yr | Professional cost: manning agency dependency; missed certification = missed promotion cycle | Computability: Fully deterministic**

STCW Tables A-II/1 (deck) and A-III/1 (engine) specify exact sea service thresholds for each COC level — e.g., Officer in Charge of Navigational Watch requires 12 months approved sea service on ships ≥500 GT or 36 months on ships any size (reduced from prior requirements in 2024 course overhaul). These thresholds are precise, deterministic conditional rules, but navigating them across 20+ rank levels × 2 departments × historical vs. current requirements × waiver conditions requires specialist knowledge that seafarers access through manning agency HR departments or paid advisors. A pathway calculator: inputs are current rank, sea service log, vessel GRT/kW, COC/COP held → output is exact additional sea service needed for next COC level, prerequisite training, and estimated timeline. This is the career advancement equivalent of inheritance computation — the statute defines the formula; the specialist merely reads the table.

### Rank 8–12 (score 4.05): GSIS, OFW Pre-Departure, LTO, PRC CPD, BFP FSIC

**D-GSIS-1: GSIS BMP Retirement Pension + Option 1/2 Decision (4.05)**
The GSIS BMP formula — 0.025 × (AMC + ₱700) × PPP; max 90% of AMC — is identical in structure to the SSS BMP, but for 2.12M government employees. The Option 1/2 NPV decision (60-month advance lump sum vs. 18-month lump sum + immediate pension) is irreversible and worth ₱5,000–₱20,000/month in pension differential over decades. For pre-1997 employees, the legacy law selection (RA 660 "Magic 87" / RA 1616 "Take All" / PD 1146 / RA 8291) multiplies the decision complexity. GSIS provides free processing but no NPV comparison tool. A calculator that models all three formulas, runs the Option 1/2 NPV comparison with a mortality-adjusted discount rate, and identifies whether a pre-1997 employee qualifies for a legacy law delivers irreversible financial value that GSIS's own systems do not provide.

**E-OFW-2: OFW Total Pre-Departure Cost Calculator (4.05)**
Before any OFW departs, they face fees across six agencies: OWWA membership (USD 25/contract); OFW Pass (₱100 biometric); PhilHealth (5% of BMS × contract months); Pag-IBIG (₱100/month); NBI clearance; PSA birth certificate. The path differs for new hires vs. balik-manggagawa (returning workers with existing accounts). No DMW, OWWA, or PhilHealth portal aggregates these — each agency's fee is listed separately on separate websites. A pre-departure planner: input is employment contract details → output is total pre-departure budget (itemized by agency), which documents require in-person vs. online processing, and timeline. The tool bundles naturally with E-OFW-1 (fee legality check) into an "OFW Pre-Deployment Kit."

**G-LTO-1: MVUC + Total Annual Registration Cost Calculator (4.05)**
With 14M+ registered vehicles and 14M annual registration renewal transactions, LTO is the highest-market-size domain in the atlas. The MVUC graduated table (motorcycle ₱240 → heavy car ₱8,000), EV/hybrid discount (30%/15%), 50% late surcharge, emission test fee, CTPL insurance, and plate/sticker fees combine into a total registration cost that most motorists cannot pre-compute. LTO's LTMS portal handles transactions but not pre-computation. Moat was downgraded from 3→2 after validation: multiple free consumer guides and an illegal fixer market mean no legitimate professional intermediary exists. **H-PRC-1: PRC CPD Compliance Eligibility & Unit Gap Calculator (4.05)**

A unit gap calculator: input is profession, current compliance period, CPD units earned by provider type → output is units still needed, SDL budget remaining, qualifying activities to close the gap. The tool reduces the per-renewal confusion that manifests as late renewal surcharges (50%) and suspended practice licenses.

**N-BFP-2: FSIC Compliance Calendar + FSMR Tracker (4.05)**

The January 20 crunch (coinciding with LGU business permit renewal) creates simultaneous pressure that many businesses miss, resulting in delayed FSICs, permit denials, and BFP closure orders. A compliance calendar integrated with LGU permit renewal tracking and FSMR appointment scheduling is the "FSIC orchestrator" that 1.2M businesses need annually.

### Rank 13–16 (score 4.00): SSS Maternity, PhilHealth Case Rate, BOC Landed Cost, Business Compliance Calendar

**A-SSS-2: SSS Maternity Benefit (4.00)**
The maternity benefit formula — 100% ADSC × leave credits (60 days normal / 90 days CS or multiple / 105 days solo parent) — requires three sub-computations: ADSC (12-month payroll ÷ 180), leave credit amount (delivery type × ADSC), and salary differential (employer obligation = employee regular pay − SSS reimbursement). The 30-day advance filing requirement + DAEM enrollment adds urgency. The common employer error — computing 60 days flat for all deliveries, missing solo parent extensions, or computing the salary differential incorrectly — generates SSS employer delinquency. A maternity benefit calculator that determines ADSC, leave credits, SSS reimbursement, and employer salary differential in one computation fills the gap that no SSS-provided tool addresses.

**B-PHI-1: PhilHealth Case Rate Benefit Application (4.00)**
This is the unusual Moat=1 domain: hospitals process case rate deductions, not patients. The opportunity is transparency, not professional displacement. PC2024-0037 defines approximately 9,000 fixed case rates — the exact peso amount PhilHealth will pay the hospital for each diagnosis/procedure. A patient admitted for appendectomy is entitled to a specific deduction from their bill; the hospital is legally required to apply it. No patient-facing tool exists to verify whether the hospital applied the correct case rate. The "PhilHealth Bill Checker" inputs a diagnosis code or procedure → outputs the applicable case rate, expected deduction, and patient's maximum OOP exposure under the case rate system. For 112M UHC-covered Filipinos, this is a transparency engine that converts opaque hospital billing into a verifiable computation.

**F-BOC-1: BOC Landed Cost Calculator (4.00)**
Every formal import entry requires a licensed customs broker (mandatory by statute), who charges ₱1,300–₱5,300+ per shipment. The landed cost computation — CIF × AHTN duty rate + 12% VAT on landed cost (CIF + duty) + brokerage fee + inspection processing fee — involves HS code classification (judgment element) + arithmetic (deterministic). The de minimis threshold (₱10,000 FOB) exempts most informal/e-commerce imports from formal entry, but those importers still need to know total landed cost for budgeting. A landed cost calculator that combines HS code lookup (AHTN search), duty rate retrieval, VAT computation, and broker fee estimation fills the gap for millions of e-commerce importers and 100K+ SMEs that currently use broker estimates without independent verification.

**R-DTI-1: Annual Business Compliance Calendar (4.00)**
5M+ sole proprietors face a multi-deadline obligation cycle: DTI business name renewal (5-year expiry, 50% surcharge if late); LGU annual business permit (January 20 deadline, 25%+2%/month penalty); quarterly LBT installments (January/April/July/October 20); BIR filing calendar (varies by taxpayer type); SSS/PhilHealth/Pag-IBIG monthly remittance. No single government portal aggregates all these deadlines. The "Negosyo Calendar" — a personalized compliance calendar generated from DTI registration date + LGU location + business type + BIR tax type — is a fully deterministic deadline engine. The tool's moat is not computability complexity but completeness: no existing free tool covers all five compliance streams for sole proprietors simultaneously.
