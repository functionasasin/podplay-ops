# TAM: SSS Benefit & Contribution Tools (A-SSS-1 through A-SSS-5)

**Tools covered:**
- A-SSS-1 — SSS Monthly Retirement Pension / BMP (Score: 4.30)
- A-SSS-2 — SSS Maternity Benefit Computation (Score: 4.00)
- A-SSS-3 — SSS Contribution Computation & Remittance (Score: 4.50 — highest in cluster)
- A-SSS-4 — SSS Sickness Benefit (Score: 3.75)
- A-SSS-5 — SSS Death Benefit / Survivor's Pension (Score: 3.75)

**Loop source:** regulatory-atlas

## Shared Population Base

All five tools share the SSS member universe as their addressable market. SSS is the primary social security institution covering private-sector employees, self-employed individuals, OFWs, and voluntary members.

### A-SSS-3: SSS Contribution Computation & Remittance
*(Score: 4.50 — ranked #2 overall in regulatory-atlas)*

**Consumer Segment**

**Who:** Self-employed and voluntary SSS members computing their own monthly MSC and contribution amounts; micro and small employers manually calculating monthly SSS remittances; kasambahay employers computing household worker contributions under special rules; employers computing the 3%/month penalty for late remittance

**Population:**
- **2.5 million** self-employed SSS members — Source: ASEAN-SSA SSS Corporate Profile (circa 2020) — Confidence: OFFICIAL (2020 baseline)
- **4.6 million** voluntary SSS members — Source: ASEAN-SSA SSS Corporate Profile (circa 2020) — Confidence: OFFICIAL (2020 baseline)
- Combined self-employed + voluntary who manage their own contributions: **7.1 million** registered; active contributors estimated at **~1.5 million** (60% activity rate; new self-employed registrants ~200K–225K/yr suggests significant lapse in legacy registrations) — Confidence: DERIVED
- **~884,000** micro and small employers without dedicated payroll staff who self-compute SSS contributions (DTI MSME 2023: ~994,000 micro + small establishments; 70% estimated to self-manage SSS without dedicated HR or payroll software) — Confidence: ESTIMATED

**Addressable fraction:**
- Active self-employed/voluntary contributors (1.5M): 45% urban + digitally active = **675,000**
- Micro/small self-managing employers (884K): 30% use or would use a digital calculation tool = **265,000**
- **Total addressable consumer population: ~940,000**

**Current professional cost:** BIR/SSS-accredited bookkeepers charge ₱500–₱2,000/consultation for MSC classification + penalty computation; SSS branch visits for verification average 2-hour wait

**Consumer price:** ₱199/month (recurring — monthly contribution cycle justifies monthly subscription)

**Consumer TAM:** 940,000 × ₱199 × 12 = **₱2,245M/year**

**Professional Segment**

**Who:** Accountants and bookkeeping service firms managing SSS remittance for multiple employer clients; payroll service bureaus; HR practitioners in medium/large establishments

**Population:**
- **~38,000** HR practitioners — ESTIMATED (see shared base)
- **~12,000** active bookkeeping/accounting service firms handling SSS remittance for clients — ESTIMATED (BIR-accredited bookkeepers: ~40,000 nationwide per prior TAM research; ~30% actively process SSS contributions for multiple clients as a service line)
- **Total professional addressable: ~50,000**

**B2B price per seat:** ₱999/month

**Professional TAM:** 50,000 × ₱999 × 12 = **₱599M/year**

**Total TAM — A-SSS-3:**

| Segment | Population | Price | Annual TAM |
|---------|-----------|-------|-----------|
| Consumer (self-employed/voluntary + micro-employers) | 940,000 addressable | ₱199/mo | ₱2,245M |

### A-SSS-1: SSS Monthly Retirement Pension / BMP
*(Score: 4.30)*

**Consumer Segment**

**Who:** Active SSS members planning for retirement (age 45–65) computing projected monthly pension under the 3-formula system and deciding between Option 1 (cash conversion of up to 18 months' pension) vs. Option 2 (retain all as monthly pension, NPV comparison); current monthly pensioners verifying computation accuracy

**Population:**
- **2.2 million** current retirement benefit payees in 2023 (of whom 1.923M are monthly pensioners) — Source: SSS VP Villacorta, PNA March 2024 — Confidence: OFFICIAL
- **~5 million** active SSS members aged 45–65 approaching retirement (DERIVED: PSA LFS December 2023 — 62.7% of employed are wage/salary workers; ~15% of total employed persons are aged 45–64 = ~7.6M; applying SSS private-sector coverage rate ~65% of these = ~4.9M SSS-covered workers within 5–20 years of retirement) — Confidence: ESTIMATED
- **Total potential users: ~7.1 million** (current pensioners + near-retirement workers)

**Addressable fraction:**
- Near-retirement workers (45–65): 50% urban/semi-urban with digital access and sufficient retirement-planning motivation = **~2.45 million**
- Current pensioners: 15% would use a digital tool to verify or recompute = **288,000**
- **Total addressable: ~2,738,000**

**One-time vs. recurring pricing note:** Retirement planning is typically a one-time or annual calculation, not monthly. Using ₱199 one-time per user (vs. ₱199/month subscription) is the realistic consumer model here.

**Consumer price:** ₱199 one-time per calculation

**Consumer TAM (one-time basis):** 2,738,000 × ₱199 = **₱545M/year** (treating the addressable population as annual unique users, which is aggressive; conservative estimate applying 20% annual active rate to addressable = 547,600 × ₱199 = **₱109M/year**)

*Using mid-point: ₱199 × 1,000,000 reasonable annual active users = ₱199M/year*

**Professional Segment**

**Who:** HR practitioners processing retirement separations and advising near-retirees; financial planners/advisors helping SSS members maximize pension; labor lawyers advising on separation vs. early retirement

**Population:**
- **~38,000** HR practitioners — ESTIMATED (see shared base)
- **~2,000–5,000** Certified Financial Planners (CFP) Philippines has ~2,000 active members; proxy for financial planners actively using SSS retirement tools — Source: CFP Philippines (approximate membership, not officially published) — Confidence: ESTIMATED
- **Total professional addressable: ~43,000**

**B2B price per seat:** ₱999/month

**Professional TAM:** 43,000 × ₱999 × 12 = **₱515M/year**

**Total TAM — A-SSS-1:**

| Segment | Population | Price | Annual TAM |
|---------|-----------|-------|-----------|
| Consumer (at-retirement planning, ₱199 one-time) | ~1,000,000 active annual users | ₱199 one-time | ₱199M |
| Professional (HR + financial planners) | 43,000 | ₱999/mo/seat | ₱515M |
| **Total TAM** | | | **₱714M** |

### A-SSS-2: SSS Maternity Benefit Computation
*(Score: 4.00)*

**Consumer Segment**

**Who:** Female SSS members who are pregnant or recently gave birth, needing to compute their exact maternity benefit (ADSC calculation, 60/90/105-day entitlement by delivery type, solo parent 120-day extension check, salary differential obligation for employer-covered members)

**Population:**
- **357,000** SSS maternity benefit recipients in 2023 — Source: SSS VP Villacorta, PNA March 2024 — Confidence: OFFICIAL
- **1,448,522** total registered live births in 2023 — Source: PSA, "Registered Live Births in the Philippines, 2023" — Confidence: OFFICIAL
- **Capture gap:** 357K SSS claimants vs. 1.45M total births = 1.09M births did not generate an SSS maternity claim in the same year; attributable to informal employment, voluntary member lapse, or use of 10-year filing window
- **~600,000** estimated female SSS members giving birth annually who are eligible but did not claim in 2023 — Source: ESTIMATED (357K claimed + estimated 245K eligible non-claimers among the ~730K gap attributable to SSS-covered women in informal/self-employed status)
- **Total annual tool users: ~957,000** (357K confirmed claimants + 600K eligible non-claimers who need the computation)

**Addressable fraction:** 65% of employed/self-employed women in this category have digital access and motivation to use an online calculator = **~622,000/year**

**Current professional cost:** HR staff and consultants charge ₱500–₱1,500 for maternity benefit computation assistance; missed claims represent ₱14,100 average per maternity claim (₱14.1B ÷ 357K)

**Consumer TAM:** 622,000 × ₱199 = **₱124M/year**

**B2B price per seat:** ₱999/month

**Population:**
- **376,000** sickness benefit recipients in 2023 — Source: SSS VP Villacorta, PNA March 2024 — Confidence: OFFICIAL

### A-SSS-5: SSS Death Benefit / Survivor's Pension
*(Score: 3.75)*

**Consumer Segment**

**Who:** Surviving primary beneficiaries (spouse/children) of deceased SSS members needing to determine: (a) monthly pension eligibility (≥36 monthly contributions) vs. lump sum (< 36 contributions), (b) dependent children's additional benefit (10% BMP + ₱250 per child, max 5 children), (c) BMP computation under the same 3-formula system as retirement

**Population:**
- **1.4 million** death benefit recipients in 2023 — Source: SSS VP Villacorta, PNA March 2024 — Confidence: OFFICIAL
- *Clarification:* This figure represents cumulative monthly pensioners from prior-year deaths (ongoing pension obligations), not annual new death events
- **Annual new SSS death benefit claims:** PSA registered deaths in the Philippines ~693,000–716,000/year (PSA Vital Statistics 2022: 693,127 deaths registered); applying estimated SSS private-sector coverage rate of 35% among deceased = **~243,000–250,000 new annual death benefit claims** — Source: ESTIMATED from PSA vital statistics + SSS coverage fraction
- **Total annual addressable new beneficiary population: ~247,000**

**Addressable fraction:** 40% urban/digitally active surviving beneficiaries = **~99,000/year**

**Current professional cost:** Labor lawyers charge ₱3,000–₱15,000 for death benefit claim processing; estate consultants ₱5,000–₱20,000; beneficiaries commonly unaware of lump-sum vs. pension eligibility distinction

## Notes & Caveats

1. **SSS membership-type data is dated.** The only publicly indexed breakdown by member type (employed/self-employed/voluntary/OFW) is from the ASEAN-SSA Corporate Profile using ~2020 data. The SSS 2022 and 2023 Annual Report PDFs are available at sss.gov.ph/sss-annual-reports/ but their content could not be extracted via web fetch (size limit exceeded). These PDFs likely contain more current breakdowns.

2. **Professional TAM is bundled, not additive.** The ~35,000–50,000 HR/payroll practitioners who would buy an SSS suite tool appear in all five tool calculations. The correct professional TAM for the bundle is a single ₱999/month subscription covering all five tools, not the sum of five separate ₱999/month charges. The bundle correction reduces professional TAM from ₱2,491M to ₱599M/year.

3. **Consumer TAMs for A-SSS-1/2/4/5 are one-time events.** Retirement planning, maternity computation, sickness claims, and death benefit assessment are low-frequency events (once per qualifying event, not monthly). The ₱199/month subscription model is inappropriate for these four tools; ₱199 one-time per event is the realistic revenue model. Only A-SSS-3 (contribution remittance) has genuine monthly recurring consumer TAM potential.

4. **A-SSS-3 dominates.** The self-employed + voluntary member segment (2.5M + 4.6M = 7.1M registered; ~1.5M actively contributing) is the only consumer segment with monthly recurring tool demand. A-SSS-3's ₱2,245M consumer TAM is 84% of the total consumer TAM for the entire cluster.

5. **Death benefit beneficiary count ambiguity.** The published "1.4 million death benefit recipients" figure represents ongoing monthly survivor pensioners across all prior-year deaths, not annual new claims. Annual new death events involving SSS members are estimated at ~247,000 based on PSA death registration (~693K–716K total deaths/year) × 35% SSS coverage rate. This estimate carries ±30% uncertainty.

6. **Maternity TAM is sensitive to coverage rate assumption.** The 357K actual SSS maternity claims vs. 1.45M total births implies a 24.6% SSS maternity capture rate. The remaining 75% of births may include SSS-eligible women who will file within the 10-year window — potentially a larger recoverable TAM. However, many of these are informal workers genuinely outside SSS coverage.
