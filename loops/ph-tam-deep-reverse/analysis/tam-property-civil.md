### F1: Real Property Tax (RPT) + SEF Calculator
**Score:** 4.10 | **Loop source:** compliance-moats

#### Consumer Segment

**Who:** Landowners and property owners across the Philippines computing annual RPT liability (basic RPT + Special Education Fund levy), particularly those in LGUs undergoing RPVARA-mandated revaluation (2024–2026) who need to understand their new assessed value × applicable rate.

**Population:**
- 21,577,285 — households owning or amortizing housing (PSA 2020 CPH) — Confidence: OFFICIAL
- ~50,000,000 — total property units subject to RPT (DOST-PCIEERD, broadest) — Confidence: ESTIMATED
- Using 21.6M households as the addressable residential base; commercial + industrial properties add additional addressable units (not separately counted in public sources — data gap)

**Addressable fraction:** 35% — PSA 2023 digital adoption + urban/semi-urban concentration; RPT payers skew older and semi-rural, significantly reducing digital addressability vs. average working-age population. Urban property owners (NCR, Metro Cebu, Metro Davao, regional cities) are more likely to engage a digital tool; rural landowners rely on barangay assessors.

**Addressable consumer population:** 21,577,285 × 35% = **7,552,050**

**Current professional cost:** LGU assessor's office provides this service free, but queues are long; private fixers charge ₱500–₱2,000 per computation/consultation; no standard market price for RPT calculation services.

**Our consumer price:** ₱199/month

**Consumer TAM:** 7,552,050 × ₱199/mo × 12 = **₱18.03B/year**
*(Note: This is total TAM, not achievable market. High because the underlying property-owner population is large; SAM below reflects realistic adoption)*

#### Professional Segment

**Who:** Real estate brokers, appraisers, and property lawyers computing RPT for clients as part of property due diligence, sale advisory, and RPVARA compliance advisory.

**Population:**
- ~28,500 PRC-licensed real estate service practitioners (ESTIMATED, ~2018–2020)
- ~3,500 new licensees/year (based on 2023–2025 PRC exam pass rates) → estimated active pool ~30,000–35,000 (ESTIMATED)
- Relevant subset: brokers + appraisers handling active transactions — ~20,000 (ESTIMATED)

**Clients per professional per year:** 15–40 RPT computations annually for an active broker/appraiser; each client transaction typically requires RPT verification.

**B2B price per seat:** ₱999/month

**Professional TAM:** 20,000 × ₱999/mo × 12 = **₱239.8M/year**

### F3: RPVARA Tax Amnesty Calculator ⚠️ DEADLINE: JULY 5, 2026
**Score:** 4.50 | **Loop source:** compliance-moats

#### Consumer Segment

**Who:** Real property owners with unpaid RPT and penalties who qualify for the RPVARA (RA 12001, signed June 5, 2024) tax amnesty program — waiving surcharges and interests on delinquent RPT if paid by July 5, 2026. Particularly relevant to owners of properties with outdated Schedule of Market Values who face large retroactive assessments.

**Population:**
- ~21,577,285 household property owners (PSA 2020 CPH, OFFICIAL) — base universe
- ~80.7% of provinces and cities have outdated SMVs (BLGF, OFFICIAL, 2023) — this is the fraction facing RPVARA revaluation-related delinquency
- Delinquent RPT accounts: no national count publicly available; BLGF does not publish a national delinquency rate — DATA GAP
- Proxy: RPT collection rate vs. target; FY2021 target was ₱76.03B, actual collections consistently fall short of targets by 20–30% in many LGUs — suggesting **4–6 million** delinquent accounts nationwide (ESTIMATED by triangulation)

**Addressable fraction:** 40% — Property owners aware of and motivated to act on the amnesty window; those with larger delinquencies (>₱10,000) have stronger incentive; smaller landholders in rural areas may not engage digital tools.

**Addressable consumer population (for amnesty computation):** 4,000,000–6,000,000 × 40% = **1,600,000–2,400,000** (use 2,000,000)

**Urgency note:** This is a once-in-a-generation amnesty with a hard cutoff of July 5, 2026 — approximately 4 months from the research date (March 2026). Conversion rates should be higher than typical compliance tools due to time pressure and large penalty stakes.

**Current professional cost:** Tax consultants charge ₱5,000–₱20,000 to compute amnesty liability and navigate LGU procedures; lawyers charge ₱15,000–₱50,000 for contested properties.

**Our consumer price:** ₱199 one-time (or ₱199/month subscription with this as the lead acquisition tool)

**Consumer TAM:** 2,000,000 × ₱199 one-time = **₱398M** (or ₱199/mo × 12 = ₱4,776M if subscribed)

Using one-time computation model for amnesty TAM: **₱398M** within the 4-month window.

#### Professional Segment

**Who:** Real estate lawyers, LGU-accredited assessors advising clients, accounting firms with property-owning clients undergoing RPVARA revaluation.

**Population:**
- ~20,000 real estate service practitioners (brokers + appraisers) — ESTIMATED
- ~5,000–10,000 real estate lawyers and property tax consultants (ESTIMATED subset of 84,236 total lawyers)

**B2B price per seat:** ₱999/month (with higher conversion urgency given deadline)

**Professional TAM:** 10,000 practitioners × ₱999/mo × 4 months (window) = **₱39.96M** within window; annualized if tool pivots post-amnesty: ₱120M/year.

### F4: Maceda Law Cash Surrender Value Calculator
**Score:** 4.30 | **Loop source:** compliance-moats

#### Consumer Segment

**Who:** Buyers of real property on installment (subdivision lots, condominium units) who have paid at least 2 years of installments and either (a) are being threatened with cancellation by the developer, or (b) want to know their Cash Surrender Value (CSV) entitlement under RA 6552 Sec. 3–6 before negotiating with the developer.

**Population:**
- 1,860,000 — households using formal financing for purchased housing (PSA 2020 CPH, DERIVED — 9.8% + 5% + 4.3% of 9.75M housing purchasers)
- Note: PSA CPH captures stock (existing households), not annual flow; this understates the total active installment market which includes newer buyers not captured by the 2020 census

- Using 3,000,000 as conservative addressable base

**Addressable fraction:** 55% — Maceda Law issues arise most acutely in urban subdivisions and condominiums where buyers are more digitally active; rural subdivision buyers are less likely to use a digital tool but face identical exposure.

**Addressable consumer population:** 3,000,000 × 55% = **1,650,000**

**Current professional cost:** Lawyers charge ₱15,000–₱50,000 for a Maceda Law demand letter and HSAC complaint filing; CSV calculation errors of ₱100K–₱500K+ are common (developers consistently understate CSV). The tool's value proposition is primarily defensive: preventing the buyer from accepting a low or zero refund.

**Clients per professional per year:** Lawyers: 20–80 Maceda Law matters annually; brokers: 5–20 situations where a buyer asks about their rights.

### G1: Legal Interest Computation Engine
**Score:** 4.55 (RANK 2 overall) | **Loop source:** compliance-moats

#### Consumer Segment

**Who:** Judgment creditors and debtors, loan borrowers, and any party in a money dispute who needs to compute interest under the Nacar v. Gallery Frames (GR No. 189871, 2013) formula: 6% per annum from demand/filing until judgment, then 6% per annum from judgment to full satisfaction.

**Population:**
- ~600,000–800,000 — pending cases in first- and second-level courts (SC/IPA research, 2010–2021 range, OFFICIAL)
- ~31,785 — NLRC cases received annually (OFFICIAL average, 2001–2010)
- Direct users: parties in active civil litigation, NLRC cases, and enforcement proceedings — approximately **700,000–900,000 active litigants** (DERIVED: pending court cases × 2 parties + NLRC cases × 2 parties)
- Broader consumers: anyone with a personal loan, rent dispute, or contractor payment dispute who wants to know the interest clock

**Addressable fraction:** 40% — Litigants with representation and digital access; self-represented litigants (very common in small claims) also have strong motivation. Small claims court alone handles >200,000 cases/year in the Philippines per legal aid reports.

**Addressable consumer population:** 900,000 active litigants × 40% = **360,000** (core litigant market; broader informal dispute market is potentially 5–10× larger but lower conversion)

**Current professional cost:** Lawyers charge ₱5,000–₱20,000 per appearance/pleading where interest computations are included; interest computation errors in NLRC awards routinely run to ₱50,000–₱500,000 (per documented SC reversal cases). There is no standalone market for interest computation — it is bundled into legal services.

**Our consumer price:** ₱199/month

**Consumer TAM:** 360,000 × ₱199/mo × 12 = **₱860M/year**

#### Professional Segment

**Who:** All active litigating lawyers across all practice areas — every money judgment eventually involves Nacar interest. This is universal cross-domain infrastructure. Labor lawyers, civil litigators, family lawyers, estate lawyers, property lawyers, and criminal lawyers (with civil liability components) all need this.

**Population:**
- 84,236 lawyers in Roll of Attorneys (OFFICIAL, Nov 2022) — base
- ~50,000 estimated active practitioners (ESTIMATED, 50–60% of Roll)
- Subset who actively litigate and produce pleadings with interest computations: ~25,000–35,000 (ESTIMATED)

**Clients per professional per year:** A litigating lawyer handles 20–100 active matters; each money claim requires Nacar computation at multiple stages (complaint, hearing, execution).

**B2B price per seat:** ₱999/month

**Professional TAM:** 30,000 litigating lawyers × ₱999/mo × 12 = **₱359.6M/year**

#### Total TAM — G1

| Segment | Population | Price | Annual TAM |
|---------|-----------|-------|-----------|
| Consumer (direct) | 360,000 addressable litigants | ₱199/mo | ₱860M |
| Professional (B2B) | 30,000 litigating lawyers | ₱999/mo/seat | ₱360M |

### G2: Prescriptive Period Deadline Calculator
**Score:** 4.05 | **Loop source:** compliance-moats

#### Consumer Segment

**Who:** Potential claimants who need to know whether their legal claim has prescribed (expired), or to compute the exact deadline for filing — covering Civil Code Art. 1139–1155 (written contracts: 10 years; oral: 6 years; quasi-delict: 4 years; oral quasi-contract: 6 years), plus special laws (labor, tax, criminal).

**Population:**
- ~600,000–800,000 pending cases (SC data, OFFICIAL) — existing litigants who may need deadline confirmation
- Broader: any person who has been wronged (breach of contract, negligence, tort, labor violation) and needs to know if they can still file
- Estimated 2–5 million potential claimants annually who could benefit from prescriptive period guidance (ESTIMATED: extrapolated from case filing rates + typical ratio of potential claims to filed claims)

**Addressable fraction:** 25% — Lower than other tools because prescriptive period computation is a one-time need; people with lawyers already get this advice bundled; self-represented potential claimants are the target but have lower willingness to pay.

**Addressable consumer population:** 2,000,000 potential claimants × 25% = **500,000**

**Current professional cost:** Law firm consultations: ₱3,000–₱10,000 initial consultation; for many, this is a barrier that means they abandon claims before knowing their deadline.

**Our consumer price:** ₱199/month (or ₱99 one-time query)

**Consumer TAM:** 500,000 × ₱199/mo × 12 = **₱1,194M/year**
*(One-time engagement dominates; actual retention in subscription model is low without bundling)*

#### Professional Segment

**Who:** All litigating and transactional lawyers who need to track prescriptive deadlines across multiple client matters simultaneously.

**Population:**
- ~50,000 active lawyers (ESTIMATED)
- All practice areas use prescriptive period rules; the entire practicing bar is addressable

**B2B price per seat:** ₱999/month

**Professional TAM:** 50,000 lawyers × ₱999/mo × 12 = **₱599.4M/year**

#### Total TAM — G2

### G4: Loss of Earning Capacity (LEC) Calculator
**Score:** 4.30 | **Loop source:** compliance-moats

#### Consumer Segment

**Who:** Heirs of road accident fatalities, injured workers, and personal injury claimants who need to compute Loss of Earning Capacity (LEC) under the Villa Rey Transit formula: LEC = 2/3 × (80 − age) × net income per annum. Used in wrongful death actions, CTPL claims, work accident claims.

**Population:**
- 11,955 — road fatalities (PNP-HPG 2022, OFFICIAL)
- ~41,000 — road injuries requiring hospitalization (DERIVED from HPG 2022 data)
- Work accident fatalities and permanent disabilities: DOLE OSHC reports ~1,500–2,000 work-related fatalities/year (ESTIMATED from OSHC Annual Report range, not confirmed from public index in this research)
- Total heirs/claimants with LEC claims annually: ~15,000–20,000 (DERIVED: road fatalities + severe injury claimants pursuing civil action; not all victims file)

**Addressable fraction:** 30% — Many families settle CTPL claims through the insurer (CTPL pays ₱100,000 for death, ₱100,000 for injury without LEC computation needed); those who pursue full civil claims or OWCP claims need LEC; lawyers representing these families are the more realistic market.

**Addressable consumer population:** 17,500 claimants × 30% = **5,250**

**Current professional cost:** Personal injury lawyers compute LEC as part of contingency-fee engagements (20–30% of award); the computation is bundled. No standalone consumer market exists for this computation — it is predominantly a professional tool.

**Consumer TAM:** 5,250 × ₱199 one-time = **₱1.05M/year** *(minimal standalone consumer TAM)*

## Notes & Caveats

1. **RPT population gap:** No official national count of distinct RPT taxpayers exists (RPVARA mandates RPIS database — being built now). The 21.6M PSA household figure is the best available proxy for residential RPT payers; commercial and industrial properties are not separately counted in publicly indexed data.

2. **Maceda Law installment buyers:** DHSUD does not publish active installment contract counts. The 3–5M ESTIMATED figure is derived from PSA 2020 CPH housing finance data + known scale of HLURB-licensed subdivision projects. This figure requires FOI confirmation.

3. **Annulment petition volumes (G3):** PSA records registered legal separations but not annulment petitions (RTC jurisdiction, not PSA). The 10,000–25,000 estimate is from media reports; a precise figure requires SC statistical branch data.

4. **G1 cross-domain multiplier:** The Legal Interest Engine (G1) is embedded in 7 other tools (D3, D4, D5, D6, F4, G3, G4). The standalone TAM of ₱1.22B understates its full value. When G1 is deployed as shared infrastructure, it accelerates time-to-market for all downstream tools and creates bundling opportunities.

5. **F3 deadline urgency:** RPVARA tax amnesty expires July 5, 2026 — approximately 4 months from this research date. F3 is a time-limited high-conversion opportunity that should be prioritized for immediate deployment. Post-deadline, the tool repositions as F1 (standard RPT calculator).

6. **F1 and F2 annual-engagement caveat:** Both RPT (F1) and LBT/Business Permit (F2) are annual events, not recurring monthly needs. The full subscription TAM numbers overstate realistic revenue; per-computation pricing or a bundled annual compliance calendar model would better match the usage pattern.

7. **G5 consumer engagement caveat:** Life insurance CSV is typically verified only at surrender (5–10% of policies annually), not as a regular monitoring activity. Subscription TAM overstates realistic consumer revenue; the tool is better positioned as a one-time audit product or embedded in a financial planning platform.
