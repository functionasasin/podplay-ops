## Consumer Segment

**Who:** Any natural or juridical person (individual, corporation, estate, or trust) who donates or transfers property gratuitously — whether cash, real property, personal property, or shares of stock — to another party. Must file BIR Form 1800 within 30 days of each donation and pay 6% donor's tax on cumulative donations exceeding ₱250,000 per calendar year (post-TRAIN Law, effective 2018). Includes: parents donating to children as estate-planning transfers, OFW remittance-backed land gifts, corporate donations, gratuitous real property transfers between relatives.

## Notes & Caveats

1. **Critical data gap — Form 1800 annual count:** BIR does not publicly report annual Form 1800 filings. The 45,000–100,000 total estimate has low confidence. BIR's issuance of RMC 10-2026 specifically on cash donations suggests non-compliance is widespread and the actual filed returns may be lower than the taxable event count.

2. **Massive compliance gap for cash donations:** Post-TRAIN Law, many OFW families who receive remittances exceeding ₱250,000 and use them to buy property "in the name of" a family member technically trigger donor's tax obligations that are broadly uncomplied with. The tool's educational value may be as large as its computation value.

3. **Estate tax amnesty (RA 11213, 2019) interaction:** The estate tax amnesty created a large one-time wave of estate/donor's tax activity in 2019–2023. The post-amnesty period (ending June 2023) has reduced that spike, but succession-driven property transfers remain a steady market.

4. **Professional TAM is primarily estate-planning focused:** The highest-value B2B users are lawyers and CPAs serving high-net-worth Filipino families with significant real estate holdings — a specific, addressable niche. The tool would work alongside estate tax computation tools.

5. **Bundling:** C1 (Donor's Tax) pairs naturally with A3 (CGT Real Property), C5 (PTT), and C4 (DST) — since many property transfers trigger multiple taxes simultaneously. A "Property Transfer Tax Suite" bundling all four would serve the same professional user and justify Practice-tier pricing (₱2,999/month).
