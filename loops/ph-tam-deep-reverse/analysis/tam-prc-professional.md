# TAM: PRC Professional Licensing Tools (H-PRC-1 through H-PRC-3)

**Loop source:** regulatory-atlas

---

## Tools Covered

| Tool ID | Name | Description |
|---------|------|-------------|
| H-PRC-1 | CPD Compliance Tracker | Tracks CPD units earned vs. required per profession; shows progress toward 3-year renewal cycle completion |
| H-PRC-2 | PRC License Renewal Calculator | Calculates renewal timeline, fees, required documents, and CPD status for PIC (Professional Identification Card) renewal |
| H-PRC-3 | CPD Unit Optimizer | Identifies the most cost-effective combination of CPD programs to meet required units; compares online vs. face-to-face providers; finds SDL-eligible activities |

---

## Shared Population Base

All three tools share the same universe: PRC-registered professionals subject to the Continuing Professional Development Act (RA 10912) and the 3-year PIC renewal cycle. The PRC administers 43 Professional Regulatory Boards covering licensed professions from nursing to engineering to real estate.

| Profession Category | CPD Units Required | Per Cycle | Governing Law | Confidence |
|--------------------|--------------------|-----------|--------------|------------|

| Real Estate Service (Brokers, Appraisers, Consultants) | 45 units | Every 3 years | RA 9646 (Real Estate Service Act); cannot be reduced by PRC resolution | OFFICIAL |
| Social Workers | 45 units | Every 3 years | RA 10847 (Philippine Social Work Act); mandated 45 units | OFFICIAL |
| CPAs (BOA accreditation for international standards) | 120 units | Every 3 years | IFAC standards; separate from PIC renewal | OFFICIAL |

**Key post-December 2022 change:** The PRC reduced the CPD requirement from 45 units to 15 units for most professions via Board Resolution. Real estate and social work professions retain 45 units as their requirements are embedded in their enabling laws and cannot be reduced by PRC resolution.

## H-PRC-1: CPD Compliance Tracker

### Consumer Segment

**Who:** PRC-registered professionals subject to CPD renewal tracking — any professional who needs to track their CPD unit accumulation across a 3-year cycle, verify provider accreditation, and ensure compliance before their renewal deadline.

**Population:**
- **4,900,000+** total PRC-registered professionals — Source: PRC History page, prc.gov.ph, "serves more than 4.9 million professionals as of October 2022" — Confidence: OFFICIAL
- **~821,000** PIC renewal transactions processed in 2023 — Source: PRC 2023 Annual Report secondary citation via grokipedia.com — Confidence: ESTIMATED
- **319,513** new professionals added in 2024 — Source: PRC 2024 Annual Report (cited in search results) — Confidence: OFFICIAL

**Addressable fraction:**
- Universe: 4.9M registered professionals
- OFW exemption: ~600,000 OFWs abroad with professional licenses (approximation based on PSA OFW data; no official PRC-OFW breakdown published) = reduce by ~12%
- First-renewal exemption: ~300,000 new passers per year × portion in first renewal = reduce by ~6%
- CPD Undertaking deferrals: Through June 2026, many are deferring — reduces urgency but not total addressable
- Net addressable: ~4.9M × 80% (excluding OFWs, first-renewals, long-inactive lapsed licenses) = **3,920,000**
- Internet access: Professional demographic skews urban and higher education; PSA 2023 household internet penetration = 54% national, but professionals are higher. Conservative estimate: **75% digital access** for this segment
- Addressable consumer population: 3,920,000 × 75% = **2,940,000**

**Current professional cost:** No dedicated paid CPD tracking tool exists in the Philippines as of research date. Professionals use Excel spreadsheets, notebooks, or rely on CPDAS portal (free but difficult to navigate). No professional-grade paid alternative was identified. The cost is compliance failure risk: a late renewal incurs a ₱30 surcharge and potential lapse in licensed status.

**Our consumer price:** ₱199/month (ongoing compliance tracking; renewed every month through the 3-year cycle)

**Consumer TAM:**
- Active paying subscribers (10% of addressable, monthly): 294,000 × ₱199 × 12 = ₱702M/year (theoretical max)
- Realistic TAM (professionals motivated to pay for CPD tracking): 2,940,000 × ₱199 × 1 month/year (one-time annual check): ₱585M/year
- Conservative annual TAM: 2,940,000 × ₱99 (one-time annual compliance check transaction): **₱291M/year**
- **Working TAM: ₱291M/year** (conservative one-time annual transaction model)

## H-PRC-2: PRC License Renewal Calculator

### Consumer Segment

**Who:** PRC-registered professionals approaching their PIC renewal deadline. Every professional must renew their PIC every 3 years. Renewal requires: (1) CPD compliance (15 units for most professions), (2) payment of ₱1,050 renewal fee, (3) submission via LERIS (online.prc.gov.ph). The calculator helps professionals determine: renewal deadline (based on birth month), required CPD units remaining, fee computation, and document checklist.

**Population:**
- **~821,000** PIC renewal transactions in 2023 — Source: Secondary citation of PRC 2023 Annual Report — Confidence: ESTIMATED
- **DERIVED annual renewal volume:** 4.9M professionals / 3-year cycle = ~1,633,000 renewals/year theoretically; actual processed renewals lower due to inactive/lapsed professionals and backlog. The 821,000 figure suggests ~50% of expected renewals are processed annually — significant compliance gap.
- **319,513** new professionals added in 2024 (who will renew in first cycle in ~3 years) — Source: PRC 2024 Annual Report — Confidence: OFFICIAL

**Addressable fraction:**
- ~821,000 renewal transactions/year × 75% digital = **615,750 addressable renewal events/year**

**Addressable consumer population:** ~615,750 renewal events/year

**Current professional cost:** Professional staff at law firms and accounting firms may use in-house HR to track renewal deadlines. Individual professionals pay ₱500–₱2,000 at service centers for someone to process their LERIS renewal for them (ESTIMATED from community reports). No dedicated digital renewal tool exists.

**Our consumer price:** ₱99 one-time (at renewal event — one-time transaction per 3-year cycle)

**Consumer TAM:** 615,750 × ₱99 = **₱61.0M/year**
Alternative (₱199/year subscription through the 3-year cycle): 615,750/3 = 205,250 active subscribers × ₱199 × 12 = ₱490M/year (theoretical max)
**Working TAM: ₱61M/year** (transaction model; renewal is a 3-year event)

## H-PRC-3: CPD Unit Optimizer

### Consumer Segment

**Who:** PRC-registered professionals seeking to minimize the cost and time burden of meeting CPD requirements. The optimizer shows cheapest combination of accredited programs to reach required unit count, weighing online vs. face-to-face options, SDL-eligible activities (capped at 30% of units), and professional track seminars. Most compelling for: (1) Real estate professionals (45 units, highest burden), (2) CPAs seeking BOA accreditation (120 units), (3) nurses and teachers managing 15 units on a budget.

**Population:**
- **4.9M total PRC-registered professionals** as base
- Highest-optimization-value segments:
  - Real estate service practitioners (45 units required): **No official PRC 2023 count published.** Proxy from PRC Board of Real Estate Service: estimated ~80,000–120,000 registered brokers, appraisers, and consultants — Confidence: ESTIMATED (no public 2023 PRC RESAR board data found)
  - CPAs seeking BOA accreditation (120 units): **~15,000–25,000 practicing CPAs in accounting/audit firms** — ESTIMATED (200,000 total CPAs; only those in public practice need BOA accreditation; BOA accreditation data not published separately)
  - Nurses (966,000 registered, 15 units required): cost-sensitive demographic, many OFWs using online CPD
  - Teachers (2,080,000 registered, 15 units required, enforcement started Jan 2025): largest volume segment; DepEd teachers can use DepEd-provided CPD (free), so private school teachers are primary commercial targets

**Addressable population for CPD optimizer:**
- Addressable = 4.9M × 80% (active license holders) × 75% digital = **2,940,000** (same as H-PRC-1 base)
- Particularly motivated segment (high CPD cost burden): ~350,000 (real estate + BOA CPAs + nurses without employer-provided CPD) × 75% = **262,500 high-value users**

**Our consumer price:** ₱99 one-time (tool used once per renewal cycle to plan CPD portfolio)

**Consumer TAM:**
- Full addressable base: 2,940,000 × ₱99 = ₱291M/year (same as H-PRC-1 consumer TAM; tools are bundled in practice)
- High-value motivated segment only: 262,500 × ₱199 × 12 months = ₱626M/year (full subscription) OR ₱262,500 × ₱99 = ₱26M/year (one-time)
- **Working TAM: ₱26M/year** (standalone one-time transaction model for the optimizer specifically; the full bundle TAM is captured under H-PRC-1)

Note: In practice, H-PRC-1 (tracker), H-PRC-2 (renewal calculator), and H-PRC-3 (optimizer) would be bundled into a single PRC Compliance product. The combined consumer TAM for the bundle is **₱291M/year** (not additive across all three tools' separate TAMs).

## Notes & Caveats

1. **PRC Annual Report PDFs are binary-encoded/image-based.** The PRC 2023 and 2024 Annual Report PDFs at prc.gov.ph are not text-extractable. The figures for 821,231 PIC renewals (2023) and 5.55M total registered professionals (late 2024) come from grokipedia.com's secondary summary of these reports. These should be treated as ESTIMATED until direct PDF access or PRC confirmation. The 4.9M figure (Oct 2022) from PRC's own history page remains the most reliable OFFICIAL baseline.

2. **Per-profession breakdown for 2023 is incomplete.** Only Teachers, Nurses, and Criminologists from the 43 professional boards have publicly accessible 2023 counts. The 2012 Official Gazette data is the last publicly available full per-profession breakdown. Ongoing totals are not published in accessible format.

3. **CPD enforcement timeline.** RA 10912 has been inconsistently enforced since 2016. The CPD Undertaking extension (allowed through June 30, 2026) means that a significant portion of the 4.9M professionals have not yet demonstrated CPD compliance. The enforcement cliff in mid-2026 will likely trigger mass demand for CPD tracking and optimization tools. This creates a time-bounded TAM expansion event.

4. **Professional teachers: CPD enforcement started January 2025.** The 2,080,000 registered teachers were largely exempt from strict CPD enforcement until January 1, 2025. This is the largest single profession in PRC's registry and represents a new enforcement cohort as of research date. DepEd regional offices being CPD providers reduces the commercial opportunity for private providers for teachers, but compliance tracking tools are still applicable.

5. **CPD cost estimates.** Face-to-face seminar costs (₱2,000–₱12,000/day) come from Rappler and media reports. Online CPD costs (₱840–₱1,400 for 7 units at Philippine CPD Online) are from provider pricing. The large cost gap between online and face-to-face is the key commercial opportunity for the optimizer tool.

6. **Real estate CPD burden.** Real estate service practitioners (brokers, appraisers, consultants registered under RA 9646) face the heaviest CPD burden at 45 units per 3-year cycle (3× the standard). Their specific population count from PRC's RESAR Board was not found in publicly indexed sources for 2023. The 2012 baseline was also not separately broken out in publicly available data. An FOI request to the PRC RESAR Board would be needed for current count.
