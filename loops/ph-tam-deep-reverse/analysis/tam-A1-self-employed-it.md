## Consumer Segment

**Who:** Self-employed individuals and professionals who file BIR Forms 1701, 1701A, or 1701Q — i.e., those with income from business or professional practice (freelancers, doctors, lawyers, accountants, engineers, consultants, sole proprietors). These are taxpayers who must choose between the 8% flat rate option and the graduated rate, and who are currently over-paying by filing under the wrong option or by paying a CPA to make this determination.

**Population:**
- **2,186,544** — Source: BIR CY2023 Annual Report, Table 3 (individual self-employed and professional taxpayers who filed 1701/1701A/1701Q in 2023) — Confidence: OFFICIAL
- **13,236,000–13,640,000** own-account workers without paid employees — Source: PSA LFS June 2023 and December 2023 rounds (27.1% of ~48.84M–50.52M employed) — Confidence: DERIVED
  - Note: The PSA own-account worker count is far larger because it includes informal sector workers (sari-sari store operators, tricycle drivers, sidewalk vendors) who are not BIR-registered. The BIR count of 2,186,544 is the actual addressable market — only BIR-registered filers have a filing obligation and thus a tool need.

**Addressable fraction:**
- Internet penetration among adults: 73.1% (DataReportal Digital 2023, total population basis; 85.16M users out of 116M population)
- Target demographic (urban self-employed professionals: freelancers, CPAs, engineers, doctors) skews significantly higher than national average; NCR household internet penetration is 74.6% (PSA 2020 Census)
- Use **75%** as the addressable internet fraction for this demographic

**Addressable consumer population:** 2,186,544 × 75% = **1,639,908** ≈ **1.64M**

**Current professional cost:**
- Monthly CPA retainer for bookkeeping + BIR compliance (self-employed): ₱3,000–₱6,000/month (Source: loft.ph 2025, PinoyExchange community quotes 2023–2024)
- Annual ITR (1701/1701A) standalone preparation by CPA: ₱3,000–₱10,000 per return (Source: loft.ph, Davao Accountants 2025)
- Total annual compliance spend for a typical self-employed individual with CPA: ₱36,000–₱72,000/year (monthly retainer) or ₱3,000–₱10,000 for ITR-only engagement
- The 8% vs. graduated rate optimization alone can save ₱5,000–₱50,000+ per year in tax, dwarfing any tool subscription cost

**Our consumer price:** ₱199/month = ₱2,388/year

**Consumer TAM:** 1,639,908 × ₱2,388/year = **₱3,916M/year ≈ ₱3.92B/year**

## Professional Segment

**Who:** CPAs and BIR-accredited tax practitioners who prepare individual income tax returns for self-employed and professional clients, and who would use a tool to optimize 8% vs. graduated rate selection across their client base.

**Population:**
- **~200,000** total registered CPAs — Source: BOA Chairman statement, Manila Times May 2023 — Confidence: ESTIMATED
  - Annual new CPA licensees: 3,058 (December 2024 exam) + ~2,239 (May 2023 exam) → ~5,000–6,000 new CPAs/year
  - Total is growing; ~200,000 is the end-2023 estimate
- **Public practice CPAs** (in CPA firms, as tax/audit practitioners): Estimated 25–30% of total = **50,000–60,000** — Source: BOA/PICPA sector surveys cited in IFAC Philippines profile; no published breakdown — Confidence: ESTIMATED
- **CPAs actively doing individual income tax returns** (subset of public practice): Estimated 50–60% of public practice CPAs = **25,000–35,000**. Using conservative **30,000**. — Confidence: ESTIMATED
- **BIR-accredited tax practitioners** (RNAB national + regional RRAB rolls): **~12,000 active enrollees** — Source: BIR RMO 10-2010 registry (cited in PROMPT.md template); RNAB national list alone = 165 (BIR RNAB 2022 Scribd list) but regional RRABs add substantially more — Confidence: ESTIMATED
  - The 12,000 figure is the estimated total across all regional accreditation boards; the RNAB national-only list (165) is clearly a subset
  - Use **12,000** as the minimum professional addressable market, acknowledging the true base of individual-tax-focused CPAs is nearer **30,000**

**Clients per professional per year:**
- A CPA who focuses on individual tax compliance typically handles 50–150 individual ITR clients per filing season
- Large volume tax preparers in Metro Manila/Cebu handle 200–500 clients
- Source: Professional practice estimate based on common knowledge of PH CPA practice; no published survey found; Confidence: ESTIMATED

**B2B price per seat:** ₱999/month (Solo Pro tier)

**Professional TAM:** 30,000 professionals × ₱999/month × 12 months = **₱360M/year**

## Notes & Caveats

1. **8% option eligibility creates a real decision**: The 8% option is only available to self-employed with gross receipts below ₱3M and whose income is not subject to special tax rates. The tool must correctly screen for eligibility before running the optimization — otherwise it produces meaningless output for ineligible filers.

2. **BIR filer count vs. PSA own-account worker count**: The 10× gap between BIR filers (2.19M) and PSA own-account workers (13.2M) reflects massive non-registration/non-filing in the informal sector. The tool's addressable market is anchored to BIR registrants who have a legal filing obligation, not the broader self-employment population.

3. **Consumer TAM assumes annual subscription model**: At ₱199/month, the tool is priced far below the CPA alternative (₱36K–₱72K/year retainer). However, many self-employed file only once per year, making a monthly subscription feel oversized. A per-filing transaction price (₱499–₱999 per annual ITR) might convert better; TAM under that model would be lower (₱819M–₱1.95B based on 1.64M addressable × one transaction per year).

4. **Data gap**: No official BIR breakdown of the 2,186,544 by 8% option electors vs. graduated rate filers is publicly available. A BIR annual report chapter on form-level data would clarify what fraction is currently using the suboptimal path and thus has urgent tool value.

5. **Quarterly filer note**: The 1701Q filer count is likely a subset of 1701/1701A annual filers (not all 1701A filers file quarterly). The 2,186,544 represents unique taxpayer-filer-year combinations rather than individual heads, but for TAM purposes this is the right proxy for the annual addressable population.
