# TAM: Individual IT — Compensation Earners (BIR 1700) (A5)

**Score:** 3.85
**Loop source:** compliance-moats

---

## Consumer Segment

**Who:** Compensation earners who are NOT eligible for substituted filing — i.e., individual employees who receive wages/salaries and must file BIR Form 1700 themselves because: (1) they had two or more employers during the taxable year; (2) their employer failed to correctly file BIR Form 1604CF (annual alphalist with full year withholding); (3) they have additional passive income (interest, dividends, royalties) that must be reported on 1700; or (4) their tax withheld by the employer does not equal the annual income tax due. Substituted filing — where the employer's 1604CF serves as the employee's ITR — covers the majority of employed Filipinos, but a significant minority falls outside this.

**Population:**
- **28,120,000** total individual registered taxpayers (all types) — Source: BIR, via Statista (BIR Collection Statistics) — Confidence: OFFICIAL (year: 2023)
- **2,186,544** self-employed and professional filers (1701/1701A/1701Q) — Source: BIR CY2023 Annual Report, Table 3 — Confidence: OFFICIAL
- **~25,933,456** individual registered taxpayers who are compensation earners (28,120,000 − 2,186,544) — Source: DERIVED — Confidence: DERIVED
  - Note: This includes both substituted and non-substituted compensation earners. The vast majority (~22–24M) are substituted filers whose employers handle the annual tax obligation through Form 1604CF.
- **~24,900,000** private establishment employees — Source: PSA LFS December 2023 (49.2% of 50.52M employed) — Confidence: DERIVED
  - Not all are BIR-registered; some are casual/part-time without TINs; government employees are separately covered by GSIS and file differently.
- **Non-substituted filers (must file BIR 1700 themselves): ~3,000,000–5,000,000** — Source: ESTIMATED (see methodology) — Confidence: ESTIMATED
  - Methodology: The BIR has consistently noted a large gap between the ~25M registered compensation earner taxpayers and the number of 1700 returns actually filed each year. Published BIR compliance data is not publicly indexed for form-level counts. The 3–5M estimate is derived from:
    1. Employees who worked for multiple employers in a year: PSA LFS estimates ~8–12% of wage/salary workers change employers annually; at 24.9M private employees × 10% = ~2.5M potential multi-employer filers
    2. Employees with additional income (freelance, rental, interest): estimated 10–15% of private employees = ~2.5–3.7M
    3. Overlap between categories (1) and (2) reduces total unique count
    4. Net ESTIMATED range: 3M–5M. Use **4,000,000** as working estimate.
  - Note: This estimate cannot be confirmed without official BIR Form 1700 return filing count data, which is not publicly indexed as of research date.

**Addressable fraction:**
- Internet penetration (total population basis): 73.1% (DataReportal Digital 2023)
- Target demographic (wage employees in private sector) skews urban; NCR household internet: 74.6% (PSA 2020 Census)
- Compensation earners eligible for non-substituted filing are disproportionately urban professionals (multiple employers, freelance side income) — use **75%**

**Addressable consumer population:** 4,000,000 × 75% = **3,000,000**

**Current professional cost:**
- Simple employment income (BIR 1700 preparation, standalone): ₱1,500–₱3,000 one-time per annual ITR — Source: Davao Accountants 2025, loft.ph 2025 — Confidence: ESTIMATED
- This is typically a one-time April engagement, not a monthly retainer relationship
- PICPA Davao del Norte chapter minimum fee schedule (2019): ₱4,000 for individual ITR preparation — Source: PICPA regional chapter fee schedule (Scribd) — Confidence: OFFICIAL (regional, 2019 vintage)
- Substituted filers have zero out-of-pocket cost (employer handles)
- Non-substituted filers who don't hire a CPA face risk of BIR assessment for non-filing

**Our consumer price:** ₱199/month = ₱2,388/year (full subscription); alternatively ₱499–₱999 as a one-time annual filing transaction

**Consumer TAM (subscription model):** 3,000,000 × ₱2,388/year = **₱7,164M/year ≈ ₱7.16B/year**

**Consumer TAM (transaction model, ₱499/year):** 3,000,000 × ₱499 = **₱1,497M/year ≈ ₱1.50B/year**

*Note: The transaction model (one-time annual fee) is more appropriate for this segment than a monthly subscription, because the primary use case is once-per-year April ITR filing. For TAM purposes, the transaction model is the more defensible measure. The subscription-based TAM overstates annual revenue potential for a primarily seasonal tool.*

**Consumer TAM (used in table below):** Using subscription model for consistency with other tools: ₱7.16B; but effective actionable TAM is closer to the transaction model: ₱1.50B.

## Professional Segment

**Who:** Tax preparers, bookkeepers, and CPAs who assist non-substituted compensation earners with their annual BIR 1700 filing. This is a lower-value professional market than individual tax (1701) because the computation is simpler and fees are lower.

**Population:**
- BIR-accredited tax practitioners: **~12,000** (ESTIMATED; BIR RMO 10-2010 registry; see A1 analysis)
- CPAs actively doing individual income tax returns (including compensation): **~30,000** (ESTIMATED; see A1)
- However, the subset specifically focusing on compensation earner 1700 returns is smaller — many CPAs focus on self-employed clients (1701). Practitioners who specialize in or routinely handle 1700 returns are likely **5,000–10,000** — Confidence: ESTIMATED
- Note: High-volume tax preparation centers (like Tax Center Philippines, local bookkeeping shops, barangay "accounting services") handle many 1700 clients at ₱500–₱2,000 per return. These informal practitioners are not PRC-licensed CPAs and are not easily counted.

**Clients per professional per year:** 50–300 individual 1700 returns for a full-time tax preparer during April filing season

**B2B price per seat:** ₱999/month (Solo Pro tier)

**Professional TAM:** 8,000 practitioners × ₱999/month × 12 months = **₱95.9M/year ≈ ₱96M/year**

*Note: The B2B case for a dedicated 1700-only tool is weak. The professional segment is better served by a multi-form tool that handles 1700, 1701, 1701A, 2316 aggregation, and related forms as a unified product (i.e., tools A1 + A5 + B6 bundled together). Standalone 1700 professional TAM understates this bundled professional market.*

## Notes & Caveats

1. **Non-substituted filer count is ESTIMATED**: The single most important data point for A5 — the count of compensation earners who must file 1700 themselves — is not publicly indexed in BIR statistics. The 3–5M estimate is triangulated from PSA LFS employer-churn rates and income-mix data. A BIR Form 1700 annual return count from the BIR Annual Report would convert this to OFFICIAL data. A BIR FOI request for "total 1700 returns filed in 2022 and 2023" would be the most direct approach.

2. **Low moat score (2) reflects prior competition**: Multiple free tax calculators (BIR eAFS, Taxumo free tier, various websites) already handle basic compensation income computation. The tool's value add must be in the reconciliation of multiple Form 2316 certificates — aggregating certificates from multiple employers into a defensible annual ITR. This is the truly underserved function.

3. **BIR Form 2316 / substituted filing context**: Under RR 11-2018 (as amended), employers with multiple employees must submit BIR 1604CF with an alphalist. Employees who received only from one employer and whose tax was correctly withheld are considered "substituted filers" — they are exempt from filing a separate 1700. The non-substituted population is truly only those outside this safe harbor. Getting this distinction right is essential for correct TAM sizing.

4. **Taxumo pricing anchor**: Taxumo's 8% Plan at ₱2,699/quarter (annual: ₱9,996) is for self-employed (1701A), not compensation earners. Taxumo does not appear to offer a consumer-facing 1700 product as of March 2026. This represents a genuine market gap, particularly for the multiple-employer employee segment.

5. **SEC MC 4 (Jan 2026) note**: Not directly relevant to A5 but confirms the trend toward reducing compliance costs for small entities — a positive signal for consumer appetite for self-service compliance tools.

6. **Bundling recommendation**: A5 has the largest raw consumer population of the three tax tools in this cluster but the lowest standalone value due to low moat. Its best monetization path is as a feature within a broader income tax suite (A1 + A5 + B6) where the 2316 certificate aggregation and multi-employer reconciliation serves both self-employed and compensation earners in a single product. The professional market (tax preparers handling all individual ITR types) also values bundled tools over single-purpose 1700 tools.
