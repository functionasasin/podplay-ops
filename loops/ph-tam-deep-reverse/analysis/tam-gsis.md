## D-GSIS-1: GSIS BMP Retirement Pension + Option 1/2 NPV Decision

*(Score: 4.05 — #8 ranked in regulatory-atlas; highest in GSIS cluster)*

### Consumer Segment

**Who:** Active government employees making the irreversible retirement election — Option 1 (60 × BMP lump sum, no monthly pension) vs Option 2 (18 × BMP lump sum + lifetime monthly pension). The BMP formula is fully deterministic (BMP = 0.025 × Average Monthly Compensation × years of Pensionable Period of Service; max 90% of AMC; +₱700 floor) but the Option 1 vs 2 NPV comparison requires break-even calculation that most employees compute incorrectly. A wrong choice can mean ₱200,000–₱1,000,000+ in lifetime benefit forgone. Also: pre-retirees projecting BMP years in advance to plan retirement timing.

**Population:**
- **2,120,340** active GSIS members — Source: GCG GOCC Profile, Sept 2025 — Confidence: OFFICIAL
- **~68,293** estimated annual new retirees (annual new pensioners added to roll) — Source: DERIVED from 373,949 (2018) → 442,242 (2019) increment in ADB TAC 52012-001 — Confidence: DERIVED
- **~476,000** active members in 10-year pre-retirement window (age ~55–64) — Source: DERIVED (68,293/yr × 10-year planning horizon; consistent with ~22% of active workforce in this age bracket) — Confidence: DERIVED

**Addressable fraction:**
- 70% internet penetration (ESTIMATED for government employees; see shared base above)
- Pre-retirement cohort (476,000): 476,000 × 70% = **333,200 addressable** pre-retirees
- All active members (planning/projection use): 2,120,340 × 70% = **1,484,238 addressable**
- Working addressable: **333,200** (pre-retirement cohort — the segment with genuine near-term decision need)

**Addressable consumer population:** 333,200

**Current professional cost:** No published fee schedule exists. Private GSIS consultants and retired government HR officials charge an estimated ₱5,000–₱20,000 for retirement planning consultations. Labor lawyers specializing in government employment charge ₱20,000+ for full retirement planning analysis — Source: ESTIMATED from user testimonials on Philippine government employee forums (PinoyExchange, government employee Facebook groups); no official fee schedule published.

**Our consumer price:** ₱199/month

**Consumer TAM:**

- Realistic (average 3-month engagement around retirement event): 333,200 × ₱199 × 3 = **₱198.9M/year**
- **Working TAM: ₱199M/year** (realistic engagement model)

## D-GSIS-2: GSIS Legacy Law Selection (RA 660/1616/PD 1146 vs RA 8291)

*(Score: 3.75)*

### Consumer Segment

**Who:** Government employees who entered service before June 24, 1997 (the effective date of RA 8291) who retain the option to elect their retirement law. These employees choose between: RA 660 (lifetime actuarially reduced pension), RA 1616 (high refund of contributions, lower pension), PD 1146 (moderate pension + lump sum), or RA 8291 (standardized pension — the default for all post-1997 entrants). The "Magic 87" formula (age + years of service ≥ 87 enables optional retirement), "Take All" option, and variable break-even calculations make this a multi-law comparison with a ₱5,000–₱20,000/month pension differential and no recourse after election.

**Population:**
- **~200,000** active government employees who entered service before June 24, 1997 and have not yet retired — Source: ESTIMATED per regulatory-atlas scored-domains.md tool description ("200K+ pre-1997 employees"); no official published count exists — Confidence: ESTIMATED
  - Cross-check: GSIS active membership = 2,120,340 (Sept 2025). Pre-1997 entrants still active in 2025 must have entered government at age ≤37 to still be below mandatory retirement age (65). Given government employment in 1997 was ~1.2–1.5M, and many have already retired, the ~200K residual cohort is plausible.
- **646,303** current pensioners have already made this election — not addressable (decision irrevocably made)

**Addressable fraction:**
- 200,000 × 70% internet penetration = **140,000 addressable**
- Annual cohort actively making the decision (near mandatory retirement age 60–65): ~15,000–20,000/year × 70% = ~12,000–14,000/year

**Addressable consumer population:** 140,000 (full planning pool); ~13,000/year (at-decision cohort)

**Current professional cost:** GSIS consultants and specialized labor lawyers charge an estimated ₱10,000–₱50,000 for legacy law selection analysis — irreversible, high-stakes, multi-variable computation requiring multi-scenario NPV modeling — Source: ESTIMATED from government employee forum testimonials; no official fee schedule.

**Our consumer price:** ₱199/month

**Consumer TAM:**
- Max (full pool, year-round): 140,000 × ₱199 × 12 = **₱334M/year**
- Realistic (2-month engagement during pre-retirement decision window): 140,000 × ₱199 × 2 = **₱55.7M/year**
- **Working TAM: ₱56M/year** (realistic engagement model)

**Population:**
- **~7,200 ESTIMATED** AAOs in agencies with pre-1997 employee cohorts (~50% of all 14,469 AAOs) — Confidence: ESTIMATED (agencies established before 1997 include all national agencies, most LGUs; newer GOCCs and recently-created SUCs have fewer pre-1997 entrants)

## D-GSIS-4: GSIS Contribution Computation & Employer Remittance

*(Score: 3.60)*

### Consumer Segment

**Who:** Government employees verifying their monthly GSIS deductions (9% employee share of Basic Monthly Salary + PERA, with PERA partially excluded from contribution base under RA 8291 Sec. 5); employees who received irregular/hazard pay wanting to confirm contribution base; new government hires understanding their GSIS coverage and projected benefits for the first time. Note: the criminal liability provision (RA 8291 Sec. 52 — agency heads face criminal liability for late remittance) creates strong institutional demand on the employer side.

**Population:**
- **2,120,340** active GSIS members — Source: GCG GOCC Profile, Sept 2025 — Confidence: OFFICIAL
- Fraction who actively verify or need to compute their contributions (vs. trusting payroll deduction): ESTIMATED **15%** (government employees who question deductions, new hires, those with variable pay components) = **318,051**
- Addressable: 318,051 × 70% internet = **222,636**

**Addressable consumer population:** 222,636

**Current professional cost:** Employees visit GSIS branch offices or agency HR; GSIS branch average wait time 2–4 hours; no consumer-facing computation tool exists. Opportunity cost only — no professional fee for individual contribution verification.

**Our consumer price:** ₱199/month (primarily for employees with irregular/variable pay who need ongoing verification)

**Consumer TAM:** 222,636 × ₱199 × 12 = **₱531.9M/year** (max)
Realistic (transaction-based at ₱99/use for occasional verifiers): 222,636 × ₱99 = **₱22.0M/year**
Working TAM: **₱22M/year** (consumer side; primary value is professional/institutional)

### Professional Segment (Primary Market for D-GSIS-4)

**Who:** Government Agency Authorized Officers (AAOs) and HR administrators responsible for computing and remitting GSIS contributions for their entire agency workforce each month. RA 8291 Sec. 52 imposes criminal liability on agency heads for late remittance — this is the highest compliance-severity factor in the GSIS cluster. Additionally: HR officers computing 21% total contribution (9% EE + 12% employer) split accurately across complex payroll structures (plantilla + casuals + contractuals).

**Population:**
- **14,469** AAOs designated to handle GSIS remittances — Source: PNA citing GSIS July 2023 operations data — Confidence: OFFICIAL

- Core market: **14,469 AAOs** (each computes and certifies contributions for their entire agency)

**Clients per professional:** Each AAO services the entire payroll of their assigned unit. A DepEd AAO in a large division manages 500–2,000 teachers; an LGU AAO manages 50–300 regular employees. Median: ~150–300 employees per AAO.

**B2B price per seat:** ₱2,999/month (Practice tier — computing and remitting for entire agency workforce justifies agency-level pricing)

## D-GSIS-3: GSIS Portability Totalization (RA 7699)

*(Score: 3.50)*

### Consumer Segment

**Who:** Workers who moved between government and private sectors without accumulating the minimum 15 years of service to independently qualify for pension in either GSIS or SSS. Under RA 7699 (Portability Law, enacted May 1, 1994), GSIS and SSS totalize credited service to determine pension eligibility; each system then pays only its proportionate share (own PPP / total combined PPP). Target user: government employee with 8–14 years government service who moved to private sector, or private employee with ~10 years SSS who also has government service periods.

**Population:**
- **No official published count** of RA 7699 totalization claims or active portability cases — confirmed via web search; no GSIS or SSS statistical report publishes this breakdown
- Tool description references "2M+ government-private switchers" — this appears to be a cumulative career-history figure (workers who have ever worked in both sectors), not the active RA 7699 claimant count — Confidence: ESTIMATED (source: regulatory-atlas scored-domains.md)
- **Annual new RA 7699 claims:** ESTIMATED **5,000–15,000/year** — DERIVED from the retirement flow (~68K new GSIS retirees/yr; RA 7699 invokers are the subset who don't qualify independently; perhaps 10–15% of career switchers) — Confidence: ESTIMATED
- **Accumulated pool (workers with incomplete records in both systems who may invoke RA 7699 at retirement):** ESTIMATED **200,000–500,000** — Confidence: ESTIMATED

**Addressable fraction:**
- 40% of the estimated 200K–500K pool are digitally active and would use a tool for this calculation
- Addressable: **~100,000** (midpoint ESTIMATED)

**Addressable consumer population:** ~100,000 (ESTIMATED)

**Our consumer price:** ₱199/month (short engagement; typically a one-time calculation)

**Consumer TAM:**
- Realistic (2-month engagement): 100,000 × ₱199 × 2 = **₱39.8M/year**

- **Working TAM: ₱40M/year**

## D-GSIS-6: GSIS Disability Benefit

*(Score: 3.00)*

### Consumer Segment

**Who:** Government employees who suffer a permanent total disability (PTD), permanent partial disability (PPD), or temporary total disability (TTD) and need to compute expected GSIS disability benefit (PTD = BMP + 18×BMP cash advance if ≥180 contributions; TTD = 75% of daily salary, floor ₱70/ceiling ₱340/day). Separate from Employees Compensation (EC) disability benefits, which are funded through the ECC/Employees Compensation Fund.

**Population:**
- **1,517** new non-EC disability cases in 2019 — Source: ADB TAC 52012-001 (from GSIS annual report data) — Confidence: OFFICIAL (2019)
- **~1,500–1,700 DERIVED** estimated annual cases in 2023–2024 (stable 2017–2019 trend: 1,143 / 1,095 / 1,517) — Confidence: DERIVED
- Addressable: ~1,600 × 70% = **~1,120/year**

**Addressable consumer population:** ~1,120 events/year — very small market

**Our consumer price:** ₱199/month (during claim processing period, ~6 months average)

**Consumer TAM:** 1,120 × ₱199 × 6 = **₱1.3M/year**

---

### Professional Segment

D-GSIS-6 disability benefit computation is best treated as an add-on feature bundled within D-GSIS-4 (Contribution Computation tool) rather than a standalone professional product. The disability benefit market is too small (~1,500 new cases/year) to justify a dedicated B2B go-to-market effort.

**Professional TAM:** Included in D-GSIS-4 bundle — counted there; **₱0 additional standalone TAM** to avoid double-counting.

## Notes & Caveats

1. **GSIS annual report access blocked:** GSIS's full annual report PDFs are blocked from external access by their web application firewall (confirmed during research). The 2017–2019 per-type benefit breakdowns come entirely from an ADB TAC Report (No. 52012-001, Project No. 52012-001) that incorporated GSIS annual report data. More recent type-level breakdowns were not found in any publicly indexed source. The PSA OpenStat database (openstat.psa.gov.ph, Element 10: Social Security) contains GSIS pensioner counts by category through 2022 accessible interactively — but this requires direct portal access and was not retrievable in this session.

2. **AAO count interpretation:** 14,469 AAOs represents individual designated officers, not agency count. Large employers like DepEd, DOH, and DPWH have hundreds of AAOs at regional/division/district level. The ~2,200 employer-unit estimate represents distinct GSIS-registered employer entities.

3. **Pre-1997 employee count (ESTIMATED):** No official GSIS census by service-entry cohort exists in publicly accessible data. The "200K+" figure used here originates from the regulatory-atlas tool description text. It is structurally plausible (government employment ~1.2M in 1997; many pre-1997 entrants have retired; residual ~200K cohort still active) but cannot be verified from current public sources.

4. **RA 7699 portability (critical data gap):** Zero published statistics on annual RA 7699 totalization claims were found in any indexed source (GSIS, SSS, or academic). The "2M+ government-private switchers" figure from the tool description appears to be a lifetime career-history estimate, not an official published statistic. D-GSIS-3 TAM figures carry the lowest confidence in this cluster.

5. **D-GSIS-1 pricing model consideration:** The Option 1 vs Option 2 NPV election is an irreversible one-time decision. A monthly subscription (₱199/mo) makes sense only if the product offers multi-year pre-retirement projection and planning features. A ₱499 one-time computation at the point of retirement may better match the use case; the TAM under that model = 47,600 addressable annual retirees × ₱499 = ₱23.8M/year (much smaller but more honest).

6. **D-GSIS-4 pricing rationale:** ₱2,999/month (Practice tier) is justified because the AAO is computing contributions for an entire agency workforce — the value scales with headcount, not individual use. An LGU AAO computing for 300 employees is equivalent to a Practice-tier CPA firm running payroll for multiple clients. ₱173.5M (Solo Pro) is the conservative floor; ₱521M (Practice) is the ceiling.

7. **Government SaaS adoption friction:** GSIS tools face a structural adoption challenge — government agencies are subject to procurement law (RA 9184) for software purchases above threshold, meaning a subscription SaaS model requires either (a) staying below the competitive bidding threshold (~₱1M/year per agency) or (b) securing a framework agreement. Consumer-direct (government employee's personal subscription) bypasses procurement entirely and is the faster initial go-to-market.
