# TAM: Labor Separation Tools (D4, D5, D6, D9)

**Tools covered:**
- D4 — Retirement Pay Calculator (RA7641 Statutory Minimum) (Score: 4.20)
- D5 — Separation Pay Calculator (Authorized Causes) (Score: 4.30)
- D6 — Back Wages Estimator (Illegal Dismissal) (Score: 4.10)
- D9 — SEnA Request Navigator (Score: 3.90)

**Loop source:** compliance-moats

## Shared Population Base

All four tools address the lifecycle of employment separation in the Philippines — from authorized retrenchment/retirement (D4, D5) to contested dismissals (D6) to pre-arbitration mediation (D9). The overlapping but distinct consumer populations are:

### Involuntary Separations from Authorized Causes (D4, D5 primary market)

**Population:**
- **422,689** workers displaced via retrenchment or permanent closure in 2023, from 10,602 establishments — Source: DOLE Bureau of Local Employment Job Displacement Report (JDR) 2023, cited in BusinessMirror (Feb 2023) and PNA — Confidence: OFFICIAL
- **412,753** workers displaced in 2022, from 10,947 establishments (369,465 retrenched from 9,233 establishments; 43,288 from 1,714 permanent closures) — Source: DOLE JDR 2022, BusinessMirror Feb 24, 2023 — Confidence: OFFICIAL
- **386,819** workers displaced in 2021 — Source: DOLE JDR 2021, same BusinessMirror article — Confidence: OFFICIAL
- **Important caveat:** DOLE JDR covers only establishments that filed displacement reports (required under Labor Code for 10+ workers). Individual dismissals from small firms, voluntary resignations, and end-of-contract (project-based) workers are not captured. Total actual annual separations including all categories is substantially higher.

**Broader separation proxy:**
- **~24.9 million** private-sector wage workers (PSA LFS December 2023, DERIVED) — the pool from which all separations occur
- Applying the ILO/OECD benchmark of 15–20% annual turnover in developing economies: estimated **~3.7–5.0 million total annual separations** (all causes, formal + informal) — Confidence: ESTIMATED (no direct PH-published figure for total annual separations)
- The DOLE JDR 422,689 figure represents the compulsory-reporting tip of the iceberg: **approximately 8–11% of estimated total separations** are formally documented authorized-cause separations

### Retirement-Age Workers (D4 specific)

**Population:**
- **2,200,000** SSS retirement benefit recipients (pensioners) in 2023 — Source: SSS 2023 Annual Report, Philippine News Agency article 2024 (https://www.pna.gov.ph/articles/1220393) — Confidence: OFFICIAL
  - Note: This is the cumulative stock of living retirees receiving monthly SSS pension, not new claimants in 2023
- **Estimated ~130,000–160,000** new SSS retirement benefit awardees per year — Source: DERIVED from 2.2M pensioners ÷ approximate 14–17 year pension receipt period (life expectancy at 60 minus 60 = 17 years average remaining life per PSA Life Tables 2020) — Confidence: DERIVED
- **SSS retirement benefit payments in 2023:** ₱156.7 billion — Source: PNA 2024 (https://www.pna.gov.ph/articles/1220393) — Confidence: OFFICIAL
- Workers aged 60+ in the Philippine workforce: PSA 2020 Census shows ~5.2% of 15+ population aged 60–64 still employed; applying this to LFS 2023 employed base (50.52M): **estimated ~2.6M workers aged 60+** — Confidence: ESTIMATED
- RA7641 statutory minimum retirement applies to private-sector employees (not GSIS-covered government workers, not those with superior CBA/company plan) with at least 5 years of service and who are at least 60 years old (optional) or 65 (compulsory). The addressable RA7641 computation market is the **annual cohort of new private-sector retirees**: estimated **~100,000–130,000/year** (excluding those covered by superior plans or self-employed) — Confidence: ESTIMATED

### NLRC / Illegal Dismissal Case Volume (D6 primary market)

**Population:**
- **No publicly indexed national annual NLRC case filing total for 2022–2024.** The NLRC CaseTable portal (nlrc.dole.gov.ph/CaseTable) returned 403 errors during research. Annual totals require FOI request or NLRC Annual Report PDF — Confidence: DATA GAP
- **Historical proxy:** NLRC 2001–2010 cumulative: ~31,785 total cases = ~3,178/year average, but this may reflect only one RAB or a subcategory — Source: academic citation from stepacademic.net citing NLRC official website — Confidence: ESTIMATED (uncertain basis)
- **Regional anchor:** NLRC-RAB 7 (Central Visayas, pop ~8.08M) received **2,944 cases** in 2018 — Source: Philippine News Agency, PNA article 1065020 — Confidence: OFFICIAL (regional only)
- **National extrapolation from RAB 7:** Region 7 accounts for ~7.3% of national population (8.08M ÷ 111M). However, NCR accounts for ~30–35% of formal employment. Using population-weighted extrapolation from RAB 7: 2,944 ÷ 0.073 = **~40,300 national cases/year** — Confidence: ESTIMATED (population-weighted extrapolation from single regional data point; treats regional caseload as population-proportional, which likely understates NCR)
- **SEnA pipeline corroboration:** If ~40,000 cases reach NLRC arbitration annually, and NLRC settlement/dismissal rate is ~40–50%, total cases entering the SEnA pipeline before NLRC should be 2–3× higher. The 2024 SEnA figure of 83,836 RFAs is consistent with ~35,000–42,000 cases reaching NLRC after SEnA settlement failures (SEnA settles ~50–60% of RFAs)
- **Best estimate for current annual NLRC case filings: ~35,000–45,000/year** — Confidence: ESTIMATED (triangulated from regional data + SEnA pipeline)
- Illegal dismissal cases: conventionally the largest single case type at NLRC (~40–60% of all labor cases per law review commentaries); estimated **~14,000–27,000 illegal dismissal cases filed per year** — Confidence: ESTIMATED

### SEnA Requests for Assistance (D9 primary market)

**Population:**
- **83,836** total RFAs (Requests for Assistance) processed by DOLE + NCMB + NLRC in 2024 — Source: Philippine Tribune, March 3, 2025 (https://tribune.net.ph/2025/03/03/dole-processed-over-83k-requests-for-assistance-in-2024) — Confidence: OFFICIAL
- **58,212** workers benefited from 2024 SEnA; ₱2.9 billion in monetary benefits — Source: same — Confidence: OFFICIAL
- **53,058** workers benefited from 2022 conciliation-mediation (DOLE + NCMB); ₱3.44 billion awarded — Source: DOLE official press release (https://www.dole.gov.ph/news/dole-3-4-billion-pesos-conciliation-mediation-benefits-awarded-to-53058-workers/) — Confidence: OFFICIAL
  - 2022 breakdown: NCMB 19,802 cases, PHP 2.353B, 28,466 workers; SEnA at DOLE regional offices 14,960 workers, PHP 618M
- **109,000+** workers assisted in 2025 (partial year, January 2026 report); ₱8.34 billion awarded — Source: Manila Times, Jan 2, 2026 (https://www.manilatimes.net/2026/01/02/news/national/dole-reports-98-case-disposition-rate/2251754) — Confidence: OFFICIAL

### SSS Unemployment Benefit (D5/D6 adjacent signal)

- **64,000** SSS unemployment benefit claimants in 2023; ₱914.1 million disbursed — Source: PNA (https://www.pna.gov.ph/articles/1220393) — Confidence: OFFICIAL
- **75,000** SSS unemployment benefit claimants in 2022; ₱961.4 million disbursed — Source: same — Confidence: OFFICIAL
- Note: SSS unemployment benefit requires at least 36 monthly contributions and covers involuntary separation; the ~64,000–75,000 annual claimants are a subset of total involuntary separations (only those with sufficient SSS contribution history who actually filed)

### Labor Lawyers (Professional Segment Base)

**Population:**
- **84,236** cumulative lawyers in the Roll of Attorneys as of November 30, 2022 — Source: Deborja Law masterlist citing Supreme Court Roll of Attorneys — Confidence: OFFICIAL (cumulative, not active)
- **+3,992** new bar admissions in 2023; **+3,812** in 2024 (2023 Bar exam passers); **+3,962** in 2025 (2024 Bar exam passers) — Source: Supreme Court of the Philippines press releases — Confidence: OFFICIAL
- **Estimated ~92,000–96,000** cumulative Roll of Attorneys by end-2024 (84,236 + ~11,766 from 2022–2024 cohorts, gross; less deaths/disbarments/inactive estimated at ~3–5% attrition) — Confidence: DERIVED
- **Active practitioners:** IBP does not publish a count of active vs. inactive enrolled attorneys; MCLE (Mandatory Continuing Legal Education) compliance would be the proxy but aggregate compliance data is not publicly indexed — Confidence: DATA GAP
- **Estimated active bar members:** Using IBP membership fee compliance as a rough proxy: IBP-PH commentary suggests ~55–65% of enrolled lawyers are considered "active" (regularly filing appearances, paying IBP dues); **~50,000–60,000 active practicing lawyers** — Confidence: ESTIMATED
- **Labor law practitioners (NLRC-focused):** No PRC disaggregation (IBP does not regulate by specialty); labor law is among the most common specialties in Metro Manila. Estimated **~10–15%** of active bar = **~5,000–9,000 labor/NLRC practitioners** — Confidence: ESTIMATED

## D4 — Retirement Pay Calculator (RA7641) (Score: 4.20)

### Consumer Segment

**Who:** (1) Private-sector employees approaching optional retirement age (60 years old with 5+ years service) or compulsory retirement (65 years old) who need to verify their statutory minimum retirement pay entitlement under RA7641; (2) Employers with no CBA/superior company plan computing the RA7641 floor amount at employee separation; (3) Employees terminated for authorized causes who may have concurrent retirement pay entitlement.

**Population:**
- **~100,000–130,000** new private-sector retirees per year (RA7641-eligible cohort) — Source: DERIVED from 2.2M SSS pensioners (PNA 2024) ÷ 15–17 year average pension receipt period — Confidence: DERIVED
- **DOLE JDR 422,689 authorized-cause separations in 2023** — a subset of these involve retirement-age workers with concurrent RA7641 and separation pay claims — Confidence: OFFICIAL (not disaggregated by age)
- **Employers with obligation:** All private-sector establishments (~1.1–1.2M SSS-registered employers) who do not have a superior retirement plan have RA7641 as the floor obligation — Confidence: DERIVED

**Addressable fraction:** 60% — RA7641 computation is typically a one-time event at retirement; digitally active retirees + HR managers who would use a tool. Skews toward urban/formal sector workers who are aware of the statutory floor.

**Addressable consumer population:** 115,000 midpoint annual retirees × 60% digital = **~69,000 annual retirement events**

**Current professional cost:** Labor lawyer consultation for retirement pay computation/dispute: ₱5,000–₱25,000 per engagement; HR consultant: ₱3,000–₱8,000 per case. Many employers compute RA7641 manually using the "½ month salary per year of service" formula but errors in defining "½ month" (base + 5 days SIL + 1/12 of 13th month) are common and create disputes.

**Our consumer price:** ₱199/transaction (event-driven one-time use)

**Consumer TAM:** 69,000 × ₱199 = **₱13.7M/year**

### Professional Segment

**Who:** HR officers computing retirement separation packages at scale; labor lawyers handling RA7641 disputes at NLRC; payroll service firms processing retirement off-boarding for client companies.

**Population:**
- **~38,000** HR practitioners in formal sector — ESTIMATED (from PSA ASPBI 2022, same base as labor-payroll TAM)
- **~5,000–9,000** labor/NLRC practitioners — ESTIMATED
- Retirement-related NLRC cases: a small but recurring fraction of total money-claims cases; ~5–10% of NLRC caseload involves disputed retirement pay computation
- Combined professional addressable: 38,000 HR + 5,000 labor lawyers (conservative) = **43,000 professionals**

**Clients per professional per year:** HR officers handle company's annual retirees (~2–5 retirement separations/year for small firms; 20–50 for large); labor lawyers: 5–15 retirement pay dispute cases/year.

**B2B price per seat:** ₱999/month (Solo Pro tier)

**Professional TAM:** 43,000 × ₱999/mo × 12 = **₱515.5M/year**

| Segment | Population | Price | Annual TAM |
|---------|-----------|-------|-----------|
| Consumer (retirees/employers, transaction) | ~69,000 annual events | ₱199/use | ₱13.7M |

| **Total TAM** | | | **₱529.2M** |

**SOM Year 3 (5%):** ₱7.9M

## D5 — Separation Pay Calculator (Authorized Causes) (Score: 4.30)

### Consumer Segment

**Who:** (1) Employees retrenched, made redundant, or separated due to authorized causes (Articles 298–299, Labor Code) who need to verify their statutory separation pay entitlement (1 month or ½ month per year of service, whichever is higher, depending on cause); (2) Employers computing defensible separation pay to avoid NLRC exposure; (3) Workers separated via disease (Article 299) or installation of labor-saving devices who have distinct pay formulas.

**Population:**
- **422,689** workers displaced via retrenchment/closure in 2023 — Source: DOLE JDR 2023, BusinessMirror — Confidence: OFFICIAL
- **412,753** workers displaced in 2022 — Source: DOLE JDR 2022 — Confidence: OFFICIAL
- **DOLE JDR undercounting caveat:** Reporting is mandatory only for 10+ worker separations; individual authorized-cause dismissals from micro/small firms are excluded. Estimated true total (including small-establishment authorized causes): **~600,000–800,000/year** — Confidence: ESTIMATED
- **10,947 establishments** filed displacement reports in 2022 — Confidence: OFFICIAL
- 2022 sector breakdown: Construction 117,750 displaced; Other services 65,056; Admin/support 56,937; Manufacturing 56,372 (+53.6% YoY)

**Addressable fraction:** 70% — separation is a high-urgency, one-time event. Workers and employers are highly motivated to verify the correct amount. Digital accessibility is relevant but urgency drives higher adoption than average compliance tools.

**Addressable consumer population:** 422,689 × 70% digital/motivated = **~295,882 annual events**

**Current professional cost:** Labor lawyer: ₱5,000–₱30,000 per separation case (higher if disputed and goes to NLRC); HR consultant: ₱3,000–₱10,000 per engagement for employer-side computation. NLRC filing involves 10% success fee if worker wins (computed on total monetary award).

**Our consumer price:** ₱199/transaction (one-time, event-driven; higher-urgency justifies same price point as D3 Final Pay)

**Consumer TAM:** 295,882 × ₱199 = **₱58.9M/year**

### Professional Segment

**Who:** Labor lawyers computing authorized-cause separation packages for employer clients or preparing demand letters for employee clients; HR officers computing mass retrenchment packages for cost-cutting exercises; payroll service firms off-boarding retrenched workers.

**Population:**
- **~38,000** HR practitioners — ESTIMATED
- **~5,000–9,000** labor/NLRC practitioners — ESTIMATED
- The ~10,947 establishments that filed DOLE JDR reports in 2022 all needed a separation pay computation; most of these have HR/legal support
- Combined: 38,000 HR + 7,000 labor lawyers (midpoint) = **45,000 professionals**

**B2B price per seat:** ₱999/month

**Professional TAM:** 45,000 × ₱999/mo × 12 = **₱539.5M/year**

### Total TAM — D5

|---------|-----------|-------|-----------|
| Consumer (retrenched workers/employers, transaction) | ~296K annual events | ₱199/use | ₱58.9M |
| Professional (B2B seats) | ~45,000 professionals | ₱999/mo | ₱539.5M |
| **Total TAM** | | | **₱598.4M** |

**SAM:** ₱179M — formal sector with reporting obligations + high-urgency individual workers; excludes informal micro-sector

## D6 — Back Wages Estimator (Illegal Dismissal) (Score: 4.10)

### Consumer Segment

**Who:** (1) Employees who believe they have been illegally dismissed and want to estimate back wages (full pay from dismissal to reinstatement/finality of decision) before deciding whether to file at NLRC; (2) Workers already in NLRC proceedings needing to track accruing back wages; (3) Employers quantifying financial exposure from a pending illegal dismissal case.

**Population:**
- **~35,000–45,000** NLRC cases filed per year (national estimate, all case types) — Source: ESTIMATED from RAB 7 regional anchor (2,944 cases in 2018 per PNA article 1065020) extrapolated nationally using population weights (7.3% of PH population) — Confidence: ESTIMATED
- **~14,000–27,000** illegal dismissal cases per year (40–60% of NLRC caseload per legal commentary) — Confidence: ESTIMATED
- **83,836** total SEnA RFAs in 2024 (upstream proxy — SEnA is the mandatory first step before NLRC filing) — Source: Philippine Tribune, March 3, 2025 — Confidence: OFFICIAL
  - Of SEnA RFAs, approximately 40–50% fail mediation and may proceed to NLRC: estimated **~33,000–42,000 potential NLRC filings/year**
- **Corroborating signal — SSS unemployment benefit:** 64,000 claimants in 2023 (OFFICIAL) — these are a subset of involuntary separations with possible illegal dismissal claims; not all SSS unemployment claimants have NLRC cases

**Addressable fraction:** 55% — illegal dismissal cases are high-stakes, multi-year proceedings (average 3–5 years at NLRC); workers who would use a back-wages tool are those with active cases or serious intent to file. Lower than D5 because many workers abandon the NLRC process due to cost and delay.

**Addressable consumer population:** 20,500 midpoint illegal dismissal cases × 55% = **~11,275 annual users**

**Current professional cost:** Labor lawyer on contingency: typically 10–20% of total monetary award if won (back wages + separation pay can total ₱500K–₱5M+ for a multi-year case); NLRC filing fee: calculated on monetary claim amount. Back wages estimation before filing is valuable precisely because most workers don't know if the case is worth pursuing.

**Our consumer price:** ₱199/transaction or ₱499 (higher given complexity and stakes)

**Consumer TAM (at ₱199):** 11,275 × ₱199 = **₱2.2M/year**
**Consumer TAM (at ₱499):** 11,275 × ₱499 = **₱5.6M/year**

Using ₱199: **₱2.2M/year** (conservative)

### Professional Segment

**Who:** Labor lawyers tracking accruing back wages across their portfolio of illegal dismissal cases; NLRC practitioners quantifying total monetary claims in position papers; labor law professors/clinics computing case value for students.

**Population:**
- **~7,000** labor/NLRC practitioners (midpoint of 5,000–9,000 range) — ESTIMATED
- At NLRC, every pending illegal dismissal case requires both parties' lawyers to reference the back wages formula (RA6715: full pay without deduction or qualification from dismissal to actual reinstatement or from dismissal to finality)
- B2B use case: law firm subscription where each lawyer tracks multiple active NLRC cases

**Clients per professional per year:** Labor lawyer handling active NLRC cases: 10–40 cases/year with some having multi-year accruing back wages.

**B2B price per seat:** ₱999/month

**Professional TAM:** 7,000 × ₱999/mo × 12 = **₱83.9M/year**

### Total TAM — D6

| Segment | Population | Price | Annual TAM |
|---------|-----------|-------|-----------|
| Consumer (workers/employers at NLRC) | ~11,275 annual cases | ₱199/use | ₱2.2M |
| Professional (labor lawyer B2B seats) | ~7,000 NLRC practitioners | ₱999/mo | ₱83.9M |
| **Total TAM** | | | **₱86.1M** |

**SAM:** ₱25.8M — active NLRC practitioners + high-value illegal dismissal case workers in NCR and major cities; excludes self-represented litigants without digital access
**SOM Year 1 (1%):** ₱258K
**SOM Year 3 (5%):** ₱1.3M

**Note:** D6 has the smallest consumer TAM of the cluster due to narrow addressable market (only workers in active NLRC proceedings). Its professional TAM is meaningful as a B2B tool for labor law firms managing NLRC portfolios. Best positioned as a feature within a broader B2B labor compliance platform rather than a standalone consumer product.

## D9 — SEnA Request Navigator (Score: 3.90)

### Consumer Segment

**Who:** (1) Workers filing or about to file a Request for Assistance (RFA) at DOLE's Single Entry Approach desk — SEnA is the mandatory pre-filing step for labor money claims, illegal dismissal, and other individual labor disputes; (2) Employers who receive SEnA summons and need to understand the process; (3) Workers or employers navigating the NCMB (National Conciliation and Mediation Board) conciliation process.

**Population:**
- **83,836** total RFAs filed in 2024 — Source: Philippine Tribune, March 3, 2025 (https://tribune.net.ph/2025/03/03/dole-processed-over-83k-requests-for-assistance-in-2024) — Confidence: OFFICIAL
- **53,058** workers benefited from DOLE/NCMB conciliation-mediation in 2022 — Source: DOLE official press release — Confidence: OFFICIAL
- **109,000+** workers assisted in 2025 (preliminary) — Source: Manila Times, Jan 2, 2026 — Confidence: OFFICIAL
- NCMB H1 2023: 1,725 RFAs at NCMB desk alone; 1,107 settled (64.2% settlement rate); ₱151.2M in claims; 2,094 workers — Source: NCMB press release — Confidence: OFFICIAL (NCMB only, excludes DOLE regional SEnA desks)
- 2022 NCMB breakdown: 19,802 cases disposed; ₱2.353B; 28,466 workers — Source: DOLE press release — Confidence: OFFICIAL
- **Trend:** Growing from ~35,000–36,000 RFAs (2016–2018 per DOLE ILS policy brief) to 83,836 in 2024 → approximately 2.4× growth; likely reflects expanded e-filing (DOLE SENA Online launched during COVID), rising awareness, and post-pandemic labor disputes

**Addressable fraction:** 65% — SEnA is a mandatory pre-step with real stakes; workers and employers who would use a navigator tool are those who want to understand the process before their first DOLE appearance. High urgency drives adoption. Skews toward formal-sector workers with internet access.

**Addressable consumer population:** 83,836 × 65% = **~54,493 annual RFA filers** (2024 baseline; using 2024 figure as current annual run rate)

**Current professional cost:** Labor lawyer for SEnA appearance/assistance: ₱3,000–₱15,000 per case; many workers self-represent at SEnA (it is designed to be accessible without a lawyer), but employer-side almost always has counsel.

**Our consumer price:** ₱199/case (one-time navigator fee — helps filer prepare their RFA, understand what to bring, what to expect at the SEnA conference)

**Consumer TAM:** 54,493 × ₱199 = **₱10.8M/year**

### Professional Segment

**Who:** Labor lawyers preparing clients for SEnA conferences; HR officers managing SEnA summons response procedures; DOLE-accredited conciliators and mediators (professional context: understanding procedural requirements per RO).

**Population:**
- **~7,000** labor/NLRC practitioners — ESTIMATED (same base as D6)
- HR officers at established companies: all 38,000 HR practitioners deal with occasional SEnA summons; those at large/medium employers (7,362 ASPBI-identified) have recurring SEnA exposure
- Combined addressable: 7,000 labor lawyers + 15,000 HR at large/medium establishments = **22,000 professionals**

**B2B price per seat:** ₱999/month (practitioners with ongoing SEnA caseloads benefit from procedural reference tool per regional office)

**Professional TAM:** 22,000 × ₱999/mo × 12 = **₱263.7M/year**

### Total TAM — D9

| Segment | Population | Price | Annual TAM |
|---------|-----------|-------|-----------|
| Consumer (SEnA filers, per-case) | ~54,493 annual RFA filers | ₱199/use | ₱10.8M |
| Professional (B2B seats) | ~22,000 professionals | ₱999/mo | ₱263.7M |
| **Total TAM** | | | **₱274.5M** |

**SAM:** ₱82.4M — urban SEnA filers with digital access + active labor law practitioners; excludes remote regions and self-represented litigants who prefer free DOLE walk-in assistance
**SOM Year 1 (1%):** ₱824K
**SOM Year 3 (5%):** ₱4.1M

## Notes & Caveats

1. **NLRC national case filing data is the biggest gap in this cluster.** No publicly indexed annual aggregate exists for 2022–2024. The ~35,000–45,000/year estimate is derived from a single 2018 regional data point (RAB 7) and cross-checked against the SEnA pipeline volume. An FOI request to NLRC (foi.gov.ph/agencies/nlrcom/) for annual case filings by case type would resolve this; the portal shows prior FOI requests have been fulfilled for incumbent arbiter lists (Dec 2023 request). This estimate should be treated as ±50% range.

2. **DOLE JDR undercounts total separation pay events significantly.** The 422,689 figure covers only establishments that filed displacement reports (mandatory for 10+ worker separations under Labor Code Article 298–299). Individual authorized-cause terminations from small firms, project-based workers, and sub-10-employee establishments are excluded. The true total annual separation pay computation events is likely 1.5–2× higher, but no official source quantifies the gap.

3. **D4 (RA7641) consumer TAM is small relative to professional TAM.** The ~100,000–130,000 annual private-sector retirees is an event-driven market with modest per-transaction revenue. The tool's value is more defensible as a B2B professional feature (HR officers computing dozens of retirements per year for client companies) than as a standalone consumer product. Bundling with D5 (separation pay) increases capture rate since many RA7641 cases also involve authorized-cause separation pay.

4. **D6 (Back Wages) is the narrowest market in this cluster.** Only workers in active illegal dismissal proceedings need a back-wages tracker; this is a subset of the ~14,000–27,000 estimated illegal dismissal case filers per year, and many of these are represented by lawyers who would be the B2B subscriber. The consumer TAM of ₱2.2M/year is too small to justify a standalone consumer product. Best positioned as a B2B feature for labor law firms.

5. **SEnA growth trajectory:** From ~35,000 RFAs/year (2016–2018) to 83,836 in 2024 and 109,000+ workers assisted in 2025 (prelimnary). The rapid growth suggests rising labor disputes and/or improved filing access (DOLE SENA Online). Projecting 2025 full-year RFAs at ~100,000–120,000 makes D9's consumer TAM the most dynamic in this cluster.

6. **IBP active lawyer count remains unverified officially.** The 84,236 cumulative Roll of Attorneys figure (Nov 2022) represents all lawyers ever admitted to the bar, not active practitioners. Attrition from retirement, death, and disbarment over the 60+ year span of the roll could reduce the active pool substantially. The ~50,000–60,000 active estimate assumes ~35–40% cumulative attrition since 1960s, which is standard for comparable bar populations but is not sourced from an IBP official statistic.

7. **Professional segment overlap across D4/D5/D6/D9:** All four tools' professional segments are drawn from the same population of ~38,000 HR practitioners and ~7,000 labor lawyers. A bundled "labor separation suite" product (one subscription covering all four tools) priced at the Practice tier (₱2,999/mo) captures the professional TAM more accurately than summing individual tool professional TAMs.
