# TAM: Maritime Seafarer Compliance Tools (I-MAR-1 through I-MAR-4)

**Loop source:** regulatory-atlas

---

## Tools Covered

| Tool ID | Name | Description |
|---------|------|-------------|
| I-MAR-1 | STCW Cost Calculator | Calculates total cost for STCW certificate training packages (BST, AFF, PSCRB, MEFA, etc.) from MARINA-accredited centers |
| I-MAR-2 | STCW Pathway Navigator | Shows which certificates a seafarer needs for a given rank/career progression target |
| I-MAR-3 | COC Revalidation Checklist | Tracks Certificate of Competency revalidation requirements: sea service months, required refresher training, documentary checklist, deadlines |
| I-MAR-4 | Tonnage Fee Calculator | Computes MARINA annual tonnage fees (ATF) for domestic vessel operators based on gross tonnage; includes MARINA certification fees for vessel transactions |

---

## Shared Population Base

The four maritime tools address two distinct but overlapping populations:

1. **Seafarers (I-MAR-1, I-MAR-2, I-MAR-3):** Individual Filipino seafarers who hold or are pursuing MARINA-issued documents (SIRB/SID/SRB, COC, COP, GMDSS certificates). This is the largest consumer segment.

2. **Vessel operators / domestic shipping companies (I-MAR-4):** Philippine-registered domestic ship operators required to pay the Annual Tonnage Fee (ATF) to MARINA. Separate from the seafarer population.

### Consumer Segment

**Who:** All Filipino seafarers seeking initial STCW certification or tracking costs for certificate renewals/refresher training every 5 years. Primarily new seafarers entering the profession and seafarers within 1–2 years of their 5-year revalidation deadline.

**Population:**
- **Total registered seafarers (SIRB/SID/SRB holders):** ~1,000,000 — Source: MARINA 2018 SIRB census; MARINA Administrator quotes ~800,000–1.1M in various years — Confidence: OFFICIAL (dated, range)
- **Annual new seafarers entering profession (new SID issued):** 64,098 in 2025 — Source: BusinessMirror/MARINA 2025 stats — Confidence: OFFICIAL (proxy; 2025 figure used as best available)
- **Annual STCW revalidation cycle:** COCs are valid for 5 years. At steady state, ~1/5 of COC holders revalidate each year. With 39,397 COCs issued in 2025 as a flow proxy, the stock of active COC holders requiring eventual revalidation is approximately 39,397 × 5 = ~197,000 COC holders. But total COP holders (ratings) are higher (520,000+ COPs issued annually × 5 years implies ~2.6M cumulative active COPs if all were different people — however, individual seafarers hold multiple COPs simultaneously, so the person-count is much lower than the COP count). **The most defensible headcount for active seafarers who hold STCW certifications: ~600,000–800,000 active registered seafarers** — Confidence: ESTIMATED

**Addressable fraction:**
- OFW seafarers are a high-digital-adoption segment (deployed internationally, high smartphone use, process government documents online via MISMO)
- PSA 2023 national internet penetration: 54% households; OFW/seafarer demographic adjustment upward: **75% internet penetration** (consistent with OFW TAM methodology in this loop)
- Addressable: 800,000 × 75% = **600,000 addressable seafarers**

**Addressable consumer population:** ~600,000 active registered seafarers with internet access

**Current professional cost / alternative:** Seafarers currently rely on:
- Manning agencies (which often shoulder STCW training costs for deployed crew, but individual seafarers bear costs for initial training and unsponsored revalidations)
- Physical visits to MARINA STCW office (Bonifacio Drive, Port Area, Manila) or regional offices
- MISMO portal (free, but no cost-comparison or pathway-guidance features)
- Community Facebook groups (e.g., Filipino Seafarers groups) for informal fee comparisons
- No official government cost calculator exists
- Out-of-pocket cost for full STCW package: ₱15,000–₱30,000 (one-time at career start); revalidation refresher courses: ₱8,000–₱15,000 per cycle

**Our consumer price:** ₱99 one-time (cost calculation event, triggered at training planning stage; career decision point)
Alternative: ₱199/month for 1–2 months during training/revalidation planning period

**Consumer TAM:**
- Transaction model: 600,000 × ₱99 = **₱59.4M/year** (if entire active base uses it once per year — realistically triggered by new entrants + revalidation cohort)
- More targeted: (64,098 new/year + ~120,000–150,000 in annual revalidation window) × ₱99 ≈ 200,000 events × ₱99 = **₱19.8M/year**
- **Working TAM: ₱20M/year** (annual trigger events: new STCW seekers + revalidation cohort)

## I-MAR-2: STCW Pathway Navigator

*(Tool: Given a seafarer's current certificates and target rank, generates the specific STCW certificate sequence needed, including prerequisites, typical sequence, estimated total cost and time)*

### Consumer Segment

**Who:** Seafarers planning career progression — entry-level ratings seeking to become officers, OOWs seeking to become Chief Mates or Masters, Chief Engineers planning management-level upgrades. Also: maritime school graduates planning their first STCW package, and returning seafarers after career breaks who need to re-qualify.

**Population:**
- Total registered seafarers: ~1,000,000 — same base as I-MAR-1
- Career progression decisions happen at specific milestones: entry into profession, first officer upgrade, management-level upgrade. These are not annual events per person — typically 2–4 such decisions per career (over 20–30 year career span).
- **Annual new entrants:** ~64,098 SIDs issued in 2025 — most of these are career-start events where the pathway tool is highly relevant — Confidence: OFFICIAL
- **Annual career-progression COC applications:** ~39,397 COCs issued in 2025 — these are the officer certification pipeline events — Confidence: OFFICIAL
- **Annual officer promotions + new entrants needing pathway guidance:** ~100,000–150,000 events/year — Confidence: ESTIMATED (sum of new SIDs + fraction of COC revalidations involving rank change)

**Addressable fraction:** 75% internet × 150,000 = **~112,500 addressable events/year**

**Current professional cost / alternative:** No official MARINA pathway tool exists. Seafarers rely on:
- Manning agency HR staff guidance (free but biased toward agency's preferred training centers)
- Community forums (Facebook groups for Filipino seafarers)
- Maritime training center admissions staff
- The MARINA MISMO portal (lists requirements but does not provide a guided pathway)
Errors in career pathway planning cost seafarers real money (paying for wrong certificates, having to retake prerequisites). Common mistake: spending ₱5,000–₱15,000 on a COP not needed for their target rank.

**Our consumer price:** ₱199/month (1–2 months engagement at career planning stage)

**Consumer TAM:** 112,500 × ₱199 × 1.5 months average = **₱33.6M/year**
Simplified: 112,500 × ₱199 = **₱22.4M/year** (1-month model)
**Working TAM: ₱22M/year**

## I-MAR-3: COC Revalidation Checklist

*(Tool: Generates a personalized COC revalidation checklist based on current rank, certificate type, and expiry date — including sea service requirements (12 months in preceding 5 years OR 3 months in preceding 6 months), mandatory refresher training (BT, AFF, PSCRB, MEFA), document list, MARINA MISMO steps, fees, and deadline tracker)*

### Consumer Segment

**Who:** Officers holding Certificates of Competency (COC) approaching the 5-year revalidation deadline. COC holders include Master Mariners, Chief Engineers, Chief Mates, Second Engineers, and Officers in Charge of Navigational/Engineering Watch (OOW/OEW). The tool is triggered within 6–12 months before COC expiry.

**Population — Annual COC Revalidation Events:**

- **Annual COC issuances/revalidations combined (2025):** 39,397 — Source: BusinessMirror/MARINA 2025 — Confidence: OFFICIAL
  - This figure includes both new COC issuances (officers qualifying for the first time) and revalidations. No split between new issuances and revalidations was found in publicly indexed sources.
  - Assumed split: ~30% new issuances (officers qualifying for the first time) + ~70% revalidations (officers revalidating existing COC) = ~27,578 revalidation transactions in 2025 — Confidence: ESTIMATED
- **Total active COC holders (stock):** If ~39,000 COCs are processed annually (combined new + revalidation) and COCs are valid for 5 years, the stock is approximately **39,000 × 5 = ~195,000 active COC holders** — Confidence: DERIVED
  - Cross-check: PRC data shows 73,250 registered Marine Deck Officers (2012) + 63,879 Marine Engineers (2012) = ~137,000 combined, trending toward ~170,000–200,000 by 2023 at historical growth rates — consistent with the DERIVED estimate of ~195,000
- **Annual revalidation cohort (those due to revalidate each year):** ~195,000 ÷ 5 = **~39,000 COC revalidations per year** — Confidence: DERIVED (consistent with the annual COC volume figure above)

**Addressable fraction:**
- COC holders are officers — the highest-digital-adoption segment of seafarers (higher education level, regular computer access for MISMO online applications)
- Apply 80% internet/digital tool adoption (higher than general seafarer estimate)
- 39,000 × 80% = **~31,200 addressable revalidation events/year**

**Current professional cost / alternative:**
- COC revalidation is a high-stakes event (failure to revalidate on time = cannot work at sea; overtime penalty: ₱500 late filing fee + potential loss of employment contract)
- Seafarers currently use: MARINA MISMO portal (official, but checklist-only, no guidance), community Facebook groups, manning agency HR coordinators, and private maritime consultants (₱2,000–₱5,000 for guided documentation assistance) — Source: ESTIMATED from forum reports
- Common errors: missing refresher course deadlines, insufficient documented sea service, wrong document format — costly to correct mid-application

**Our consumer price:** ₱199/month (2–3 months engagement during revalidation preparation window)

**Consumer TAM:** 31,200 × ₱199 × 2.5 months = **₱15.5M/year**
**Working TAM: ₱15M/year**

### Professional Segment

**Who:** Manning agency HR coordinators who track COC revalidation deadlines for their crew roster (agencies managing 50–500+ active seafarers each need to track revalidation status across their entire deployed fleet); MARINA-accredited assessment centers that process COC revalidation applications.

**Population:**
- ~500 active sea-based manning agencies — each managing a crew roster with multiple COC revalidations due each year
- **Per agency: crew roster size varies widely:** Large agencies (e.g., Magsaysay, CF Sharp, Eastern Shipping) manage 5,000–15,000 active seafarers; mid-size agencies manage 200–2,000; small agencies manage 50–200. Top 20 agencies deploy the majority of Philippine seafarers. Average roster size across 500 agencies: ESTIMATED 1,242 seafarers/agency (621,484 total deployments ÷ 500 agencies). Annual COC revalidations per average-sized agency: ~1,242 × (proportion holding COCs) × (1/5 revalidating annually) = ~1,242 × 52% officers × 20% = ~130 COC revalidation events per agency per year — Confidence: DERIVED
- **The COC tracking tool has clear B2B value for agencies:** Without a system, agencies manually track revalidation dates; missing a revalidation means a crew member cannot be deployed (lost revenue for agency)
- 31 Accredited Assessment Centers also process COC revalidations

**Working professional estimate:** 500 manning agencies (primary B2B target)

**B2B price per seat:** ₱2,999/month (Practice tier — crew management tool, not just a single-user tool; manages multiple seafarers' certificates)

**Professional TAM:** 500 × ₱2,999 × 12 = **₱18.0M/year**
Alternative at ₱999/month (Solo Pro): 500 × ₱999 × 12 = ₱6.0M/year
**Working TAM (at ₱999 Solo Pro, conservative):** ₱6.0M/year

## I-MAR-4: Tonnage Fee Calculator

*(Tool: Computes MARINA Annual Tonnage Fee (ATF) for Philippine-registered domestic vessels based on gross tonnage, vessel class, and fleet size; also calculates MARINA certification fees for vessel transactions — registration, acquisition, accreditation fees)*

### Background: The Annual Tonnage Fee (ATF)

- **Who pays:** All owners/operators or charterers of Philippine-registered ships in domestic trade, except fishing vessels and non-motorized vessels below 3 GT — Source: MARINA Memorandum Circular Nos. 2008-05 and 2008-07 — Confidence: OFFICIAL
- **Basis:** Total gross tonnage of the operated fleet as of December 31 of the preceding year — Confidence: OFFICIAL
- **Fee schedule:** Governed by MC 2008-05/2008-07 (original ATF rates), updated by MC 2015-05 (20% increase effective 2015), and now superseded by MC GC-2026-01 (Omnibus MARINA Schedule of Fees, approved Nov 2025, effective Jan 2026) — Confidence: OFFICIAL
- **Total ATF collected 2020–2021 combined:** ₱48,457,124.02 — Source: Commission on Audit (COA) report via Manila Bulletin, Sep 12, 2022 — Confidence: OFFICIAL
  - This low collection figure (~₱24M/year average) reflects MARINA's documented recordkeeping failures: COA found MARINA lacked updated vessel masterlists, with 6.16% of ATF collected representing fees for 2010–2017, and 8.85% coming from operators not in MARINA's submitted masterlists
- **Specific ATF rate per GT:** The exact per-GT rate table from MC 2015-05 and MC 2008-05 was not accessible from publicly indexed sources (PDFs blocked). The 2015 increase was 20% above the 2008 rates. The 2026 Omnibus MC updates these rates further.

## Notes & Caveats

1. **No 2023-specific MARINA annual statistical report data extracted:** The 2023 MARINA Statistical Report PDF (marina.gov.ph/wp-content/uploads/2024/11/2023-MARINA-Statistical-Report.pdf) exceeds the content length limit and could not be directly extracted. The 2025 MARINA statistics (from BusinessMirror, published Jan 1, 2026) are used as the most recent published annual figures and are assumed to be comparable to 2023 levels given the stable COP volumes (2021: 523,509; 2025: 520,759) and stable COC volumes.

2. **MARINA SIRB/SRB/SID total registered seafarer count — data gap:** No single official published figure for total registered seafarers as of 2023 was found in publicly indexed web sources. The MARINA 2018 figure (~1 million SIRB holders) and the administrator quotes of 800,000–1.1 million provide a range. The 2023 figure is likely in the range of 900,000–1,100,000 given: (a) MARINA has recovered from COVID-era attrition, (b) new SID issuances of ~64,000/year add to the stock, (c) attrition from retirement/career changes reduces it. An FOI request to MARINA or accessing the 2023 Statistical Report PDF directly would yield the exact figure.

3. **COC annual volume splits (new vs. revalidation):** The 39,397 COCs issued in 2025 includes both new COC issuances (first-time officers qualifying after PRC board exam passage + MARINA assessment) and revalidations (existing COC holders renewing before 5-year expiry). MARINA does not appear to publish this split in publicly accessible sources. The 70/30 split (revalidation vs. new) is an estimate.

4. **Training center pricing — only Globe Maritime fully accessible:** Globe Maritime Training Center is the only training center that published a complete fee schedule accessible via WebFetch during this research session. PNTC, CTSI, MAAP-ASTC (the other major centers) direct inquiries to Messenger/phone for pricing, suggesting their published fee pages are dynamically loaded or gated. The Globe Maritime prices are treated as the market reference point; TMTCP's ₱3,500 BT price (from a defunct domain, cited in multiple sources) confirms Globe Maritime's ₱4,999 BT price represents the current mid-market range (training costs have increased since pre-COVID).

5. **Sea-based manning agencies — nationwide count data gap:** The most granular data found is the Metro Manila sub-counts from the Captain's Mode/POEA database: Makati (88), Malate (80), Pasay (28), Pasig (12), QC (5) = 213 confirmed in those districts. With Ermita, Binondo, Manila proper, and other Metro Manila areas plus Cebu/Davao/Iloilo clusters, the nationwide total is estimated at 400–600 active sea-based manning agencies. This is distinct from land-based recruitment agencies; the two should not be conflated. No official published total active sea-based manning agency count was found.

6. **I-MAR-4 context — ATF compliance problem:** The COA finding that MARINA collected only ~₱48.5M in ATF across 2020–2021 (combined, ~₱24M/year) reflects a systemic compliance and recordkeeping failure at MARINA, not the full scope of fees legally owed. A proper ATF calculator could help vessel operators self-compute correct ATF and reduce underpayment risk, but the primary beneficiary of improved compliance would be MARINA's revenue base. The commercial opportunity for a private tool is narrower than the regulatory compliance need suggests.

7. **Professional TAM pricing note for I-MAR-3:** Manning agencies managing large crew rosters (100+ active COC holders) have a stronger case for the ₱2,999/month Practice tier (crew management platform) than for a single-user ₱999 tool. The ₱999 figure used in the master summary is conservative. Agencies managing 500+ active crew would likely pay ₱2,999/month for a proper crew certification tracking system, making the I-MAR-3 professional TAM potentially as high as ₱18M/year (500 agencies × ₱2,999 × 12).

8. **STCW 2026 amendments create a TAM catalyst:** Resolution MSC.560(108) (May 2024) mandates a new PSSR module (harassment/SASH prevention) effective January 1, 2026. This requires every active seafarer to take a new Updating Course (estimated ₱1,500 per person at Globe Maritime). With ~600,000 active seafarers needing this update by Jan 2026, total industry training expenditure on this single mandatory update = ₱900M (600,000 × ₱1,500). This creates a time-limited but large cost-calculation event that is precisely the use case for I-MAR-1. (Note: the 2026 PSSR update is a one-time event, not a recurring TAM driver, but demonstrates the scale of compliance-driven expenditure in this sector.)
