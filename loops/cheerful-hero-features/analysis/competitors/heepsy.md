# Competitor Deep-Dive: Heepsy

**Wave 2a Analysis — SMB / Prosumer Tier**
**Date:** 2026-02-28

---

## Company Overview

| Field | Detail |
|-------|--------|
| **Founded** | 2016 |
| **Founder** | Xabier Vicuna |
| **HQ** | Loiu / Bilbao, Spain |
| **Funding** | Bootstrapped (no public funding rounds) |
| **Target Market** | SMBs, agencies, eCommerce brands new to influencer marketing |
| **Database** | 11M+ profiles (Instagram, TikTok, YouTube, Twitch) |
| **Pricing** | Free → $89 → $249 → $369/month |
| **G2 Rating** | 4.5/5 (40 reviews) |
| **Capterra Rating** | 4.5/5 (72 reviews) |

Heepsy is a **budget-friendly influencer discovery and outreach platform** targeting small-to-mid-sized brands and agencies who want a simple entry point into influencer marketing. Its value proposition is affordability + ease of use over deep functionality. It competes primarily on price against Modash ($99–$399/mo) and HypeAuditor, while offering fewer features than GRIN, Upfluence, or enterprise tiers.

---

## Feature Inventory (Mapped to Wave 1 Categories)

### Discovery & Search
- **11M+ profiles** across Instagram, TikTok, YouTube, Twitch — small vs Modash (350M+), HypeAuditor (218M+)
- **30+ search filters**: niche/category, follower count, engagement rate, location, language, platform
- **Audience analytics**: demographics (age, gender, location), interests, brand affinity
- **Lookalike search**: find influencers similar to a known creator — differentiator at SMB price point
- **Minimum follower threshold**: 3K–5K followers minimum — blocks nano-influencer campaigns
- **Fake follower detection** ("Audience Authenticity Filter"): ~85% accuracy, sampling-based (vs HypeAuditor's 95.5%)
- Audience analytics and advanced filters (age, gender, location) locked to Plus/Advanced plans

### Outreach & CRM
- **Bulk outreach** with pre-made templates; customizable key fields
- **No AI-generated personalized outreach** — templates only, no dynamic generation
- Basic follow-up sequences
- CRM-lite: add creators to boards, outline collaboration terms
- No pipeline view, no deal stage tracking
- Users report only ~5% of outreach results in collaborations (G2 review)
- G2 reviewers note it needs "relationship management CRM or campaign reporting" — teams adopt external tools

**Sources:**
- [Heepsy Reviews G2](https://www.g2.com/products/heepsy/reviews)
- [Heepsy Capterra Reviews](https://www.capterra.com/p/202552/Heepsy/reviews/)

### Campaign Management
- Users can publish unlimited campaign offers (gifting, paid, affiliate, UGC)
- Basic campaign tracking: monitor performance metrics, media, payments in one view
- No structured campaign pipeline (brief → shortlist → negotiate → execute → report)
- Campaign reporting available on Plus plan ($249/mo) and above
- No approval workflows or content review stages

### Content Collaboration
- **Instagram Monitoring** (new feature): auto-collects posts and stories from tracked collaborators in real-time
- Media gallery on Plus plan and above
- No content approval workflow, no creative brief builder, no revision tracking

### Payments & Contracts
- Payment processing available within the platform
- No details on contract templates or e-signature
- No tax compliance or W-9/W-8 handling mentioned
- Commission on payments cited as "high" in Capterra reviews

### Analytics & Reporting
- Campaign reporting on Plus/Advanced plans
- Estimates CPM before collaborations
- Personalized tracking links for referred clicks
- Shopify-based sales attribution (Advanced plan only, $369/mo)
- No advanced ROI dashboards, no competitive benchmarking
- No share-of-voice or audience overlap analytics

### Integrations & Ecosystem
- **Shopify**: primary integration — gifting, affiliate links, sales tracking (Advanced plan only)
- **WooCommerce**: Advanced plan only
- **No API** — not available, blocking custom automation
- No CRM integrations (no HubSpot, Salesforce, Klaviyo)
- No email platform integrations (no Mailchimp, SendGrid)
- No Slack, no ad platform integrations
- Very narrow integration surface vs GRIN (Adobe Commerce, BigCommerce, Shopify, WooCommerce) or Upfluence

### AI & Automation
- AI-driven search engine (search relevance + recommendation)
- Lookalike influencer finder
- No AI outreach generation
- No predictive analytics
- No workflow automation

### Marketplace & Network
- **Creator Marketplace**: influencers browse and apply to brand campaigns
- Creators filter campaigns by location, network, category
- Not a closed network — self-service marketplace model

### Team Collaboration
- Boards for organizing influencer lists
- No multi-user role management detailed in reviews
- No audit trails, no approval workflows for internal teams

## Unique Differentiators

1. **Lookalike search at SMB price point** — most platforms offering lookalike are enterprise-tier; Heepsy provides it at $89–$369/mo
2. **Instagram Monitoring** — real-time auto-capture of collaborator posts/stories without manual tracking; unique vs peers at this price range
3. **Free tier** — actual discovery functionality (vs Modash's free trial, GRIN's no trial); lowers acquisition friction
4. **Creator Marketplace dual-sided** — both outbound search and inbound creator applications in one tool

---

## Weaknesses

1. **Tiny database (11M)** relative to competitors: Modash 350M+, HypeAuditor 218M+, Sprout Social 250M+
2. **No API** — zero custom automation; users must operate entirely within Heepsy's UI
3. **Shopify-only integration** — narrows to one eCommerce platform; no direct CRM, email, or ad platform connections
4. **Feature gating** is aggressive: basic age/gender/location filters require Plus/Advanced plans
5. **Fake follower detection ~85% accuracy** — below HypeAuditor's 95.5% with 35 audience quality metrics
6. **No AI outreach** — template-only, no personalization at scale
7. **No nano-influencer support** — 3K–5K follower floor blocks micro/nano campaigns
8. **Blanket no-refund policy** — top complaint on G2 and Capterra; damages trust and retention

## Pricing Model

| Plan | Price | Key Limits | Lock-In Mechanism |
|------|-------|-----------|------------------|
| Free | $0 | Discovery only, no exports | Leads to paid funnel |
| Starter | $89/mo | 15K results, 50 profiles, 50 emails | Annual billing available |
| Plus | $249/mo | 40K results, 1K profiles, 1K emails | Bulk outreach + reporting unlocked |
| Advanced | $369/mo | 60K results, 3K profiles, 3K emails | Shopify + affiliate tracking |

**Lock-in mechanisms:**
- Data locked in boards/lists (no export on free plan)
- Shopify integration creates stickiness for eCommerce brands on Advanced
- No API means no easy data migration
- Annual billing discounts encourage multi-year commitment

## Workflow Integration Depth

**Low-to-medium.** Heepsy sits primarily at the **discovery + initial outreach** stage. It does not penetrate deeply into:
- Campaign execution (no creative brief, approval flow, or content tracking beyond basic monitoring)
- Analytics (no ROI dashboards, no competitive intelligence)
- Team workflow (no multi-user collaboration beyond lists)
- Financial workflow (no robust payments/contracts/tax compliance)

Teams using Heepsy typically adopt external tools for: CRM (HubSpot), contracts (DocuSign), payments (wire/ACH), reporting (Google Sheets, Looker), and content management (Drive/Notion).

**Daily active usage:** Primarily at campaign launch (discovery + outreach) and reporting (post-campaign). Not a daily-use tool for most users.

## What Cheerful Can Learn

### Gaps Where Cheerful Is Stronger
Based on the Cheerful spec (110+ API endpoints, 35+ data tables, Temporal workflows, 20 integrations, 89 user stories):

- **Cheerful has deeper outreach automation** — email sequences, AI personalization, inbox management vs Heepsy's template-only bulk outreach
- **Cheerful's integration surface is broader** — 20 integrations vs Heepsy's Shopify-only
- **Cheerful has campaign pipeline management** — structured stages vs Heepsy's basic tracking
- **Cheerful should have API access** — critical differentiator Heepsy completely lacks

### Gaps Where Heepsy Is Ahead of Cheerful
1. **Lookalike creator search** — Heepsy offers this at SMB price; if Cheerful lacks it, it's a discovery gap
2. **Instagram Monitoring / auto-capture** — passive content tracking without manual effort; worth replicating
3. **Creator Marketplace (inbound)** — creators can apply to campaigns; creates dual-sided network effects Cheerful may not have
4. **Free tier with real functionality** — discovery without payment lowers acquisition friction (relevant for Cheerful's go-to-market)

### Key Takeaway
Heepsy's 4.5/5 ratings despite clear limitations reveal that **ease of use and low price** drive SMB adoption more than feature depth. Cheerful must ensure its UX is similarly frictionless for SMB users, even as it out-features Heepsy on every dimension. The biggest competitive moat Cheerful can build against Heepsy: **API access + deep integrations + workflow automation** that lock teams in at the ops layer — where Heepsy has zero presence.
