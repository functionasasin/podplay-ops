## Feature Inventory (Mapped to Wave 1 Categories)

### Discovery & Search
- **Influencer identification by mention tracking**: finds creators already discussing your brand/competitors/industry topics — organic advocates, not cold prospecting
- **Influence Score** (1–10): composite of follower reach, engagement rate, posting frequency, topic authority
- **Influencer tab**: ranks all mention authors by Influence Score; filterable by score threshold + geolocation + language
- **Top Public Profiles**: highest-reach profiles in any monitored topic
- **Lab24 Influencer Analysis**: in-depth per-creator analytics module
- **Competitor creator intelligence**: track which creators mention competitor brands → recruiting pipeline
- **NO pre-indexed creator database**: cannot search for creators not yet mentioning your topic

### Social Listening (Core Product — no direct Cheerful equivalent)
- **25M+ source coverage**: Instagram, TikTok, X/Twitter, YouTube, Facebook, LinkedIn, Reddit, Quora, Twitch, Telegram, news sites, blogs, forums, podcasts, newsletters, review sites
- **Real-time alerts** (< 60 seconds from post to notification)
- **Storm alerts**: early warning on mention volume spikes (crisis detection)
- **Anomaly Detector**: AI flags unusual patterns automatically
- **Hashtag + keyword + brand tracking** (unlimited keywords at higher tiers)

### Analytics & Reporting
- **Presence Score**: proprietary brand awareness composite metric
- **Reputation Score**: sentiment-weighted brand health score
- **Share of Voice**: brand's % of total category conversation vs. competitors
- **Sentiment analysis**: 95% accuracy, 90+ languages
- **Emotion analysis**: joy, anger, fear, sadness, admiration, disgust (goes beyond positive/negative)
- **AI Insights**: trending topics, volume anomalies, SOV shifts — proactive highlight cards
- **AI Brand Assistant**: conversational chatbot — ask "What were the top complaints this week?" or "How did our SOV change vs. competitors?"
- **Custom + automated reports**: PDF/export; scheduled delivery; branding customization (limited)
- **Topic Analysis, Metrics Analysis, Intents Analysis**

### Campaign Management
- **None** — Brand24 has no campaign brief, creator workflow, or deliverable tracking

### Outreach & CRM
- **None** — Brand24 explicitly redirects outreach to third-party tools (e.g., Waalaxy)
- Cannot send email, DMs, or any outreach from within Brand24
- No creator CRM, pipeline, contact notes, or communication history

### Content Collaboration
- **None** — No content submission, review, approval, or UGC asset library

### Payments & Contracts
- **None** — No payment disbursement, contract generation, e-signature, or tax compliance

### Team Collaboration
- **Multi-user on Team+ plans** (no per-seat charges at Pro/Enterprise)
- Shared dashboards and project monitoring
- **Slack + MS Teams integration** — drops mentions into team channels in real time
- **No role-based permissions** beyond plan-level access

### Integrations & Ecosystem
- **Slack** — real-time mention alerts in channels
- **MS Teams** — same as Slack
- **Google Sheets** — export mention data + reports
- **Zapier** — trigger automations (e.g., create CRM record on high-influence mention)
- **Semrush connector** — blend social listening data with SEO keyword data (unique in market)
- **API access** — export Brand24 data to custom integrations
- **Email Digest** — scheduled mention summaries to inbox
- **LinkedIn integration** — requires OAuth connection for private profile tracking

### AI & Automation
- **AI Brand Assistant** — conversational analytics chatbot (GPT-powered)
- **Anomaly Detector** — automatic spike/dip detection
- **AI Smart Filtering** — sort by importance score, sentiment, domain authority, influence
- **AI Insights cards** — proactive summaries pushed without user query
- **Emotion analysis engine** — contextual model trained on sarcasm/idioms

### Marketplace / Network
- **None** — No creator marketplace, opt-in database, or ambassador program infrastructure

## Unique Differentiators

1. **Organic Advocate Discovery (listening-first)**: Finds creators already organically talking about your brand/category — highest-quality signal for authentic partnership (echoes Brandwatch's social listening → creator discovery model, but at 1/50th the price)

2. **Influence Score at SMB price point**: Composite creator authority metric ($199/mo) vs. paying $25K+/yr for CreatorIQ's equivalent

3. **Emotion analysis beyond sentiment**: joy/anger/fear/sadness/admiration/disgust — more nuanced than positive/negative/neutral competitors

4. **Semrush SEO connector**: Unique cross-channel intelligence — blend social SOV with SEO keyword volume to see where brand conversation and search intent intersect

5. **AI Brand Assistant (conversational analytics)**: Natural language queries on your own mention data — "What did people say about our product launch last week?" — competitors charge enterprise pricing for this

6. **Adobe-trajectory**: Post-Semrush acquisition by Adobe (Nov 2025), Brand24 may eventually connect to Adobe Analytics, Experience Cloud, and Marketo — which would make it a much more serious integration threat

7. **No annual contract required**: Monthly billing with transparent pricing; no lock-in at lower tiers (competitive advantage over CreatorIQ/Traackr/GRIN annual-only contracts)

---

## Weaknesses

1. **NOT an outreach or execution platform**: Brand24's influencer workflow ends at identification — zero built-in outreach, CRM, campaign management, payments, or contracts (redirects users to Waalaxy and similar tools). This is a fundamental capability gap vs. purpose-built influencer marketing platforms.

2. **Reactive, not proactive creator discovery**: Can only find creators already discussing your topic/brand — cannot proactively discover creators by niche, follower count, or audience demographics like Modash/HypeAuditor's pre-indexed databases

3. **No creator database depth**: ~100,000 brands tracked but no standalone creator profile count — creators appear only when they mention tracked keywords; Modash has 250M+ pre-indexed profiles

4. **Keyword limits are restrictive on lower tiers**: Individual plan (3 keywords) severely limits what you can monitor for the price; Team (7 keywords) forces upgrades for multi-brand or multi-category monitoring

5. **Historical data gated by plan**: Lower tiers limit how far back you can analyze trends — competitive intelligence diminished for new subscribers

6. **No outreach automation**: Explicitly redirects to Waalaxy; alert overload without automated triage means users spend time managing noise rather than acting

7. **Sentiment accuracy varies by language**: Non-English nuance + sarcasm + industry jargon requires manual correction — limits global use cases despite "90+ language" claim

8. **Filter noise**: Mentions of common brand names (e.g., "brand" in generic text) require significant manual filtering to exclude irrelevant results — limits accuracy on generic product categories

9. **No Creator-facing features**: Creators cannot be invited to register, complete briefs, submit content, or receive payments — no two-sided platform capability

## Workflow Integration Depth

Brand24 sits at the **top of the influencer marketing funnel only** — brand intelligence and creator identification:

```
[Brand24 workflow]
Monitor keywords → Receive mention alerts → Review Influence Score tab →
Export creator list → (EXIT TO THIRD-PARTY) → Outreach → Campaign → Payment
```

Brand24 **exits the workflow immediately after identification** — brands must manually export creator lists and pivot to a separate tool for every execution step. This is a significant daily friction point that Cheerful can directly exploit.

## What Cheerful Can Learn

### 1. Brand Mention → Warm Creator Targeting (HIGH PRIORITY)
Brand24's core discovery signal — "creators already talking about your brand" — is the highest-quality influencer recruitment signal because these creators have demonstrated organic brand affinity. Cheerful's `PostTrackingWorkflow` already tracks posts by managed creators, but it has **no brand mention detection** for unmanaged creators. An "Always-On Brand Mention Tracker" that surfaces new creators mentioning your brand would plug directly into Cheerful's outreach engine — turning a passive signal into an active recruitment trigger.

**Cross-reference**: Wave 1 `discovery-search.md` identified brand-mention discovery as a top gap; `archive.md` competitor also validated zero-signup mention tracking as a hero feature. This is now confirmed by a third data point (Brand24).

### 2. Influence Score Methodology
Brand24's 1–10 Influence Score composite (reach + engagement + topic authority + posting frequency) is cited as users' most-loved feature. Cheerful already collects `avg_engagement_rate`, `follower_count`, and `paid_promotion_rate` per creator — synthesizing these into a single **Cheerful Creator Score** would directly compete with Brand24's most-loved feature at the SMB price point.

### 3. Share of Voice Dashboard
Brand24's SOV + Presence Score + Reputation Score are campaign outcome metrics that brands use to justify influencer spend. Cheerful currently tracks posts and metrics per campaign but has no **brand SOV change measurement** — i.e., "did our influencer campaign increase the % of social conversation that mentions our brand?" This would require the brand to either authorize Brand24 API pull or Cheerful to provide social listening capabilities.

### 4. Emotion Analysis Layer on Creator Content
Brand24's emotion analysis (beyond sentiment) on post text and comments is validated by reviewers as uniquely insightful. Adding emotion analysis to Cheerful's creator post content and comment processing (using Claude) would differentiate Cheerful's analytics from basic positive/negative sentiment.

### 5. AI Conversational Analytics (Brand Assistant pattern)
Brand24's AI Brand Assistant lets users query their own data in natural language. Cheerful's MCP tools are internal-only (Slack bot). A user-facing "Ask Cheerful" conversational interface for campaign data queries would replicate this pattern directly in the execution layer — and Cheerful's data would be richer (campaign performance, per-creator ROI, email thread history).

## Top 3 Hero Feature Candidates

### Hero Feature A: Always-On Brand Mention Monitor → Warm Creator Pipeline
**Problem**: Brands using Brand24 identify organic brand advocates but have no path to outreach them from within the tool. Brands using Cheerful have no way to discover creators already talking about them.

**Cheerful implementation**:
- Background monitoring job (Temporal workflow) that tracks brand mentions across Instagram + TikTok + YouTube
- When an unmanaged creator with Influence Score > threshold mentions the brand → automatically adds to "Warm Prospects" queue with mention context
- One-click to initiate outreach campaign with mention-aware personalization ("I noticed you posted about [product] last week...")
- **Builds on**: `PostTrackingWorkflow` (existing), Apify scraping (existing), email outreach stack (existing)
- **Stickiness**: Creates a continuously-growing warm prospect pipeline that brands cannot recreate elsewhere without Brand24 + manual effort

**Competitive landscape**: Brandwatch, Archive, and Brand24 all detect brand mentions but cannot convert to outreach. Cheerful would be the only platform that closes the loop.

### Hero Feature B: Cheerful Creator Score (branded authority metric)
**Problem**: Brands use 3-5 different metrics to evaluate creator quality; no single number exists in Cheerful.

**Cheerful implementation**:
- Composite score (1–100): follower count + engagement rate + posting consistency + paid promotion history + campaign performance history (Cheerful-exclusive)
- Score displayed on every creator profile; sortable in campaign creator lists
- Over time, Cheerful's score includes campaign performance data competitors cannot replicate (data accumulation stickiness)
- **Builds on**: existing `creator` table fields (`avg_engagement_rate`, `follower_count`, `avg_views`), `campaign_creator` performance data

### Hero Feature C: Post-Campaign Brand SOV Shift Report
**Problem**: Brands cannot quantify how much their influencer spend moved brand conversation share.

**Cheerful implementation**:
- Pre-campaign: capture baseline mention volume for brand keywords via Brand24 API (or Apify)
- Post-campaign: re-capture and calculate SOV delta
- Report shows: before/after SOV %, estimated incremental reach, per-creator contribution to SOV lift
- Positions Cheerful as the analytics layer that justifies influencer budget to CMOs
- **Build complexity**: requires social listening data integration (Brand24 API, or internal keyword tracking — significant build but very high stickiness)
