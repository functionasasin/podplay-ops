## Company Overview

- **Founded:** 2015 (originally as Instagram social listening/analytics tool; evolved to full platform by 2017)
- **Acquired:** May 2025 by Publicis Groupe for ~$150M (per Wall Street Journal)
- **Structure post-acquisition:** Integrated with Influential (acquired by Publicis for $500M in 2024) + Epsilon CoreID identity layer; operates within Publicis Connected Media
- **Team:** CEO Krishna Subramanian remains in charge
- **Scale:** 15M+ verified creators; 95% of influencers with 5,000+ followers; 120 countries; 2.5 billion social posts analyzed annually
- **Clients:** Honda, Walmart, Kraft Heinz, Fox, Dr. Pepper, Nissan, Miller Lite; 15,000+ campaigns powered
- **Market position:** G2 #1 Enterprise Influencer Platform — 9 consecutive quarters; Forrester TEI validated 374% ROI for clients

## Unique Differentiators

1. **Snapchat API Official Partnership** — only enterprise platform with native Snap Star discovery + measurement; 397M DAU / 750M MAU; reaches 90%+ of 13–24 year olds. No other platform at this tier has this.

2. **DEI Search Filters** — the only platform in the market with ethnicity and sexual orientation creator filters. Recurring G2 mention for DEI campaign use cases (though not 100% accurate due to data availability).

3. **Brand & Media Safety at 300M+ sources** — extends beyond social to the entire open web. IAB Content Taxonomy risk scoring per creator. Launched June 2025 — most recent brand safety feature in the market.

4. **Social Listening as Foundation** — unlike competitors that bolted social listening on later, Captiv8 built upward from social intelligence. Results in deeper engagement timing data (hour-by-hour 24h breakdown) than most discovery-first platforms.

5. **Perplexity AI Wrap Reports** — automated post-campaign narrative summaries in minutes (not days). Direct competitive pressure on the agency-hours model.

6. **Publicis/Influential/Epsilon Integration** — post-acquisition, access to 3.5M Influential network, Epsilon CoreID for cross-channel identity resolution. No pure-play SaaS competitor can match this media infrastructure.

---

## Weaknesses

1. **Creator onboarding friction** — most-cited product complaint: "it's really difficult to get creators not currently on the platform" (G2). Multi-stage process vs. smoother alternatives.
2. **Duplicate data** — G2 mentions of duplicate creator profiles.
3. **High price + rigid contracts** — $25K+/year, annual commitment, $3K onboarding fee. No modular pricing. "More than we needed" for mid-market / SMB teams.
4. **Customer support** — "slow or inconsistent" repeatedly mentioned (G2, Capterra). Email-only for most issues.
5. **Learning curve** — new team members require additional training; enterprise-grade complexity.
6. **No Gmail-native outreach** — like all non-Cheerful competitors, Captiv8 sends outreach from platform servers, NOT the brand's own Gmail. This is a fundamental reply-rate disadvantage vs. Cheerful's architecture.
7. **Acquisition integration risk** — newly part of Publicis with Influential; platform direction may shift toward agency-first use cases rather than brand-direct SaaS.

## What Cheerful Can Learn

### 1. Snapchat Discovery Integration
Captiv8's Snapchat API partnership unlocks a 750M MAU platform with 90% reach of 13–24 year olds — an entirely uncontested channel for Cheerful's current Instagram/YouTube focus. Cheerful's Apify scraping architecture could be extended to Snapchat discovery without full API partnership initially.

### 2. Brand & Media Safety Scoring
The IAB Content Taxonomy risk scoring per creator (launched June 2025) is a clear market signal: enterprise brands need safety scores before outreach. Cheerful lacks any brand safety layer. Scoring creators on brand fit risk (not just engagement) before a campaign even starts would be a meaningful differentiator — especially given Cheerful's LLM capabilities can analyze creator content at scale.
- **Cheerful implementation path:** Analyze scraped creator content (bio, recent posts, hashtags) against configurable risk categories using Claude. Store as `creator_brand_safety_score` field in `creator` table.

### 3. AI-Powered Wrap Reports (Perplexity-style)
Captiv8's automated post-campaign narrative summaries in minutes is a direct attack on agency time. Cheerful already exports metrics to Google Sheets; an LLM-generated wrap report that compiles campaign summary + creator performance + key insights would reduce agency labor and become a shareworthy deliverable.
- **Cheerful implementation path:** New `campaign_wrap_report` Temporal workflow triggered on campaign end; pulls all metrics, creator posts, opt-in data, payment totals; generates narrative PDF/HTML via Claude; delivers to client's email.

### 4. Lookalike Creator Engine
Captiv8's AI lookalike scaling is a proven discovery accelerator. Notably, **Cheerful already has a `campaign_lookalike_suggestion` table in the schema** (noted in prior impact.com analysis) — this is architecturally anticipated. The Captiv8 model confirms: lookalike is table stakes for enterprise.

### 5. Public Shareable Report Links (No Login Required)
Simple but high-impact: Captiv8 lets you share campaign dashboards via a URL with no login. Cheerful has no client-facing reporting layer. This drives agency-client trust, reduces reporting calls, and becomes a daily touch point that Cheerful controls.

### 6. Social Commerce Revenue Loop (TikTok Shop + Shopify)
Captiv8 closes the full commerce loop: creator content → affiliate click → conversion → commission tracking. Cheerful's Shopify integration is shallow (GoAffPro gifting only — no revenue attribution). The Captiv8 model shows this is a major enterprise retention mechanism.

## Hero Feature Candidates for Cheerful

### HF-A: Creator Intelligence Pre-Brief (Captiv8-Inspired)
**Signal:** Captiv8's 500+ filter discovery + brand safety scoring + DEI filters
**Gap vs. Cheerful:** Cheerful has basic Instagram/YouTube search with ~10 filters and no safety layer
**Hero Feature:** Pre-brief creator intelligence card — before adding a creator to a campaign, auto-generate a creator assessment: brand fit score, content risk flags, audience demographics, engagement quality, suggested rate range
**Stickiness rationale:** Embedded in the campaign creation daily workflow; accumulates judgment data per brand; becomes the pre-flight checklist no team will skip

### HF-B: Automated Campaign Wrap Report
**Signal:** Captiv8's Perplexity-powered automated wrap reports (minutes, not days)
**Gap vs. Cheerful:** Cheerful exports raw metrics to Sheets; no narrative synthesis
**Hero Feature:** One-click post-campaign wrap report — LLM compiles all metrics, creator posts, key wins, and brand recommendations into a polished PDF/HTML deliverable; shareable link for clients
**Stickiness rationale:** Becomes the artifact that justifies budget; agencies start building client reviews around it; switching means losing the report history + format

### HF-C: Social Commerce Attribution Pipeline
**Signal:** Captiv8's TikTok Shop + Shopify + Refersion closed-loop
**Gap vs. Cheerful:** GoAffPro gifting only; no revenue-per-creator attribution
**Hero Feature:** Creator Revenue Dashboard — UTM + promo code + Shopify webhook → per-creator revenue card visible in campaign view
**Stickiness rationale:** Revenue data accumulates and is irreplaceable; connects payment decisions to proven ROI; brands will never want to start over with attribution history in another platform
