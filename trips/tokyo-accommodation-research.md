## 1. Hotel Villa Fontaine Grand Haneda (Jan 21-22)
**Location:** Haneda Airport

### Work Spots & Cafes

| Spot | Description | Why Visit |
|------|-------------|-----------|
| **Wakuraba Cafe** | Hidden gem in Terminal 2, uncrowded oasis with ~30 seats and power outlets | Quiet work spot, great for late-night arrival recovery |
| **Terminal.0 HANEDA** | Experimental shared workspace with meeting rooms, tatami areas, mini kitchen, sauna, and nap room | Cutting-edge co-working concept by TANSEISHA |
| **Private Work Cubicles** | Official Haneda work pods in terminals | Quick focused work sessions |

### Design & Aesthetic

- Terminal 2 features a gently curving stainless steel roof with clerestories and skylights
- Voted world's cleanest airport for 7 consecutive years
- Observation decks with sweeping runway and Tokyo Bay views

### Novel Experiences

- **Edo Koji (Terminal 3, 4F):** Recreated Edo-period streetscape with restaurants
- Plane-watching cafes with runway views
- Haneda Airport Garden complex (new retail/dining expansion)

## 2. Sequence Miyashita Park Shibuya (Jan 22-24)
**Location:** Shibuya, Tokyo (2 nights)

### Work-Friendly Cafes

| Spot | Description | Why Visit |
|------|-------------|-----------|
| **Valley Park Stand** | Top-floor cafe connecting hotel and park, full-height windows, wooden tables with power plugs | Best remote work cafe in Shibuya, open late into night |
| **Starbucks (Miyashita Park)** | Same floor as park, open atmosphere | Reliable backup option |
| **Cafe Kitsune** | Inside Maison Kitsune boutique (North Building 2F) | French-Japanese aesthetic, fashion-forward |

### Design Stores & Galleries (Cat Street Area)

| Spot | Description | Distance |
|------|-------------|----------|
| **Design Festa Gallery** | 2 exhibition halls showcasing young independent artists, urban village-like layout | ~15 min walk via Cat Street |
| **Omotesando Hills** | 100+ stores with sleek Tadao Ando architecture | ~10 min walk |
| **Cat Street (Ura-Harajuku)** | 1km of vintage shops, galleries, brand flagships | Connects Shibuya to Harajuku |

### Novel Experiences

- **Miyashita Park Rooftop:** Aerial park with greenery above the shopping complex
- **Rayard Miyashita Park:** 90 unique stores including record shops, craft beer bars, matcha cafes
- Walk to Shibuya Crossing, Yoyogi Park, Harajuku Takeshita Street

## 3. Resort Inn Murata - Togari Onsen (Jan 24 - Feb 1)
**Location:** Iiyama, Nagano (8 nights snowboarding base)

### Togari Onsen Area

#### Cafes & Work Spots

| Spot | Description |
|------|-------------|
| **Cafe 18** | Base of ski resort - coffee, focaccia, Cornish pasties, apple pies |
| **Sky Top Cafe** | NEW (2025) - Mountaintop cafe with lattes and apple pie, scenic views |
| **Mitsuwaya Cafe** | Near train station, family-run 40+ years, hearty set meals |
| **Ambis** | Cafe/bar at ski area entrance |

#### Restaurants

| Spot | Specialty |
|------|-----------|
| **Ishidaya** | Best soba - grow their own buckwheat, hand-make "Bokuchi Soba" unique to Iiyama |
| **Yakiniku Dragon** | NEW (Dec 2025) - By famous Tokyo Yakiniku Jumbo, Wagyu lunch specials |
| **Chuka Soba Tsukemen Hajime** | NEW (Dec 2025) - Popular Harajuku ramen shop |
| **Hashiba** | Retro izakaya atmosphere, yakitori, huge menu |
| **Lamp** | Base area, local "Miyuki" pork katsu curry |

#### Novel Experiences

- **Noroshi Nabe:** Local hot pot with miso, vegetables, Miyuki pork (named after Sengoku-era signal fires)
- **Kadoguchi Sake Brewery:** Hokko Masamune sake (best for atsukan/hot sake)
- **Tanakaya Sake Brewery:** Famous Mizuo sake (best chilled)

### Nozawa Onsen (~30 min from Togari)

#### Design-Forward Cafes

| Spot | Description | Why Visit |
|------|-------------|-----------|
| **Shichirohei Coffee Shop** | French-Japanese fusion, wooden floors/tables, great views | Hidden gem, elegant presentations |
| **The Craft Room** | Original craft beer taproom, first All Press espresso in town, near gondola | Open 8am, Breakfast Bagel popular |
| **Tanuki Cafe & Dining** | Boutique coffee, local ingredients, cozy atmosphere | Heart of Nozawa |
| **78 Cafe (Nanahachi)** | Laid-back, quirky antique Japanese toys, homemade cakes | Taco rice specialty |
| **Shizen Cafe** | Vegan, drip coffee only, vegan pancakes | Unique plant-based option |

#### Design-Forward Restaurants

| Spot | Description |
|------|-------------|
| **Trattoria Bivacco** | Chef from Tokyo Michelin Star, homemade pasta, organic Italian wines |
| **Kongo Food Hall** | Converted steel factory, 6 vendors, seats 100 | Industrial aesthetic |
| **IVY Tapas Wine & Cheese** | Quality wine selection, nostalgic charm |

#### Unique Experiences

| Activity | Description |
|----------|-------------|
| **Onsen-Meguri (Onsen Hopping)** | 13 free public baths maintained since Edo period. Try Kumanoteara (where the wounded bear was found) |
| **Ogama Hot Spring** | Watch locals cook vegetables and eggs in 100° natural spring water |
| **AJB Craft Brewery (Libushi)** | British-Japanese brewery, soba stout, Nozawa Onsen beer |
| **Tanakaya/Kadoguchi Sake** | Two historic sake breweries, tasting and purchases |
| **Akebi Zaiku Shop** | 1883 kura storehouse filled with handmade goods |
| **Tanukiya** | Modern goods from Nagano region |
| **Oyaki Cooking Class** | Learn to make traditional steamed buns with local family |
| **Nozawa Stamp Trail** | 27 stamps around village, collect 10+ for a free towel |
| **Sunday Morning Market** | Local vegetables, pickles, crafts (May-Dec) |

#### Day Trip: Obuse Town
Historic town with winding streets, chestnut dishes, sake breweries, and Hokusai's "Phoenix" ceiling painting at Ganshoin Temple.

## 4. one@ Tokyo Insomnia (Feb 1-2)
**Location:** Oshiage, near Tokyo Skytree (1 night)

### Hotel Design

Designed by **Kengo Kuma** - industrial-chic with arty wooden facade, acid-washed metal, and reclaimed wood interiors. Concept: "Installation for Time Travel towards Historic Tokyo"

### Work Spots (In-Hotel)

| Spot | Description |
|------|-------------|
| **@lobby** | 15m-long reception counter, white tones with wooden furniture, large floor-to-ceiling windows |
| **24-Hour Lounge** | Free drinks, bread, snacks anytime (insomnia brand specialty) |
| **@rooftop Terrace** | 180° Skytree views, white sofas, bar space |

### Tokyo Solamachi (300+ shops at Skytree base)

#### Design & Craft Shops

| Shop | Specialty |
|------|-----------|
| **Kamism Lab** | Traditional washi paper with modern designs for interiors (2 min from Oshiage B2 exit) |
| **Varco** | Premium natural wood and leather accessories |
| **Ameshin** | Exquisite candy craftsmanship |
| **Kiwa Seisakujo** | Handmade accessory parts and tools, craft your own |
| **Neue** | Select stationery from Kobe, Japanese and international |
| **Nippon Ichi** | Traditional crafts and local specialties from across Japan |
| **Edo Kiriko shops** | Traditional cut glass craftsmanship |

### Novel Experiences

| Activity | Description |
|----------|-------------|

| **Traditional Craftsman Workshops** | Area known for washi paper, folding screens, chopsticks since Edo period |
| **Skytree Observation** | Tallest tower in Japan |

| **Station Cafe** | Nice interior, good coffee/cakes, friendly staff |

| Spot | Description |
