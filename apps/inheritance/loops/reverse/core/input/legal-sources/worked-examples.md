## Example 1: Testate — Simple Will with 2 Legitimate Children (Legitime Respected)

**Facts**:
- Decedent (D) dies testate.
- Net distributable estate: ₱10,000,000
- Surviving heirs: 2 legitimate children (LC1, LC2)
- No surviving spouse.
- Will provides: "I leave ₱2,000,000 to my friend F from the free portion. The rest to my children equally."

**Heir Classification**:
- LC1: compulsory heir (legitimate child) — Art. 887(1)
- LC2: compulsory heir (legitimate child) — Art. 887(1)
- F: voluntary heir (stranger) — instituted in will

**Step 1 — Compute Legitime** (Art. 888):
- Legitimate children's collective legitime = ½ of estate = ₱5,000,000
- Per child: ₱5,000,000 ÷ 2 = ₱2,500,000 each

**Step 2 — Compute Free Portion**:
- Free portion = ₱10,000,000 - ₱5,000,000 = ₱5,000,000

**Step 3 — Validate Will**:
- Will gives F ₱2,000,000 from free portion — this does NOT impair any legitime.
- Remaining free portion: ₱5,000,000 - ₱2,000,000 = ₱3,000,000
- Will says "the rest to my children equally" → ₱3,000,000 ÷ 2 = ₱1,500,000 each from free portion.

**Step 4 — Final Distribution**:

| Heir | Legitime | Free Portion | Total | Basis |
|------|----------|-------------|-------|-------|
| LC1 | ₱2,500,000 | ₱1,500,000 | ₱4,000,000 | Art. 888, will disposition |
| LC2 | ₱2,500,000 | ₱1,500,000 | ₱4,000,000 | Art. 888, will disposition |
| F | — | ₱2,000,000 | ₱2,000,000 | Will disposition (legacy) |
| **Total** | **₱5,000,000** | **₱5,000,000** | **₱10,000,000** | |

**Sample Narrative (LC1)**:
> **LC1 (legitimate child)** receives **₱4,000,000**.
> As a legitimate child (Art. 887), LC1 is a compulsory heir entitled to a legitime. Under Art. 888 of the Civil Code, the legitime of legitimate children is one-half (½) of the estate, shared equally. With 2 legitimate children, LC1's legitime is ₱2,500,000. Additionally, the testator's will directs that the remaining free portion (after the ₱2,000,000 legacy to F) be divided equally among the children, giving LC1 an additional ₱1,500,000 from the free portion.

## Example 2: Testate — Inofficious Will (Free Portion Exceeded)

**Facts**:
- Decedent (D) dies testate.
- Net distributable estate: ₱10,000,000
- Surviving heirs: 1 legitimate child (LC1), surviving spouse (S)
- Will provides: "I leave ₱6,000,000 to my friend F. The rest to my child LC1."
- Will makes NO provision for surviving spouse.

**Heir Classification**:
- LC1: compulsory heir (legitimate child) — Art. 887(1)
- S: compulsory heir (surviving spouse) — Art. 887(3)
- F: voluntary heir (stranger) — instituted in will

**Step 1 — Compute Legitime** (Arts. 888, 892):
- LC1's legitime = ½ of estate = ₱5,000,000 (Art. 888; only 1 legitimate child)
- S's legitime = ¼ of estate = ₱2,500,000 (Art. 892 ¶1; 1 legitimate child + spouse)

**Step 2 — Compute Free Portion**:
- Total legitime = ₱5,000,000 + ₱2,500,000 = ₱7,500,000
- Free portion = ₱10,000,000 - ₱7,500,000 = ₱2,500,000

**Step 3 — Validate Will**:
- Will gives F ₱6,000,000 but the free portion is only ₱2,500,000.
- The disposition to F is **inofficious** by ₱3,500,000 (Art. 911).
- Reduce F's legacy to ₱2,500,000 (the maximum free portion).
- Note: Spouse was not mentioned in the will but this is NOT preterition (Art. 854 applies only to heirs in the direct line). Spouse still gets her compulsory legitime.

**Step 4 — Final Distribution**:

| Heir | Legitime | Free Portion | Total | Basis |
|------|----------|-------------|-------|-------|
| LC1 | ₱5,000,000 | — | ₱5,000,000 | Art. 888 |
| S | ₱2,500,000 | — | ₱2,500,000 | Art. 892 |
| F | — | ₱2,500,000 | ₱2,500,000 | Will (reduced per Art. 911) |
| **Total** | **₱7,500,000** | **₱2,500,000** | **₱10,000,000** | |

**Sample Narrative (F)**:
> **F (friend, voluntary heir)** receives **₱2,500,000**.
> The testator's will provided a legacy of ₱6,000,000 to F. However, under Art. 911 of the Civil Code, testamentary dispositions that impair the legitime of compulsory heirs must be reduced. The total legitime of the compulsory heirs (LC1 + S) is ₱7,500,000, leaving a free portion of only ₱2,500,000. F's legacy is therefore reduced from ₱6,000,000 to ₱2,500,000 to respect the compulsory heirs' legitime.

## Example 3: Testate — Preterition (Omission of Compulsory Heir in Direct Line)

**Facts**:
- Decedent (D) dies testate.
- Net distributable estate: ₱12,000,000
- Surviving heirs: 3 legitimate children (LC1, LC2, LC3), surviving spouse (S)
- Will provides: "I institute LC1 and LC2 as my sole heirs, in equal shares." (LC3 is completely omitted — neither instituted nor disinherited.)

**Heir Classification**:
- LC1, LC2, LC3: compulsory heirs (legitimate children) — Art. 887(1)
- S: compulsory heir (surviving spouse) — Art. 887(3)

**Step 1 — Check for Preterition** (Art. 854):
- LC3 is a compulsory heir in the direct line.
- LC3 was totally omitted from the will (not instituted, not disinherited, no legacy/devise).
- **Preterition exists** → the institution of heirs is ANNULLED.
- Devises and legacies remain valid if not inofficious (but this will has none beyond the institution).

**Step 2 — Effect of Annulment**:
- Since the institution is annulled, the estate passes as if D died **intestate** (Art. 854).
- No legacies/devises to preserve → entire estate distributes per intestate rules.

**Step 3 — Intestate Distribution** (Art. 996):
- Rule: Spouse gets same share as each child.
- Total shares: 4 (3 children + 1 spouse)
- Each share: ₱12,000,000 ÷ 4 = ₱3,000,000

**Step 4 — Final Distribution**:

| Heir | Share | Amount | Basis |
|------|-------|--------|-------|
| LC1 | ¼ | ₱3,000,000 | Art. 854 (annulment), Art. 996 |
| LC2 | ¼ | ₱3,000,000 | Art. 854 (annulment), Art. 996 |
| LC3 | ¼ | ₱3,000,000 | Art. 854 (annulment), Art. 996 |
| S | ¼ | ₱3,000,000 | Art. 854 (annulment), Art. 996 |
| **Total** | | **₱12,000,000** | |

**Sample Narrative (LC3)**:
> **LC3 (legitimate child)** receives **₱3,000,000**.
> LC3, a compulsory heir in the direct line, was completely omitted from the testator's will. Under Art. 854 of the Civil Code, the preterition (total omission) of a compulsory heir in the direct line annuls the institution of heirs in the will. Since no separate legacies or devises exist, the entire estate distributes under intestate succession rules. Under Art. 996, the surviving spouse receives a share equal to that of each legitimate child, resulting in equal shares of ₱3,000,000 each.

## Example 4: Testate — Valid Disinheritance (Descendants Take Disinherited Heir's Place)

**Facts**:
- Decedent (D) dies testate.
- Net distributable estate: ₱12,000,000
- D had 3 legitimate children: LC1, LC2, LC3.
- LC2 was validly disinherited in the will for maltreatment (Art. 919(6)).
- LC2 has 2 children (GC1, GC2) who survive D.
- Surviving spouse (S) also survives.
- Will provides: "I disinherit LC2. I leave the free portion to charity C."

**Heir Classification**:
- LC1: compulsory heir (legitimate child) — Art. 887(1)
- LC2: disinherited (Art. 919(6)) — loses all inheritance rights
- LC3: compulsory heir (legitimate child) — Art. 887(1)
- GC1, GC2: compulsory heirs by representation (Art. 923) — take LC2's place
- S: compulsory heir (surviving spouse) — Art. 887(3)
- C: voluntary heir (charity) — instituted in will

**Step 1 — Effect of Disinheritance** (Art. 923):
- LC2 is excluded from the estate entirely.
- GC1 and GC2 **take LC2's place** and preserve the rights of compulsory heirs with respect to the legitime.
- LC2 has no usufruct or administration over what GC1/GC2 inherit.

**Step 2 — Compute Legitime**:
- For legitime computation, treat the estate as having 3 "lines" of legitimate children (LC1, [GC1+GC2 representing LC2], LC3).
- Legitimate children's collective legitime = ½ of estate = ₱6,000,000 (Art. 888)
- Per child-line: ₱6,000,000 ÷ 3 = ₱2,000,000
  - LC1: ₱2,000,000
  - GC1 + GC2 (representing LC2): ₱2,000,000 → ₱1,000,000 each (per stirpes)
  - LC3: ₱2,000,000
- S's legitime: equal to one child-line's share = ₱2,000,000 (Art. 892; 3 children + spouse → spouse = one child's share, charged to free portion)

**Step 3 — Compute Free Portion**:
- Total legitime = ₱6,000,000 (children) + ₱2,000,000 (spouse) = ₱8,000,000
- Free portion = ₱12,000,000 - ₱8,000,000 = ₱4,000,000
- Will directs free portion to charity C.

**Step 4 — Final Distribution**:

| Heir | Legitime | Free Portion | Total | Basis |
|------|----------|-------------|-------|-------|
| LC1 | ₱2,000,000 | — | ₱2,000,000 | Art. 888 |
| GC1 | ₱1,000,000 | — | ₱1,000,000 | Arts. 923, 970 (representation) |
| GC2 | ₱1,000,000 | — | ₱1,000,000 | Arts. 923, 970 (representation) |
| LC3 | ₱2,000,000 | — | ₱2,000,000 | Art. 888 |
| S | ₱2,000,000 | — | ₱2,000,000 | Art. 892 |
| C | — | ₱4,000,000 | ₱4,000,000 | Will disposition |
| **Total** | **₱8,000,000** | **₱4,000,000** | **₱12,000,000** | |

**Sample Narrative (GC1)**:
> **GC1 (grandchild, by representation)** receives **₱1,000,000**.
> GC1's parent LC2 was validly disinherited by the testator for maltreatment (Art. 919(6)). Under Art. 923 of the Civil Code, the children of a disinherited heir take the disinherited heir's place and preserve the rights of compulsory heirs with respect to the legitime. The legitimate children's collective legitime is ½ of the estate (₱6,000,000), divided into 3 lines of ₱2,000,000 each. GC1 and GC2 share LC2's line equally by right of representation (Art. 970), receiving ₱1,000,000 each.

## Example 5: Testate — Adopted Child Concurring with Biological Legitimate Children

**Facts**:
- Decedent (D) dies testate.
- Net distributable estate: ₱15,000,000
- D has 2 biological legitimate children (LC1, LC2) and 1 adopted child (AC1, adopted under RA 8552).
- Surviving spouse (S) also survives.
- Will provides: "I leave the free portion to my alma mater, University U."

**Heir Classification** (RA 8552, Sec. 17-18):
- LC1, LC2: compulsory heirs (legitimate children) — Art. 887(1)
- AC1: compulsory heir (legitimate child by legal fiction) — RA 8552 Sec. 17 ("The adoptee shall be considered the legitimate son/daughter of the adopter(s) for all intents and purposes")
- S: compulsory heir (surviving spouse) — Art. 887(3)
- University U: voluntary heir — instituted in will

**Step 1 — Key Rule**: Under RA 8552 Sec. 17, adopted children have the **same successional rights as legitimate children**. AC1 is treated identically to LC1 and LC2 for all computation purposes.

**Step 2 — Compute Legitime** (Art. 888, 892):
- 3 legitimate children (including AC1) + spouse
- Children's collective legitime = ½ of ₱15M = ₱7,500,000
- Per child: ₱7,500,000 ÷ 3 = ₱2,500,000
- Spouse's legitime = equal to one child's share = ₱2,500,000 (Art. 892 ¶2; charged to free portion)

**Step 3 — Compute Free Portion**:
- Total legitime = ₱7,500,000 + ₱2,500,000 = ₱10,000,000
- Free portion = ₱15,000,000 - ₱10,000,000 = ₱5,000,000
- Will directs free portion to University U.

**Step 4 — Final Distribution**:

| Heir | Legitime | Free Portion | Total | Basis |
|------|----------|-------------|-------|-------|
| LC1 | ₱2,500,000 | — | ₱2,500,000 | Art. 888 |
| LC2 | ₱2,500,000 | — | ₱2,500,000 | Art. 888 |
| AC1 | ₱2,500,000 | — | ₱2,500,000 | Art. 888, RA 8552 Sec. 17 |
| S | ₱2,500,000 | — | ₱2,500,000 | Art. 892 |
| University U | — | ₱5,000,000 | ₱5,000,000 | Will disposition |
| **Total** | **₱10,000,000** | **₱5,000,000** | **₱15,000,000** | |

**Sample Narrative (AC1)**:
> **AC1 (adopted child)** receives **₱2,500,000**.
> Under RA 8552 (Domestic Adoption Act), Sec. 17, an adopted child is considered the legitimate child of the adopter for all intents and purposes, including succession. AC1 therefore has the same successional rights as the biological legitimate children LC1 and LC2. Under Art. 888, the collective legitime of the legitimate children is ½ of the estate (₱7,500,000), divided equally among 3 children. AC1's legitime is ₱2,500,000.

## Example 6: Testate — Mixed Succession (Partial Will, Remainder Intestate)

**Facts**:
- Decedent (D) dies testate.
- Net distributable estate: ₱10,000,000
- Surviving heirs: 2 legitimate children (LC1, LC2), surviving spouse (S)
- Will provides ONLY: "I bequeath ₱1,000,000 to my friend F as a legacy."
- Will does NOT institute any heirs and makes no other dispositions.

**Heir Classification**:
- LC1, LC2: compulsory heirs (legitimate children) — Art. 887(1)
- S: compulsory heir (surviving spouse) — Art. 887(3)
- F: legatee — named in will

**Step 1 — Determine Succession Type**:
- Will exists but only contains a legacy (₱1,000,000 to F), not an institution of heirs.
- This is **mixed succession**: the legacy is valid (testate), but the remainder passes intestate (Art. 960(2): "Legal or intestate succession takes place... when the will does not dispose of all the property belonging to the testator").

**Step 2 — Validate the Legacy**:
- Compute legitime first:
  - Children's collective legitime = ½ of ₱10M = ₱5,000,000 (Art. 888)
  - Spouse's legitime = equal to one child = ₱2,500,000 (Art. 892; 2 children + spouse)
  - Total legitime = ₱7,500,000
  - Free portion = ₱2,500,000
- Legacy to F = ₱1,000,000 ≤ free portion of ₱2,500,000 → **NOT inofficious**. Legacy stands.

**Step 3 — Distribute the Remainder Intestate**:
- Remainder after legacy: ₱10,000,000 - ₱1,000,000 = ₱9,000,000
- This ₱9,000,000 distributes per intestate rules (Art. 996): spouse = one child's share.
- Total shares: 3 (2 children + 1 spouse)
- Per share: ₱9,000,000 ÷ 3 = ₱3,000,000

**Step 4 — Final Distribution**:

| Heir | From Legacy | From Intestate | Total | Basis |
|------|-----------|---------------|-------|-------|
| LC1 | — | ₱3,000,000 | ₱3,000,000 | Art. 996 |
| LC2 | — | ₱3,000,000 | ₱3,000,000 | Art. 996 |
| S | — | ₱3,000,000 | ₱3,000,000 | Art. 996 |
| F | ₱1,000,000 | — | ₱1,000,000 | Will (legacy) |
| **Total** | **₱1,000,000** | **₱9,000,000** | **₱10,000,000** | |

**Verification**: Each compulsory heir receives at least their legitime:
- LC1: ₱3,000,000 ≥ ₱2,500,000 legitime ✓
- LC2: ₱3,000,000 ≥ ₱2,500,000 legitime ✓
- S: ₱3,000,000 ≥ ₱2,500,000 legitime ✓

**Sample Narrative (S)**:
> **S (surviving spouse)** receives **₱3,000,000**.
> The testator's will contained only a legacy of ₱1,000,000 to F and did not institute any heirs. Under Art. 960(2), when a will does not dispose of all property, the undisposed remainder passes by intestate succession. The ₱9,000,000 remainder is distributed under Art. 996, where the surviving spouse receives a share equal to one child's share. With 2 children and the spouse, there are 3 equal shares of ₱3,000,000 each.

## Example 7: Testate — Collation of Inter Vivos Donation

**Facts**:
- Decedent (D) dies testate.
- Net distributable estate at death: ₱8,000,000
- D has 3 legitimate children: LC1, LC2, LC3. No surviving spouse.
- During D's lifetime, D donated ₱2,000,000 to LC1 as an advance on inheritance (donation inter vivos subject to collation under Art. 1061).
- Will provides: "I leave ₱1,000,000 to charity C. The rest to my children equally."

**Heir Classification**:
- LC1, LC2, LC3: compulsory heirs (legitimate children) — Art. 887(1)
- C: voluntary heir (charity) — instituted in will

**Step 1 — Collation** (Art. 1061):
- The ₱2,000,000 donation to LC1 must be fictitiously added back to the estate for computing legitime.
- Collated estate = ₱8,000,000 + ₱2,000,000 = ₱10,000,000

**Step 2 — Compute Legitime on Collated Estate**:
- Children's collective legitime = ½ of ₱10,000,000 = ₱5,000,000 (Art. 888)
- Per child: ₱5,000,000 ÷ 3 = ₱1,666,667

**Step 3 — Charge Donation Against LC1's Legitime** (Art. 1064):
- LC1 already received ₱2,000,000 as donation.
- LC1's legitime = ₱1,666,667.
- Donation (₱2,000,000) exceeds LC1's legitime (₱1,666,667) by ₱333,333.
- The excess ₱333,333 is charged against the free portion (Art. 1064: if value of donation exceeds the legitime, the excess is charged to the free portion).

**Step 4 — Compute Free Portion**:
- Free portion from collated estate = ₱10,000,000 - ₱5,000,000 = ₱5,000,000
- Less excess donation charged to free portion: ₱5,000,000 - ₱333,333 = ₱4,666,667
- Less charity legacy: ₱4,666,667 - ₱1,000,000 = ₱3,666,667 remaining free portion

**Step 5 — Distribute**:
- Will says "rest to my children equally": ₱3,666,667 ÷ 3 = ₱1,222,222 each from remaining free portion
- But actual estate to distribute is ₱8,000,000 (the donation already left D's estate during lifetime)

**Step 6 — Actual Cash Distribution from Estate** (₱8,000,000):
- Charity C: ₱1,000,000
- Remaining: ₱7,000,000
- LC1 has already received ₱2,000,000 via donation. From the estate, LC1 receives nothing from legitime (already exceeded) and ₱1,222,222 from free portion.
  - Wait — let's recalculate more carefully:
  - LC1's total entitlement (legitime + free portion share) = ₱1,666,667 + ₱1,222,222 = ₱2,888,889
  - LC1 already received ₱2,000,000 → LC1 receives ₱888,889 from the estate
- LC2 total entitlement = ₱1,666,667 + ₱1,222,222 = ₱2,888,889 (all from estate)
- LC3 total entitlement = ₱1,666,667 + ₱1,222,222 = ₱2,888,889 (all from estate)

**Verification**: ₱888,889 + ₱2,888,889 + ₱2,888,889 + ₱1,000,000 = ₱7,666,667 — but we only have ₱8,000,000. Let's recheck:
- Total entitlements: LC1 = ₱2,888,889, LC2 = ₱2,888,889, LC3 = ₱2,888,889, C = ₱1,000,000 → total = ₱9,666,667
- Minus LC1's advance: ₱9,666,667 - ₱2,000,000 = ₱7,666,667 needed from estate
- But estate is ₱8,000,000. Difference = ₱333,333.
- This ₱333,333 is the excess of LC1's donation over legitime charged to free portion. The remaining free portion after all distributions = ₱333,333, which the will directed "to children equally" → ₱111,111 each.

**Corrected Final Distribution from Actual Estate (₱8,000,000)**:

| Heir | Already Received | From Estate | Total | Basis |
|------|-----------------|-------------|-------|-------|
| LC1 | ₱2,000,000 (donation) | ₱1,000,000 | ₱3,000,000 | Art. 888, 1061, 1064 |
| LC2 | — | ₱3,000,000 | ₱3,000,000 | Art. 888 |
| LC3 | — | ₱3,000,000 | ₱3,000,000 | Art. 888 |
| C | — | ₱1,000,000 | ₱1,000,000 | Will (legacy) |
| **Total** | **₱2,000,000** | **₱8,000,000** | **₱10,000,000** | |

> **Simplified approach**: Collated estate = ₱10M. After deducting charity (₱1M), remaining ₱9M to 3 children equally = ₱3M each. LC1 already received ₱2M, so gets ₱1M from estate. LC2 and LC3 each get ₱3M from estate. Total from estate: ₱1M + ₱3M + ₱3M + ₱1M = ₱8M ✓

**Sample Narrative (LC1)**:
> **LC1 (legitimate child)** receives **₱1,000,000 from the estate** (plus ₱2,000,000 previously received as a donation, for a total of ₱3,000,000).
> Under Art. 1061 of the Civil Code, the ₱2,000,000 donation inter vivos to LC1 must be collated (fictitiously added back) to the estate. The collated estate is ₱10,000,000. After the ₱1,000,000 charity legacy, ₱9,000,000 remains for the 3 children equally (₱3,000,000 each). Since LC1 already received ₱2,000,000 as an advance on inheritance, LC1 receives only ₱1,000,000 from the actual estate to complete LC1's ₱3,000,000 share.

## Example 8: Intestate — Complex: Legitimate + Illegitimate Children + Surviving Spouse + Representation

**Facts**:
- Decedent (D) dies intestate.
- Net distributable estate: ₱14,000,000
- D had 2 legitimate children: LC1 (alive), LC2 (predeceased D, leaving 2 children: GC1, GC2).
- D also had 1 illegitimate child: IC1 (alive).
- Surviving spouse (S) alive.

**Heir Classification**:
- LC1: legitimate child, inherits in own right — Art. 887(1)
- GC1, GC2: legitimate grandchildren, inherit by representation of LC2 — Arts. 970-972
- IC1: illegitimate child — Art. 887(5), Art. 176 Family Code
- S: surviving spouse — Art. 887(3)

**Step 1 — Determine Applicable Rule**:
- Intestate: legitimate children + illegitimate child + surviving spouse → Art. 999 / Art. 983 / Art. 895
- Representation applies: GC1 and GC2 step into LC2's shoes (Art. 970, 981)

**Step 2 — Apply Unit/Ratio Method** (Arts. 983, 895, 999):
- Each legitimate child line = 2 units
- Each illegitimate child = 1 unit (½ of legitimate per Art. 895)
- Surviving spouse = 2 units (same as one legitimate child per Art. 999)

| Heir/Line | Units |
|-----------|-------|
| LC1 | 2 |
| LC2's line (GC1+GC2) | 2 |
| IC1 | 1 |
| S | 2 |
| **Total** | **7** |

**Step 3 — Compute Per-Unit Value**:
- Per unit: ₱14,000,000 ÷ 7 = ₱2,000,000

**Step 4 — Final Distribution**:

| Heir | Units | Share | Amount | Basis |
|------|-------|-------|--------|-------|
| LC1 | 2 | 2/7 | ₱4,000,000 | Art. 980 |
| GC1 | 1 | 1/7 | ₱2,000,000 | Arts. 970, 981 (representation of LC2) |
| GC2 | 1 | 1/7 | ₱2,000,000 | Arts. 970, 981 (representation of LC2) |
