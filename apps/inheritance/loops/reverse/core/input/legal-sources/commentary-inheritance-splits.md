## Part 1: Compulsory Heir Classification

### Categories (Art. 887, Civil Code)

| Category | Type | Excluded By | Concurs With |
|----------|------|-------------|--------------|
| Legitimate children/descendants | Primary | None (highest priority) | Spouse, illegitimate children |
| Legitimate parents/ascendants | Secondary | Legitimate children/descendants | Spouse, illegitimate children |
| Surviving spouse | Concurring | Never excluded (always inherits if surviving) | All other categories |
| Illegitimate children | Concurring | Never excluded by categories 1-2 | Legitimate children, parents, spouse |

### Key Exclusion Rules:
- **Primary excludes Secondary**: If there are ANY legitimate children or descendants, legitimate parents/ascendants get NO legitime
- **Concurring heirs never excluded**: Surviving spouse and illegitimate children always get their share regardless of who else survives
- **Within a category**: Nearer excludes more remote (children exclude grandchildren, except by representation)

## Part 2: Master Legitime Table (Testate Succession)

This table shows the **legitime** (reserved portion) for each compulsory heir type, organized by which heirs survive. The "Free Portion" is what the testator can dispose of freely by will.

### Scenario 1: Legitimate Children Only

| Heir | Legitime | Basis |
|------|----------|-------|
| All legitimate children (shared equally) | ½ of estate | Art. 888 |
| Free portion | ½ of estate | |

**Example** (Estate = ₱10,000,000; 3 legitimate children):
- Each child: ₱10M × ½ ÷ 3 = ₱1,666,667 (legitime)
- Free portion: ₱5,000,000

### Scenario 2: Legitimate Children + Surviving Spouse

| Heir | Legitime | Basis |
|------|----------|-------|
| All legitimate children (shared equally) | ½ of estate | Art. 888 |
| Surviving spouse (1 child) | ¼ of estate | Art. 892 ¶1 |
| Surviving spouse (2+ children) | Equal to one child's share of the ½ | Art. 892 ¶2 |
| Free portion | Remainder after above | |

**Note**: Spouse's legitime comes from the free portion per Art. 892 ¶3.

**Example A** (Estate = ₱10,000,000; 1 legitimate child + spouse):
- Child's legitime: ₱10M × ½ = ₱5,000,000
- Spouse's legitime: ₱10M × ¼ = ₱2,500,000 (from free portion)
- Free portion remaining: ₱10M - ₱5M - ₱2.5M = ₱2,500,000

**Example B** (Estate = ₱10,000,000; 3 legitimate children + spouse):
- Children's total legitime: ₱10M × ½ = ₱5,000,000 → each child = ₱1,666,667
- Spouse's legitime: equal to one child = ₱1,666,667 (from free portion)
- Free portion remaining: ₱10M - ₱5M - ₱1,666,667 = ₱3,333,333

### Scenario 3: Legitimate Children + Surviving Spouse + Illegitimate Children

| Heir | Legitime | Basis |
|------|----------|-------|
| All legitimate children (shared equally) | ½ of estate | Art. 888 |
| Surviving spouse | Equal to one legitimate child's share (from free portion) | Art. 897 |
| Each illegitimate child | ½ of one legitimate child's legitime (from free portion) | Art. 895, Art. 176 FC |
| Free portion | Remainder | |

**Constraint**: Total illegitimate children's legitime cannot exceed the free portion, and spouse's legitime must be satisfied first (Art. 895 ¶3).

**Example** (Estate = ₱10,000,000; 2 legitimate children + 1 illegitimate child + spouse):
- Legitimate children's total legitime: ₱5,000,000 → each = ₱2,500,000
- Spouse's legitime: ₱2,500,000 (equal to one legitimate child, from free portion)
- Illegitimate child's legitime: ₱1,250,000 (½ of legitimate child's legitime, from free portion)
- Free portion remaining: ₱10M - ₱5M - ₱2.5M - ₱1.25M = ₱1,250,000

### Scenario 4: Legitimate Children + Illegitimate Children (No Spouse)

| Heir | Legitime | Basis |
|------|----------|-------|
| All legitimate children (shared equally) | ½ of estate | Art. 888 |
| Each illegitimate child | ½ of one legitimate child's legitime (from free portion) | Art. 895 |
| Free portion | Remainder | |

**Example** (Estate = ₱10,000,000; 2 legitimate + 2 illegitimate children):
- Each legitimate child's legitime: ₱10M × ½ ÷ 2 = ₱2,500,000
- Each illegitimate child's legitime: ₱2,500,000 × ½ = ₱1,250,000
- Total legitime: ₱5M + ₱2.5M = ₱7,500,000
- Free portion: ₱2,500,000

### Scenario 5: Legitimate Parents/Ascendants Only (No Descendants)

| Heir | Legitime | Basis |
|------|----------|-------|
| Legitimate parents (shared equally, or all to survivor) | ½ of estate | Art. 889, 890 |
| Free portion | ½ of estate | |

**Example** (Estate = ₱10,000,000; both parents alive):
- Father: ₱2,500,000
- Mother: ₱2,500,000
- Free portion: ₱5,000,000

### Scenario 6: Legitimate Parents/Ascendants + Surviving Spouse

| Heir | Legitime | Basis |
|------|----------|-------|
| Legitimate parents/ascendants | ½ of estate | Art. 889 |
| Surviving spouse | ¼ of estate (from free portion) | Art. 893 |
| Free portion | ¼ of estate | |

**Example** (Estate = ₱10,000,000; both parents + spouse):
- Parents: ₱5,000,000 (₱2.5M each)
- Spouse: ₱2,500,000
- Free portion: ₱2,500,000

### Scenario 7: Legitimate Parents + Illegitimate Children

| Heir | Legitime | Basis |
|------|----------|-------|
| Legitimate parents/ascendants | ½ of estate | Art. 889 |
| Illegitimate children (total) | ¼ of estate (from free portion) | Art. 896 |
| Free portion | ¼ of estate | |

### Scenario 8: Legitimate Parents + Surviving Spouse + Illegitimate Children

| Heir | Legitime | Basis |
|------|----------|-------|
| Legitimate parents/ascendants | ½ of estate | Art. 889 |
| Surviving spouse | ⅛ of estate | Art. 899 |
| Illegitimate children (total) | ¼ of estate | Art. 899 |
| Free portion | ⅛ of estate | |

**Example** (Estate = ₱10,000,000; both parents + spouse + 2 illegitimate children):
- Parents: ₱5,000,000 (₱2.5M each)
- Spouse: ₱1,250,000
- Each illegitimate child: ₱1,250,000 (₱2.5M ÷ 2)
- Free portion: ₱1,250,000

### Scenario 9: Illegitimate Children + Surviving Spouse (No Legitimate Heirs)

| Heir | Legitime | Basis |
|------|----------|-------|
| Surviving spouse | ⅓ of estate | Art. 894 |
| Illegitimate children (total) | ⅓ of estate | Art. 894 |
| Free portion | ⅓ of estate | |

**Example** (Estate = ₱10,000,000; spouse + 3 illegitimate children):
- Spouse: ₱3,333,333
- Each illegitimate child: ₱1,111,111
- Free portion: ₱3,333,333

### Scenario 10: Illegitimate Children Only

| Heir | Legitime | Basis |
|------|----------|-------|
| Illegitimate children (shared equally) | ½ of estate | Art. 901 |
| Free portion | ½ of estate | |

### Scenario 11: Surviving Spouse Only

| Heir | Legitime | Basis |
|------|----------|-------|
| Surviving spouse | ½ of estate | Art. 900 |
| Free portion | ½ of estate | |

**Exception** (Art. 900 ¶2): If marriage was in articulo mortis and decedent died within 3 months, spouse's legitime = ⅓ (not ½), unless they lived as husband and wife for 5+ years.

## Part 3: Intestate Distribution Table

When there is NO will, the entire estate distributes per these rules (no "free portion" concept — everything goes to intestate heirs).

| # | Surviving Heirs | Distribution | Basis |
|---|----------------|-------------|-------|
| 1 | Legitimate children only | Equal shares | Art. 980 |
| 2 | Legitimate children + spouse | Spouse = one child's share; children divide equally; all shares equal | Art. 996 |
| 3 | Legitimate + illegitimate children (no spouse) | Legitimate children per Art. 983 (referencing Art. 895: illegitimate = ½ of legitimate share) | Art. 983 |
| 4 | Legitimate + illegitimate children + spouse | Spouse = one legitimate child's share; illegitimate = ½ of legitimate share | Art. 999 |
| 5 | Legitimate parents/ascendants only | Equal shares (or all to survivor per Art. 986) | Art. 985 |
| 6 | Legitimate parents + spouse | ½ to parents, ½ to spouse | Art. 997 |
| 7 | Illegitimate children only | Equal shares (entire estate) | Art. 988 |
| 8 | Illegitimate children + spouse | ½ to spouse, ½ to illegitimate children | Art. 998 |
| 9 | Legitimate ascendants + illegitimate children | ½ to ascendants, ½ to illegitimate children | Art. 991 |
| 10 | Legitimate ascendants + illegitimate children + spouse | ½ to ascendants, ¼ to illegitimate children, ¼ to spouse | Art. 1000 |
| 11 | Spouse only (no descendants, ascendants, illegitimate children) | Entire estate to spouse (but see Art. 1001) | Art. 995 |
| 12 | Spouse + siblings/nephews/nieces | ½ to spouse, ½ to siblings/their children | Art. 1001 |
| 13 | Siblings only | Equal shares (full blood); half blood = ½ of full blood | Art. 1004, 1006 |
| 14 | Collateral relatives (within 5th degree) | Per degree of relationship | Art. 1009, 1010 |
| 15 | No heirs | Entire estate to the State | Art. 1011 |

## Part 4: Worked Examples (Intestate)

### Example 1: Standard Family — Married with 3 Legitimate Children

**Facts**: Decedent (D) dies intestate. Net estate = ₱12,000,000. Survived by spouse (S) and 3 legitimate children (C1, C2, C3).

**Rule**: Art. 996 — Spouse gets same share as each child.

**Computation**:
- Total shares: 4 (3 children + 1 spouse)
- Each share: ₱12,000,000 ÷ 4 = ₱3,000,000

| Heir | Share | Amount |
|------|-------|--------|
| C1 | ¼ | ₱3,000,000 |
| C2 | ¼ | ₱3,000,000 |
| C3 | ¼ | ₱3,000,000 |
| S | ¼ | ₱3,000,000 |
| **Total** | | **₱12,000,000** |

### Example 2: Legitimate + Illegitimate Children + Spouse

**Facts**: D dies intestate. Net estate = ₱10,000,000. Survived by spouse (S), 2 legitimate children (LC1, LC2), 1 illegitimate child (IC1).

**Rule**: Art. 999 — Spouse = one legitimate child's share. Art. 983/895 — Illegitimate child = ½ of legitimate child's share.

**Computation using ratio method**:
- Legitimate child = 2 units
- Illegitimate child = 1 unit (½ of legitimate)
- Spouse = 2 units (same as one legitimate child)
- Total units: 2 + 2 + 1 + 2 = 7

| Heir | Units | Share | Amount |
|------|-------|-------|--------|
| LC1 | 2 | 2/7 | ₱2,857,143 |
| LC2 | 2 | 2/7 | ₱2,857,143 |
| IC1 | 1 | 1/7 | ₱1,428,571 |
| S | 2 | 2/7 | ₱2,857,143 |
| **Total** | **7** | | **₱10,000,000** |

### Example 3: Illegitimate Children + Spouse (No Legitimate Heirs)

**Facts**: D (unmarried under Philippine law) dies intestate. Net estate = ₱6,000,000. Survived by surviving spouse (S) and 2 illegitimate children (IC1, IC2).

**Rule**: Art. 998 — ½ to spouse, ½ to illegitimate children.

| Heir | Share | Amount |
|------|-------|--------|
| S | ½ | ₱3,000,000 |
| IC1 | ¼ | ₱1,500,000 |
| IC2 | ¼ | ₱1,500,000 |
| **Total** | | **₱6,000,000** |

### Example 4: Surviving Parents + Spouse

**Facts**: D dies intestate with no children. Net estate = ₱8,000,000. Survived by both parents (F, M) and spouse (S).

**Rule**: Art. 997 — ½ to parents, ½ to spouse.

| Heir | Share | Amount |
|------|-------|--------|
| F | ¼ | ₱2,000,000 |
| M | ¼ | ₱2,000,000 |
| S | ½ | ₱4,000,000 |
| **Total** | | **₱8,000,000** |

### Example 5: Legitimate Parents + Illegitimate Children + Spouse

**Facts**: D dies intestate with no legitimate children. Net estate = ₱8,000,000. Survived by both parents (F, M), spouse (S), and 2 illegitimate children (IC1, IC2).

**Rule**: Art. 1000 — ½ to ascendants, ¼ to illegitimate children, ¼ to spouse.

| Heir | Share | Amount |
|------|-------|--------|
| F | ¼ | ₱2,000,000 |
| M | ¼ | ₱2,000,000 |
| S | ¼ | ₱2,000,000 |
| IC1 | ⅛ | ₱1,000,000 |
| IC2 | ⅛ | ₱1,000,000 |
| **Total** | | **₱8,000,000** |

### Example 6: Representation — Predeceased Child with Grandchildren

**Facts**: D dies intestate. Net estate = ₱9,000,000. D had 3 legitimate children (C1, C2, C3). C2 predeceased D, leaving 2 children (GC1, GC2).

**Rule**: Arts. 981, 974 — C2's share goes to GC1 and GC2 by representation (per stirpes).

| Heir | Basis | Share | Amount |
|------|-------|-------|--------|
| C1 | Own right | ⅓ | ₱3,000,000 |
| GC1 | Representation of C2 | ⅙ | ₱1,500,000 |
| GC2 | Representation of C2 | ⅙ | ₱1,500,000 |
| C3 | Own right | ⅓ | ₱3,000,000 |
| **Total** | | | **₱9,000,000** |

### Example 7: Conjugal Property Scenario

**Facts**: D and Spouse own conjugal property worth ₱20,000,000. D dies intestate. 4 legitimate children survive.

**Step 1**: Determine estate
- Conjugal property: ₱20,000,000
- Spouse's share of conjugal property: ₱10,000,000 (not part of D's estate)
- D's net estate: ₱10,000,000

**Step 2**: Distribute D's estate (Art. 996)
- Total shares: 5 (4 children + spouse)
- Each share: ₱10,000,000 ÷ 5 = ₱2,000,000

| Heir | From Conjugal | From Inheritance | Total |
|------|--------------|-----------------|-------|
| Spouse | ₱10,000,000 | ₱2,000,000 | ₱12,000,000 |
| C1 | — | ₱2,000,000 | ₱2,000,000 |
| C2 | — | ₱2,000,000 | ₱2,000,000 |
| C3 | — | ₱2,000,000 | ₱2,000,000 |
| C4 | — | ₱2,000,000 | ₱2,000,000 |
| **Total** | | | **₱20,000,000** |

> **Note for engine**: The engine takes net distributable estate (after tax, after conjugal property separation) as input. The conjugal property separation is upstream of this engine.

## Part 5: Special Rules

### The Iron Curtain Rule (Art. 992)

An illegitimate child has NO right to inherit ab intestato from the legitimate children and relatives of his father or mother; nor shall such legitimate children or relatives inherit from the illegitimate child.

**Effect**: There is a complete barrier between the legitimate and illegitimate families in intestate succession. An illegitimate child can only inherit from:
- His/her parents (directly)
- His/her own descendants
- His/her surviving spouse

### Preterition (Art. 854)

If a compulsory heir in the direct line is totally omitted from the will (neither instituted nor disinherited):
1. The institution of heirs is **annulled** (the entire testamentary institution is voided)
2. Legacies and devises remain valid **if not inofficious** (if they don't impair the legitime)
3. Result: intestate succession applies to the bulk of the estate

### Disinheritance Effect (Art. 923)

When a compulsory heir is validly disinherited:
- The disinherited heir's children/descendants **take their place** and preserve the rights of compulsory heirs
- The disinherited parent cannot have usufruct or administration of the property inherited by their children

### Articulo Mortis Marriage (Art. 900 ¶2)

If the marriage was solemnized when the testator was at the point of death and the testator died within 3 months:
- Spouse's legitime as sole heir = ⅓ (not ½)
- Exception: If they had been living as husband and wife for more than 5 years

## Part 6: Computation Pipeline Summary

For any inheritance distribution computation:

```
1. INPUTS:
   - Net distributable estate (after tax, after conjugal property separation)
   - Family tree with classifications
   - Will (if any)

2. CLASSIFY HEIRS:
   - Determine which compulsory heir categories are present
   - Check for representation (predeceased heirs with living descendants)
   - Verify adopted children (RA 8552)
   - Classify all illegitimate children uniformly (FC Art. 176)

3. IF TESTATE (will exists):
   a. Compute each compulsory heir's legitime
   b. Check if will respects all legitimes
   c. If will impairs legitime → reduce inofficious dispositions (Art. 911)
   d. If preterition → annul institution (Art. 854)
   e. Distribute free portion per will
   f. Any undisposed remainder → intestate rules

4. IF INTESTATE (no will):
   a. Determine which intestate distribution rule applies (per table above)
   b. Compute shares per applicable articles
   c. Apply representation where needed

5. OUTPUT:
   - Per-heir peso amounts
   - Per-heir narrative explanation
```
