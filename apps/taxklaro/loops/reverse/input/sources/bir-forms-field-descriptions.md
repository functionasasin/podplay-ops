## 1. BIR FORM 1701 — Annual Income Tax Return for Individuals (Including Mixed Income Earners), Estates and Trusts

**Current version**: January 2018 (ENCS). Note: BIR Form 1701-MS (August 2024 ENCS) is a modified-simplified variant released under RMC No. 34-2025 for individuals who received mixed income during the period. It uses the same field structure with minor additions.

**Who files**: (a) Resident citizens with trade/business/profession income inside and outside Philippines; (b) Non-resident citizens, resident aliens, and non-resident aliens with income from inside Philippines only; (c) Mixed income earners (compensation + business/profession). Does NOT cover purely compensation earners (they use 1700) or purely business/profession earners who are eligible for 1701A (see below).

**Filing deadline**: On or before April 15 of the following calendar year. Two-installment option: first installment by April 15, second installment by October 15 (only for tax due > ₱2,000).

**Pages**: 4 pages total (Page 1 = Parts I–III; Page 2 = Part IV + Schedule 1/2; Pages 3–4 = Schedules 3–6).

**General instructions**: All entries in CAPITAL LETTERS, BLACK ink. Do NOT enter centavos (49 centavos or less → drop down; 50 centavos or more → round up). Mark applicable boxes with "X". File 2 copies with BIR, retain 1.

---

### PAGE 1 — Parts I, II, III

#### HEADER (For BIR Use Only)
- **BCS/Item**: BIR Control System item number (BIR use only)
- **DLN**: Document Locator Number (BIR use only)
- **PSIC**: Philippine Standard Industrial Classification code (BIR use only)

---

#### TOP SECTION (Pre-Part I)
- **Item 1** — For the Year (MM/YYYY): The taxable year being reported (not the filing date). Calendar or fiscal year end date.
- **Item 2** — Amended Return? (Yes/No): Check "Yes" if this is a corrected return replacing a previously filed return.
- **Item 3** — Short Period Return? (Yes/No): Check "Yes" if the taxable year is less than 12 months (e.g., newly registered mid-year, or fiscal year change).

---

#### PART I — Background Information on Taxpayer/Filer

- **Item 4** — Taxpayer Identification Number (TIN): 12-digit TIN (9-digit base + 3-digit branch code, typically 000 for individuals). Column A = Taxpayer/Filer, Column B = Spouse (if applicable). If no TIN, file BIR Form 1901/1905 first.
- **Item 5** — RDO Code: 3-digit Revenue District Office code where the taxpayer is registered.
- **Item 6** — Line of Business / Occupation: Nature of trade, business, or profession being reported.
- **Item 7** — Alphanumeric Tax Code (ATC): Select ONE applicable code:
  - **II011** — Compensation Income (for mixed income earners who are primarily employees)
  - **II012** — Business Income – Graduated IT Rates
  - **II013** — Mixed Income – Graduated IT Rates
  - **II014** — Income from Profession – Graduated IT Rates
  - **II015** — Business Income – 8% IT Rate
  - **II016** — Mixed Income – 8% IT Rate
  - **II017** — Income from Profession – 8% IT Rate
- **Item 8** — Taxpayer/Filer Type: Single Proprietor | Professional | Estate | Trust
- **Item 9** — Taxpayer's Name: Last Name, First Name, Middle Name (as registered on BIR Form 1901/1905/2303).
- **Item 10** — Trade Name / Registered Name: Business trade name if applicable.
- **Item 11** — Registered Address (Line 1, Line 2, City/Municipality, Province, Zip Code): Registered address with the BIR. If different from current address, update via BIR Form 1905.
- **Item 12** — Date of Birth (MM/DD/YYYY): Taxpayer's birthdate.
- **Item 13** — Contact Number: Telephone/mobile number.
- **Item 14** — Email Address: For official correspondence.
- **Item 15** — Civil Status: Single | Married | Legally Separated | Widow/Widower
- **Item 16** — If Married: Does spouse have income? Yes | No
- **Item 17** — Citizenship: Resident Citizen | Non-Resident Citizen | Resident Alien | Non-Resident Alien Engaged in Trade/Business | Non-Resident Alien Not Engaged in Trade/Business
- **Item 18** — Filing Status: Separate Filing | Joint Filing (if joint, both spouses' info required in Column A and Column B respectively)
- **Item 19** — Income EXEMPT from Income Tax? Yes | No. If Yes, include nature and legal basis.
- **Item 20** — Income subject to SPECIAL/PREFERENTIAL RATE? Yes | No. If Yes, fill out also Part X (Consolidation of ALL Activities per Tax Regime).
- **Item 21** — Tax Rate for Business/Profession Income:
  - ☐ Graduated Rates (Sec. 24(A))
  - ☐ 8% in lieu of Graduated Rates and Percentage Tax (Sec. 24(A)(2)(b)) — available ONLY if gross sales/receipts ≤ ₱3,000,000
- **Item 21A** — Method of Deduction (choose one; irrevocable for the taxable year based on first quarter election):
  - ☐ Itemized Deduction (Sec. 34(A)-(J))
  - ☐ Optional Standard Deduction — OSD (40% of gross sales/receipts per Sec. 34(L))

---

#### PART II — Total Tax Payable

*(DO NOT enter centavos. 49 centavos or less: drop down. 50 or more: round up. Two columns: Column A = Taxpayer/Filer, Column B = Spouse)*

- **Item 22** — Tax Due: Transfer from Part VI, Item 5. This is the total income tax due before credits.
- **Item 23** — Less: Total Tax Credits/Payments: Transfer from Part VII, Item 10. All prepaid taxes and credits.
- **Item 24** — Tax Payable/(Overpayment): Item 22 minus Item 23. Positive = tax payable; negative = overpayment.
- **Item 25** — Less: Portion of Tax Payable Allowed for 2nd Installment (50% or less of Item 22): Only applicable if taxpayer elects the two-installment payment. Enter the portion payable on October 15. Cannot exceed 50% of Item 22.
- **Item 26** — Amount of Tax Payable/(Overpayment) (Item 24 Less Item 25): First installment amount due by April 15.

  **Overpayment election** (mark ONE only — irrevocable once filed):
  - ☐ To be Refunded
  - ☐ To be Issued a Tax Credit Certificate (TCC)
  - ☐ To be Carried Over as Tax Credit for Next Year/Quarter

- **Item 27** — Surcharge: 25% of tax not paid when due (or 50% if fraudulent/false return).
- **Item 28** — Interest: 12% per annum from due date to actual payment date, computed as: (Tax Due × 12%) × (days late / 365).
- **Item 29** — Compromise: BIR-assessed compromise penalty per schedule.
- **Item 30** — Total Penalties: Item 27 + Item 28 + Item 29.
- **Item 31** — Total Amount Payable/(Overpayment): Item 26 + Item 30.
- **Item 32** — Aggregate Amount Payable/(Overpayment): Combined total for both Taxpayer and Spouse columns.

---

#### PART III — Details of Payment

- **Item 33** — Name of Bank / Agency: Authorized Agent Bank (AAB) where payment was made, or Revenue Collection Officer (RCO) name.
- **Item 34** — Cash/Bank Debit Memo: Date and amount of cash payment or bank debit.
- **Item 35** — Check: Check number, drawee bank, date, and amount.
- **Item 36** — Tax Debit Memo: TDM reference number, date issued, amount.
- **Item 37** — Others: Specify other payment method (e.g., GCash, PayMaya, online banking); reference number and amount.
- **Machine Validation / Revenue Official Receipt Details**: BIR stamp details if filed manually over-the-counter at RDO.

---

### PAGE 2 — Part IV (Computation), Schedules 1 & 2

#### PART IV — Computation of Tax
*(Complete Part IV before Part II. Part IV has two sub-sections: Background Info on Spouse (left), and Schedules 1 & 2 feeding into Part VI)*

Note: Part IV numbering continues from prior versions. In the January 2018 ENCS, the spouse information appears here with items numbered separately (see below), while the income computation appears in Schedule references.

#### PART IV — Background Information of Spouse (only if married with filing status = Joint Filing or if spouse has income)

- **Item 1** — Spouse's TIN
- **Item 2** — Spouse's RDO Code
- **Item 3** — Spouse's Taxpayer/Filer Type: Single Proprietor | Professional | Compensation Earner
- **Item 4** — Spouse's ATC: Same options as Item 7 above (II011, II012, II013, II014, II015, II016, II017)
- **Item 5** — Spouse's Name: Last Name, First Name, Middle Name
- **Item 6** — Spouse's Contact Number
- **Item 7** — Spouse's Line of Business / Occupation
- **Item 8** — Spouse's Date of Birth
- **Item 9** — Spouse's Citizenship: Same options as taxpayer
- **Item 10** — Spouse's Income EXEMPT from Income Tax? Yes | No
- **Item 11** — Spouse's Income subject to SPECIAL/PREFERENTIAL RATE? Yes | No
- **Item 12** — Spouse's Tax Rate: ☐ Graduated | ☐ 8%
- **Item 12A** — Spouse's Method of Deduction: ☐ Itemized | ☐ OSD

---

#### SCHEDULE 1 — Employer Information and Compensation Income

*(Only for mixed income earners — skip if purely business/profession)*

Columns: (A) Name of Employer, (B) Employer TIN, (C) Gross Compensation Income, (D) Non-Taxable Compensation, (E) Taxable Compensation, (F) Tax Withheld

- **Item 1** — First Employer (within the year): Enter employer details and compensation amounts
- **Item 2** — Second/Other Employer(s): If applicable (changed jobs during the year)
- **Item 3A** — Totals (Taxpayer): Sum of all employers' Gross Compensation, Taxable Compensation, Tax Withheld
- **Item 3B** — Totals (Spouse): If spouse also has compensation income

Fields within Schedule 1:
  - **Name of Employer**: Legal name of employer
  - **Employer TIN**: Employer's TIN
  - **Gross Compensation**: Total gross pay before any deductions (from BIR Form 2316)
  - **Non-Taxable Compensation**: SSS, GSIS, PhilHealth, HDMF contributions; 13th month pay up to ₱90,000; de minimis benefits within limits
  - **Taxable Compensation**: Gross Compensation minus Non-Taxable Compensation
  - **Tax Withheld**: Total EWT withheld by employer per BIR Form 2316

---

#### SCHEDULE 2 — Taxable Compensation Income

*(Only for mixed income earners — skip if purely business/profession)*

- **Item 1** — Total Gross Compensation Income (from Schedule 1, Item 3A): Sum of all compensation
- **Item 2** — Less: Non-Taxable Compensation Income: Contributions + exempt 13th month/bonuses
- **Item 3** — Taxable Compensation Income: Item 1 minus Item 2 → feeds into Part V/VI computation
- **Item 4A** — Personal Exemption (pre-2018 only; eliminated by TRAIN Law effective Jan 1, 2018): ₱0 (not applicable for 2018 and beyond — TRAIN eliminated personal exemptions)
- **Item 4B** — Additional Exemption for Dependents (eliminated by TRAIN Law): ₱0

Note: For taxable years 2018 onwards, there are NO personal exemptions. The ₱250,000 zero-rated bracket in the graduated tax table effectively provides an exemption equivalent.

---

### PAGES 3-4 — Schedules 3-6 (Business/Profession Income and Deductions)

#### SCHEDULE 3 — Taxable Business Income

*(Two sub-schedules: 3.A for Graduated Rate; 3.B for 8% Flat Rate)*

**SCHEDULE 3.A — For Graduated Income Tax Rate**
*(Fill items 1 through 24 if using graduated rates — Itemized Deductions or OSD)*

- **Item 1** — Sales/Revenues/Receipts/Fees: Total gross income from business or practice of profession for the taxable year.
- **Item 2** — Less: Sales Returns, Allowances and Discounts: Reductions to gross sales from returned goods, price adjustments, or trade discounts.
- **Item 3** — Net Sales/Revenues/Receipts/Fees (Item 1 Less Item 2): Net sales after returns and discounts.
- **Item 4** — Less: Cost of Sales/Services: Direct cost of goods sold (for merchandise business) or direct cost of services rendered (for service providers). Includes materials, direct labor, factory overhead (manufacturing); or cost of services delivered (professional services).
- **Item 5** — Gross Income from Operation (Item 3 Less Item 4): Gross profit from primary business activity.
- **Item 6** — Add: Other Non-Operating Income: Dividends, interest income (not subject to final tax), gains on property sale, rental income not from primary business, other income.
- **Item 7** — Total Gross Income (Item 5 + Item 6): Combined operating and non-operating income.
- **Item 8** — Less: Allowable Deductions (If Itemized, enter total from Schedule 4; if OSD, enter 40% of Item 3): Net deductions from total gross income.
  - If Itemized: Transfer total from Schedule 4 (Ordinary Allowable Itemized Deductions) + Schedule 5 (Special Allowable Itemized Deductions)
  - If OSD: Item 3 × 40% = OSD amount. No Schedule 4 needed.
- **Item 9** — Net Income/(Loss) from Business/Profession (Item 7 Less Item 8): Net taxable income from business before NOLCO.
- **Item 10** — Less: Net Operating Loss Carry Over (NOLCO) from Schedule 6: NOLCO applied from prior years (maximum 3 consecutive years from year of loss).
- **Item 11** — Taxable Income/(Loss) from Business/Profession (Item 9 Less Item 10): Net taxable business income after NOLCO.
- **Item 12** — Add: Taxable Compensation Income (from Schedule 2, Item 3): For mixed income earners only.
- **Item 13** — Total Taxable Income (Item 11 + Item 12): Combined business + compensation taxable income.
- **Item 14** — Income Tax Due (Apply graduated tax rate table to Item 13):
  - Table 1 (2018–2022): 0%, 20%, 25%, 30%, 32%, 35% brackets
  - Table 2 (2023+): 0%, 15%, 20%, 25%, 30%, 35% brackets
  - See lookup-tables/graduated-rate-table.md for complete formulas
- **Item 15** — Less: Tax Withheld on Compensation (from Schedule 1, Item 3A, Column F): Total EWT withheld by employers on compensation income.
- **Item 16** — Tax Due on Business Income (Item 14 Less Item 15): Net income tax applicable to business/profession portion.
- **Items 17-24** — Additional computations for special situations (equity investments, BMBE exemptions, etc.): Leave blank if not applicable.

**SCHEDULE 3.B — For 8% Flat Income Tax Rate**
*(Fill items 25 through 30 INSTEAD OF items 1-24 above. Only for NON-VAT registered taxpayers with gross receipts ≤ ₱3,000,000)*

- **Item 25** — Sales/Revenues/Receipts/Fees: Total gross receipts for the taxable year (no deduction for cost of sales).
- **Item 26** — Add: Other Non-Operating Income: Other income that forms part of the 8% tax base.
- **Item 27** — Total Gross Sales/Receipts and Other Non-Operating Income (Item 25 + Item 26)
- **Item 28** — Less: Allowable reduction from gross sales/receipts for PURELY self-employed individuals/professionals: **₱250,000** (NOT applicable if taxpayer has compensation income — i.e., mixed income earners do NOT deduct ₱250,000 here).
- **Item 29** — Taxable Income/(Loss) at 8% Rate (Item 27 Less Item 28): The base for the 8% tax.
- **Item 30** — Tax Due on Business/Profession Income at 8% (Item 29 × 8%): The flat rate income tax.
  - For MIXED income earners using 8%: Tax = (Item 29 − 0) × 8%. No ₱250,000 reduction because the ₱250,000 threshold is already consumed by the ₱250,000 zero-bracket in the graduated rate table applied to compensation income. Excess of ₱250,000 is the base.
  - NOTE: Mixed income earner formula per RMC 50-2018: Tax = (Gross Sales/Receipts from Business − ₱250,000) × 8%, but ONLY the ₱250,000 not already applied to compensation. In practice, if compensation already exceeds ₱250,000, no deduction from business gross receipts.

---

#### SCHEDULE 4 — Ordinary Allowable Itemized Deductions

*(Only if Itemized Deduction is the chosen method; skip if OSD or 8%)*

Each line requires attached documentation or schedules as part of audited financial statements.

- **Line 1** — Amortization: Amortization of intangible assets, pre-operating/organizational expenses under Sec. 34(B) and Sec. 40(C)(5).
- **Line 2** — Bad Debts: Specific receivables written off during the year as uncollectible; must be genuinely ascertained to be worthless. Subject to recapture if subsequently recovered.
- **Line 3** — Charitable and Other Contributions: Donations to accredited donee institutions and organizations under Sec. 34(H). Subject to limit of 5% of net income (individuals). Fully deductible if donated to certain priority organizations per Sec. 34(H)(2).
- **Line 4** — Depletion: For natural resource companies; cost depletion for oil/gas and mineral mines per Sec. 34(G).
- **Line 5** — Depreciation: Straight-line, declining balance, or other BIR-approved methods per Sec. 34(F). Cannot exceed cost/basis of asset.
- **Line 6** — Entertainment, Amusement and Recreation (EAR): Maximum deduction = 0.5% of net sales (for sellers of goods) or 1% of net revenue (for sellers of services) per Sec. 34(A)(1)(a)(iv).
- **Line 7** — Fringe Benefits: For employees — normally deductible if subject to fringe benefit tax (FBT) at employer level. If FBT-exempt items, deductible as ordinary expenses.
- **Line 8** — Interest: Interest paid or incurred on indebtedness in connection with the taxpayer's profession, trade, or business. Subject to reduction by 33% of interest income subjected to final tax. Formula: Allowable interest = Gross interest expense − (0.33 × interest income subjected to final tax).
- **Line 9** — Losses: Losses from fires, storms, shipwreck, or other casualties; theft or embezzlement; not compensated by insurance or otherwise. Domestic losses only; foreign losses only if resident citizen.
- **Line 10** — Pension Trusts: Contributions to pension/retirement fund for employees — initial past-service cost: 1/10 per year for 10 years; current service cost: fully deductible.
- **Line 11** — Rental: Rent paid for property used in trade/business/profession.
- **Line 12** — Research and Development: Costs paid/incurred for R&D treated as deferred expense amortized over 60 months or expensed immediately if permitted.
- **Line 13** — Salaries, Wages and Allowances: Compensation paid to employees (NOT the owner's own salary — sole proprietors cannot deduct their own salary).
- **Line 14** — SSS, GSIS, PhilHealth, HDMF and Other Contributions: Mandatory government-mandated contributions paid by employer for employees. Owner's own contributions are NOT deductible here (they may be part of non-taxable compensation if a different employer-employee relationship exists).
- **Line 15** — Taxes and Licenses: Business taxes, local government taxes, license fees. Does NOT include income tax, estate tax, donor's tax, or taxes assessed against local benefits.
- **Line 16** — Transportation and Travel: Actual transportation and travel expenses incurred in trade/business/profession. Must be substantiated with receipts.
- **Line 17** — Others (Deductions Subject to Withholding Tax and Other Expenses): Catch-all for other ordinary and necessary business expenses. Sub-items include:
  - (a) Janitorial and Messengerial Services
  - (b) Professional Fees (legal, accounting, consulting)
  - (c) Security Services
  - (d) Repair and Maintenance Expense
  - (e) Other expenses subject to withholding tax (must demonstrate withholding was done)
  - (f) Other ordinary and necessary business expenses not covered above
- **Total Ordinary Allowable Itemized Deductions** (Sum of Lines 1–17): Transfer to Schedule 3.A, Item 8.

---

#### SCHEDULE 5 — Special Allowable Itemized Deductions

*(Additional deductions available under special laws)*

- **Line 1** — Additional Compensation Expense for Senior Citizen Employees: Under RA 9994 (Expanded Senior Citizens Act), employers who hire senior citizens may deduct an additional 15% of their salaries above the standard deduction.
- **Line 2** — Additional Compensation Expense for PWD Employees: Under RA 10524, employers of persons with disability may deduct an additional 25% of the total amount paid as salaries.
- **Line 3** — Discount Granted to Senior Citizens (RA 9994): Establishments granting mandatory 20% senior citizen discount may deduct the cost of the discount (not the income forgone) from gross income.
- **Line 4** — Discount Granted to PWDs (RA 10754): Same structure as senior citizen discount deduction.
- **Line 5** — Other Special Allowable Deductions: Per other specific laws (e.g., RA 7277, RA 9257, special economic zone incentive legislation).
- **Total Special Allowable Itemized Deductions** (Sum of Lines 1–5): Add to Schedule 4 total for total deductions into Schedule 3.A, Item 8.

---

#### SCHEDULE 6 — Computation of Net Operating Loss Carry Over (NOLCO)

*(Only complete if claiming NOLCO deduction)*

For each prior year where a net operating loss was incurred (maximum 3 consecutive years):

- **Column A** — Taxable Year Incurred: The year the net loss occurred.
- **Column B** — Amount of Net Operating Loss: The unapplied NOLCO at the beginning of the current year from that loss year.
- **Column C** — NOLCO Applied in Previous Taxable Years: Cumulative NOLCO already deducted in prior years.
- **Column D** — NOLCO Expired: NOLCO that has lapsed (more than 3 years from loss year).
- **Column E** — NOLCO Applied this Current Year: Amount being claimed this year from this loss year.
- **Column F** — Net Operating Loss Balance (Unapplied): Column B minus (Column C + Column D + Column E).
- **Total NOLCO Applied This Year**: Sum of Column E, all rows → transfer to Schedule 3.A, Item 10.

---

### PART V — Tax Due (Consolidated from all Schedules)

*(Feeds from Schedule computations)*

- **Item 1** — Total Tax Due on Compensation Income: From Schedule 2 computation (graduated rate on compensation).
- **Item 2** — Total Tax Due on Business Income (Graduated): From Schedule 3.A, Item 16.
  OR
- **Item 2** — Total Tax Due on Business Income (8%): From Schedule 3.B, Item 30.
- **Item 3** — Total Tax Due (Item 1 + Item 2): Combined income tax due.
- **Item 4** — Add: Special Tax Due (if applicable from Part X — special/preferential rate activities).
- **Item 5** — Total Income Tax Due: Transfer to Part II, Item 22.

---

### PART VI — Summary of Tax Credits/Payments

*(Complete and transfer total to Part II, Item 23)*

- **Item 1** — Prior Year's Excess Credits: Overpayment from prior year's annual ITR that was carried over (not refunded, not TCC).
- **Item 2** — Tax Payments for 1st Quarter (Form 1701Q, Q1): Amount actually paid on the first quarter return.
- **Item 3** — Tax Payments for 2nd Quarter (Form 1701Q, Q2): Amount actually paid on the second quarter return.
- **Item 4** — Tax Payments for 3rd Quarter (Form 1701Q, Q3): Amount actually paid on the third quarter return.
- **Item 5** — Creditable Tax Withheld for 1st Quarter (BIR Form 2307): CWT amounts from 2307 certificates issued to taxpayer covering Q1 receipts.
- **Item 6** — Creditable Tax Withheld for 2nd Quarter (BIR Form 2307): CWT amounts covering Q2 receipts.
- **Item 7** — Creditable Tax Withheld for 3rd Quarter (BIR Form 2307): CWT amounts covering Q3 receipts.
- **Item 8** — Creditable Tax Withheld for 4th Quarter (BIR Form 2307): CWT amounts covering Q4 receipts.
- **Item 9** — Tax Withheld on Compensation (BIR Form 2316): EWT withheld by employer(s) on compensation income (for mixed income earners).
- **Item 10** — Tax Paid in Return Previously Filed, if Amended Return: For amended returns only.
- **Item 11** — Foreign Tax Credits: Taxes paid to foreign governments on income taxable in the Philippines (for resident citizens).
- **Item 12** — Other Tax Credits/Payments: Specify (e.g., approved Tax Debit Memo, BMBE exemption certificate).
- **Total Tax Credits/Payments**: Sum of Items 1–12 → Transfer to Part II, Item 23.

---

### REQUIRED ATTACHMENTS (BIR Form 1701)

1. **Statement of Management's Responsibility (SMR)**: Signed by taxpayer/authorized officer stating responsibility for the return's accuracy.
2. **Certificate of Independent CPA** (if gross quarterly sales exceed ₱150,000): CPA must be accredited by BIR. Contains the CPA's TIN, certification number, issuance and expiry dates.
3. **Account Information Form (AIF) and/or Financial Statements**: Required if gross receipts exceed ₱150,000/quarter. Must include:
   - Balance Sheet
   - Income Statement
   - Schedules on Taxes and Licenses (as part of Notes to FS)
4. **BIR Form 2304**: Certificate of Income Payments Not Subjected to Withholding Tax (for income not withheld from).
5. **BIR Form 2307**: Certificate of Creditable Tax Withheld at Source — all copies received during the year.
6. **BIR Form 2316**: Certificate of Compensation Payment/Tax Withheld — from each employer (for mixed income earners).
7. **Approved Tax Debit Memo**: If tax debit memo is claimed as credit.
8. **Proof of Prior Years' Excess Credits**: Copy of prior year annual ITR showing overpayment being carried over.
9. **SAWT (Summary Alphalist of Withholding Tax)**: Summary list of all BIR Form 2307 received, submitted electronically.

## 2. BIR FORM 1701A — Annual Income Tax Return for Individuals Earning Purely from Business/Profession

**Current version**: January 2018 (ENCS).

**Who files**: Individual taxpayers earning PURELY from business/profession (no compensation income), who are either:
- Under graduated income tax rates with OSD as the method of deduction (available to both VAT-registered and non-VAT registered), OR
- Under the 8% flat income tax rate (available ONLY to non-VAT registered taxpayers with gross sales/receipts ≤ ₱3,000,000)

**Cannot file 1701A if**: The taxpayer has any compensation income (must use 1701), is VAT-registered and using 8% (VAT-registered cannot avail 8%), or is using itemized deductions under the graduated rate (must use 1701).

**Filing deadline**: On or before April 15 of the following calendar year.

**Pages**: 2 pages only (simpler than Form 1701). Page 1 = Parts I–III; Page 2 = Part IV (computation).

**General instructions**: Same as Form 1701 (CAPITAL LETTERS, BLACK ink, no centavos, "X" for boxes, 2 copies + 1 retained).

---

### PAGE 1 — Parts I, II, III

#### HEADER (For BIR Use Only)
Same BCS/Item, DLN, PSIC fields as Form 1701.

#### TOP SECTION
- **Item 1** — For the Year (MM/YYYY): Taxable year being reported.
- **Item 2** — Amended Return? Yes | No
- **Item 3** — Short Period Return? Yes | No

---

#### PART I — Background Information on Taxpayer/Filer

- **Item 4** — TIN: 12-digit taxpayer identification number.
- **Item 5** — RDO Code: 3-digit Revenue District Office code.
- **Item 6** — Taxpayer Type: ☐ Single Proprietor | ☐ Professional. Note: "Professional" covers freelancers, practitioners, self-employed individuals. "Single Proprietor" covers registered business owners.
- **Item 7** — Alphanumeric Tax Code (ATC): Select ONE:
  - **II012** — Business Income – Graduated IT Rates (for single proprietors under OSD)
  - **II014** — Income from Profession – Graduated IT Rates (for professionals/freelancers under OSD)
  - **II015** — Business Income – 8% IT Rate (for single proprietors under 8%)
  - **II017** — Income from Profession – 8% IT Rate (for professionals/freelancers under 8%)
- **Item 8** — Taxpayer's Name: Last Name, First Name, Middle Name.
- **Item 9** — Registered Address: Complete address registered with BIR. If different from current, update via Form 1905.
- **Item 9A** — ZIP Code
- **Items 10–12** — Contact details: Line of Business/Occupation, Telephone Number, Email Address
- **Item 13** — Date of Birth (MM/DD/YYYY)
- **Item 14** — Citizenship: Same options as Form 1701.
- **Item 15** — Civil Status: Single | Married | Legally Separated | Widow/Widower
- **Item 16** — Contact Number (mobile/landline)
- **Item 17** — Income EXEMPT from Income Tax? Yes | No
- **Item 18** — Filing Status: ☐ Separate Filing | ☐ Joint Filing (Note: 1701A is almost always Separate Filing since it's for purely self-employed individuals)
- **Item 19** — Tax Rate and Method of Deduction: Choose ONE option (irrevocable for the full taxable year, must match Q1 election):
  - ☐ Graduated Income Tax Rates (Sec. 24(A)) with Optional Standard Deduction (OSD) at 40% of gross sales/receipts/revenues/fees. This option is available to BOTH VAT-registered and non-VAT registered taxpayers.
  - ☐ 8% Income Tax Rate in lieu of Graduated Rates under Sec. 24(A)(2)(a) AND Percentage Tax under Sec. 116 of NIRC. ONLY available if: (a) NOT VAT-registered, AND (b) gross sales/receipts and other non-operating income do NOT exceed ₱3,000,000 for the year.

---

#### PART II — Total Tax Payable

*(DO NOT enter centavos. Items computed from Part IV and transferred here.)*

- **Item 20** — Tax Due: Transferred from Part IV (either Item 46 if OSD/graduated, or Item 56 if 8% rate).
- **Item 21** — Less: Total Tax Credits/Payments: Transferred from Tax Credits section on Page 2.
- **Item 22** — Tax Payable/(Overpayment): Item 20 minus Item 21.
- **Item 23** — Less: Portion Allowed for 2nd Installment (≤ 50% of Item 20): If tax due > ₱2,000 and taxpayer elects two-installment payment. Amount payable October 15.
- **Item 24** — Amount of Tax Payable/(Overpayment) (Item 22 Less Item 23): Due by April 15.

  **If overpayment**, mark ONE (irrevocable):

### HOW THE QUARTERLY CUMULATIVE METHOD WORKS (1701Q)

The 1701Q does NOT compute tax for just the current quarter. It computes cumulative tax for the year-to-date and subtracts what was already paid.

**Example for Q3 (8% method, purely self-employed):**
```
Q1 gross receipts: ₱300,000
Q2 gross receipts: ₱400,000
Q3 gross receipts: ₱350,000
Cumulative gross receipts Q1–Q3: ₱1,050,000

Q3 Form 1701Q:
Item 47 = ₱350,000 (this quarter's receipts)
Item 50 = ₱700,000 (Item 51 from Q2's return, which was Q1+Q2 cumulative)
Item 51 = ₱1,050,000 (cumulative)
Item 52 = ₱250,000 (the once-a-year deduction; already deducted proportionally in Q1/Q2)

Wait — correction: ₱250,000 is the FULL deduction applied to the annual cumulative figure,
not split per quarter. In Q1, taxpayer deducted ₱250,000. In Q2 cumulative computation,
Item 52 = ₱250,000 again because it's applied to the CUMULATIVE YTD base.
In Q3, Item 52 = ₱250,000 again. The "extra" is offset by the credits (Item 56).

Item 53 = ₱1,050,000 − ₱250,000 = ₱800,000 (cumulative taxable income YTD)
Item 54 = ₱800,000 × 8% = ₱64,000 (TOTAL cumulative tax due YTD)

Tax Credits (Schedule III):
Item 55: Prior year excess credits = ₱0
Item 56: Tax paid in Q1 + Q2 quarterly returns = sum of (Q1 tax payable + Q2 tax payable)
Item 57: CWT from 2307 claimed in Q1+Q2 returns = X
Item 58: CWT from 2307 for Q3 = Y
Item 62: Total credits = Q1+Q2 payments + all CWT
Item 63: Tax Payable this quarter = ₱64,000 − (all prior payments + all CWT)
```

## 4. BIR FORM 2551Q — Quarterly Percentage Tax Return

**Current version**: January 2018 (ENCS). Released under RMC No. 26-2018.

**Who files**: Self-employed individuals, professionals, and businesses who are:
- Non-VAT registered (i.e., gross annual sales/receipts do NOT exceed ₱3,000,000), AND

- **Item 22** — Compromise: BIR-assessed penalty.
- **Item 23** — Total Penalties (Items 20 + 21 + 22)
- **Total Amount Payable/(Overpayment)** (Item 19 + Item 23): Final amount to pay.

**Details of Payment**:
- Name of Bank / Agency
- Cash/Bank Debit Memo: Date, amount
- Check: Number, bank, date, amount
- Tax Debit Memo: Reference, date, amount
- Others: Payment method, reference, amount

## 5. BIR FORM 2307 — Certificate of Creditable Tax Withheld at Source

**Current version**: January 2018 (ENCS), v3.

**Purpose**: This certificate is prepared and issued by the PAYOR (withholding agent) to the PAYEE (income recipient) to document that a portion of the payee's income was withheld as Expanded Withholding Tax (EWT) and remitted to the BIR. The payee uses this certificate as evidence of prepaid income tax, which can be credited against their quarterly and annual income tax returns.

**Who issues it**: Any withholding agent — corporations (Top Withholding Agents, government agencies), individuals who are required to withhold EWT on payments made to service providers, lessors, or other income recipients.

**Who receives it**: Freelancers, contractors, service providers, lessors, professionals — anyone receiving income from which EWT was withheld.

**When it must be issued**: On or before the **20th day of the month** following the close of the taxable quarter during which the income was paid. Example: For Q1 (Jan–Mar), issue by April 20. Upon request by the payee at time of payment, must be issued simultaneously.

**Coverage period**: One quarter. A separate 2307 is issued for each quarter.

**How the payee uses it**:
- Attach copies to quarterly income tax returns (1701Q) and claim as credit in Schedule III, Items 57 and 58.
- Attach to annual income tax return (1701 or 1701A) and claim as credit in the Tax Credits/Payments section.
- Submit a Summary Alphalist of Withholding Tax (SAWT) electronically when filing ITR.

**Pages**: 1 page (plus reference table on back). Simple form with 3 main parts.

### EWT DETERMINATION RULES FOR FREELANCERS (BIR Form 2307 Context)

**Which ATC applies to a freelancer receiving professional fees?**

The applicable ATC depends on the freelancer's/professional's gross income for the CURRENT year:

1. **If payee is an individual and gross income for the current year ≤ ₱3,000,000**: Use **WI010** at **5%**.
2. **If payee is an individual and gross income for the current year > ₱3,000,000**: Use **WI011** at **10%**.
3. **If payee is an individual and VAT-registered (regardless of gross income amount)**: Use **WI011** at **10%**.

**How does the payor know the payee's gross income?**
- Payee must submit a sworn declaration of gross income for the year to the payor at the start of the year (or before the first payment).
- If no sworn declaration is submitted, the payor may use the HIGHER rate (WI011 at 10%) to be safe.
- If the payee's income status changes mid-year (gross income crosses ₱3M), they must notify the payor to switch to the higher rate.

**EWT is computed on the GROSS INCOME PAYMENT, EXCLUDING VAT** (if payee is VAT-registered, EWT base = invoice amount excluding 12% VAT).

**When is CWT NOT required?**
- Payments to government-owned and controlled corporations (GOCCs) — they are exempt.
- Payments below minimum thresholds for certain categories.
- Payments to payees with valid tax exemption certificates.
- Purely export sales.

### HOW THE PAYEE USES FORM 2307

When filing 1701Q (quarterly ITR):
- Schedule III, Item 57: Prior quarters' CWT (from 2307s already credited)
- Schedule III, Item 58: Current quarter's CWT (from 2307s being credited now)

When filing 1701A or 1701 (annual ITR):
- Tax Credits/Payments section, Items 5–8 (one item per quarter's 2307 totals)
- Must attach all 2307 originals/copies received during the year
- Must submit SAWT (Summary Alphalist) electronically through eBIRForms

**Excess CWT (overpayment)**: If total CWT + quarterly payments > annual income tax due, the difference is an overpayment. Taxpayer can elect to:
- Carry over to next year's quarterly returns
- Apply for refund (via BIR Form 1901 application)
- Apply for Tax Credit Certificate (TCC)

  → NOT a credit against income tax (different tax type)
