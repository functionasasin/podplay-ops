# Revenue Regulations No. 8-2018 — TRAIN Law Implementing Rules for Individual Income Tax
## Including 8% Income Tax Rate Option for Self-Employed Individuals and Professionals

Source: BIR Revenue Regulations No. 8-2018, issued February 20, 2018
Related: RMO No. 23-2018 (May 21, 2018), RMC No. 32-2018, RMC No. 50-2018
Fetched: 2026-02-28 (initial), 2026-02-28 (expanded with related issuances)

---

## Overview

RR No. 8-2018 implements the income tax provisions of RA 10963 (TRAIN Law) for individual taxpayers. It clarifies:
- The new graduated income tax rate tables (Schedule 1 and Schedule 2)
- The 8% income tax rate option — eligibility, election procedure, filing obligations
- Treatment of mixed-income earners
- New rules on itemized deductions and OSD under TRAIN
- Quarterly income tax computation (cumulative method)

---

## Part I — The 8% Income Tax Rate Option

### Eligible Taxpayers

The 8% income tax rate option is available to:
1. Purely self-employed individuals (sole proprietors in trade or business)
2. Professionals practicing their profession (lawyers, doctors, CPAs, engineers, consultants, etc.)
3. Mixed-income earners (for their BUSINESS INCOME PORTION ONLY) — see separate rules

**All of the following conditions must be satisfied:**
- (a) Individual (Single Proprietor, Professional, or Mixed Income Earner) earning from self-employment and/or practice of profession
- (b) Gross sales/receipts and other non-operating income did NOT exceed ₱3,000,000 during the taxable year
- (c) Registered and subject only to Percentage Tax under Section 116 of the NIRC; OR exempt from VAT or other percentage taxes
- (d) Taxpayer has signified his/her intention to elect the 8% income tax rate

**Gross receipts/sales threshold:** Must NOT exceed ₱3,000,000 during the taxable year (the VAT registration threshold).

### Ineligible Taxpayers

The 8% option is NOT available to:
1. Purely compensation income earners (employees)
2. VAT-registered taxpayers (regardless of income level)
3. Partners in General Professional Partnerships (GPPs):
   - The GPP itself is not subject to income tax
   - Partners are taxed on their distributive share from the GPP
   - That distributive share is already NET of cost and expenses incurred by the GPP
   - Partners cannot claim further deductions from their distributive share
   - Partners cannot avail of the 8% income tax rate option on their GPP distributive share
4. Individuals exempt from income tax (e.g., BMBEs under RA 9178)
5. Individuals subject to OTHER percentage taxes under the NIRC (Sections 117-128), other than Section 116

### Effect of the 8% Election

The 8% income tax rate is **in lieu of BOTH:**
1. The graduated income tax rates under Section 24(A)(2)(a)
2. The percentage tax under Section 116 (3% OPT)

**Therefore:** No separate BIR Form 2551Q (Quarterly Percentage Tax Return) is required when the 8% option is elected.

**Also:** No deductions (itemized or OSD) are available under the 8% option. The computation is based on GROSS sales/receipts (minus only sales returns/allowances and discounts, not business expenses).

### Computation of 8% Tax

**For purely self-employed (NO compensation income):**
```
8% Tax Due = (Gross Sales/Receipts + Other Non-Operating Income − ₱250,000) × 0.08
```
- The ₱250,000 deduction represents the tax-exempt bracket
- "Other non-operating income" includes interest income from business bank accounts, rent income on business properties used in trade, etc.
- Sales returns, allowances, and discounts are deducted before applying the formula (i.e., use NET gross receipts)

**For mixed-income earners (WITH compensation income):**
```
8% Tax on Business Income = (Gross Business Receipts + Other Non-Operating Income) × 0.08
```
- The ₱250,000 deduction does NOT apply
- Reason: The ₱250,000 exemption is already embedded in Bracket 1 of the graduated rate table applied to compensation income
- Compensation income is computed separately under graduated rates

**Note from RMC 50-2018:** The excess of ₱250,000 over the actual taxable compensation income is NOT deductible against the taxable income from business/practice of profession under the 8% option.

### How to Elect the 8% Option

**New business registrants:**
1. At time of registration using BIR Form 1901 (Application for Registration)
2. OR on the initial quarterly return (BIR Form 1701Q for Q1) of the taxable year after commencement of business
3. OR on the initial BIR Form 2551Q (Quarterly Percentage Tax Return) for Q1

**Existing taxpayers (election at the beginning of each taxable year):**
Option A: File BIR Form 1905 (Application for Registration Information Update) at the beginning of the taxable year, BEFORE the filing deadline of the percentage tax return, to end-date the quarterly percentage tax form type
Option B: Check the 8% election box (Item No. 16) in BIR Form 1701Q for Q1 when filing
Option C: File BIR Form 2551Q for Q1 with a zero-tax-due and a notation: "availing of the 8% income tax rate option for taxable year [YEAR]"

**Default:** If no election is made, the taxpayer is considered to have availed of the GRADUATED RATES.

**Procedure when registering via Form 1905:**
1. File BIR Form 1905 at the beginning of the year (before Q1 percentage tax deadline)
2. Submit Certificate of Registration (COR) for updating together with the Form 1905 (for first-time 8% electors)
3. If Form 1905 cannot be filed in time:
   - File BIR Form 2551Q for Q1 with zero-tax amount and notation of 8% election
   - Still file BIR Form 1701Q for Q1 with the 8% election signified

### Irrevocability

Once elected, the 8% option is **irrevocable for the entire taxable year.** The taxpayer cannot switch back to graduated rates for that year. If Q1 1701Q is filed with the 8% election ticked, this cannot be undone.

Exception: Mid-year breach of ₱3M threshold forces an automatic switch to graduated rates (see Part III).

At the start of the next taxable year, the election resets — the taxpayer must again choose between 8% and graduated.

### Annual Re-Election Required

The 8% option does NOT automatically carry over to the next year. It must be elected anew each taxable year:
- Failure to elect defaults to graduated rates
- Election is via BIR Form 1701Q Q1, BIR Form 2551Q Q1, or BIR Form 1905 at the start of the year

### Deregistration from Percentage Tax

If a previously percentage-tax-paying taxpayer elects the 8% option, they should:
1. File BIR Form 1905 to update registration and deregister from percentage tax
2. File a NIL BIR Form 2551Q for Q1 with the annotation that they elected the 8% income tax rate

## Part II — Mixed-Income Earners

### Definition

A mixed-income earner is a taxpayer who derives income from BOTH:
- Employment (compensation income) — mandatory graduated rates
- Business or practice of profession (self-employment income) — can choose 8% or graduated

### How Mixed-Income is Treated

**Compensation income:**
- Always subject to graduated income tax rates under Section 24(A)
- Cannot be taxed at 8%
- Personal and additional exemptions no longer apply (replaced by ₱250,000 exempt bracket in graduated table)

**Business/professional income:**
- CAN be taxed at 8% if gross receipts ≤ ₱3,000,000

**The ₱250,000 exemption for mixed-income earners:**
- The ₱250,000 threshold deduction is NOT applied to the business income portion of a mixed-income earner
- Reason: the ₱250,000 exemption is already factored into Bracket 1 of the graduated rate table for compensation income
- Formula for mixed-income earner business income under 8% option:
  `8% Tax on Business Income = Gross Business Receipts × 0.08` (NO ₱250,000 deduction)

**Total tax due for mixed-income earner (8% option elected for business):**
```
Total Tax = Tax on Compensation (graduated rates) + Tax on Business Income (8% of gross)
```

**Purely self-employed (no compensation income):**
```
8% Tax = (Gross Receipts − ₱250,000) × 0.08
```

### Annual Income Tax Filing for Mixed-Income Earners

Mixed-income earners file BIR Form 1701 (Annual ITR) — NOT Form 1701A. Form 1701A is only for:
- Purely self-employed under graduated rates with OSD or itemized deductions
- Purely self-employed under 8% option
- NOT for mixed-income earners

## Part III — Mid-Year Breach of the ₱3M Threshold

### When a Taxpayer Exceeds ₱3,000,000 Mid-Year

If a taxpayer who elected the 8% option finds that their CUMULATIVE gross receipts have exceeded ₱3,000,000 during the year:

1. The 8% option is **automatically disqualified** from that point forward
2. The taxpayer must recompute income tax under graduated rates for the ENTIRE year
3. All 8% quarterly payments already made are credited against the graduated-rate annual tax due as a TAX CREDIT (not merely as a deduction)
4. The taxpayer must update BIR registration to VAT-registered status within the month following the month of breach (e.g., breach in October → update registration in November)
5. Going forward (from the month of breach), the taxpayer is subject to 12% VAT (not 3% percentage tax)

**Percentage tax obligation for year of breach:**
- Percentage tax is due from January 1 through the month immediately preceding the month of VAT registration
- Retroactive 3% percentage tax must be paid for those months even though 8% option was originally elected

### BIR Registration Update

Once ₱3M is exceeded, the taxpayer must:
- File BIR Form 1905 to update registration from non-VAT to VAT
- Register for 12% VAT and file BIR Form 2550Q/2550M going forward
- Annual ITR for that year will be filed on BIR Form 1701 (not 1701A)

### VAT Reversal (Returning to Non-VAT)

A taxpayer who exceeded ₱3M and became VAT-registered can revert to NON-VAT status if their gross sales/receipts remain BELOW ₱3,000,000 for THREE (3) CONSECUTIVE TAXABLE YEARS following the year of breach. After completing those three years below threshold, the taxpayer may:
- File BIR Form 1905 to cancel VAT registration
- Re-elect to be subject to 3% percentage tax under Section 116
- Once non-VAT again, may elect the 8% option in subsequent years

## Part VI — Deduction Method Choice Under Graduated Rates

When a self-employed individual opts for the GRADUATED RATE (not 8%), they must choose between:
- **Itemized Deductions** (Sec. 34(A)-(K)): Actual substantiated expenses
- **OSD** (Sec. 34(L)): 40% of gross sales/receipts, no substantiation needed

**Key rule from RR 8-2018 (consistent with RR 16-2008):**
The deduction method choice made in the **first quarterly return (Q1 1701Q)** is **irrevocable for the entire taxable year.** The taxpayer cannot switch methods during the year.

**OSD for individuals:** 40% of GROSS SALES OR GROSS RECEIPTS (not gross income — cost of sales/services is NOT deducted first)

**When OSD is elected:** Taxpayer is NOT required to submit financial statements when filing the annual return; however, must keep records related to gross sales.

**When itemized deductions are elected:** Must substantiate each expense with receipts/invoices and maintain books of accounts.

## Part VII — Quarterly Income Tax Computation (Cumulative Method)

Per RMC 32-2018 (1701Q guidelines), quarterly income tax is computed on a CUMULATIVE basis:

**Q1 Return (Jan–Mar, due May 15):**
```
Taxable Income Q1 = Gross Receipts (Jan-Mar) − Deductions Q1
Tax Due Q1 = Apply graduated table to Taxable Income Q1
Tax Payable Q1 = Tax Due Q1 − CWT Credits Q1 − Prior quarterly payments (₱0 for Q1)
```

**Q2 Return (Jan–Jun, due Aug 15):**
```
Taxable Income Cumulative Q2 = Gross Receipts (Jan-Jun) − Deductions (Jan-Jun)
Tax Due Cumulative Q2 = Apply graduated table to Taxable Income Cumulative Q2
Tax Payable Q2 = Tax Due Cumulative Q2 − CWT Credits (Jan-Jun) − Tax Paid Q1
```

**Q3 Return (Jan–Sep, due Nov 15):**
```
Taxable Income Cumulative Q3 = Gross Receipts (Jan-Sep) − Deductions (Jan-Sep)
Tax Due Cumulative Q3 = Apply graduated table to Taxable Income Cumulative Q3
Tax Payable Q3 = Tax Due Cumulative Q3 − CWT Credits (Jan-Sep) − Tax Paid Q1 − Tax Paid Q2
```

**Annual Return (Jan–Dec, due Apr 15 following year):**
```
Taxable Income Annual = Gross Receipts (Full Year) − Deductions (Full Year)
Tax Due Annual = Apply graduated table to Taxable Income Annual
Tax Payable Annual = Tax Due Annual − CWT Credits (Full Year) − Tax Paid Q1 − Tax Paid Q2 − Tax Paid Q3
```
- If Tax Payable Annual > 0: Balance due, payable with annual return (or in 2 installments if > ₱2,000)
- If Tax Payable Annual < 0: Excess tax — taxpayer may claim refund or carry over as credit

**Installment payment rule:** If the annual tax due exceeds ₱2,000, the taxpayer may pay in TWO equal installments:
- First installment: Due on April 15 (filing date)
- Second installment: Due on July 15

## Part VIII — RMO No. 23-2018 — Step-by-Step Procedures for 8% Availment

Issued May 21, 2018. Prescribes operational policies for the 8% election.

### Procedure for New Registrant Electing 8%

1. File BIR Form 1901 (Application for Registration) with the 8% option box checked
2. Alternatively, file initial 1701Q for Q1 with 8% election ticked (Item 16 on the form)
3. No separate Form 2551Q required going forward

### Procedure for Existing Taxpayer Electing 8% at Beginning of Year

Step 1: At the beginning of the taxable year, BEFORE the Q1 percentage tax filing deadline:
   - File BIR Form 1905 at the RDO where registered
   - Submit original Certificate of Registration (COR) for updating
   - Request end-dating of the quarterly percentage tax (2551Q) form type

Step 2: File BIR Form 1701Q for Q1 with the 8% income tax rate option ticked

Step 3: If BIR Form 1905 could not be filed in time:
   - File BIR Form 2551Q for Q1 with ZERO amount and notation: "Availing of 8% Income Tax Rate Option for TY [year]"
   - Then file BIR Form 1701Q for Q1 with 8% option ticked

Step 4: NO further BIR Form 2551Q filings required for Q2 and Q3

### Procedure When ₱3M Threshold is Exceeded Mid-Year

Step 1: Determine the month in which cumulative gross receipts exceeded ₱3,000,000
Step 2: In the month FOLLOWING the breach month, file BIR Form 1905 to:
   - Update tax type from non-VAT to VAT
   - Cancel the OPT registration (Section 116)
   - Add VAT registration (12%)
Step 3: Recompute income tax for the entire year under graduated rates
Step 4: All prior 8% quarterly payments become tax credits on the annual return
Step 5: File BIR Form 2550Q/2550M (VAT returns) for months after breach
Step 6: File annual ITR on BIR Form 1701 (not 1701A) for the year of breach

## Part IX — RMC No. 32-2018 — BIR Form 1701Q (TRAIN Version) Key Fields

**Item 16 — Tax Regime Selection:**
- Option A: Graduated Income Tax rates
- Option B: 8% Income Tax rate on Gross Sales/Receipts (only for qualified taxpayers)
- Selection here is irrevocable for the year

**Item related to deduction method (under Option A only):**
- Itemized Deductions
- Optional Standard Deduction (40%)
- Selection here is irrevocable for the year

**Cumulative gross receipts fields:** Enter CUMULATIVE (year-to-date) amounts, not just the current quarter

**Prior quarter tax payments field:** Enter total of all prior quarterly payments for the year

**CWT credits field (Form 2307):** Enter cumulative CWT withheld year-to-date

**For 8% option — quarterly form procedure:**
- Item for ₱250,000 deduction: Input ₱250,000 ONLY if purely self-employed (no compensation income)
- If mixed-income earner: Leave ₱250,000 field BLANK (deduction does not apply)
- Net taxable income under 8% = Gross Receipts (minus ₱250,000 if applicable)
- Tax due = Net taxable × 8%
- Tax payable = Tax due − CWT credits − Prior quarterly payments

## Part X — RMC No. 50-2018 — Clarifications on RR 8-2018 and RR 11-2018

Issued to clarify certain provisions:

**On GPP Partners:**
- Partners comprising a GPP cannot claim further deductions from their distributive share in the net income of the GPP
- The GPP's net income is already net of all cost and expenses incurred by the GPP
- Partners are NOT allowed to avail of the 8% Income Tax rate option on their GPP distributive share

**On Threshold Determination:**
- The ₱3,000,000 threshold is based on GROSS SALES/RECEIPTS AND OTHER NON-OPERATING INCOME for the year
- "Other non-operating income" is included in the threshold computation
- If at any point during the year the cumulative gross receipts and other non-operating income exceeds ₱3M, the 8% option is disqualified

**On Mixed-Income Earner ₱250,000 Treatment:**
- The ₱250,000 is already incorporated in the first tier of the graduated income tax rates applicable to compensation income
- For mixed earners under 8%: The excess of ₱250,000 over actual taxable compensation income is NOT deductible against business income
- Example: If compensation taxable income is ₱150,000 (below ₱250,000), the remaining ₱100,000 of the ₱250,000 exemption is LOST — it cannot be applied to the business income side

**On Compensation Income:**
- Minimum wage earners remain exempt from income tax on: statutory minimum wage, holiday pay, overtime, night shift differentials, hazard pay
- Any other income received by minimum wage earners (e.g., honoraria, commissions) is taxable

**On De Minimis Benefits:**
- Benefits given in excess of the maximum amount allowed as de minimis benefits are included in "other benefits" subject to the ₱90,000 ceiling
- Any employee incentive is compensation subject to income tax unless exempt under special law

## Part XI — RR No. 11-2018 (Withholding Tax Implementing Rules)

Issued alongside RR 8-2018 to implement withholding tax provisions under TRAIN. Key provisions relevant to self-employed:

**Creditable Withholding Tax (CWT) Rates for Professional Fees:**

| Gross Income of Payee | EWT Rate |
|----------------------|----------|
| Those whose annual gross receipts (in the current year or the preceding year) do NOT exceed ₱3,000,000 | 5% |
| Those whose annual gross receipts (in the current year or the preceding year) EXCEED ₱3,000,000 | 10% |
| VAT-registered professionals | 10% |

**Procedure:**
1. Self-employed individuals must submit to their clients a sworn declaration (BIR Form 2316 substitute) stating whether their annual gross receipts are above or below ₱3M
2. This declaration determines the EWT rate applicable
3. The client issues BIR Form 2307 per quarter to the payee
4. The payee uses the Form 2307 amounts as tax credits when filing quarterly and annual returns

**Key change under EOPT Act (RA 11976, effective 2024):**
- Prior to EOPT: Withholding taxes had to be paid/remitted as a prerequisite for the payor to claim the expense as a deduction
- After EOPT: Withholding prerequisite for deductibility is REPEALED — expenses are deductible even if withholding was not done (though the payor is still liable for the unwithheld tax)
