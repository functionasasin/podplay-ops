# Itemized Deductions — Complete Reference Table
## Philippine Freelance & Self-Employed Income Tax Optimizer

**Legal basis:** NIRC Sections 34(A)–(K) and 36, as amended by TRAIN (RA 10963) and EOPT Act (RA 11976)
**Applies to:** All taxpayers electing Path A (Graduated + Itemized Deductions)
**Key EOPT change:** As of January 1, 2024 (RMC 60-2024), failure to withhold is NO LONGER a prerequisite for deductibility. Expenses are deductible if ordinary, necessary, and duly substantiated.

## Part 3: Interest Expense (34B) — Arbitrage Reduction Formula

**Legal basis:** NIRC Sec. 34(B)(1), as retained by TRAIN

### Formula
```
// Step 1: Identify interest income subject to final withholding tax
interest_income_final_taxed = sum of all interest earned on bank deposits, money market, etc.
                               that is subject to 20% final withholding tax (NOT interest from
                               business loans receivable that is included in gross income)

// Step 2: Compute arbitrage adjustment
arbitrage_adjustment = interest_income_final_taxed * 0.33

// Step 3: Compute deductible interest
deductible_interest_expense = MAX(0, gross_business_interest_expense - arbitrage_adjustment)
```

### Rationale
The 33% reduction prevents "tax arbitrage": borrowing money and deducting interest at the marginal rate (15%-35%) while earning interest income taxed at a flat 20% final tax. The 33% factor approximates the combined rate differential.

### Worked Example
```
Gross business interest expense: ₱50,000 (loan for business equipment)
Interest income on savings deposit (subject to 20% FWT): ₱30,000

Arbitrage adjustment = ₱30,000 × 0.33 = ₱9,900
Deductible interest = ₱50,000 − ₱9,900 = ₱40,100
```

### Engine Input Required
```
interest_income_subject_to_final_tax: decimal  # from bank deposits, money market, time deposits
gross_business_interest_expense: decimal        # total interest paid on business loans
// Engine computes:
arbitrage_adjustment = interest_income_subject_to_final_tax * 0.33
deductible_interest = MAX(0, gross_business_interest_expense - arbitrage_adjustment)
```

## Part 4: EAR (Entertainment, Amusement, and Recreation) Limit Computation

**Legal basis:** NIRC Sec. 34(A)(1); RR No. 10-2002

### Cap Formula
```
// For service providers (freelancers, professionals):
ear_cap = gross_receipts_this_year * 0.01

// For sellers of goods:
ear_cap = net_sales_this_year * 0.005
  where net_sales = gross_sales - sales_returns - sales_allowances

// Deductible EAR = MIN(actual_ear_expenses, ear_cap)
deductible_ear = min(actual_ear_expenses, ear_cap)
```

## Part 5: NOLCO (Net Operating Loss Carry-Over) Rules

**Legal basis:** NIRC Sec. 34(D)(3)

### NOLCO Availability Matrix
| Deduction Method | NOLCO Available? |
|-----------------|-----------------|
| Path A (Itemized Deductions) | YES — NOLCO may be claimed from prior years |
| Path B (OSD) | NO — OSD replaces all deductions including NOLCO |
| Path C (8% Flat Rate) | NO — 8% has no deductions; NOLCO suspended |

### Key Rules
1. **Net Operating Loss (NOL)** = Gross Income − Allowable Itemized Deductions, when result is negative (i.e., business expenses exceed gross income)
2. NOL from year Y may be carried forward as an additional deduction in years Y+1, Y+2, and Y+3
3. The NOL may be fully deducted in one of those years or spread across multiple years — but never carried back to prior years
4. If the taxpayer was exempt from income tax in year Y, the NOL from year Y is NOT available for carry-over
5. NOLCO is suspended (not available) in years when the taxpayer uses OSD or 8% option
6. When the taxpayer returns to itemized deductions, unexpired NOLCO from prior itemized years may be resumed

### NOLCO Tracking Algorithm
```
struct NOLCOEntry {
  origin_year: int          # year the loss was incurred
  original_amount: decimal  # original NOL from that year
  used_amounts: [           # list of {year, amount_used} pairs
    {year: int, amount: decimal}
  ]
  remaining: decimal        # original_amount - sum(used_amounts)
  expires_after: int        # origin_year + 3 (last year it may be used)
  is_suspended: bool        # true if taxpayer was exempt in origin_year
}

function apply_nolco(available_nolco_entries, net_income_before_nolco):
  """
  INPUTS:
    available_nolco_entries: list[NOLCOEntry], sorted by origin_year ascending (FIFO)
    net_income_before_nolco: decimal — income after all other deductions but before NOLCO

  RETURNS:
    nolco_applied: decimal — total NOLCO deducted this year
    remaining_entries: list[NOLCOEntry] — entries with updated remaining amounts

  RULES:
    1. Apply oldest entries first (FIFO — earliest expiry used first)
    2. Each entry: use MIN(entry.remaining, remaining_income)
    3. Stop when net_income_before_nolco is reduced to 0 (no negative NTI from NOLCO)
    4. Expired entries (current_year > entry.expires_after): skip and discard
    5. Suspended entries (entry.is_suspended): skip
  """
  nolco_applied = 0
  remaining_income = net_income_before_nolco
  for entry in available_nolco_entries:
    if entry.is_suspended:
      continue
    if current_year > entry.expires_after:
      continue  # expired
    if remaining_income <= 0:
      break
    use = min(entry.remaining, remaining_income)
    nolco_applied += use
    remaining_income -= use
    entry.remaining -= use
    entry.used_amounts.append({year: current_year, amount: use})
  return nolco_applied
```

### NOLCO Worked Example
```
Year 2023: Gross Receipts = ₱500,000; Itemized Deductions = ₱700,000
  Net Operating Loss = ₱500,000 - ₱700,000 = -₱200,000
  NOL 2023: ₱200,000 (expires after 2026)

Year 2024: Gross Receipts = ₱800,000; Other Itemized Deductions = ₱600,000
  Net Income Before NOLCO = ₱800,000 - ₱600,000 = ₱200,000
  Apply NOL 2023: USE ₱200,000 (now fully exhausted)
  Net Taxable Income = ₱0 → Income Tax = ₱0

Year 2025: Gross Receipts = ₱1,200,000; Other Itemized Deductions = ₱700,000
  Net Income Before NOLCO = ₱500,000
  NOL 2023 remaining: ₱0 (fully used in 2024)
  Net Taxable Income = ₱500,000 → Income Tax = graduated_tax(₱500,000)
```

## Part 6: Depreciation Rules and Useful Life Schedule

**Legal basis:** NIRC Sec. 34(F)

### Depreciation Methods Available
| Method | How It Works | When to Use |
|--------|-------------|-------------|
| Straight-Line (SL) | Cost divided by useful life | Most common; approved by BIR for all assets |
| Declining Balance (DB) | Rate applied to book value; maximum 200% of SL rate | Accelerates deductions; must use consistently |
| Sum of Years' Digits (SYD) | Accelerated based on years remaining / sum of years | Alternative acceleration method |

### Straight-Line Depreciation Formula
```
annual_depreciation_sl = (asset_cost - salvage_value) / useful_life_years

// Where:
//   asset_cost = purchase price + capitalized installation/delivery costs
//   salvage_value = estimated residual value at end of useful life (often ₱0 for electronics)
//   useful_life_years = estimated useful life (see BIR-accepted ranges below)

// Annual deduction:
depreciation_deduction = annual_depreciation_sl * business_use_percentage
// business_use_percentage = 1.00 for dedicated business assets; < 1.00 for dual-use assets
```

### BIR-Accepted Useful Life Ranges (Common Freelancer Assets)
| Asset Type | Minimum Life | Maximum Life | Typical BIR-Accepted Life | Notes |
|------------|-------------|-------------|--------------------------|-------|
| Desktop computer / workstation | 3 years | 5 years | 5 years | Standard for electronics |
| Laptop computer | 3 years | 5 years | 3–5 years | Shorter life accepted for portables |
| Mobile phone (business) | 2 years | 5 years | 3 years | Must show business use |
| Camera / video equipment | 3 years | 8 years | 5 years | For photographers/videographers |
| Audio equipment (recording) | 3 years | 8 years | 5 years | For podcasters, musicians |
| Furniture (office) | 5 years | 10 years | 10 years | Desks, chairs, bookshelves |
| Office equipment (printer, scanner) | 3 years | 10 years | 5 years | |
| Motor vehicle (sedan, SUV) | 5 years | 10 years | 5 years | Luxury vehicle cost ceiling ₱2,400,000 |
| Motor vehicle (motorcycle) | 3 years | 5 years | 5 years | For couriers/delivery businesses |
| Tools and equipment (specialized) | 3 years | 10 years | 5 years | Engineering, construction tools |
| Building improvements | 10 years | 40 years | Remaining lease term or 20 years | Leasehold improvements amortized over shorter of lease or 20 years |
| Software (purchased) | 1 year | 5 years | 3 years | May deduct in year of purchase if cost < ₱10,000 |

### Vehicle Cost Ceiling (Luxury Vehicles)
Per BIR Revenue Regulations guidance:
- **Sedans and passenger cars:** Cost ceiling = ₱2,400,000 (only this amount may be depreciated)
- **SUVs and utility vehicles:** Use actual cost if used for business; subject to BIR challenge if clearly luxury
- **Motorcycles:** No ceiling
- If actual cost exceeds ceiling: `depreciable_amount = min(actual_cost, ceiling); salvage_value = 0`

### Worked Depreciation Example (Laptop, SL)
```
Asset: MacBook Pro
Purchase price: ₱120,000
Salvage value: ₱0
Useful life: 5 years
Business use percentage: 80% (also used personally 20%)

Annual depreciation = (₱120,000 - ₱0) / 5 = ₱24,000
Business portion = ₱24,000 × 0.80 = ₱19,200 deductible per year

Year 1 deduction: ₱19,200
Year 2 deduction: ₱19,200
Year 3 deduction: ₱19,200
Year 4 deduction: ₱19,200
Year 5 deduction: ₱19,200
Total deducted: ₱96,000 (= ₱120,000 × 80%)
```

## Part 7: Home Office Deduction Rules

**Legal basis:** NIRC Sec. 34(A)(1); BIR Administrative Order No. 2-90; no specific Philippine RR on home office deduction — treated as general business expense

### Eligibility Requirements
1. The space must be used **exclusively and regularly** for business — not a dining table that is occasionally used for work
2. The space must be the **principal place of business** (where the taxpayer performs the primary activities of their profession/business)
3. For a rented residence: deductible = proportion of rent attributable to business space
4. For an owned residence: deductible = proportion of property costs (depreciation + RPT + insurance) attributable to business space

### Computation Formula
```
// Floor area method (most defensible for BIR):
business_use_percentage = business_floor_area_sqm / total_home_floor_area_sqm

// Monthly rent allocation:
monthly_home_office_deduction = monthly_total_rent * business_use_percentage

// Annual home office deduction:
annual_home_office_rent = monthly_home_office_deduction * 12_months

// Utility allocation (electricity, water, internet):
annual_home_office_utilities = (annual_total_utilities * business_use_percentage)
  // OR: dedicated business internet line is 100% deductible
```

### Required Documentation
- Floor plan showing the dedicated business area with dimensions labeled
- Lease/rental agreement (for renters) or property tax documents (for owners)
- Utility bills for the property
- Photographs showing the dedicated workspace (recommended for audit defense)
- Statement that the space is used exclusively and regularly for business

### Engine Behavior for Home Office
```
// Engine inputs:
has_home_office: boolean
home_total_area_sqm: decimal
home_business_area_sqm: decimal
monthly_home_rent: decimal          // 0 if owned
annual_home_utilities: decimal       // electricity, water (internet treated separately)

// Engine computation:
business_use_pct = home_business_area_sqm / home_total_area_sqm
deductible_rent = monthly_home_rent * business_use_pct * 12
deductible_home_utilities = annual_home_utilities * business_use_pct
// Note: internet is typically 100% deductible if primary use is business
```

### Engine Flag for Home Office
Trigger: `has_home_office = true`
Display MRF-003 variant: "Home office expenses are deductible only for the portion of your home used exclusively for business. BIR may challenge this deduction during audit — maintain photos and a floor plan showing the dedicated workspace."

## Part 9: Total Allowable Itemized Deductions — Computation Pseudocode

```
function compute_itemized_deductions(d: ItemizedDeductions, gross_receipts: decimal, is_service_provider: boolean, net_sales: decimal = 0):
  """
  INPUTS:
    d: ItemizedDeductions struct (from user input)
    gross_receipts: decimal — total business gross receipts for the year
    is_service_provider: boolean — true if professional/service, false if trader
    net_sales: decimal — for goods sellers: gross_sales - returns - allowances

  RETURNS:
    AllowableDeductions {
      subtotals: dict[str, decimal],   // deduction by section
      nolco_applied: decimal,
      total_allowable: decimal,
      ear_applied: decimal,            // actual EAR deducted (after cap)
      ear_cap: decimal,                // maximum allowable EAR
      interest_arbitrage_reduction: decimal,
      charitable_npo_cap: decimal,
      warnings: list[str]              // any capping or adjustment notices
    }
  """
  warnings = []

  // 34A — Ordinary and Necessary
  // EAR cap
  if is_service_provider:
    ear_cap = gross_receipts * 0.01
  else:
    ear_cap = net_sales * 0.005
  ear_applied = min(d.ear_expense, ear_cap)
  if d.ear_expense > ear_cap:
    warnings.append(f"EAR expenses capped at ₱{ear_cap:,.2f} (actual ₱{d.ear_expense:,.2f} claimed)")

  sec_34a = (
    d.salaries_wages
    + d.employer_sss_philhealth_pagibig
    + d.rent_expense
    + d.communication_expense
    + d.office_supplies
    + d.travel_expense
    + d.professional_fees_paid
    + d.advertising_expense
    + d.repairs_maintenance
    + d.utilities_expense
    + d.insurance_expense
    + ear_applied
    + d.home_office_expense
    + d.other_operating_expenses
  )

  // 34B — Interest (with arbitrage reduction)
  arbitrage_reduction = d.interest_income_final_taxed * 0.33
  deductible_interest = max(0, d.gross_interest_expense - arbitrage_reduction)
  sec_34b = deductible_interest

  // 34C — Taxes
  sec_34c = (
    d.local_business_tax
    + d.real_property_tax_business
    + d.professional_regulation_fees
    + d.other_deductible_taxes
  )

  // 34D — Losses (casualty and inventory only; NOLCO applied separately below)
  sec_34d_losses = d.casualty_loss_not_insured + d.inventory_write_offs

  // 34E — Bad Debts
  sec_34e = d.bad_debts_written_off

  // 34F — Depreciation
  sec_34f = 0
  for asset in d.depreciation_schedule:
    if asset.method == "SL":
      annual_dep = (asset.cost - asset.salvage_value) / asset.useful_life
    elif asset.method == "DB":
      // Declining balance on book value (requires tracking book value per asset)
      db_rate = min((1.0 / asset.useful_life) * 2.0, 1.0 / asset.useful_life * 2.0)
      book_value = asset.cost * (1 - db_rate) ** (current_year - asset.year_of_purchase)
      annual_dep = book_value * db_rate
    annual_dep_business = annual_dep * asset.business_use_pct
    sec_34f += annual_dep_business

  // 34H — Charitable Contributions
  // Government (no ceiling):
  charitable_gov = d.charitable_contributions_government

  // NPO (ceiling: 10% of NTI before this deduction):
  // Pre-NOLCO NTI estimate (approximate — NPO ceiling depends on NTI after other deductions)
  pre_charitable_nti = gross_receipts - sec_34a - sec_34b - sec_34c - sec_34d_losses - sec_34e - sec_34f
  charitable_npo_cap = max(0, pre_charitable_nti * 0.10)
  charitable_npo_applied = min(d.charitable_contributions_npo, charitable_npo_cap)
  if d.charitable_contributions_npo > charitable_npo_cap:
    warnings.append(f"NPO charitable contributions capped at ₱{charitable_npo_cap:,.2f} (10% of pre-deduction NTI)")
  sec_34h = charitable_gov + charitable_npo_applied

  // 34I — R&D
  // Immediate deductions:
  rd_immediate = d.rd_expenses_immediate
  // Amortized deductions (sum monthly amounts for current year):
  rd_amortized = sum(entry.monthly_amortization * months_in_current_year(entry) for entry in d.rd_expenses_amortized)
  sec_34i = rd_immediate + rd_amortized

  // 34J — Pension
  sec_34j = d.pension_trust_contributions

  // Subtotal before NOLCO
  total_before_nolco = (
    sec_34a + sec_34b + sec_34c + sec_34d_losses +
    sec_34e + sec_34f + sec_34h + sec_34i + sec_34j
  )

  // Net Income Before NOLCO (cannot go below 0 from regular deductions)
  net_income_before_nolco = max(0, gross_receipts - total_before_nolco)

  // 34D — NOLCO (applied after all other deductions)
  nolco_applied = apply_nolco(d.nolco_available, net_income_before_nolco)
  // Note: NOLCO cannot create a negative NTI — apply_nolco caps application at net_income_before_nolco

  // Final Net Taxable Income
  net_taxable_income = net_income_before_nolco - nolco_applied
  total_allowable = total_before_nolco + nolco_applied

  return AllowableDeductions {
    subtotals: {
      "34A_operating": sec_34a,
      "34B_interest": sec_34b,
      "34C_taxes": sec_34c,
      "34D_losses": sec_34d_losses,
      "34E_bad_debts": sec_34e,
      "34F_depreciation": sec_34f,
      "34H_charitable": sec_34h,
      "34I_rd": sec_34i,
      "34J_pension": sec_34j,
      "34D_nolco": nolco_applied,
    },
    nolco_applied: nolco_applied,
    total_allowable: total_allowable,
    net_taxable_income: net_taxable_income,
    ear_applied: ear_applied,
    ear_cap: ear_cap,
    interest_arbitrage_reduction: arbitrage_reduction,
    charitable_npo_cap: charitable_npo_cap,
    warnings: warnings,
  }
```

## Part 10: Itemized Deductions — Practical Notes for Common Freelancer Expense Types

### Internet and Phone (Most Common Freelancer Expense)
- **Dedicated business internet line:** 100% deductible if registered in business name
- **Home internet (personal + business):** Prorate. Acceptable proration methods:
  - Time-based: (business hours / total waking hours per day) × annual bill
  - Percentage: If business is primary use, 80-90% commonly accepted; be consistent
- **Smartphone:** Prorate business vs. personal use. 50% business use = 50% of plan cost and depreciation deductible

### Subscriptions and Software
- **Business software (Adobe Creative Cloud, Microsoft 365, project management tools):** Fully deductible as operating expense (not capitalized if annual subscription)
- **Domain registration and web hosting:** Fully deductible
- **Professional development platforms (Udemy, LinkedIn Learning):** Deductible ONLY if directly related to current registered business activity (not general self-improvement)
- **Cloud storage for business files:** Fully deductible

### Home Office Setup Costs
- **Desk, chair, filing cabinet purchased for home office:** Capitalize and depreciate (useful life 5–10 years); not expense in full if cost > ₱10,000 per item
- **Items below ₱10,000 per unit (de minimis capitalization):** May expense in full year of purchase (BIR practice; taxpayer should document this threshold consistently)
- **Ergonomic accessories, webcam, headset:** Expense if below ₱10,000; capitalize if above

### Foreign Income and Expenses
- **Income earned from foreign clients (Upwork, Fiverr, etc.):** Taxable as Philippine-sourced business income if the freelancer performs the work from the Philippines (NIRC Sec. 42(A) — income from sources within Philippines)
- **Expenses incurred in earning foreign-sourced income:** Fully deductible as ordinary business expenses
- **Foreign currency expenses:** Convert to Philippine Peso at the BSP rate on the date of transaction (or use average rate for the period if consistently applied)
