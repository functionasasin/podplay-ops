# Creditable Withholding Tax (CWT) — Expanded Withholding Tax (EWT) Rate Tables

**Legal basis:** NIRC Sec. 57–58; RR No. 2-98 (as amended); RR No. 11-2018 (TRAIN EWT implementing rules); RR No. 14-2002; RR No. 16-2023 (e-marketplace)
**Last updated in spec:** 2026-03-01
**Currency of rates:** All rates effective as of January 1, 2023 (post-TRAIN, post-RR 11-2018 amendments)

---

## 1. What Is EWT and How It Flows to Income Tax

Expanded Withholding Tax (EWT) is withheld by the PAYOR (client) from payments made to service providers. The amount withheld:
1. Is remitted directly to BIR by the payor using BIR Form 1601-EQ (quarterly EWT return)
2. Is documented to the payee via BIR Form 2307 (Certificate of Creditable Tax Withheld at Source)
3. Is claimed by the payee as a CREDIT against their income tax due on 1701Q (quarterly) and 1701/1701A (annual)

**The CWT is NOT a final tax.** It is a prepayment of the payee's income tax liability. The payee's actual income tax is computed under whichever tax path (A, B, or C) they elect; the EWT simply reduces the balance payable at filing time.

## 4. EWT Rate Determination Algorithm for Professional Fees

When the payor must determine which ATC applies to a freelancer's/professional's income payment, the following algorithm governs:

```
function determine_ewt_atc_individual_professional(
  payee_is_vat_registered: bool,
  payee_gross_income_current_year_estimate: Decimal,   // from Sworn Declaration or payor's best knowledge
  sworn_declaration_submitted: bool,
  sworn_declaration_period: date | null
) -> (atc: str, rate: Decimal, basis: str):

  // Step 1: VAT-registered always gets 10%
  if payee_is_vat_registered == true:
    return (atc="WI011", rate=0.10, basis="VAT-registered payee — WI011 applies regardless of gross income")

  // Step 2: Above ₱3M gross gets 10%
  if payee_gross_income_current_year_estimate > 3_000_000:
    return (atc="WI011", rate=0.10, basis="Payee's gross income > ₱3M — WI011 applies")

  // Step 3: No sworn declaration — conservative: use 10%
  // (RR 11-2018 Sec. 2.57.3: if payee does not submit sworn declaration, payor must use higher rate)
  if sworn_declaration_submitted == false:
    return (atc="WI011", rate=0.10, basis="No sworn declaration submitted — payor applied conservative rate WI011")

  // Step 4: Sworn declaration submitted, gross ≤ ₱3M — use 5%
  return (atc="WI010", rate=0.05, basis="Sworn declaration submitted, gross ≤ ₱3M — WI010 applies")
```

**Key rule:** The threshold for INDIVIDUAL payees is **₱3,000,000** gross income for the current year.
The threshold for NON-INDIVIDUAL payees is **₱720,000** gross income for the current year.

**Important distinction:** The EWT rate threshold uses GROSS INCOME (total receipts), not NET TAXABLE INCOME. This is the SAME ₱3M figure used for the 8% eligibility threshold, but it is independent — a taxpayer may be on graduated rates with the 5% EWT rate.

## 5. Sworn Declaration of Gross Income

**Legal basis:** RR No. 11-2018, Sec. 2.57.3; RMC No. 1-2021

The payee submits a written sworn declaration to each payor at the START of the calendar year (or before first payment) declaring:
1. Name, TIN, registered address
2. That their gross income for the current year is below ₱3,000,000 (for individuals) or ₱720,000 (for corporations)
3. That they are not VAT-registered
4. Signature under oath

**Filing deadline for Sworn Declaration:** January 15 of each taxable year (or before the first payment of the year, whichever is earlier).

**Consequences of NOT submitting:**
- Payor must withhold at the HIGHER rate (WI011 at 10%)
- Over-withholding is a cash flow problem for the payee but results in a larger CWT credit at filing
- NOT submitting is not a penalty trigger for the PAYEE, but it means more CWT withheld

**Mid-year gross income crosses ₱3M:**
- Payee must IMMEDIATELY notify each payor
- Payor switches to WI011 (10%) for all subsequent payments
- Previously withheld at 5% remains credited as-is (no retroactive adjustment)

**Engine note:** The engine does NOT generate or store sworn declaration documents. It uses the user-inputted ATC codes from their actual 2307 certificates to determine the rate applied. If the user reports discrepancies (e.g., client used 10% when 5% should apply), this is MRF-021.

## 6. EWT Base — What Is the "Income Payment"?

For most freelancers, the EWT base = the **gross amount of the invoice** for services rendered, EXCLUDING VAT if the payee is VAT-registered.

| Payee Type | Invoice Amount | VAT Status | EWT Base | EWT Amount |
|------------|---------------|------------|----------|------------|
| Non-VAT registered, ≤₱3M | ₱100,000 (invoice total) | No VAT | ₱100,000 | ₱5,000 (WI010, 5%) |
| Non-VAT registered, >₱3M | ₱100,000 (invoice total) | No VAT | ₱100,000 | ₱10,000 (WI011, 10%) |
| VAT-registered | ₱112,000 (incl. 12% VAT) | VAT-exclusive: ₱100,000 | ₱100,000 | ₱10,000 (WI011, 10%) |

**Formula:**
```
ewt_base = invoice_amount_excluding_vat
         = invoice_total / 1.12     // if invoice is VAT-inclusive
         = invoice_total            // if invoice is VAT-exclusive (non-VAT registered)

ewt_amount = ewt_base * ewt_rate
```

## 7. Timing of 2307 Issuance and Coverage

| Quarter Covered | Income Payment Period | 2307 Issuance Deadline |
|-----------------|-----------------------|------------------------|
| Q1 | January 1 – March 31 | April 20 |
| Q2 | April 1 – June 30 | July 20 |
| Q3 | July 1 – September 30 | October 20 |
| Q4 | October 1 – December 31 | January 20 (next year) |

**Key rule:** The 2307 covers the quarter in which the INCOME PAYMENT was made (accrual for VAT-registered, cash for non-VAT under old rules; post-EOPT, accrual basis generally for sales but CWT triggering event is still the actual payment date per RR 11-2018 Sec. 2.57.4).

**For the engine:** The user inputs the quarter (Q1–Q4) from the 2307 header field "For the Period From / To". The engine assigns the CWT to that quarter for crediting purposes.

## 8. SAWT — Summary Alphalist of Withholding Taxes

**What it is:** An electronic alphalist of all 2307 certificates received during the year, submitted electronically via eBIRForms (SAWT) when filing the annual return.

**Required attachments to annual return:** All original or certified copies of Form 2307 received plus the SAWT file.

**Content of SAWT per 2307:**
- Payor TIN
- Payor name
- Income payment amount
- Tax withheld amount
- ATC code
- Quarter covered

**Engine output:** The tool generates a SAWT-ready data export (CSV) for the user containing one row per 2307 entered, with all required fields populated. This eliminates manual alphalist preparation.

**Penalty for non-submission of SAWT:** BIR may disallow the CWT credit if SAWT is not submitted or does not match the 2307 copies. Engine must flag this requirement prominently in the results view.
